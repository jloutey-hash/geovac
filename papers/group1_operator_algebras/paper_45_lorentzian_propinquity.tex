\begin{abstract}
We construct truncated Krein spectral triples over the compact-temporal
Wick-rotated manifold $\Manifold = \sthree \times \SoneT$:\
$\Krein_{\nmax,\Nt} := \HGV^{\nmax} \otimes \C^{\Nt}$ is the
chirality-doubled Camporesi--Higuchi spinor space tensored with the
$\Nt$-mode Fourier truncation of $L^{2}(\SoneT)$, with fundamental
symmetry $\JL = \JL^{\spat} \otimes I_{\Nt}$ at the
Bizi--Brouder--Besnard~\cite{bizi_brouder_besnard2018} classification
$(m, n) = (4, 6)$, Lorentzian Dirac
$\DL = i(\gamma^{0} \otimes \partial_{t} + \DGV \otimes I_{\Nt})$
per van~den~Dungen~2016~\cite{vandungen2016} Proposition~4.1, and
operator system built from chirality-doubled scalar $3$-$Y$ spatial
multipliers tensored with momentum-diagonal temporal multipliers.
The natural device for a ``Lorentzian propinquity'' on this family is
to compress to the Krein-positive subspace $\Kplus$, where the Krein
product is a positive-definite Hilbert inner product, and to apply
Riemannian quantum-metric machinery to the compressed triple
$(\Pplus\Op^{L}\Pplus, \Kplus, \Pplus\DL\Pplus)$. Our main theorem
shows that this device fails structurally
(Theorem~\ref{thm:kplus_annihilation}):\ Krein-self-adjointness of
the anti-Hermitian spatial block forces
$\{\JL, \DGV \otimes I_{\Nt}\} = 0$, so the compression annihilates
the spatial Dirac, $\Pplus(\DGV \otimes I)\Pplus = 0$, while the
momentum-diagonal temporal multipliers commute with the surviving
temporal block;\ the compressed-Dirac Lipschitz seminorm therefore
vanishes \emph{identically} on the operator system \emph{built from
spatial and momentum-diagonal multipliers} (verified bit-exactly at
$(\nmax, \Nt) \in \{(2,3), (3,5)\}$:\ kernel $= 100\%$ of that
multiplier basis), and no quantum Gromov--Hausdorff-type distance is
defined on it. The annihilation is thus \emph{multiplier-algebra
specific}, not absolute:\ the Toeplitz temporal multipliers named below
as the open target \emph{survive} the same compression
($s_{\Kplus}(I_s \otimes S_q) = \omega_q$ exactly; Status note
2026-06-18), but the surviving seminorm carries no indefinite-signature
content even under a genuine temporal Krein involution --- a sharpened
``weakens-to-convention'' verdict. What does hold:\ the operator-level
$\JL$-equivariance of the Berezin/truncation pair;\ the structural
identity $[\DL, a_{s} \otimes a_{t}] = i[\DGV, a_{s}] \otimes a_{t}$,
which is precisely the diagnosis that the momentum-diagonal temporal
algebra is metrically invisible;\ and a spatial convergence statement
(Proposition~\ref{prop:spatial_conditional}):\ the spatial truncations
converge in van~Suijlekom's state-space Gromov--Hausdorff
distance~\cite{vs2021_jgp,gaudillot_vs2023} at the companion-paper
rate $O(\log\nmax/\nmax)$ --- unconditional, via the companion paper's
translation-seminorm theorem --- with the temporal factor a metrically
invisible spectator carried $\JL$-equivariantly. The genuine
Lorentzian/Krein quantum-metric convergence problem --- a temporal
multiplier algebra the Dirac can see (Toeplitz compressions of
$e^{iqt}$) and a Krein-compatible Lipschitz seminorm that does not
compress $\DL$ --- is stated as an open target with a sharpened
specification (\S~\ref{sec:open}). An earlier draft of this paper
claimed a convergence theorem through the compression device;\ that
claim is retracted (Remark~\ref{rem:history}).
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}\label{sec:intro}
% =====================================================================

This paper concerns the convergence-theorem leg of the
Lorentzian-extension programme for truncated spectral triples of the
Geometric Vacuum (GeoVac) framework~\cite{paper32} --- and proves
that the leg's most natural device fails structurally --- following the math.OA quartet Papers
38--40~\cite{paper38,paper39,paper40_unified} (Riemannian state-space
Gromov--Hausdorff convergence on $\SU(2)$, on tensor products of $\SU(2)$ triples at
distinct focal lengths, and on general compact Lie groups with
bi-invariant metric) and the Lorentzian extensions
Papers~42--44~\cite{paper42,paper43,paper44} (Tomita--Takesaki modular
structure on Riemannian truncations;\ four-witness Wick-rotation at
the operator-system level on the Lorentzian Krein wedge;\ operator-system
substrate of the Lorentzian Krein spectral triple at finite cutoff).

The dominant blocker for transferring quantum Gromov--Hausdorff
convergence~\cite{latremoliere_metric_st_2017,latremoliere2018,vs2021_jgp}
to the Lorentzian setting is the absence of a published metric on
Krein-signature spectral triples in their own right:\ the Riemannian
constructions crucially use the operator-norm Lipschitz seminorm via
the Dirac commutator $\opnorm{[D, \cdot]}$, well-defined on Hilbert
space but requiring an indefinite-inner-product analog on a Krein
space (with the appropriate Krein-self-adjointness of $\DL$ per
van~den~Dungen~2016~\cite{vandungen2016} /
BBB~\cite{bizi_brouder_besnard2018}). The natural attempt to sidestep
this gap is to compress to the Krein-positive subspace $\Kplus$,
where the Krein product reduces to a positive-definite Hilbert inner
product, and to apply the Riemannian machinery to the compressed
triple — the \emph{$\Kplus$-restricted weak-form} device
(Definition~\ref{def:weak_form_propinquity}). The central result of
this paper is that the sidestep fails structurally
(Theorem~\ref{thm:kplus_annihilation}):\ Krein-self-adjointness
forces the compression to annihilate the spatial Dirac, and the
resulting Lipschitz seminorm vanishes identically on the operator
system. The gap is real, and the repaired target is stated in
\S~\ref{sec:open}.

\subsection{What this paper does}\label{sec:what_this_paper_does}

We work on the compact-temporal Wick-rotated geometry $\Manifold$,
with Riemannian metric $ds^{2} = d\chi^{2} + \sin^{2}\chi\,d\Omega^{2}
+ d\tau^{2}$ at canonical circumference $T = 2\pi$. The Krein structure
$\JL$ is preserved as an \emph{algebraic} object (operator system plus
symmetry, in the BBB $(4, 6)$ classification) but no
Lorentzian-causal-structure claim is made;\ the closed timelike-curve
obstruction (Geroch~\cite{geroch1967}) is acknowledged explicitly via
the Wick-rotated convention. The paper's content is organized as
follows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(L1$'$)] The Lorentzian operator-system substrate
$\Op^{L}_{\nmax,\Nt,T}$ of Paper~44~\cite{paper44}, with propagation
number $2$ on the achievable envelope and trivial Krein-positivity at
the operator-multiplier level.
\item[(L2)] A joint central spectral Fej\'er kernel
$\Kjoint = \KSU_{\nmax} \otimes \KU_{\Nt,T}$ on $\SU(2) \times \Uone$.
The smoothing map is unital completely positive with
$\cbnorm{S_{\Kjoint}} = 1$;\ the value $2/(\nmax+1)$ is the maximum
of the SU(2) Plancherel \emph{mass distribution}, a distinct object
(Lemma~\ref{lem:L2}).
\item[(L3)] The joint Lipschitz comparison
$\opnorm{[\DL, a]} \le \Cthreejoint \norm{\nabla^{\mathrm{joint}} a}_{\infty}$
with $\Cthreejoint \le 1$, via the structural identity
$[\DL, a_{s} \otimes a_{t}] = i[\DGV, a_{s}] \otimes a_{t}$ — read
correctly, this identity is the \emph{diagnosis} that the temporal
factor is metrically invisible to the seminorm, not a simplification.
\item[(L4)] A joint Berezin map $\Bjoint_{\nmax,\Nt,T}$ (convolution
form) with positivity, contractivity, approximate identity (rate
$\gammajoint = O(\log\nmax/\nmax + T/\Nt)$), and spatial L3
compatibility;\ its temporal image is the Toeplitz compression
algebra, not the momentum-diagonal subalgebra of $\Op^{L}$
(Remark~\ref{rem:berezin_inequivalence}).
\item[(Main)] The annihilation theorem
(Theorem~\ref{thm:kplus_annihilation}), an anatomy of why the
natural tunneling-pair assembly produces no distance bound
(\S~\ref{sec:withdrawn_assembly}), and the spatial convergence
statement (Proposition~\ref{prop:spatial_conditional}), unconditional
via the companion paper's translation-seminorm theorem.
\end{enumerate}

\begin{remark}[History and retraction]\label{rem:history}
An earlier draft of this paper (May 2026;\ archived in the repository
history and the corresponding Zenodo deposit) claimed a
``$\Kplus$-restricted weak-form Lorentzian propinquity'' convergence
theorem through Definition~\ref{def:weak_form_propinquity}, resting
on theorem numbers that do not exist in the cited
source~\cite{latremoliere_metric_st_2017} and on a cb-norm statement
that conflated the kernel's mass distribution with its multiplier
symbol. A verification pass (2026-06-09;\ audit record in the
repository \texttt{CHANGELOG}, v3.106.0) found the degeneracy proved
in Theorem~\ref{thm:kplus_annihilation};\ the convergence claim is
retracted. Dependent claims in the companion papers carry Status
notes;\ the companion $\sthree$ paper's spatial convergence statement
survives in corrected form, now unconditional via its
translation-seminorm theorem.
\end{remark}

\subsection{Honest scope and named gaps}\label{sec:scope_intro}

The $\Kplus$-preservation algebra holds at the operator level
(Remark~\ref{rem:Kplus_tunnel_valid}) — but it preserves a structure
whose Lipschitz seminorm is identically zero
(Theorem~\ref{thm:kplus_annihilation}), so there is no metric content
to preserve. The named gaps:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(G1)] \emph{Lorentzian/Krein quantum-metric convergence.} A
quantum-metric structure on Krein-signature spectral triples is not in
the published literature as of June~2026, and the present paper shows
that the obvious $\Kplus$-compression device cannot supply it:\ the
compressed Dirac loses its entire spatial part by Krein-self-adjointness.
Two ingredients are jointly required:\ a temporal multiplier algebra
the Dirac can see (Toeplitz compressions of multiplication operators
$e^{iqt}$, in the Connes--van~Suijlekom $\Sone$
pattern~\cite{connes_vs2021}, rather than momentum-diagonal
polynomials), and a Krein-compatible Lipschitz seminorm that does not
compress $\DL$. This is the reopened named research target
(\S~\ref{sec:open}, Q1).
\item[(G2)] \emph{De-compactification limit $T \to \infty$.} The
norm-resolvent / spectral level of this limit is closed in
Paper~47~\cite{paper47}\,\S~4--5 (outer arrow:\ norm-resolvent
convergence of the Lorentzian Dirac on $\sthree\times\SoneT$ to
$\sthree\times\R_{t}$ with exponential tail
$O(e^{-|\mathrm{Im}\,z|\,T/2})$ on compact-support subspaces);\ that
arrow is operator-level, does not use the Lipschitz seminorm, and is
unaffected by the degeneracy. The metric level of the limit is open,
downstream of G1 (a ``zero-cost temporal extent element'' through the
degenerate quantity does not close it).
\item[(G3)] \emph{Cross-manifold extensions:\ CLOSED as structural
impossibility.}  Paper~32 Theorem~7.4 proves that the tensor product
$\Tcal_{\sthree} \otimes \Tcal_{\mathrm{Hardy}(S^{5})}$ cannot
preserve both the Riemannian-Dirac character of $S^3$ and the
$\pi$-free Bargmann character of $S^5$ within the standard NCG
framework.  The obstruction is categorical (Bertrand $+$ Fock/HO
rigidity), propagating to five structural layers
(Paper~24~\cite{paper24}\,\S~V).
\item[(G4)] \emph{Inner-factor calibration data} (the Higgs / Yukawa
selection question of the Connes--Marcolli SM construction) is
orthogonal to the present convergence theorem.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Related work}\label{sec:related_work}

Two adjacent Riemannian convergence frameworks exist.
Latr\'emoli\`ere's quantum Gromov--Hausdorff
propinquity~\cite{latremoliere2018} (Trans. AMS 2016) and its
metric-spectral-triple extension~\cite{latremoliere_metric_st_2017}
(Adv.~Math. 2022) define convergence via tunnels carried by quantum
metric spaces with quantum isometries;\ the most recent continuity
result in that line is Farsi--Latr\'emoli\`ere~2025~\cite{farsi_latremoliere2025},
treating analytic paths of Riemannian metrics. Separately,
van~Suijlekom's state-space Gromov--Hausdorff
framework~\cite{vs2021_jgp} bounds the distance between truncated and
full state spaces via approximation maps in both directions;\ this is
the framework the constructions of this paper (and of the companion
Papers~38--40) actually instantiate — Latr\'emoli\`ere's propinquity
imposes tunnel hypotheses not available on these truncations. None of
these constructions extend to Krein signature.

Connes--van~Suijlekom~2021~\cite{connes_vs2021} introduces the
operator-system viewpoint on spectral truncations and proves on the
circle $\Sone$ that the truncated state-space distance dominates the
Kantorovich metric, deferring Gromov--Hausdorff convergence
(``reported elsewhere'');\ the convergence itself is established in
van~Suijlekom~\cite{vs2021_jgp} and extended to all compact metric
groups by Gaudillot-Estrada--van~Suijlekom~\cite{gaudillot_vs2023}.
The adjacent program~\cite{leimbach_vs2024,hekkelman2021,hekkelman_mcdonald2024b,toyota2023}
covers $\mathbb{T}^{d}$, truncated circle geometry, noncommutative
integrals on truncations, and groups with polynomial growth, with the
$4/\pi$ rate asymptote on compact Lie groups computed in
Paper~40~\cite{paper40_unified} (also adapted to $\SU(2)$ in
Paper~38~\cite{paper38};\ both with the mass-distribution/multiplier-symbol
distinction of Lemma~\ref{lem:L2}). All of these are strictly Riemannian.

The Lorentzian Krein-spectral-triple framework derives from
Strohmaier~\cite{strohmaier2006} (axiomatic foundation),
Franco--Eckstein~\cite{franco_eckstein2014} (algebraic causality),
van~den~Dungen~2016~\cite{vandungen2016} (Krein spectral triples and
fermionic action; the Wick-rotation lift used here),
Bizi--Brouder--Besnard~2018~\cite{bizi_brouder_besnard2018}
($(m, n)$ classification of indefinite signatures used at $(4, 6)$
West-coast in the present paper),
Devastato--Lizzi--Martinetti~\cite{devastato_lizzi_martinetti2018},
and the recent SU(1,1) construction~\cite{de_groot2026_su11}; none of
these works define a metric on the algebra of operator systems
extracted from a Krein triple. The closest algebraic alternative is
the Nieuviarts twist morphism~\cite{nieuviarts2025a,nieuviarts2025b_proceedings},
which produces pseudo-Riemannian spectral triples from Riemannian ones
via a twist on twisted spectral triples;\ this is not a metric and
moreover (as noted in Paper~42~\S~3.2) is restricted to even-dimensional
manifolds, so does not apply to $\sthree$ at KO-dimension~3.

The adjacent synthetic Lorentzian Gromov--Hausdorff program of
Mondino--S\"amann~\cite{mondino_samann2025} extends
Minguzzi--Suhr's earlier work~\cite{minguzzi_suhr2024} to Lorentzian pre-length
spaces via $\epsilon$-nets of causal diamonds; a downstream pure-metric
extension via the intrinsic timed-Hausdorff distance appears
in Che--Perales--Sormani~\cite{che_perales_sormani2025}. This is
conceptually adjacent (all are ``Lorentzian Gromov--Hausdorff-type
convergence'') but lives on a categorically different mathematical
object:\ pre-length spaces with causal-diamond / timed-Hausdorff
geometry, not operator-algebraic spectral triples. The two families
of constructions are not in competition;\ neither implies the other.

\subsection{Compact-temporal manifold and Wick-rotated metric}
\label{sec:manifold}

The compact-temporal Lorentzian / Wick-rotated manifold is
$\Manifold$, with $\sthree = \SU(2)$ the round unit three-sphere
(spatial slot) and $\SoneT = \R / T\Z$ the temporal circle of
circumference $T$. The Riemannian / Wick-rotated metric is
\begin{equation}\label{eq:wick_metric}
   ds^{2} \;=\; d\chi^{2} + \sin^{2}\chi\, d\Omega_{2}^{2} + d\tau^{2},
\end{equation}
with $\chi \in [0, \pi]$ the polar angle on $\sthree$, $d\Omega_{2}^{2}$
the round metric on the $\mathbb{S}^{2}$ base of the Hopf fibration,
and $\tau \in \R/T\Z$ the temporal coordinate. We treat the Krein
structure $\JL$ defined below as an \emph{algebraic} object that
encodes the BBB $(4, 6)$ chirality grading without claiming a
Lorentzian-causal interpretation of the underlying metric:\ Geroch's
no-CTC obstruction~\cite{geroch1967} prevents a Lorentzian metric on
$\Manifold$ in which $\SoneT$ is timelike, so the present construction
operates on the Wick-rotated geometry and lifts the Krein structure as
indefinite-inner-product datum (per Strohmaier~\cite{strohmaier2006},
vdD~\cite{vandungen2016}, BBB~\cite{bizi_brouder_besnard2018}).

The canonical choice $T = 2\pi$ matches the
Bisognano--Wichmann~\cite{bisognano_wichmann1976} modular period at
unit surface gravity;\ this is consistent with the four-witness arc
closure of Paper~43~\cite{paper43}. The proof of the main theorem
holds for any $T > 0$;\ explicit dependence on $T$ enters the
$\Uone$ factor of the joint mass-concentration moment as $O(T/\Nt)$.

\subsection{Krein space and fundamental symmetry}\label{sec:krein_space}

Per Paper~43~\cite{paper43}\S~2 / Paper~44~\cite{paper44}\S~2, the
Krein space is
\begin{equation}\label{eq:Krein_def}
   \Krein_{\nmax,\Nt} \;:=\; \HGV^{\nmax} \otimes \C^{\Nt},
\end{equation}
where $\HGV^{\nmax}$ is the chirality-doubled
Camporesi--Higuchi~\cite{camporesi_higuchi1996} spinor Hilbert space
truncated at the $\nmax$-th Fock shell, of dimension
\begin{equation}\label{eq:H_GV_dim}
   \dim\HGV^{\nmax} \;=\; \tfrac{2}{3}\,\nmax(\nmax+1)(\nmax+2),
\end{equation}
and $\C^{\Nt}$ is the $\Nt$-mode Fourier truncation of
$L^{2}(\SoneT)$. The fundamental symmetry is
\begin{equation}\label{eq:J_def}
   \JL \;=\; \JL^{\spat} \otimes I_{\Nt},
\end{equation}
with $\JL^{\spat} = \gamma^{0}$ the chirality-swap in the
Peskin--Schroeder chiral basis, $(\JL^{\spat})^{2} = +I$.

The BBB $(m, n)$ classification at signature $(s, t) = (3, 1)$
West-coast assigns $(m, n) = (s + t, t - s \bmod 8) = (4, 6)$ via
BBB~\cite{bizi_brouder_besnard2018} Table~3, fixing
$\epsilon = +1, \epsilon'' = -1, \kappa = -1, \kappa'' = +1$. These
signs were verified bit-exact at finite cutoff in
Paper~43~\S~5~\cite{paper43}.

\subsection{Lorentzian Dirac operator}\label{sec:lorentzian_dirac}

The Lorentzian Dirac, per vdD~2016~\cite{vandungen2016} Prop.~4.1
applied at signature $(3, 1)$, reads
\begin{equation}\label{eq:DL_def}
   \DL \;=\; i\,\bigl(\gamma^{0} \otimes \partial_{t}
                       + \DGV \otimes I_{\Nt}\bigr),
\end{equation}
with $\partial_{t}$ Fourier-diagonal anti-Hermitian on $\C^{\Nt}$:\
in the momentum eigenbasis $\{|k\rangle : k \in \Z, |k| \le \Kmax\}$
with $\Kmax := \lfloor(\Nt - 1)/2\rfloor$ for odd $\Nt$ or $\Nt/2$ for
even $\Nt$,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:partial_t_def}
   \partial_{t} \;=\; i\,\mathrm{diag}\bigl(\omega_{k}\bigr),
   \qquad \omega_{k} = 2\pi k / T,
\end{equation}
so $\partial_{t}^{\dagger} = -\partial_{t}$. The spatial part $\DGV$ is
the truthful Camporesi--Higuchi spatial Dirac on $\HGV^{\nmax}$,
diagonal in the spinor basis with eigenvalue $\chi(n + 1/2)$ where
$\chi \in \{+1, -1\}$ is the spinor chirality and $n$ the Fock-shell
index. Krein-self-adjointness $\DL^{\times} := \JL\,\DL^{\dagger}\,\JL
= \DL$ is verified by direct computation:\ $\gamma^{0\,\dagger} =
\gamma^{0}$ (Hermitian chirality-swap), $\{\gamma^{0}, \DGV\} = 0$ in
the chiral basis, $\partial_{t}^{\dagger} = -\partial_{t}$, and
$\JL^{2} = +I$ combine to give the stated identity. The sign $i^{t} =
+i$ in (\ref{eq:DL_def}) is forced by this requirement (an opposite
sign would give an anti-Krein-self-adjoint $\DL$);\ see
Paper~43~\S~3~\cite{paper43} for the explicit derivation.

\subsection{Truncated Lorentzian operator system}\label{sec:op_system}

Per Paper~44~\cite{paper44}, the truncated Lorentzian operator system
is the pure-tensor span
\begin{equation}\label{eq:op_system_def}
   \Op^{L}_{\nmax,\Nt,T}
   \;:=\; \mathrm{span}_{\C}\bigl\{
            M^{\spat}_{N,L,M} \otimes M^{\temp}_{p}
            \;:\; (N, L, M) \in \mathcal{I}^{\spat}_{\nmax},\;
                  p \in \{0, 1, \ldots, \Nt - 1\}
         \bigr\},
\end{equation}
where each spatial multiplier
$M^{\spat}_{N,L,M} = \mathrm{blkdiag}(W_{N,L,M}, W_{N,L,M})$ is the
chirality-doubled lift of the Avery--Wen--Avery Weyl-sector
multiplier $W_{N,L,M}$ (the Clebsch--Gordan / Avery 3-$Y$ integral
construction of Paper~32~\cite{paper32}\,\S~III), and the temporal
multipliers $M^{\temp}_{p}$ are momentum-polynomial diagonal:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:temp_multipliers}
   M^{\temp}_{p} \;=\; \mathrm{diag}\bigl(\omega_{k}^{p}\bigr)_{|k| \le \Kmax}.
\end{equation}
Vandermonde invertibility at $\Nt$ distinct frequencies $\omega_{k}$
ensures that the span (\ref{eq:op_system_def}) coincides with the
span of Fourier-mode indicators $M^{\temp}_{q} =
\mathrm{diag}(\delta_{k,q})$, and we use whichever basis is more
convenient.

By Paper~44~\S~3, $\Op^{L}_{\nmax,\Nt,T}$ is a $\ast$-closed linear
subspace of $\Bcal(\Krein_{\nmax,\Nt})$, contains the identity, and
is \emph{not} multiplicatively closed (a witness pair is constructed
in Paper~44~\S~5). The propagation number is
$\prop(\Op^{L}_{\nmax,\Nt}) = 2$ on the Weyl-doubled achievable
envelope, matching the Riemannian Paper~32~\S~III prop=2 verbatim;
$\prop = \infty$ on the full $\Bcal(\Krein)$ envelope due to the
chirality-doubling and the commutative temporal subalgebra.

\subsection{Joint central spectral Fej\'er kernel}\label{sec:joint_fejer}

The SU(2) factor is the Paper~38~\cite{paper38}\,L2-DEF central
spectral Fej\'er kernel
\begin{equation}\label{eq:K_SU2_def}
   \KSU_{\nmax}(g) \;:=\; \frac{1}{Z^{\SU(2)}_{\nmax}}
   \Bigl|\sum_{j \le j_{\max}}
      \sqrt{2j+1}\,\chi_{j}(g)\Bigr|^{2},
\end{equation}
with $j_{\max} = (\nmax-1)/2$ and
$Z^{\SU(2)}_{\nmax} = \nmax(\nmax+1)/2$. Its \emph{Plancherel mass
distribution} (the squared Plancherel weights of the amplitude
$h = \sum_{j \le j_{\max}}\sqrt{2j+1}\,\chi_{j}$, a probability
distribution on the truncated dual) is
\begin{equation}\label{eq:K_SU2_plancherel}
   m^{\SU(2)}_{\nmax}(j) \;=\;
   \frac{2j + 1}{Z^{\SU(2)}_{\nmax}}\,\mathbf{1}_{\{j \le j_{\max}\}}.
\end{equation}
The multiplier (Fourier) symbol $\sigma^{\SU(2)}_{\nmax}(j')$ of
$\KSU$ — the scalar by which convolution against $\KSU$ acts on the
$j'$-isotypic block — is a \emph{different} object:\ it is given by
the Clebsch--Gordan sum recorded in Appendix~\ref{app:plancherel},
satisfies $\sigma^{\SU(2)}(0) = 1$ (forced by normalization),
$0 \le \sigma^{\SU(2)} \le 1$, and is supported on
$j' \le 2 j_{\max}$. At $\nmax = 2$ the values are
$(1, \sqrt2/3, 2/9)$ at $j' = (0, \tfrac12, 1)$. The two objects must
not be conflated (Lemma~\ref{lem:L2};\
\S~\ref{sec:withdrawn_assembly}).

The $\Uone$ factor is the standard Fej\'er kernel on $\SoneT$,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:K_U1_def}
   \KU_{\Nt, T}(\theta) \;:=\; \frac{1}{\Nt}
   \Bigl|\sum_{|k| \le \Kmax} e^{i \, 2\pi k\theta / T}\Bigr|^{2},
   \qquad \Kmax = (\Nt - 1)/2 \;\; (\Nt \text{ odd}),
\end{equation}
with multiplier symbol
\begin{equation}\label{eq:K_U1_plancherel}
   \sigma^{\Uone}_{\Nt}(k) \;=\;
   \max\!\Bigl(0,\, \tfrac{\Nt - |k|}{\Nt}\Bigr)
\end{equation}
and uniform
Plancherel mass distribution $m^{\Uone}_{\Nt}(k) = \Nt^{-1}
\mathbf{1}_{\{|k| \le \Kmax\}}$. The joint kernel is the tensor
product
\begin{equation}\label{eq:K_joint_def}
   \Kjoint_{\nmax,\Nt,T}(g, \theta)
   \;:=\; \KSU_{\nmax}(g) \cdot \KU_{\Nt, T}(\theta),
\end{equation}
with factorized multiplier symbol
\begin{equation}\label{eq:K_joint_plancherel}
   \sigma^{\Kjoint}(j', k)
   \;=\; \sigma^{\SU(2)}_{\nmax}(j') \cdot
         \sigma^{\Uone}_{\Nt}(k),
\end{equation}
and factorized mass distribution $m^{\Kjoint} = m^{\SU(2)} \cdot
m^{\Uone}$. Both factors are non-negative, normalized ($L^{1}$ norm
$= 1$), and central (class functions on their respective groups);\
the tensor product inherits these properties on $\SU(2) \times \Uone$.
The two conventions must not be mixed:\ pairing the SU(2)
\emph{mass} maximum ($2/(\nmax+1)$ at $j_{\max}$) with the $\Uone$
\emph{symbol} maximum ($1$ at $k = 0$) produces a hybrid quantity
that is neither a mass maximum (that is
$2/(\nmax+1) \cdot \Nt^{-1}$) nor a cb-norm (that is $1$;\
Lemma~\ref{lem:L2}).

\subsection{Krein-positive subspace and weak-form propinquity}
\label{sec:Kplus_definition}

The Krein-positive subspace is
\begin{equation}\label{eq:Kplus_def}
   \Kplus \;:=\; \{|\psi\rangle \in \Krein \;:\;
                  \JL|\psi\rangle = +|\psi\rangle\},
\end{equation}
the $+1$-eigenspace of $\JL$, of dimension
$\dim\Kplus = \dim\Krein / 2 = \tfrac{1}{3}\,\nmax(\nmax+1)(\nmax+2)\Nt$.
The Krein inner product
$\langle \psi, \phi \rangle_{\JL} := \langle \psi, \JL \phi\rangle$
restricted to $\Kplus$ reduces to the standard Hilbert inner product
$\langle \psi, \phi\rangle$, since $\JL|\phi\rangle = |\phi\rangle$ on
$\Kplus$. Hence $\Kplus$ is a genuine Hilbert space.

The orthogonal projection onto $\Kplus$ is $\Pplus := (I + \JL)/2$
(and dually $\Pminus := (I - \JL)/2$ for the $-1$-eigenspace).

\begin{definition}[$\Kplus$-restricted weak-form Lorentzian propinquity]
\label{def:weak_form_propinquity}
For two Lorentzian truncated Krein metric spectral triples
$\Tcal^{L}_{i} = (\Op^{L}_{i}, \Krein_{i}, D^{L}_{i}, J^{L}_{i})$ with
the construction above, the $\Kplus$-restricted weak-form Lorentzian
propinquity is
\begin{equation}\label{eq:weak_form_propinquity_def}
   \Lprop\bigl(\Tcal^{L}_{1}, \Tcal^{L}_{2}\bigr)
   \;:=\; \Lambdab\bigl(\Tcal^{+}_{1}, \Tcal^{+}_{2}\bigr),
\end{equation}
where $\Tcal^{+}_{i} := (P^{+}_{i} \Op^{L}_{i} P^{+}_{i}, \Krein^{+}_{i},
P^{+}_{i} D^{L}_{i} P^{+}_{i})$ is the $\Kplus$-compressed candidate
triple and $\Lambdab$ is a quantum Gromov--Hausdorff-type distance on
metric spectral triples (the applicable Riemannian framework is
van~Suijlekom's state-space distance~\cite{vs2021_jgp};\
Latr\'emoli\`ere's propinquity~\cite{latremoliere_metric_st_2017}
imposes tunnel hypotheses not available here).
\end{definition}

Definition~\ref{def:weak_form_propinquity} is the natural
candidate — and it is \emph{degenerate}:\
Theorem~\ref{thm:kplus_annihilation} shows the compressed triple
$\Tcal^{+}$ has Lipschitz seminorm identically zero on its operator
system, so it is not a compact quantum metric space in any framework
(Latr\'emoli\`ere's or van~Suijlekom's), and $\Lprop$ as defined is
not a distance. The operator-level $\JL$-equivariance that makes the
compression well-defined (every Berezin-image operator and every
truncation projection commutes with $\JL$;\ Paper~44~\S~4) is genuine
— but what it preserves carries no metric content.

\subsection{Joint gradient norms}\label{sec:gradient_norms}

For a pure-tensor symbol $f = f_{s} \otimes f_{t}$ on $\Manifold$,
the $L^{1}$-additive and $L^{2}$-Pythagorean joint gradient norms are
\begin{align}
   \norm{\nabla^{\mathrm{joint}, L^{1}} f}_{\infty}
   &:= \norm{\nabla_{x} f_{s}}_{\infty} \cdot \norm{f_{t}}_{\infty}
     + \norm{f_{s}}_{\infty} \cdot \norm{\partial_{t} f_{t}}_{\infty},
   \label{eq:joint_L1} \\
   \norm{\nabla^{\mathrm{joint}, L^{2}} f}_{\infty}
   &:= \sup_{(x, t)} \sqrt{
        |\nabla_{x} f(x, t)|^{2} + |\partial_{t} f(x, t)|^{2}}.
   \label{eq:joint_L2}
\end{align}
For general $f$, both extend by linearity over the Peter--Weyl
$\times$ Fourier expansion and obey the standard triangle inequalities.
The $L^{2}$ form is dominated by the $L^{1}$ form via
$\sqrt{a^{2} + b^{2}} \le a + b$ for $a, b \ge 0$;\ both forms
upper-bound the same Lipschitz seminorm with the same constant in the
proof of Lemma~\ref{lem:L3}.

\section{Lemma L1$'$: the operator-system substrate}\label{sec:L1prime}

the four subsequent lemmas operate. It is the closure result of

\begin{lemma}[L1$'$, Paper~44]\label{lem:L1prime}
The collection
$\Tcal^{L}_{\nmax,\Nt,T} := (\Op^{L}_{\nmax,\Nt,T},
\Krein_{\nmax,\Nt}, D^{L}_{\nmax,\Nt,T}, J^{L}_{\nmax,\Nt})$ defined in
\S~\ref{sec:krein_space}--\ref{sec:op_system} is a Lorentzian
truncated Krein metric spectral triple in the sense of
vdD~\cite{vandungen2016} / BBB~\cite{bizi_brouder_besnard2018}:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] $\Op^{L}$ is a unital $\ast$-closed linear subspace of
$\Bcal(\Krein)$.
\item[(b)] $\DL$ is Krein-self-adjoint, $\DL^{\times} = \DL$.
\item[(c)] $\JL$ is a fundamental symmetry, $\JL^{2} = +I$,
$\JL^{\dagger} = \JL$.
\item[(d)] The propagation number $\prop(\Op^{L}) = 2$ on the

\item[(e)] Every multiplier in $\Op^{L}$ commutes with $\JL$:\
$[\JL, a] = 0$ bit-exact for every $a \in \Op^{L}$.
\item[(f)] At $\Nt = 1$, the construction reduces bit-exactly to the
Riemannian chirality-doubled truncated spectral triple on
$\HGV^{\nmax}$ (Frobenius residual $0.0$ in float64).
\end{enumerate}
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
(a)--(c) are direct from the construction and Paper~43~\S~2,3,5.
(d) is Paper~44~\S~3 (via the structural identity $\dim((\Op^{L})^{2})
= \dim_{\Weyl}^{2} \cdot \Nt$ on the Weyl-doubled envelope).
(e) follows from $[\gamma^{0}, M^{\spat}] = 0$ (chirality-swap acts
trivially on the diagonal block-doubled spatial multiplier) and
$[I_{\Nt}, M^{\temp}] = 0$ (trivially). (f) is Paper~44~\S~2 (load-bearing
falsifier F1). \qedhere
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Krein-positivity at the operator-multiplier level is
trivial]\label{rem:Kplus_trivial}
By Lemma~\ref{lem:L1prime}(e), every operator $a \in \Op^{L}$
preserves both $\Kplus$ and $\Kminus$. Consequently, the
$\Kplus$-restricted operator system $\Op^{L,+} := \Pplus
\Op^{L} \Pplus$ has the same propagation number $2$ as the
unrestricted system. No quantum-metric machinery, however, applies
to $(\Op^{L,+}, \Kplus, \Pplus \DL \Pplus)$:\ the
compressed Dirac $\Pplus \DL \Pplus$ loses its entire spatial part
(Theorem~\ref{thm:kplus_annihilation}(i)), the induced Lipschitz
seminorm vanishes identically, and no quantum-metric machinery —
Latr\'emoli\`ere's or van~Suijlekom's — has its hypotheses satisfied
on this triple.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Validity of the $\Kplus$-restricted tunneling pair]
\label{rem:Kplus_tunnel_valid}
Every element of the standard Latr\'emoli\`ere tunneling pair
$(\Bjoint, \Pjoint)$ used in \S~\ref{sec:main} commutes with $\JL$
at the operator level:\ $[\JL, \Bjoint(f)] = 0$ for every
$f \in C^{\infty}(\Manifold)$ (this is Lemma~\ref{lem:L4}(e), the
joint Berezin's Krein-positivity preservation, proved factor-wise via
$[\gamma^{0}, M^{\spat}_{N,L,M}] = 0$ on the chirality-doubled spatial
block and $[I_{\Nt}, M^{\temp}_{q}] = 0$ on the temporal slot), and
$[\JL, \Pjoint] = 0$ because $\Pjoint = P^{\SU(2)}_{\nmax} \otimes
P^{\Uone}_{\Nt}$ is a tensor of Stinespring orthogonal projections
into the same multiplier-label subspaces preserved by $\Bjoint$.
Consequently the $\Kplus$-restricted pair
$(\Pplus\,\Bjoint(\cdot)\,\Pplus,\, \Pplus\,\Pjoint(\cdot)\,\Pplus)$ is
well-defined as a UCP pair on the compressed objects of
Definition~\ref{def:weak_form_propinquity} — the trivial-multiplier
$\Kplus$-preservation feature inherited from the L1$'$ substrate
construction of Paper~44~\cite{paper44}\,\S~3--4. The equivariance
is genuine, but the structure it
preserves carries a Lipschitz seminorm that is identically zero
(Theorem~\ref{thm:kplus_annihilation}), so there is no metric
bookkeeping to transport. Equivariance without separation is the
precise failure mode.
\end{remark}

\subsection{Lemma L2:\ joint cb-norm of the central Schur multiplier}
\label{sec:L2}
% ----------------------------------------------------------

\begin{lemma}[L2:\ smoothing map and mass distribution]
\label{lem:L2}
For all $\nmax \ge 1$ and odd $\Nt \ge 1$:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] The joint kernel $\Kjoint$ is non-negative, central, and
normalized:\ $\norm{\Kjoint}_{L^{1}} = 1$.
\item[(b)] The smoothing map
$S_{\Kjoint} : f \mapsto \Kjoint \ast f$ (equivalently, the central
Fourier multiplier with symbol $\sigma^{\Kjoint}$ of
(\ref{eq:K_joint_plancherel})) is unital and completely positive,
hence
\begin{equation}\label{eq:L2_main}
   \cbnorm{S_{\Kjoint_{\nmax,\Nt,T}}} \;=\; 1.
\end{equation}
Its multiplier symbol satisfies $\sigma^{\Kjoint}(0, 0) = 1$,
$0 \le \sigma^{\Kjoint} \le 1$, with support
$j' \le 2j_{\max}$, $|k| \le \Nt - 1$.
\item[(c)] The joint Plancherel mass distribution
$m^{\Kjoint}(j, k) = m^{\SU(2)}(j)\,m^{\Uone}(k)$ is a probability
distribution on the truncated joint dual with maximum
$\tfrac{2}{\nmax+1} \cdot \tfrac{1}{\Nt}$, attained at
$(j, k) = (j_{\max}, \text{any } |k| \le \Kmax)$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
(a) Both factors are $|{\cdot}|^{2}/Z$-normalized;\ Haar measure on
the product factorizes. (b) $S_{\Kjoint}$ is convolution against a
probability density:\ positivity is immediate, and unitality
($S_{\Kjoint}\mathbf{1} = \mathbf{1}$, i.e.\
$\sigma^{\Kjoint}(0,0) = \int \Kjoint = 1$) is forced by (a). A
unital positive map on a commutative C$^{*}$-algebra is completely
positive, and a unital completely positive map has cb-norm exactly
$1$~\cite{paulsen2002}. On the truncated noncommutative side, the
induced central multiplier acts block-scalar on
$\bigoplus_{\pi} \Mat_{d_{\pi}}(\C)$, and a block-scalar map with
symbol $\sigma$ has cb-norm $\sup_{\pi}|\sigma(\pi)| \le 1$, with
equality attained on the trivial block. The symbol bounds and support
are Appendix~\ref{app:plancherel}. (c) Direct from
(\ref{eq:K_SU2_plancherel}) and $m^{\Uone} = \Nt^{-1}$ uniform:\ the
SU(2) mass $(2j+1)/Z$ increases in $j$ with maximum
$\nmax/Z^{\SU(2)}_{\nmax} = 2/(\nmax+1)$ at $j_{\max}$. \qedhere
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[The mass maximum is not a cb-norm]\label{rem:cb_Nt_indep}
The mass-distribution maximum $2/(\nmax+1)$ of Lemma~\ref{lem:L2}(c)
is not the cb-norm of any map appearing in this paper;\ the smoothing
map is unital completely positive with cb-norm $1$
(Lemma~\ref{lem:L2}(b)), an elementary statement requiring no
amenability input. Conflating the two objects has two consequences
worth flagging:\ (i) it would assign the UCP map $\Bjoint$ a
``cb-norm'' below $1$, which is impossible;\ (ii) it would suggest a
bounded partial inverse of $\Bjoint$ ``at the same scale'' — invalid,
since an inverse is governed by the symbol \emph{minimum} on the
relevant window (see \S~\ref{sec:withdrawn_assembly}).
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[$4/\pi$ asymptote on the SU(2) factor]\label{rem:4_pi}
Paper~38~\S~3.2 (with the universal Plancherel-$\times$-Vandermonde
cancellation theorem from Paper~40~\S~3.2~\cite{paper40_unified}) gives
the asymptotic rate constant
\begin{equation*}
   \lim_{\nmax \to \infty} \frac{\nmax \cdot \gammaSU_{\nmax}}{\log \nmax}
   \;=\; \frac{4}{\pi}.
\end{equation*}
This is the universal compact-Lie-group rate constant on the bi-invariant
metric and is independent of rank, Weyl-group order, and Lie type
within the class. The $\Uone$ factor inherits a separate
Fej\'er-on-the-circle rate.
\end{remark}

\subsection{Lemma L3:\ joint Lichnerowicz / Lipschitz comparison}
\label{sec:L3}
% ----------------------------------------------------------

\begin{lemma}[L3, joint Lichnerowicz]\label{lem:L3}
For every $a \in \Op^{L}_{\nmax,\Nt,T}$ and every $(\nmax, \Nt, T)$,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:L3_main}
   \opnorm{[\DL, a]}
   \;\le\; \Cthreejoint(\nmax, \Nt) \cdot \norm{\nabla^{\mathrm{joint}} a}_{\infty},
\end{equation}
where $\nabla^{\mathrm{joint}}$ is either the $L^{1}$-additive or
$L^{2}$-Pythagorean joint gradient norm (\ref{eq:joint_L1}),
(\ref{eq:joint_L2}). The constant satisfies
\begin{equation}\label{eq:C3_joint_bound}
   \Cthreejoint(\nmax, \Nt)
   \;\le\; \CthreeSU(\nmax)
   \;\le\; \sup_{2 \le N \le 2\nmax - 1} \frac{N - 1}{\sqrt{N^{2} - 1}}
   \;=\; \sqrt{1 - \frac{1}{\nmax}}
   \;\xrightarrow{\nmax \to \infty}\; 1^{-},
\end{equation}
where the supremum is taken over the natural-substrate envelope $N \le
2\nmax - 1$ — the Avery--Wen--Avery $\Delta n = \pm 1$ ladder on
$\nmax$ shells produces shell-difference labels $N$ up to $2\nmax - 1$
via the chirality-doubling at the operator-system level (see
Paper~44~\cite{paper44}~\S~3). The per-harmonic function
$(N-1)/\sqrt{N^{2} - 1}$ is Paper~38~\cite{paper38} Lemma~L3 verbatim;
the envelope range $N \le 2\nmax - 1$ extends Paper~38's $N \le \nmax$
because Paper~38's Berezin map restricts to multiplier labels
$N \le \nmax$ by Plancherel cutoff, while the Lorentzian natural
substrate's full multiplier algebra reaches the larger envelope (see
Remark~\ref{rem:envelope_v2} below).
\end{lemma}

\begin{remark}[On the supremum range]\label{rem:envelope_v2}
The supremum is taken over the natural-substrate envelope $N \le
2\nmax - 1$ rather than $N \le \nmax$. The per-harmonic functional
form, the asymptotic limit $1^{-}$, and the rate expressions of
\S~\ref{sec:withdrawn_assembly} (eq.~\ref{eq:main_bound}) are
insensitive to this distinction.
The finite-cutoff constant is sharpened by the envelope:\ at
$\nmax = 3$, the natural-substrate generator
$(N, L, M, p) = (4, 1, \pm 1, 0)$ has Lipschitz ratio $0.95$, which
exceeds the per-shell value $C_3^{(3)} = 2/\sqrt{8} = 0.707$ but is
bounded by the envelope-aware
$C_3^{\mathrm{op}}(3) = ((2 \cdot 3 - 1) - 1)/\sqrt{(2 \cdot 3 - 1)^{2} - 1} = 4/\sqrt{24} = \sqrt{2/3} \approx 0.816$.
Numerical-panel values in \S\ref{sec:panel} are unaffected (they
quote $\gammajoint$ directly, not $\Cthreejoint \cdot \gammajoint$).
The envelope refinement was identified in
Paper~46~\cite{paper46}~\S~4.3 (the strong-form companion under
$L_{\mathrm{op}}(a) := \opnorm{[\DL, a]}$); Paper~38 is unaffected
because Paper~38's Berezin map restricts multiplier labels to
$N \le \nmax$ by Plancherel cutoff, while the Lorentzian natural
substrate's chirality-doubled multiplier algebra reaches the larger
envelope (see Paper~46~Appendix~A for the cross-paper analysis).
\end{remark}

\begin{proof}
We first establish the structural identity:\ for every pure-tensor
$a = a_{s} \otimes a_{t} \in \Op^{L}$,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:L3_struct_id}
   [\DL, a_{s} \otimes a_{t}]
   \;=\; i\,[\DGV, a_{s}] \otimes a_{t}
   \qquad \text{(bit-exact).}
\end{equation}
Expand
$[\DL, a_{s} \otimes a_{t}] = i\,[\gamma^{0} \otimes \partial_{t},
a_{s} \otimes a_{t}] + i\,[\DGV \otimes I_{\Nt}, a_{s} \otimes a_{t}]$
and analyze each term.

\emph{Term A (vanishing cross term).} Since $a_{s} =
\mathrm{blkdiag}(W, W)$ has identical diagonal blocks in chirality and
$\gamma^{0}$ is the chirality-swap acting as
$\begin{pmatrix} 0 & I \\ I & 0\end{pmatrix}$ on the chirality
indices,
\begin{equation*}
   \gamma^{0}\,a_{s} \;=\; \begin{pmatrix} 0 & W \\ W & 0\end{pmatrix}
   \;=\; a_{s}\,\gamma^{0}, \qquad [\gamma^{0}, a_{s}] = 0.
\end{equation*}
Furthermore, $a_{t}$ and $\partial_{t}$ are both diagonal in the
momentum basis (the temporal multiplier algebra is the commutative
diagonal subalgebra of $\Mat_{\Nt}(\C)$), so $[\partial_{t}, a_{t}] =
0$. The cross term therefore vanishes bit-exactly:
$[\gamma^{0} \otimes \partial_{t}, a_{s} \otimes a_{t}] = 0$.

\emph{Term B (purely spatial).}
$[\DGV \otimes I_{\Nt}, a_{s} \otimes a_{t}] = [\DGV, a_{s}] \otimes a_{t}$.

Combining gives (\ref{eq:L3_struct_id}). By linearity over the
operator-system span, the same identity extends to every
$a \in \Op^{L}$ written as a finite linear combination of pure-tensor
generators.

Tensor-product operator-norm factorization
$\opnorm{A \otimes B} = \opnorm{A} \cdot \opnorm{B}$ and Paper~38
Lemma~L3 on the spatial factor give, for any pure-tensor $a = a_{s}
\otimes a_{t}$,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:L3_pure_tensor}
   \opnorm{[\DL, a]}
   \;=\; \opnorm{[\DGV, a_{s}]} \cdot \opnorm{a_{t}}
   \;\le\; \CthreeSU(\nmax) \cdot \norm{\nabla_{x} f_{s}}_{\infty} \cdot \opnorm{a_{t}},
\end{equation}
where $f_{s}$ is the Berezin pre-image of $a_{s}$.
For momentum-diagonal $a_{t}$, $\opnorm{a_{t}} = \norm{f_{t}}_{\infty}$
(with $f_{t}$ the corresponding Fourier symbol). Since
$\norm{\nabla_{x} f_{s}}_{\infty} \cdot \norm{f_{t}}_{\infty} \le
\norm{\nabla^{\mathrm{joint}, L^{1}} f}_{\infty}$ by
(\ref{eq:joint_L1}) (the second summand is non-negative), and
similarly $\le \norm{\nabla^{\mathrm{joint}, L^{2}} f}_{\infty}$ by
$\sqrt{a^{2} + b^{2}} \ge a$ for $a, b \ge 0$, we obtain
(\ref{eq:L3_main}) for pure-tensor $a$. The extension to general
$a = \sum_{j} c_{j} (a_{s}^{j} \otimes a_{t}^{j})$ follows by triangle
inequality on both sides.

The bound (\ref{eq:C3_joint_bound}) takes the Paper~38~\S~L3
per-harmonic form $(N-1)/\sqrt{N^{2}-1}$ on the natural-substrate
envelope $N \le 2\nmax - 1$ (per Remark~\ref{rem:envelope_v2});
asymptotic tightness $\to 1^{-}$ is via the per-harmonic
Avery--Wen--Avery saturation on $Y^{(3)}_{N, 0, 0}$ as $N \to 2\nmax - 1$. \qedhere
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[The temporal direction contributes zero]\label{rem:no_cross}
The identity (\ref{eq:L3_struct_id}) is the substantive structural
content of Lemma~\ref{lem:L3}. There is \emph{no} cross term and
\emph{no} separate $\Uone$ Lichnerowicz lemma:\ the joint commutator
is the spatial commutator times the temporal operator-norm, and the
temporal slot enters the bound multiplicatively at value $1$. This is
stronger than the $C_{3} \le 1 + \mathrm{cross}$ estimate one would
expect from a generic tensor extension;\ the structural reason is that
the temporal multiplier algebra is commutative diagonal and
$\partial_{t}$ is itself diagonal, so they commute bit-exactly. The
Wick-rotated convention here is essential:\ a Lorentzian-causal
temporal derivative would not be diagonal in the same basis.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[$L^{1}$ and $L^{2}$ give the same $C_{3}$]\label{rem:L1_L2_equal}
Both $L^{1}$-additive (\ref{eq:joint_L1}) and $L^{2}$-Pythagorean
(\ref{eq:joint_L2}) joint gradient norms upper-bound the right-hand
side of (\ref{eq:L3_pure_tensor}) by the same constant $\CthreeSU$:\
the temporal contribution to $\opnorm{[\DL, a]}$ is zero (by
(\ref{eq:L3_struct_id})), so only the spatial part of the joint
gradient matters in the inequality.
\end{remark}

\subsection{Lemma L4:\ joint Berezin reconstruction}
\label{sec:L4}
% ----------------------------------------------------------

\begin{definition}[Joint Berezin map, convolution form]
\label{def:joint_berezin}
The joint Berezin map is
\begin{equation}\label{eq:joint_berezin}
   \Bjoint_{\nmax,\Nt,T}(f) \;:=\; \Pjoint\,M_{\Kjoint \ast f}\,\Pjoint,
\end{equation}
where $M_{g}$ is multiplication by $g$ on $L^{2}(\Manifold)$ and
$\Pjoint := P^{\SU(2)}_{\nmax} \otimes P^{\Uone}_{\Nt}$ is the joint
truncation projection onto the subspace spanned by the kept
Peter--Weyl $\times$ Fourier modes.
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}[Sum-form pitfalls:\ weights and targets]
\label{rem:berezin_inequivalence}
Two natural-looking sum-form alternatives to
(\ref{eq:joint_berezin}) fail, and both failures are instructive.
(i) \emph{Weights:}\ a sum form weighted by the mass distribution
$m^{\SU(2)}(N)$ in place of the multiplier symbol
$\sigma^{\SU(2)}(N)$ is not unital
($\Bjoint(\mathbf{1}) = Z^{-1} I \ne I$) and maximally violates the
approximate-identity property at $f = \mathbf{1}$.
(ii) \emph{Targets:}\ under the convolution form, the image of the
Fourier mode $e^{iqt}$ is $\sigma^{\Uone}(q)$ times the
\emph{truncated shift} (Toeplitz) operator on $\C^{\Nt}$ — not
momentum-diagonal for $q \ne 0$ — so $\Bjoint$'s temporal image is
\emph{not contained} in the operator system $\Op^{L}$ of
\S~\ref{sec:op_system}, whose temporal factor is momentum-diagonal;\
and a sum form assigning $e^{iqt}$ a momentum-diagonal image is not
the compression of any multiplication operator. This target mismatch
is part of the degeneracy diagnosis of \S~\ref{sec:main}:\ the
momentum-diagonal operator system is not the algebra the Berezin map
naturally reconstructs, and that choice is precisely what makes the
temporal factor invisible to the Dirac commutator
(Lemma~\ref{lem:L3}). Rebuilding the construction with the Toeplitz
temporal algebra is the reopened target of \S~\ref{sec:open}.
\end{remark}

\begin{lemma}[L4, joint Berezin properties]\label{lem:L4}
$\Bjoint_{\nmax,\Nt,T}$ satisfies the following four properties.
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] \emph{Positivity.} If $f \ge 0$ pointwise on
$\Manifold$, then $\Bjoint(f) \ge 0$ as a positive-semidefinite
Hermitian element of $\Mat_{\dim\Krein}(\C)$.
\item[(b)] \emph{Contractivity.} $\Bjoint$ is unital completely
positive;\ $\cbnorm{\Bjoint} = 1$ and $\opnorm{\Bjoint(f)} \le
\norm{f}_{\infty}$.
\item[(c)] \emph{Approximate identity.} For every $f \in C^{\infty}(\Manifold)$,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:L4_approx_id}
   \opnorm{\Bjoint(f) - \Pjoint\,M_{f}\,\Pjoint}
   \;\le\; \gammajoint_{\nmax,\Nt,T} \cdot
            \norm{\nabla^{\mathrm{joint}} f}_{\infty},
\end{equation}
with $\gammajoint = O(\log\nmax/\nmax + T/\Nt) \to 0$ as
$(\nmax, \Nt) \to (\infty, \infty)$.
\item[(d)] \emph{Spatial L3 compatibility.} For every spatially-pure
$f = f_{s} \otimes \mathbf{1}$,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:L4_L3compat}
   \opnorm{[\DL, \Bjoint(f)]}
   \;\le\; \Cthreejoint \cdot \norm{\nabla^{\mathrm{joint}} f}_{\infty},
\end{equation}
with the same $\Cthreejoint \le 1$ as in Lemma~\ref{lem:L3}.
\emph{(For temporally-varying $f$ the convolution-form image is
Toeplitz on the temporal factor, where
$[\gamma^{0} \otimes \partial_{t}, \cdot]$ does not vanish;\ the
required estimate is precisely the Connes--van~Suijlekom
$\Sone$-type Toeplitz commutator bound~\cite{connes_vs2021} — part of
the reopened target, \S~\ref{sec:open}.)}
\item[(e)] \emph{Krein-positivity preservation.} For every $f$,
$[\JL, \Bjoint(f)] = 0$ bit-exact, hence
$\Bjoint(f)$ preserves both $\Kplus$ and $\Kminus$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
\emph{(a) Positivity.} The convolution form
$\Bjoint(f) = \Pjoint\,(\Kjoint \ast f)\,\Pjoint$ writes $\Bjoint(f)$
as a UCP compression of a non-negative function (since both factor
kernels are non-negative pointwise:\ $\KSU = |{\cdot}|^{2} \cdot Z^{-1}
\ge 0$ and $\KU = |{\cdot}|^{2} \cdot \Nt^{-1} \ge 0$). The convolution
of two non-negative integrands against the positive joint Haar measure
is non-negative, and the UCP compression of a non-negative multiplication
operator is positive-semidefinite.

\emph{(b) Contractivity.} The convolution form gives
$\opnorm{\Bjoint(f)} \le \norm{\Kjoint \ast f}_{\infty} \le
\norm{\Kjoint}_{L^{1}} \cdot \norm{f}_{\infty}$ by Young's inequality
at the sup endpoint, and $\norm{\Kjoint}_{L^{1}} = 1$ (both factors
are normalized probability densities;\ Haar on a product factorizes).
$\Bjoint$ is the composition of the unital smoothing map
$S_{\Kjoint}$ (UCP with cb-norm $1$, Lemma~\ref{lem:L2}(b)) with the
unital compression $g \mapsto \Pjoint M_{g} \Pjoint$ (UCP);\ a
composition of UCP maps is UCP, so $\cbnorm{\Bjoint} = 1$ with
unitality $\Bjoint(\mathbf{1}) = \Pjoint M_{\mathbf{1}} \Pjoint = I$
on the truncated space.

\emph{(c) Approximate identity.} For a pure-tensor symbol
$f = f_{s} \otimes f_{t}$, the convolution-deficit splits:
\begin{equation*}
   \Kjoint \ast f - f
   \;=\; (\KSU \ast f_{s} - f_{s}) \otimes (\KU \ast f_{t})
       + f_{s} \otimes (\KU \ast f_{t} - f_{t}).
\end{equation*}
By Paper~38~\cite{paper38} Lemma~L4(c) on the spatial factor,
$\norm{\KSU \ast f_{s} - f_{s}}_{\infty} \le \gammaSU_{\nmax} \cdot
\norm{\nabla_{x} f_{s}}_{\infty}$, with $\gammaSU_{\nmax} =
O(\log\nmax / \nmax)$ (Paper~38~\S~L2 quantitative rate, $4/\pi$
asymptote). By the standard Fej\'er-on-the-circle approximation rate
(Katznelson~\cite{katznelson2004}\,Ch.~I), $\norm{\KU \ast f_{t} -
f_{t}}_{\infty} \le \gammaU_{\Nt, T} \cdot \norm{\partial_{t}
f_{t}}_{\infty}$ with $\gammaU = O(T/\Nt)$. Combining with the
$L^{1}$-additive triangle decomposition and $\norm{\KU \ast f_{t}}_{\infty}
\le \norm{f_{t}}_{\infty}$ (Young) gives
\begin{equation*}
   \norm{\Kjoint \ast f - f}_{\infty}
   \;\le\; \max(\gammaSU_{\nmax}, \gammaU_{\Nt, T}) \cdot
            \norm{\nabla^{\mathrm{joint}, L^{1}} f}_{\infty}.
\end{equation*}
We define $\gammajoint := \max(\gammaSU, \gammaU)$. Compression-contraction
on $L^{2}$ does not increase the operator norm, so (\ref{eq:L4_approx_id})
follows. Linearity and the triangle inequality extend to general $f$.

\emph{(d) Spatial L3 compatibility.} For $f = f_{s} \otimes
\mathbf{1}$, the temporal convolution of a constant is the constant
and the temporal compression of $M_{\mathbf{1}}$ is $I_{\Nt}$, so
$\Bjoint(f) = B^{\SU(2)}_{\chid}(f_{s}) \otimes I_{\Nt}$ with
$B^{\SU(2)}_{\chid}$ chirality-block-diagonal with equal blocks. Then
$[\gamma^{0} \otimes \partial_{t}, B^{\SU(2)}_{\chid}(f_{s}) \otimes
I_{\Nt}] = [\gamma^{0}, B^{\SU(2)}_{\chid}(f_{s})] \otimes \partial_{t}
= 0$, and the spatial term is bounded by Paper~38~\S~L3-compatibility
with the per-harmonic constant $C_{3}^{(N)} = (N-1)/\sqrt{N^{2}-1}$,
giving (\ref{eq:L4_L3compat}).

\emph{(e) Krein-positivity preservation.} For the convolution form,
$[\JL, \Bjoint(f)] = \Pjoint\,[\JL, M_{\Kjoint \ast f}]\,\Pjoint$
(since $[\JL, \Pjoint] = 0$), and $[\JL, M_{g}] = 0$ for every
function $g$ because $\JL = \JL^{\spat} \otimes I_{\Nt}$ acts on the
chirality indices while $M_{g}$ acts scalarly on them. \qedhere
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Tensor-product factorization on pure tensors]\label{rem:L4_factorize}
For a pure-tensor $f = f_{s} \otimes f_{t}$, the joint Berezin
factorizes:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:L4_factorize}
   \Bjoint(f_{s} \otimes f_{t})
   \;=\; B^{\SU(2)}_{\chid}(f_{s}) \otimes B^{\Uone}(f_{t}),
\end{equation}
where $B^{\SU(2)}_{\chid}$ is the chirality-doubled spinor lift of
Paper~38's spatial Berezin and $B^{\Uone}$ is the Fej\'er--Toeplitz
compression on $\SoneT$ (the Connes--van~Suijlekom circle
object~\cite{connes_vs2021}, \emph{not} a momentum-diagonal map;\ cf.\
Remark~\ref{rem:berezin_inequivalence}). This factorization makes
Lemma~\ref{lem:L4}(a)--(c),(e) follow from the corresponding
factor-wise properties of Paper~38~L4 (SU(2) side) and standard
Fej\'er-on-$\Sone$ approximation theory ($\Uone$ side);\ item (d) is
spatial-only as stated.
\end{remark}

\subsection{The tunneling pair}\label{sec:tunneling_pair}

\begin{definition}[Joint tunneling pair]\label{def:tunneling_pair}
The joint tunneling pair between the truncated triple
$\Tcal^{L}_{\nmax,\Nt,T}$ and the continuum reference triple
$\Tcal^{L}_{\Manifold}$ is the pair $(\Bjoint_{\nmax,\Nt,T},
\Pjoint_{\nmax,\Nt})$, with $\Bjoint$ the joint Berezin map of
Definition~\ref{def:joint_berezin} and $\Pjoint = P^{\SU(2)}_{\nmax}
\otimes P^{\Uone}_{\Nt}$ the joint truncation projection on
$L^{2}(\Manifold)$.

The continuum reference triple is $\Tcal^{L}_{\Manifold}
:= (C^{\infty}(\Manifold), L^{2}(\sthree, \Sigma_{\mathrm{CH}}) \otimes
L^{2}(\SoneT), D^{L}_{\infty}, J^{L}_{\infty})$ with $\Sigma_{\mathrm{CH}}$
the chirality-doubled Camporesi--Higuchi spinor bundle and
$D^{L}_{\infty}$, $J^{L}_{\infty}$ the natural continuum analogs of
$\DL$ and $\JL$.
\end{definition}

By Lemmas~\ref{lem:L1prime}--\ref{lem:L4}, both $\Bjoint$ and $\Pjoint$
are UCP (cb-norm $1$;\ the projection $\Pjoint$ is a Stinespring
orthogonal projection), and both commute with $\JL$ at the operator
level, so the $\Kplus$-compression of the pair is well-defined. One
might hope the compressed pair to be a tunneling pair between the
compressed triples of Definition~\ref{def:weak_form_propinquity} in
some Riemannian framework. It is not, twice over:\ no applicable
framework admits a UCP-pair tunnel on these objects, and — more
fundamentally — the compressed triples are not quantum metric spaces
at all, as we now prove.

\subsection{The annihilation theorem}\label{sec:annihilation}

\begin{theorem}[$\Kplus$ annihilation]\label{thm:kplus_annihilation}
Let $\DL = i(\gamma^{0} \otimes \partial_{t} + \DGV \otimes I_{\Nt})$
with $\DGV$ Hermitian, $\partial_{t}$ anti-Hermitian and
momentum-diagonal, and fundamental symmetry
$\JL = \JL^{\spat} \otimes I_{\Nt}$, and suppose $\DL$ is
Krein-self-adjoint, $\DL^{\times} := \JL \DL^{\dagger} \JL = \DL$
(Lemma~\ref{lem:L1prime}(b)). Then:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] $\{\JL,\, \DGV \otimes I_{\Nt}\} = 0$, and consequently
$\Pplus\,(\DGV \otimes I_{\Nt})\,\Pplus = 0$:\ the $\Kplus$
compression annihilates the spatial Dirac.
\item[(ii)] The surviving compressed Dirac
$\Pplus \DL \Pplus = i\,\Pplus(\gamma^{0} \otimes \partial_{t})\Pplus$
is momentum-diagonal, and commutes with $\Pplus a \Pplus$ for every
$a = M^{\spat} \otimes M^{\temp} \in \Op^{L}$ (momentum-diagonal
temporal factor).
\item[(iii)] Therefore the $\Kplus$-restricted Lipschitz seminorm
vanishes identically on the operator system:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:annihilation}
   \opnorm{\bigl[\Pplus \DL \Pplus,\; \Pplus a \Pplus\bigr]} \;=\; 0
   \qquad \text{for every } a \in \Op^{L}_{\nmax,\Nt,T},
\end{equation}
and the compressed pair
$(\Pplus\Op^{L}\Pplus,\ \opnorm{[\Pplus\DL\Pplus, \cdot]})$ is not a
compact quantum metric space:\ the seminorm kernel is the whole
system, the associated Monge--Kantorovich extended metric is
degenerate, and Definition~\ref{def:weak_form_propinquity} does not
define a quantum Gromov--Hausdorff-type distance.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
(i) Write $D_{s} := \DGV \otimes I_{\Nt}$ (Hermitian) and $D_{t} :=
\gamma^{0} \otimes \partial_{t}$. Since $\partial_{t}$ is
anti-Hermitian and $[\JL, D_{t}] = 0$ (both factors commute), the term
$i D_{t}$ is Hermitian and Krein-self-adjoint on its own. For the
spatial term, $(i D_{s})^{\dagger} = -i D_{s}$, so
Krein-self-adjointness of $\DL$ forces
$\JL(-i D_{s})\JL = i D_{s}$, i.e.\ $\JL D_{s} \JL = -D_{s}$,
i.e.\ $\{\JL, D_{s}\} = 0$. Then with $\Pplus = (I + \JL)/2$,
\begin{equation*}
   \Pplus D_{s} \Pplus
   \;=\; \tfrac14\,(D_{s} + D_{s}\JL + \JL D_{s} + \JL D_{s} \JL)
   \;=\; \tfrac14\,(D_{s} + 0 - D_{s})
   \;=\; 0,
\end{equation*}
using $D_{s}\JL + \JL D_{s} = 0$ and $\JL D_{s} \JL = -D_{s}$.
(ii) $\Pplus$ commutes with $D_{t}$ and with every $a \in \Op^{L}$
(Lemma~\ref{lem:L1prime}(e)), so $\Pplus \DL \Pplus = i D_{t} \Pplus$
restricted to $\Kplus$;\ on $\Kplus$, $\JL = +1$, so this operator is
(unitarily) $\partial_{t}$-diagonal in the momentum basis. The
temporal factors $M^{\temp}$ of $\Op^{L}$ are momentum-diagonal by
construction (\S~\ref{sec:op_system}), hence commute with it;\ the
spatial factors commute with anything supported purely on the temporal
slot. (iii) Immediate from (i) + (ii):\ the spatial part of the
commutator is annihilated by (i), the temporal part vanishes by (ii).
A seminorm whose kernel strictly exceeds $\C 1$ fails the
Rieffel/Latr\'emoli\`ere Lip-norm axiom ($L(a) = 0 \Leftrightarrow
a \in \C 1$) and the van~Suijlekom state-space framework's
separation requirement alike. \qedhere
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Numerical verification, bit-exact]
\label{cor:annihilation_numerics}

restricted seminorm (\ref{eq:annihilation}) is $0.0$ in

$\norm{\{\JL, \DGV \otimes I\}}_{F} = 0.0$ and

\subsection{Anatomy of the failed assembly}\label{sec:withdrawn_assembly}

We record where each piece of the natural assembly stands — both
because the surviving estimates are genuine analytic facts about the
kernels, and because the failure points specify the repair
(\S~\ref{sec:open}). The assembly one would attempt is the bound
\begin{equation}\label{eq:propinquity_bound}
   \Lambdab\bigl(\Tcal^{+}_{\nmax,\Nt,T}, \Tcal^{+}_{\Manifold}\bigr)
   \;\le\; \max\bigl(\mathrm{reach}_{B},\,
                       \mathrm{reach}_{P},\,
                       \mathrm{height}_{B},\,
                       \mathrm{height}_{P}\bigr)
\end{equation}
for the UCP pair $(\Bjoint, \Pjoint)$, with the four constituents
measured against the \emph{classical} joint-gradient unit ball (an
auxiliary third seminorm — not the Lipschitz ball of either triple,
itself a structural defect of any such bookkeeping). No
four-constituent UCP-pair tunnel exists in Latr\'emoli\`ere's
framework~\cite{latremoliere_metric_st_2017} (tunnels there are
carried by quantum metric spaces with quantum isometries);\ the
nearest genuine framework is van~Suijlekom's state-space
Gromov--Hausdorff bound via approximation maps in both
directions~\cite{vs2021_jgp,leimbach_vs2024}. Independently of the
framework question, Theorem~\ref{thm:kplus_annihilation} shows the
left-hand side of (\ref{eq:propinquity_bound}) is not a distance, so
no assembly through Definition~\ref{def:weak_form_propinquity} can
succeed.\label{prop:reach_height} Piece by piece:
\begin{itemize}
\item \emph{reach$_{B}$ (approximate-identity deficit).} The estimate
$\opnorm{\Bjoint(f) - \Pjoint M_{f} \Pjoint} \le \gammajoint
\norm{\nabla^{\mathrm{joint}} f}_{\infty}$ (Lemma~\ref{lem:L4}(c))
survives as a genuine approximation statement for the convolution-form
Berezin map. It is an analytic fact about kernels, not a metric
statement.
\item \emph{reach$_{P}$ (dual roundtrip).} No bounded partial
inverse of $\Bjoint$ can be inferred from any forward bound:\ an
inverse is governed by the multiplier-symbol \emph{minimum} on the
relevant window (at full support the inverse norm grows like
$O(\nmax^{2})$). The correct dual-direction mechanism is the
antiderivative transference of
Leimbach--van~Suijlekom~\cite{leimbach_vs2024} (Lemmas~3.4--3.5
there);\ for scalar sectors on compact metric groups the conclusion
is covered by Gaudillot-Estrada--van~Suijlekom~\cite{gaudillot_vs2023}.
Adapting it to the chirality-doubled spinor substrate is a named gap
shared with the companion $\sthree$ paper.
\item \emph{height$_{B}$ (Lipschitz distortion).} Fails:\ for
purely temporal $f = \mathbf{1} \otimes f_{t}$ with
$\norm{\partial_{t} f_{t}}_{\infty} = 1$, the classical side gives
$\norm{f}_{\mathrm{Lip}} = 1$ while $\opnorm{[\DL, \Bjoint(f)]}$
vanishes on the momentum-diagonal reading, so the distortion is
$\ge 1$ and cannot tend to $0$. The spatial restriction survives via
Lemma~\ref{lem:L4}(d).
\item \emph{height$_{P} = 0$.} Vacuous under
Theorem~\ref{thm:kplus_annihilation}:\ every map into a
zero-seminorm system is ``Lipschitz-isometric.''
\end{itemize}

The assembly therefore produces no distance bound. The rate
expression it would have produced,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:main_bound}
   \Cthreejoint(\nmax, \Nt) \cdot \gammajoint_{\nmax, \Nt, T}
   \;=\; O\!\Bigl(\tfrac{\log\nmax}{\nmax} + \tfrac{T}{\Nt}\Bigr)
   \;\xrightarrow[(\nmax, \Nt)\to(\infty, \infty)]{}\; 0,
\end{equation}
is nonetheless a well-defined closed-form expression;\ its surviving
meaning is the spatial convergence statement of
Proposition~\ref{prop:spatial_conditional}, to which we now turn.

\subsection{The spatial convergence statement}\label{sec:main_theorem}

\begin{proposition}[Spatial sector]
\label{prop:spatial_conditional}
Fix $\Nt \ge 1$, $T > 0$. By the companion $\sthree$ paper's
(unconditional) spatial Gromov--Hausdorff convergence theorem
(van~Suijlekom state-space distance~\cite{vs2021_jgp}, with the
truncated state space metrized by the left-translation Lipschitz
seminorm;\ compression/lifted-state argument in the pattern
of~\cite{gaudillot_vs2023}), the spatial truncations underlying
$\Tcal^{L}_{\nmax,\Nt,T}$ converge to the spatial continuum reference
at the Paper~38 rate
\begin{equation}\label{eq:spatial_conditional}
   \mathrm{d}_{\mathrm{GH}}^{\,\mathrm{vS}}
   \;\le\; \CthreeSU(\nmax) \cdot \gammaSU_{\nmax}
   \;=\; O\!\Bigl(\tfrac{\log \nmax}{\nmax}\Bigr),
\end{equation}
and the temporal factor is carried $\JL$-equivariantly
(Lemma~\ref{lem:L4}(e)) but is metrically invisible:\ it contributes
nothing to any Dirac-commutator seminorm on the momentum-diagonal
operator system (Lemma~\ref{lem:L3} structural identity;\
Theorem~\ref{thm:kplus_annihilation}). In particular, no statement of
Lorentzian or product-metric content is made:\
(\ref{eq:spatial_conditional}) is a Riemannian statement about the
$\sthree$ factor, decorated with Krein bookkeeping.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}[Proof sketch]
At fixed $\Nt$, the spatially-pure sector $f = f_{s} \otimes
\mathbf{1}$ reduces, by the factorization of
Remark~\ref{rem:L4_factorize} and Lemma~\ref{lem:L4}(d), to Paper~38's
spatial construction tensored with $I_{\Nt}$;\ all estimates are the
Paper~38 estimates verbatim, now unconditional under that paper's
translation-seminorm metrization. The metric invisibility of the
temporal factor is Theorem~\ref{thm:kplus_annihilation}(ii)--(iii)
together with the structural identity (\ref{eq:L3_struct_id}).
\qedhere
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Asymptotic rate]\label{rem:asymp_rate}
The joint rate factors at leading order:
\begin{equation*}
   \gammajoint \;\le\; \gammaSU + \gammaU
   \;=\; O\!\Bigl(\tfrac{\log\nmax}{\nmax}\Bigr) + O\!\Bigl(\tfrac{T}{\Nt}\Bigr),
\end{equation*}
with the SU(2) leading constant
$\lim_{\nmax\to\infty} \nmax \gammaSU_{\nmax} / \log\nmax = 4/\pi$
rigorous (Paper~38~\S~L2 Stein--Weiss closure + Paper~40~\S~3.2
universal cancellation), and the $\Uone$ leading constant
$\gammaU_{\Nt, T} \le C\, T \log\Nt / \Nt$ standard
(Katznelson~\cite{katznelson2004}\,Ch.~I).
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[The compressed continuum object is also degenerate]
\label{rem:limit_identification}
There is no limit identification to state:\ the candidate limit
object $\Tcal^{+}_{\Manifold} = (\Pplus C^{\infty}(\Manifold)\Pplus,
\Kplus_{\infty}, \Pplus D^{L}_{\infty} \Pplus)$ is itself degenerate
— the compressed continuum Dirac loses its spatial part by the same
anticommutation mechanism as
Theorem~\ref{thm:kplus_annihilation}(i), so its Lipschitz seminorm
kernel contains all time-independent functions and its
Monge--Kantorovich structure does not separate states.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Assembly layers are not mere bookkeeping]
\label{rem:bookkeeping}
In proof shapes of this kind it is tempting to regard the assembly
layer as bookkeeping, with the analytical content concentrated in the
kernel-level lemmas. The present construction inverts that
assessment:\ the kernel-level facts
(Lemma~\ref{lem:L4}(a)--(c),(e)) are robust, while the assembly layer
is where the construction fails — a degenerate seminorm, no
admissible tunnel device, and a false constituent bound. Assembly
steps that import external theorems are exactly the steps that
require line-level hypothesis checking.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Two-rate hybrid:\ what survives]
\label{rem:two_rate_hybrid}
A two-rate hybrid composite to the non-compact carrier
$\sthree\times\R_{t}$ has two arrows. The outer (norm-resolvent)
arrow of Paper~47~\cite{paper47}~\S~4 is operator-level, does not use
the Lipschitz seminorm, and is unaffected by the degeneracy, with
rate $O(e^{-|\mathrm{Im}\,z|\,T/2})$ on compact-temporal-support
subspaces. The inner (metric) arrow would require a Q1-repaired
construction;\ the metric-level statement on both the compact and
non-compact carriers is open (Paper~47 carries its own Status note).
\end{remark}

\subsection{Product-carrier convergence in the action-seminorm framework}
\label{sec:product_action_seminorm}

The degeneracy theorem leaves a definite repair specification
(\S~\ref{sec:open}, Q1(i)):\ a temporal multiplier algebra built from
Toeplitz compressions of $e^{iqt}$ rather than momentum-diagonal
polynomials. That prerequisite has now been established, and with it

\subsection{Four-witness Wick-rotation connection}\label{sec:four_witness}

Paper~42~\cite{paper42} closes the four-witness Wick-rotation theorem
(Hartle--Hawking~\cite{hartle_hawking1976},
Sewell~\cite{sewell1982},
Bisognano--Wichmann~\cite{bisognano_wichmann1976},
Unruh~\cite{unruh1976}) at the operator-system level on the
\emph{Riemannian} truncated Camporesi--Higuchi spectral triple, via the
geometric BW-$\alpha$ and Tomita--Takesaki BW-$\gamma$ modular
Hamiltonians on a hemispheric wedge. Paper~43~\cite{paper43} extends
that closure to signature $(3, 1)$ on the Krein wedge.

Papers~42/43 close modular-structure statements at the operator
level;\ those are unaffected by the degeneracy. The convergence leg
of the arc, by contrast, is \emph{not closed}:\ the present paper
proves that the natural device for it cannot work
(Theorem~\ref{thm:kplus_annihilation}) and states the open problem
(\S~\ref{sec:open}).

\subsection{Relation to Mondino--S\"amann}\label{sec:mondino_samann}

The synthetic Lorentzian Gromov--Hausdorff program of
Mondino--S\"amann~\cite{mondino_samann2025} extends their earlier
work~\cite{minguzzi_suhr2024} to Lorentzian pre-length spaces via
$\epsilon$-nets of causal diamonds. This is adjacent at the conceptual
level (both ``Lorentzian Gromov--Hausdorff-type convergence'') but
operates on a categorically different mathematical object.

\begin{remark}[Different objects, different distances]\label{rem:mondino_samann}
Mondino--S\"amann work on pre-length spaces with causal-diamond
geometry;\ they do not have a Dirac operator or any spectral data, and
their convergence is in the synthetic-geometry metric on the space of
isometry classes of such pre-length spaces. The present paper works on
truncated Krein spectral triples — and proves a \emph{negative}
structural result about the obvious operator-algebraic convergence
device on them, rather than a convergence theorem. The two
programs remain categorically disjoint;\ as of this writing the
operator-algebraic side has \emph{no} valid Lorentzian-type
quantum Gromov--Hausdorff convergence notion, which sharpens rather
than diminishes the contrast.
\end{remark}

\section{Symbolic Plancherel calculation for the joint Fej\'er symbol}
\label{app:plancherel}
% =====================================================================

We record the kernel's two Plancherel-side objects. A caution
worth stating explicitly:\ the identity
$\int_{\SU(2)} \KSU\,\chi_{j'}\,dg = (2j'+1)/Z$ — which would make
the character coefficients equal the mass distribution — is
\emph{false} (already at $\nmax = 2$, $j' = \tfrac12$ the left side
is $2\sqrt2/3 \ne 2/3$);\ the two objects are related but distinct,
as follows.

\begin{lemma}[Mass distribution and multiplier symbol]
\label{lem:plancherel_factor}
Let $\KSU = Z^{-1}|h|^{2}$ with
$h = \sum_{j \le j_{\max}}\sqrt{2j+1}\,\chi_{j}$ and
$Z = Z^{\SU(2)}_{\nmax} = \nmax(\nmax+1)/2$, and let $\KU$ be the
Fej\'er kernel (\ref{eq:K_U1_def}). Then, exactly over the relevant
real quadratic extensions of $\Q$:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] \emph{(Mass distributions.)} The squared Plancherel weights
of the amplitudes give $m^{\SU(2)}(j) = (2j+1)/Z$ on $j \le j_{\max}$
and $m^{\Uone}(k) = \Nt^{-1}$ on $|k| \le \Kmax$;\ both are
probability distributions, and the joint mass factorizes,
$m^{\Kjoint} = m^{\SU(2)} \cdot m^{\Uone}$.
\item[(b)] \emph{(Multiplier symbols.)} Expanding
$\KSU = \sum_{j'} (c_{j'}/Z)\,\chi_{j'}$ by the SU(2) Clebsch--Gordan
rule $\chi_{j_1}\chi_{j_2} = \sum_{J=|j_1-j_2|}^{j_1+j_2}\chi_{J}$,
\begin{equation*}
   c_{j'} \;=\; \sum_{\substack{j_1, j_2 \le j_{\max} \\
                |j_1 - j_2| \le j' \le j_1 + j_2 \\
                j_1 + j_2 + j' \in \mathbb{Z}}}
                \sqrt{(2j_1+1)(2j_2+1)},
\end{equation*}
and the convolution operator $f \mapsto \KSU \ast f$ acts on the
$j'$-isotypic block by the scalar
\begin{equation*}
   \sigma^{\SU(2)}_{\nmax}(j') \;=\; \frac{c_{j'}}{(2j'+1)\,Z},
   \qquad
   \sigma^{\SU(2)}(0) = 1, \quad 0 \le \sigma^{\SU(2)} \le 1, \quad
   \mathrm{supp} \subseteq \{j' \le 2j_{\max}\}.
\end{equation*}
At $\nmax = 2$:\ $(c_{0}, c_{1/2}, c_{1}) = (3, 2\sqrt2, 2)$ and
$\sigma^{\SU(2)} = (1, \sqrt2/3, 2/9)$ at $j' = (0, \tfrac12, 1)$.
For the $\Uone$ factor, direct computation gives
$\sigma^{\Uone}(k) = \max(0, (\Nt - |k|)/\Nt)$
(Katznelson~\cite{katznelson2004}\,Ch.~I.2 at this normalization).
The joint multiplier symbol factorizes,
$\sigma^{\Kjoint} = \sigma^{\SU(2)} \cdot \sigma^{\Uone}$, via
Peter--Weyl on the direct product (characters multiply).
\end{enumerate}
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
(a) is immediate from the definition of the amplitudes (the weights
$\sqrt{2j+1}$ and the flat Dirichlet window respectively), with
normalization $\sum_j (2j+1) = Z$ and $(2\Kmax+1)/\Nt = 1$. (b):\
$|h|^{2} = \sum_{j_1, j_2} \sqrt{(2j_1+1)(2j_2+1)}\,
\chi_{j_1}\chi_{j_2}$ (SU(2) characters are real), and the
Clebsch--Gordan rule contributes each admissible $j'$ once per pair
--- $j'$ running in \emph{unit} steps from $|j_1 - j_2|$ to
$j_1 + j_2$, so only pairs with $j_1 + j_2 + j' \in \mathbb{Z}$
contribute;\
$\sigma(j') = c_{j'}/((2j'+1)Z)$ is the standard convolution
eigenvalue on the $j'$-block for a central kernel with character
coefficient $c_{j'}/Z$. Unitality at $j' = 0$ is $c_{0} = \sum_j
(2j+1) = Z$. Non-negativity and the upper bound follow since
$\sigma$ is the symbol of a positive unital map. The $\nmax = 2$
values are direct, and are verified bit-exactly against independent
Weyl-integration quadrature in the repository
(\texttt{debug/p45\_adversarial\_L2\_memo.md}). \qedhere
\end{proof}
