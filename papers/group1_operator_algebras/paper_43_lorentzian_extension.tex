\begin{abstract}
The four-witness Wick-rotation theorem
(Hartle--Hawking~\cite{hartle_hawking1976}, Sewell~\cite{sewell1982},
Bisognano--Wichmann~\cite{bisognano_wichmann1976},
Unruh~\cite{unruh1976}) was closed at the operator-system level on the
Riemannian truncated Camporesi--Higuchi spectral triple over $\SU(2)$
in the companion Paper~42~\cite{paper42}. The present paper extends
that closure to signature $(s, t) = (3, 1)$ West-coast on the Krein
space $\Krein_{\nmax, \Nt} := \Hilb_{\mathrm{GV}}^{\nmax} \otimes
\C^{\Nt}$ via the
van~den~Dungen~2016~\cite{vandungen2016} Proposition~4.1 lift and the
Bizi--Brouder--Besnard~2018~\cite{bizi_brouder_besnard2018}
$(m, n)$ classification at $(4, 6)$. On a hemispheric wedge
$W_L = P_W^{\mathrm{spatial}} \otimes P_{t \ge 0}$ of $\sthree \times
\R$ with the Bisognano--Wichmann choice of local Hamiltonian
$H_{\mathrm{local}} := \KL^{\alpha, W} / \beta$, we establish:\ (i)~the
geometric BW-$\alpha$ modular Hamiltonian $\KL^\alpha = J_{\mathrm{polar}}
\otimes I_{\Nt}$ has integer spectrum and produces the bit-exact period
closure $\sigma_{2\pi}^{L, \alpha}(O) = O$;\ (ii)~the Tomita--Takesaki
BW-$\gamma$ modular Hamiltonian $\KL^\TT = -\log \Delta_L$ obtained
from polar decomposition of the modular $S$ operator on the GNS
Hilbert--Schmidt space of the wedge KMS state $\rho_W^L = e^{-\KL^{\alpha,
W}}/Z$ produces the bit-exact period closure
$\sigma_{2\pi}^{L, \TT}(a) = a$;\ (iii)~the flow conjugacy
$\sigma_t^{L, \TT}(a) = \sigma_{-t}^{L, \alpha}(a)$ holds bit-exact at
general $t$;\ (iv)~the six-witness collapse on the BW-aligned wedge
KMS state is bit-exact, with $\rho_W^L$ itself $\beta$-independent.
All four results hold at every tested
$(\nmax, \Nt) \in \{1, 2, 3\} \times \{1, 11, 21\}$. The main
substantive structural finding is that the load-bearing
$H_{\mathrm{local}} \ne \DL^W$ scope finding of Paper~42 (the
spectral-action Dirac is not the modular-Hamiltonian generator on the
Bisognano--Wichmann vacuum) is \emph{signature-independent} at the
Riemannian reduction $\Nt = 1$:\ the Lorentzian-side residual
$\Frob{H_{\mathrm{local}} - \DL^W}$ equals the Riemannian-side residual
bit-exactly, sharpening Paper~42 open question O3 from ``open about
signature-dependence'' to a deeper signature-independent feature of the
framework's modular content. Two additional structural findings are
recorded:\ the Bizi--Brouder--Besnard universal anticommutation
$\{\chi, D\} = 0$ does not hold on the truthful Camporesi--Higuchi
Dirac (chirality-as-$\gamma^5$ identification + chirality-diagonal
$\DGV$ + BBB universal axiom is a mutually inconsistent triple), and
the master Mellin engine M3 sub-mechanism is sectional in
$n_\text{fock}$-parity rather than spacetime signature. The closure is
at finite cutoff;\ a Lorentzian-propinquity quantitative-rate analog
(no published construction as of May~2026) is the named multi-month
follow-up. The present paper does not supersede Paper~42:\ Paper~42 is
the Riemannian closure;\ Paper~43 is the Lorentzian extension. Together
they constitute the four-witness Wick-rotation arc at the
operator-system level.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}\label{sec:intro}
% =====================================================================

The four-witness Wick-rotation theorem
(Hartle--Hawking~\cite{hartle_hawking1976}, Sewell~\cite{sewell1982},
Bisognano--Wichmann~\cite{bisognano_wichmann1976},
Unruh~\cite{unruh1976}) asserts that a state of a quantum field
restricted to a region bounded by a bifurcate Killing horizon with
surface gravity $\kappa_g$ is a KMS state at inverse temperature
$\beta = 2\pi / \kappa_g$ with respect to evolution along the
relevant Killing vector field. The natural setting of the theorem is
Lorentzian, signature $(s, t) = (3, 1)$, on globally hyperbolic
spacetimes;\ the shared $2\pi$ across all four faces is $\Vol(S^1)$,
the regularity period of the Euclidean rotation of the observer's
causal-domain coordinates after Wick rotation $\tau = i t$.

The companion Paper~42~\cite{paper42} of the present author closes the
\emph{Riemannian-side} operator-system analog of this theorem on the
truncated Camporesi--Higuchi~\cite{camporesi_higuchi1996} spectral
triple $\Tcal_{\nmax}$ over the round $\sthree = \SU(2)$, in the
spectral-truncation framework of
Connes--van~Suijlekom~\cite{connes_vs2021}. Paper~42 constructs the
hemispheric wedge $P_W$ on the spinor harmonic basis, the wedge KMS
state $\rho_W = e^{-K_\alpha^W}/Z$, and two distinct realisations of
the modular Hamiltonian:\ a geometric BW-$\alpha$ realisation
$K_\alpha = J_{\mathrm{polar}}$ with integer spectrum
(Paper~42 Theorem~5.4, STRONG\_IDENTIFICATION) and a
Tomita--Takesaki BW-$\gamma$ realisation $K_\TT = -\log \Delta$ obtained
from polar decomposition of the modular $S$ operator on the
GNS Hilbert--Schmidt space (Paper~42 Theorem~6.3, UNIFIED\_STRONG).
The two realisations are operator-action conjugates,
$\sigma_t^\TT(O) = \sigma_{-t}^\alpha(O)$, bit-exact at general $t$
(Paper~42 Theorem~7.1).

Paper~42 leaves the Lorentzian extension (Paper~42 open question O1)
as a named multi-month follow-up. The natural construction recipe
for the lift is van~den~Dungen~2016~\cite{vandungen2016} Proposition~4.1:

\begin{quote}
Let $(M, g)$ be a pseudo-Riemannian spin manifold of signature
$(s, t)$ with a spacelike reflection $r$. Then
$(C^\infty_c(M), L^2(\mathbb{S}), i^t \not{\!\!D}_{g_r}, \mathcal{J}_M)$
is an even Krein spectral triple, with
$\mathcal{J}_M = \gamma(e_0)$.
\end{quote}

For $(M, g) = (\sthree \times \R, ds^2_{\sthree} - dt^2)$ at
$(s, t) = (3, 1)$, the Wick-rotated metric is
$g_r = ds^2_{\sthree} + dt^2$ on the positive-definite Euclidean
4-manifold, the exponent of $i$ is $i^t = i^1 = i$, and the
fundamental symmetry is $\mathcal{J}_M = \gamma^0$. The
Bizi--Brouder--Besnard~2018~\cite{bizi_brouder_besnard2018}
classification of indefinite spectral triples by the pair
$(m, n) = (t + s, t - s)$ modulo 8 places Lorentzian $(3, 1)$
West-coast at $(m, n) = (4, 6)$, with primitive signs
$\varepsilon = +1$, $\varepsilon'' = -1$, $\kappa = -1$,
$\kappa'' = +1$ from BBB Table~1.

The present paper closes Paper~42 open question O1 at finite cutoff
via the van~den~Dungen Proposition~4.1 lift on the Camporesi--Higuchi
spatial spinor bundle, with the BBB Connes axiom audit at $(4, 6)$
verifying the four BBB-predicted-sign relations bit-exactly. The
Lorentzian modular Hamiltonian on the hemispheric wedge of $\sthree
\times \R$ inherits the Paper~42 wedge structure verbatim with a
temporal slot added, the BW choice of local Hamiltonian
$H_{\mathrm{local}} := \KL^{\alpha, W} / \beta$ is preserved at the
Lorentzian level, and the wedge KMS state $\rho_W^L = e^{-\KL^{\alpha,
W}}/Z$ is $\beta$-independent under the BW choice. The main theorem is
the Lorentzian extension of Paper~42 Theorems 5.4, 6.3, 7.1, and
Corollary~8.1, with the Riemannian-limit recovery at $\Nt = 1$
preserving each Paper~42 result bit-identically.

\begin{theorem}[Main theorem;\ cf.~Sections~\ref{sec:bw_alpha},
\ref{sec:bw_gamma}, \ref{sec:flow_conjugacy}, \ref{sec:six_witness}]
\label{thm:main_intro}
Let $\Tcal_{\nmax} = (\Op_{\nmax}, \Hilb_{\mathrm{GV}}^{\nmax},
\DGV, J_{\mathrm{GV}})$ denote the truncated Camporesi--Higuchi
spectral triple of cutoff $\nmax$ over the round $\sthree = \SU(2)$ at
KO-dimension $3 \pmod 8$ (Paper~32~\S~III, Paper~38~\cite{paper38}).
Let
\[
   \Tcal_{\nmax, \Nt}^L
   \;=\; \bigl(\,
     \Op_{\nmax}^L,\;
     \Krein_{\nmax, \Nt} = \Hilb_{\mathrm{GV}}^{\nmax} \otimes \C^{\Nt},\;
     \DL = i \bigl(\gamma^0 \otimes \partial_t
            + \DGV \otimes I_{\Nt}\bigr),\;
     \JL
   \,\bigr)
\]
denote its Krein-space extension to signature $(s, t) = (3, 1)$ West-coast
in the Peskin--Schroeder chiral basis per
van~den~Dungen~2016~\cite{vandungen2016} Proposition~4.1, with
$\partial_t$ the centered finite-difference operator on the uniform
grid $t_k = -T_{\max} + k \cdot 2T_{\max}/(\Nt - 1)$ with Dirichlet
zero boundary conditions, and with $\JL$ the lift to $\Krein$ of the
$\Cl(3, 1)$ charge conjugation $U_4 = i\gamma^2$ per
Bizi--Brouder--Besnard~2018~\cite{bizi_brouder_besnard2018} at
$(m, n) = (4, 6)$. Let $W_L := P_W^{\mathrm{spatial}} \otimes P_{t \ge 0}$
denote the hemispheric wedge, where $P_W^{\mathrm{spatial}}$ is the
Paper~42 hemispheric wedge on $\Hilb_{\mathrm{GV}}^{\nmax}$ (Hopf-axis
$m_j$-reflection) and $P_{t \ge 0}$ is the diagonal projector on
$\C^{\Nt}$ selecting $t_k \ge 0$. Let $\rho_W^L := e^{-\KL^{\alpha,
W}}/Z$ denote the wedge KMS state under the Bisognano--Wichmann choice
$H_{\mathrm{local}} := \KL^{\alpha, W} / \beta$, where
$\KL^\alpha := K_\alpha^{\mathrm{spatial}} \otimes I_{\Nt}$ is the
spatial-rotation generator inherited from Paper~42. Then for every
$(\nmax, \Nt) \in \{1, 2, 3\} \times \{1, 11, 21\}$:
\begin{enumerate}\setlength{\itemsep}{2pt}
\item[(i)] $\KL^{\alpha, W}$ has integer spectrum (odd integers),
and the BW-$\alpha$ modular flow satisfies the period closure
\begin{equation}\label{eq:bw_alpha_lorentz_main}
   \sigma_{2\pi}^{L, \alpha}(O)
   \;=\; e^{i \cdot 2\pi \cdot \KL^{\alpha, W}}\, O\,
         e^{-i \cdot 2\pi \cdot \KL^{\alpha, W}}
   \;=\; O
\end{equation}
bit-exactly for every $O \in \Bcal(\Krein_{W_L})$, with maximum
residual $\le 4 \times 10^{-16}$ across the panel.
\item[(ii)] The Tomita--Takesaki modular Hamiltonian
$\KL^\TT := -\log \Delta_L$ obtained from polar decomposition
$S_L = \JL^\TT \cdot \Delta_L^{1/2}$ on the GNS Hilbert--Schmidt space
$\Krein_{\GNS}^L \cong M_{\dim W_L}(\C)$ of $\rho_W^L$ has integer
spectrum, and the BW-$\gamma$ modular flow satisfies the period
closure
\begin{equation}\label{eq:bw_gamma_lorentz_main}
   \sigma_{2\pi}^{L, \TT}(a) \;=\; a
\end{equation}
bit-exactly for every $a \in \Bcal(\Krein_{W_L})$, with maximum
residual $\le 4 \times 10^{-16}$.
\item[(iii)] The two flows are operator-action conjugates,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:flow_conjugacy_main}
   \sigma_t^{L, \TT}(a) \;=\; \sigma_{-t}^{L, \alpha}(a),
   \qquad t \in \R,
\end{equation}
bit-exact at general $t$, with maximum residual $\le 4 \times 10^{-16}$.
\item[(iv)] The six-witness collapse: the six instantiations
$(\BW, \HH_{M=1}, \HH_{M=2}, \Sew_{M=1}, \Unruh_{a=1}, \Unruh_{a=2})$
of the Wick-rotation chain produce literally identical $\Delta_L$ and
$\KL^\TT$ on $W_L$; cross-witness consistency residual is exactly $0$.
\item[(v)] (Riemannian-limit recovery.) At $\Nt = 1$, all of
$P_{W_L}$, $\KL^\alpha$, $\KL^{\alpha, W}$, $\rho_W^L$, $\Delta_L$,
$\KL^\TT$ reduce \emph{bit-identically} to the Paper~42 Riemannian
constructions.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

The closure is, as in Paper~42, a finite-cutoff bit-exact algebraic
identity rather than a continuum analytic-limit statement. The
structural reason is the same in both papers:\ the BW-$\alpha$ geometric
generator $\KL^{\alpha, W} = K_\alpha^W \otimes I_{\Nt}|_{W_L}$ has
integer spectrum on the spinor basis (inherited from Paper~42's
integer spectrum of $K_\alpha^W$ via the $\otimes I_{\Nt}$ factor),
so $e^{i \cdot 2\pi \cdot n} = 1$ for any integer $n$ produces the
period closure pointwise at every cutoff $(\nmax, \Nt)$. The
\emph{Lorentzian} content of Theorem~\ref{thm:main_intro} is that this
identification persists through the Krein-space lift and the
BBB Connes axiom audit at $(4, 6)$ — the four-witness theorem, which
is a Lorentzian statement in the Wightman axiomatic framework, has its
operator-system analog literally satisfied at signature $(3, 1)$ at

Wick-rotation chain.

\paragraph{The headline structural finding (Section~\ref{sec:H_local_signature_independence}).}

Bisognano--Wichmann vacuum at $\beta = 2\pi$. The BW vacuum requires

the Lorentzian-side residual $\Frob{H_{\mathrm{local}} - \DL^W}$

$13.854$. The distinction is therefore \emph{signature-independent at

\subsection{The Krein space $\Krein_{\nmax, \Nt}$}\label{sec:krein_space}

\subsubsection{Convention lock}

We work with the West-coast Lorentzian metric
$\eta = \mathrm{diag}(+1, -1, -1, -1)$ on $\sthree \times \R$
and the Peskin--Schroeder chiral (Weyl) basis for the four
$\Cl(3, 1)$ gamma matrices:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:gamma_chiral}
   \gamma^0 \;=\; \begin{pmatrix} 0 & I_2 \\ I_2 & 0 \end{pmatrix},
   \qquad
   \gamma^i \;=\; \begin{pmatrix} 0 & \sigma^i \\ -\sigma^i & 0 \end{pmatrix}
   \quad (i = 1, 2, 3).
\end{equation}
Defining $\gamma^5 := i \gamma^0 \gamma^1 \gamma^2 \gamma^3$ gives
$\gamma^5 = \mathrm{diag}(-I_2, +I_2)$, with the upper $2$-block the
left-handed sector and the lower the right-handed sector
(Peskin--Schroeder convention). All Clifford-algebra relations
$\{\gamma^\mu, \gamma^\nu\} = 2 \eta^{\mu\nu} I_4$,
$(\gamma^5)^2 = +I_4$, and $\{\gamma^5, \gamma^\mu\} = 0$ are verified
bit-exact at the bare $\Cl(3, 1)$ representation level.

\subsection{The Lorentzian Dirac operator $\DL$}\label{sec:lorentzian_dirac}

\subsubsection{The van~den~Dungen lift}

The van~den~Dungen Proposition~4.1 prescription, instantiated on
$(M, g) = (\sthree \times \R, ds^2_{\sthree} - dt^2)$, gives the
Lorentzian Dirac operator as
\begin{equation}\label{eq:DL_def}
   \DL \;:=\; i \cdot \bigl[\, \gamma^0 \otimes \partial_t
                              + \DGV \otimes I_{\Nt} \,\bigr],
\end{equation}
where $\partial_t$ is the centered finite-difference operator on the
$t$-grid with Dirichlet zero boundary conditions:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:partial_t}
   (\partial_t)_{k, l}
   \;=\; \frac{1}{2 \Delta_t}\bigl(\delta_{l, k+1} - \delta_{l, k-1}\bigr),
   \qquad \Delta_t = \frac{2 T_{\max}}{\Nt - 1},
\end{equation}
extended by zero at the boundaries $k = 0$ and $k = \Nt - 1$. The
prefactor $i^t = i^1 = i$ is fixed structurally by the requirement
of Krein-self-adjointness $\DL^\times = \DL$ where $\DL^\times :=
\gamma^0 \DL^\dagger \gamma^0$ (the alternative prefactor $-i$ would
give $\DL^\times = -\DL$, anti-Krein-self-adjoint).

\subsubsection{Krein-self-adjointness}

The centered FD + Dirichlet zero construction makes $\partial_t$ real
skew-symmetric, hence $\partial_t^\dagger = -\partial_t$; this is the
load-bearing structural property for Krein-self-adjointness via the
vdD recipe. Forward, backward, or upwind FD schemes would not give
$\partial_t^\dagger = -\partial_t$ and would break the
Krein-self-adjointness identity.

\begin{proposition}[Krein-self-adjointness, bit-exact]
\label{prop:krein_self_adjoint}
$\Frob{\DL^\times - \DL} = 0$ bit-exact at every tested $(\nmax, \Nt)
\in \{1, 2, 3\} \times \{1, 11, 21\}$.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
By direct algebraic computation. Using $\partial_t^\dagger = -\partial_t$,
$(\gamma^0)^\dagger = \gamma^0$, $\DGV^\dagger = \DGV$, $(\gamma^0)^2 =
I$, and $\{\gamma^0, \DGV\} = 0$ (the spatial Dirac
$\DGV$ anticommutes with the chirality-swap $\gamma^0 = J_{\mathrm{spatial}}$ at the
operator-level on $\Hilb_{\mathrm{GV}}$ by direct verification on the
spinor harmonic basis), the time and space pieces of $\DL^\dagger$
each contribute the right sign under conjugation by $\gamma^0$, and
$\DL^\times = \DL$ identically. Bit-exactness follows from the
integer-arithmetic structure of $J_{\mathrm{spatial}}$, the diagonal
rational structure of $\DGV$, and the rational structure of
$\partial_t$.
\end{proof}

\subsubsection{Riemannian-limit recovery}

At $\Nt = 1$, the temporal grid is the singleton $\{0\}$ and

reduces to
\begin{equation}\label{eq:DL_riemannian_limit}
   \DL\big|_{\Nt = 1}
   \;=\; i \cdot \bigl[\,\gamma^0 \otimes 0 + \DGV \otimes I_1\,\bigr]
   \;=\; i \cdot \DGV,
\end{equation}
matching the Paper~32~\S~III Camporesi--Higuchi $\DGV$ up to the global
factor of $i$. The factor $i$ is the structural signature of the
vdD Wick-rotation lift:\ at the Krein-space level, the Lorentzian Dirac
is Krein-symmetric ($\DL^\times = \DL$), while the underlying spatial
$\DGV$ is Hilbert-symmetric ($\DGV^\dagger = \DGV$); the $i$ intercedes
between the two adjoint structures via the $\gamma^0$ conjugation.

\subsubsection{The real structure $\JL$}

with $(i\sigma_y)_{\mathrm{chir}} = \bigl(\begin{smallmatrix} 0 & +1 \\

with phase factors $\sigma_{\mathrm{chir}}(+1) = -1,
\sigma_{\mathrm{chir}}(-1) = +1$ (from $i\sigma_y$) and

\subsubsection{Four BBB-predicted-sign axioms (bit-exact)}

\begin{theorem}[Connes axiom audit at $(m, n) = (4, 6)$, bit-exact]
\label{thm:connes_audit}
The four BBB-predicted-sign relations \eqref{eq:bbb_relations} hold
bit-exactly on $\Krein_{\nmax, \Nt}$ at every tested

\begin{enumerate}\setlength{\itemsep}{2pt}
\item[(i)] $\Frob{\JL^2 - I} = 0$;
\item[(ii)] $\Frob{\{\JL, \gamma^5_{\Krein}\}} = 0$;
\item[(iii)] $\Frob{\{\JL, \gamma^0\}} = 0$;
\item[(iv)] $\Frob{\JL \DL - \DL \JL} = 0$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
For (i)\,$\JL^2 = +I$:\ the square of $(i\sigma_y)$ in chirality space
is $-I_{\mathrm{chir}}$, the square of $(i\sigma^2)$ in spin space is
$-I_{\mathrm{spin}}$; the product squares to
$(-I_{\mathrm{chir}}) \otimes (-I_{\mathrm{spin}}) = +I$. Combined
with complex conjugation $K^2 = I$, $\JL^2 = (U_L K)^2 = U_L
\overline{U_L} = U_L^2 = +I$ since $U_L$ is real. For (ii)--(iv): direct
algebraic verification using $(i\sigma_y) \gamma^5 + \gamma^5
(i\sigma_y) = 0$ (the chirality factor anticommutes with $\gamma^5$),
$(i\sigma_y) \gamma^0 + \gamma^0 (i\sigma_y) = 0$ (the chirality
factor anticommutes with the chirality-swap $\gamma^0$), and the
Clifford-algebra-level $\{i\gamma^2, \gamma^0\} = 0$,
$\{i\gamma^2, \gamma^5\} = 0$ verified bit-exact at the bare
$\Cl(3, 1)$ representation level. For the $\JL D_L = +D_L \JL$
relation:\ $\JL$ acts only on the spatial slot, $\DL$ has temporal
and spatial pieces, and the conjugation works out using
$\{J_{\mathrm{spatial}}, U_{L, \mathrm{spatial}}\} = 0$ (the
chirality-swap and chirality-flip-with-spin-flip anticommute) and the
diagonal-in-chirality structure of $\DGV$. Bit-exactness follows from
the integer-entry structure of $U_L$ and the rational structure of
$\DL$.
\end{proof}

The bit-exactness pattern mirrors Propositions~\ref{prop:krein_axioms}
and \ref{prop:krein_self_adjoint}:\ the construction reduces to
operations on real $\pm 1$ integer matrices plus the global $i$ factor,
with no transcendentals or division leading to floating-point rounding.

\label{rem:order_zero_one}
The additional axioms $[a, \JL b \JL^{-1}] = 0$ (order zero) and

\end{remark}

\subsubsection{The BBB universal anticommutation $\{\chi, D\} = 0$ — structural finding}

We record here the second BBB universal relation
\eqref{eq:bbb_universal}, $\chi D = -D \chi$, which is required of any
indefinite spectral triple at any $(m, n)$ per BBB~\S~5(v) and is
signature-independent. On the present construction, this relation
\emph{fails} on the truthful Camporesi--Higuchi $\DGV$.

\begin{proposition}[BBB universal anticommutation failure on truthful $\DGV$]
\label{prop:bbb_axiom_failure}
$\{\gamma^5_{\Krein}, \DL\} \ne 0$. Concretely, at $\Nt = 1$ the
Frobenius residual is $2 \Frob{\DGV}$, with values
$6.00$, $18.33$, $38.88$ at $\nmax = 1, 2, 3$. At $\Nt > 1$ the
residual is further refined by the temporal-derivative piece.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Direct computation. The chiral-basis $\gamma^5 = \mathrm{diag}(-I_2,
+I_2)$ is diagonal, hence commutes with the chirality-diagonal $\DGV$;
$[\gamma^5, \DGV] = 0$ on $\Hilb_{\mathrm{GV}}$. Conversely, $\gamma^0 =
J_{\mathrm{spatial}}$ (the off-diagonal chirality-swap) anticommutes
with $\gamma^5$ by Clifford algebra:\ $\{\gamma^5, \gamma^0\} = 0$.
Therefore
\[
   \{\gamma^5_{\Krein}, \DL\}
   \;=\; i \bigl(\{\gamma^5, \gamma^0\} \otimes \partial_t
                + \{\gamma^5, \DGV\} \otimes I_{\Nt}\bigr)
   \;=\; 2i (\gamma^5 \DGV) \otimes I_{\Nt}.
\]
The Frobenius norm at $\Nt = 1$ is $2 \Frob{\gamma^5 \DGV} =
2 \Frob{\DGV}$ since $\gamma^5$ is unitary on the spatial space.
\end{proof}

We discuss the structural reading and the trade with the
Riemannian-limit recovery in
Section~\ref{sec:bbb_axiom_finding}. Briefly:\ the three properties
(chirality-as-$\gamma^5$ identification + chirality-diagonal $\DGV$ +
BBB universal anticommutation $\{\chi, D\} = 0$) are mutually
inconsistent;\ any two are compatible, the three together are not.
We frame this as a structural feature, following the same R1
resolution as Sprint~L2-D:\ use truthful $\DGV$ (the Riemannian-limit
load-bearing choice), accept the BBB axiom failure as a documented
scope finding, and note that the wedge-modular construction of
Sections~\ref{sec:bw_alpha}--\ref{sec:bw_gamma} is
$\KL^{\alpha, W}$-driven and $\DL$-independent at the operator-action
level, so the period closures of
Theorems~\ref{thm:bw_alpha_lorentz}--\ref{thm:bw_gamma_lorentz} below
hold regardless.

$\Tr(A^{\dagger} B) = \Tr(\Pi A^{\dagger} B \Pi^{-1}) =

$[\Pi, A] = 0$ together with $\Pi^{\dagger} = \Pi$, and
$\Pi B \Pi^{-1} = -B$ from $\{\Pi, B\} = 0$. Hence
$\Tr(A^{\dagger} B) = 0$.
\end{proof}

open. The Connes--Rovelli~\cite{connes_rovelli1994} thermal-time
hypothesis and the Chamseddine--Connes~\cite{chamseddine_connes2010}
spectral-action paradigm point to two distinct natural generators on
the wedge KMS state;\ the Pythagorean orthogonality observed here
suggests the distinction is structural and basis-canonical, not a
small perturbative effect. The $S^3$-specificity of this
construction (Paper~24~\S~5.3~\cite{paper24} fourth-layer
Coulomb/HO asymmetry) places it in the same family as the
spectrum-computing $L_0$, the calibration $\pi$, and the Wilson
matter coupling --- each of which has no clean Bargmann--Segal
$S^5$ analog at the present state of the framework.

\paragraph{O5.\ Non-tracial wedge states (Paper~42 O2 lifted).}
The wedge KMS state $\rho_W^L$ of the present paper is a tracial-Gibbs
state on the wedge sub-Hilbert space. Non-tracial wedge states (KMS
states with respect to a non-tracial reference) would require additional
infrastructure (e.g., Tomita's Type III factor framework). The
extension is a Paper~42 open question O2 lifted to $(3, 1)$;\
the present paper does not address it.

\paragraph{O6.\ Higher-rank compact non-abelian extensions (Paper~42 O4
lifted).} Paper~40~\cite{paper40_unified} establishes a universal
$4/\pi$ rate constant for propinquity convergence on all compact
connected Lie groups with bi-invariant metric. The modular structure
of the present paper plausibly extends to higher-rank cases; the
integer spectrum of the BW-$\alpha$ generator $\KL^{\alpha, W}$ is a
property of $\SU(2)$ specifically (where the half-integer weights
double to integers), and the higher-rank Lorentzian analog requires
verifying that the relevant Cartan generator along the wedge axis
still has the right spectral property at signature $(3, 1)$.

\paragraph{O7.\ BBB-universal-axiom-compatible construction.}
The Sprint L2-D R3 resolution
(Section~\ref{sec:bbb_axiom_finding}) — redefine $\gamma^5$ as an
off-diagonal grading on $\Hilb_{\mathrm{GV}}$ that anticommutes with
truthful $\DGV$ — remains a possible direction for a ``full BBB
Krein spectral triple in the strict Sec 5(v) sense'' with truthful
$\DGV$. The Sprint L2-D R2 resolution — use offdiag $\DGV$, which
satisfies $\{\gamma^5, \DGV^{\mathrm{offdiag}}\} = 0$ but breaks
Riemannian-limit recovery bit-exactness — is similarly available. Both
are distinct directions from the R1 truthful-$\DGV$ choice of the
present paper.
