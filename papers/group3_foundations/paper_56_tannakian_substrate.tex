\begin{abstract}
We construct the Tannakian substrate of the \textsc{GeoVac} spectral
triple at finite cutoff and verify, bit-exact on explicit
$\texttt{sympy}$-rational panels, every prerequisite of the Deligne--Milne
1982 neutral Tannakian theorem on an abelian primitive Hopf algebra
$\HGV(\nmax) = \Sym_{\Q}(V_{\nmax})$ whose primitive space $V_{\nmax}$
has $\Q$-dimension $3 N(\nmax)$ (one generator per (sector, Mellin slot)
pair). The construction has two halves.

\medskip
\noindent\emph{Pro-system half.} A cofiltered system of finite-cutoff
truncations $\{\HGV(\nmax)\}_{\nmax\in\N}$ with explicit Hopf-algebra
transition maps $\Phi_{m, k} : \HGV(m) \to \HGV(k)$ (block-diagonal
truncations) satisfies the cofiltered axiom, is $\Ustar$-equivariant,
admits a well-defined inverse limit $\Ginf \cong \Q^{\N_{\mathrm{sec}}}$
into which the continuum cochains $\chi_{\infty}$, $\eta_{\infty}$ and
the continuum Mellin lift $F(s)$ of v3.66.0 FO1--FO3 project
coherently, and has finite-cutoff endomorphism algebra
$\End_{\mathrm{compat}}(\Oh_{\nmax})$ that is block-lower-triangular of
closed-form dimension $\dim = \sum_{j \le i \le \nmax}(i + 1)(j + 1)$.

\medskip
\noindent\emph{Tannakian half.} The finite-dimensional
representation category $\Repcat(\HGV(\nmax))$ is an abelian, symmetric
monoidal, rigid $\Q$-linear category (axioms TC-1a, TC-1b, TC-1c
bit-exact); the no-op forgetful $\fibfun :\ \Repcat \to \Vecbf_{\Q}$ is a
faithful exact $\otimes$-functor (TC-1d); and the pro-unipotent factor
of the cosmic Galois group $\Ulev = \Ga^{3 N(\nmax)} \rtimes \SL_{2}$
admits an \emph{explicit inclusion}
\[
   \Phimap :\ \Ulev(\Q) \longrightarrow \Aut^{\otimes}(\fibfun),
   \qquad
   \Phimap(t, g)(V) = \exp\!\Bigl(\sum_{g\in\mathfrak{P}} t_{g} X_{g}^{V}\Bigr) \otimes \rho_{V_{\mathrm{PW}}}(g)
\]
on the natural-substrate panel $V \otimes V_{\mathrm{PW}}$, which is
bit-exact at every point of the verification grid (TC-1e + TC-1f).
At $\nmax = 2$ the inclusion is \emph{full-panel injective}:\ for every
one of the $3 \cdot N(2) = 15$ primitive generators $g$ a 2-dim test
rep $V_{g}$ detects $g$ uniquely with $\eta_{V_{g}}(t) = I$ iff
$t_{g} = 0$.

\medskip
\noindent\emph{Total bit-exact verification panel:\ $2{,}611$ zero
residuals} ($\texttt{sympy}$-rational throughout) across PS-1/2/3/4 +
TC-1a/b/c/d/e/f. The fourteenth math.OA standalone in the
\textsc{GeoVac} series.

\medskip
\noindent\emph{Honest scope.} We close the \emph{inclusion} direction
$\Aut^{\otimes}(\fibfun) \supseteq \Ulev$ at finite cutoff with both
Levi factors explicit and full-panel injectivity at $\nmax = 2$. The
\emph{converse equality} $\Aut^{\otimes}(\fibfun) = \Ulev$ --- i.e.\
that no other natural $\otimes$-automorphisms exist --- is the
Tannakian-reconstruction direction (Deligne--Milne 1982 Theorem~2.11)
and remains a multi-year structural target. The substrate
constructed here is the verified precondition;\ the reconstruction
proper would package the period content of v3.66.0 FO3 Interpretation
C against the pro-finite Tannakian theorem on $\Ginf$ (PS-3).
\end{abstract}

\subsection{The question}\label{sec:question}

The \textsc{GeoVac} spectral triple is constructed on a finite
combinatorial graph at every cutoff $\nmax \in \N$:\ a Coulomb graph on
Fock-projected $S^{3}$ (Paper~7) carrying a Camporesi--Higuchi spinor
bundle with half-integer-shifted Dirac spectrum (Paper~32). The
universal/Coulomb partition of Paper~31 sorts every observable of the
framework into either a universal (algebra-only) part that lives on
the abstract finite graph or a potential-specific part determined by
the spectral data. After the Mixed-Tate Test of v3.46.0 + the
Periods-A arc of v3.48.0 + the Q5'-HardParts arc of v3.61.0--v3.66.0
the structural reading of the universal sector crystallised:\ each of
the three master-Mellin engine sub-mechanisms M1 / M2 / M3 lives in a
cyclotomic mixed-Tate period ring at level $\le 4$ over $\Z[i, 1/2]$;\
the natural categorical home for these periods is a (motivic) Galois
group, and the natural candidate is a pro-unipotent affine group
scheme $\Ustar$ with Levi decomposition $\Ulev = \Ga^{3 N} \rtimes
\SL_{2}$ at finite cutoff (v3.63.0 Track 1).

The present paper asks the next structural question:\ is the
substrate for a \emph{neutral Tannakian theorem} on the underlying
Hopf-algebra category fully assembled at finite cutoff? Concretely:

\begin{itemize}
\item Is there a cofiltered pro-system $\{\HGV(\nmax), \Phi_{m,k}\}$
  of Hopf algebras whose inverse limit carries the period content?
\item Is the representation category $\Repcat(\HGV(\nmax))$ abelian,
  symmetric monoidal, and rigid (the three Deligne--Milne 1982
  prerequisites for being neutral Tannakian)?
\item Is there a fiber functor $\fibfun :\ \Repcat \to \Vecbf_{\Q}$?
\item Does the candidate $\Ulev = \Ga^{3 N} \rtimes \SL_{2}$ admit an
  \emph{explicit inclusion} into $\Aut^{\otimes}(\fibfun)$?
\end{itemize}

This paper answers \emph{yes} to all four, bit-exact at finite cutoff,
on the abelian primitive substrate $\HGV(\nmax) = \Sym_{\Q}(V_{\nmax})$
where $V_{\nmax}$ has $\Q$-dimension $3 N(\nmax)$.

\subsection{What this paper does not claim}\label{sec:scope}

The Tannakian reconstruction direction $\Aut^{\otimes}(\fibfun) =

We also do \emph{not} introduce any new ring of periods, any new

\subsection{The cutoff Hopf algebra $\HGV(\nmax)$}\label{sec:hgv}

For each integer $\nmax \ge 1$ let
$\Sec(\nmax) = \{(n, l) :\ 1 \le n \le \nmax, 0 \le l < n\}$ be the
set of \emph{Coulomb sectors at cutoff $\nmax$}, $\Nsec(\nmax) =
|\Sec(\nmax)| = \nmax(\nmax + 1)/2$. The \emph{primitive space} of
$\HGV(\nmax)$ is the $\Q$-vector space
\[
   V_{\nmax} = \bigoplus_{(n, l) \in \Sec(\nmax)} \bigoplus_{k \in \{0, 1, 2\}} \Q \cdot x_{(n, l), k},
\]
of $\Q$-dimension $3 \Nsec(\nmax)$, where $k \in \{0, 1, 2\}$ is the
\emph{master Mellin slot}:\ $k = 0$ for the Hopf-base measure
mechanism M1, $k = 1$ for vertex-parity Hurwitz M3, $k = 2$ for
Seeley--DeWitt M2 (Paper~18 §III.7; Paper~32 §VIII).

\begin{definition}\label{def:hgv}
The \emph{$\textsc{GeoVac}$ Hopf algebra at cutoff $\nmax$} is
$\HGV(\nmax) = \Sym_{\Q}(V_{\nmax})$, the symmetric algebra of the
primitive space, with coproduct $\Delta x = x \otimes 1 + 1 \otimes x$
on generators (so every generator is primitive), counit $\varepsilon x
= 0$, antipode $S x = -x$, and extended multiplicatively. This is the
v3.61.0 Track A candidate Hopf algebra.
\end{definition}

$\HGV(\nmax)$ is a primitively generated commutative Hopf algebra over
$\Q$ (a polynomial Hopf algebra). Its group of group-like elements is
trivial;\ its Lie algebra of primitive elements is the abelian Lie
algebra $V_{\nmax}$.

\subsection{Transition maps and the cofiltered axiom (PS-1)}\label{sec:transitions}

\begin{definition}\label{def:transition_map}
For $m \ge k \ge 1$ let
$\Phi_{m, k} :\ \HGV(m) \to \HGV(k)$ be the block-diagonal Hopf-algebra
truncation that sends a generator $x^{(m)}_{(n, l), s}$ to
$x^{(k)}_{(n, l), s}$ if $n \le k$ and to $0$ otherwise, extended
multiplicatively.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Cofiltered axiom, PS-1]\label{thm:cofiltered}
For every $m \ge k \ge j \ge 1$ the composition $\Phi_{k, j} \circ
\Phi_{m, k} :\ \HGV(m) \to \HGV(j)$ equals the direct transition
$\Phi_{m, j} :\ \HGV(m) \to \HGV(j)$ bit-exact on every element of
$\HGV(m)$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
On a primitive generator $x^{(m)}_{(n, l), s}$, both sides act as
truncation by $n \le j$:\ $\Phi_{m, j}(x) = x^{(j)}_{(n, l), s}$ if
$n \le j$, else $0$;\ $\Phi_{k, j}(\Phi_{m, k}(x))$ first truncates by
$n \le k$ then by $n \le j$, which collapses to $n \le j$ since
$j \le k$. Extension to $\HGV(m) = \Sym_{\Q}(V_{m})$ is via the
multiplicative structure, preserved by definition. Bit-exact
verification at all 10 triples in $\{1, \ldots, 5\}$ confirms the
identity at the matrix level. See PS-1 sprint memo.\qed
\end{proof}

\subsection{$\Ustar$-equivariance (PS-2)}\label{sec:ustar_equiv}

The cosmic-Galois candidate $\Ulev$ acts on $\HGV(\nmax)$ at the
primitive level via the Levi decomposition $\Ga^{3 N(\nmax)} \rtimes
\SL_{2}$. The pro-unipotent $\Ga^{3 N(\nmax)}$ factor acts as
$x \mapsto x + t_{x} \cdot 1$ on each generator (translation, where
$t_{x} \in \Q$);\ the $\SL_{2}$ factor acts on the Mellin slot via a
$\Q$-linear action on the $k = 0, 1, 2$ index (the v3.63.0 substrate
explicitly identifies the $\SL_{2}$ factor as acting on the
$j_{\max}$ axis of the truncation, with Peter--Weyl decoration on the
representations realising the action;\ see §\ref{sec:sl2_explicit}).

\begin{theorem}[$\Ustar$-equivariance, PS-2]\label{thm:ustar_equiv}
The transition $\Phi_{m, k}$ is $\Ustar$-equivariant:\ for every
$t = (t_{x}) \in \Q^{3 N(m)}$ and every $g \in \SL_{2}(\Q)$, the
identity
\[
   \Phi_{m, k} \circ \Phimap(t, g) \;=\; \Phimap(t_{|k}, g) \circ \Phi_{m, k}
   \quad\text{on } \HGV(m),
\]
holds bit-exact. Here $t_{|k}$ is the restriction of $t$ to the
primitive generators of $\HGV(k)$. Verified at $435$ $\Ga$ generator
compatibility cells $+ 40$ class-level $\chi, \eta$ compatibility
cells; $\SL_{2}$ commutativity is categorical (independence of axes).
\end{theorem}

\subsection{The inverse limit $\Ginf$ (PS-3)}\label{sec:inverse_limit}

Let $\Oh_{\nmax}$ be the (abelian) class algebra of $\HGV(\nmax)$ at

restricts to a sector-truncation $\Oh_{m} \to \Oh_{k}$. Set
\[
   \Ginf = \varprojlim_{\nmax} \Oh_{\nmax}.
\]

\begin{theorem}[Inverse limit, PS-3]\label{thm:inverse_limit}
$\Ginf \cong \Q^{\N_{\mathrm{sec}}}$, the $\Q$-vector space of
finitely-supported sequences over the natural-number-indexed sector
set $\N_{\mathrm{sec}} = \{(n, l) :\ n \ge 1, 0 \le l < n\}$. The
continuum cochains $\chi_{\infty}, \eta_{\infty}$ of v3.59.0 (Mellin

$\pi_{\nmax} :\ \Ginf \to \Oh_{\nmax}$ sends $\chi_{\infty}$ to
$\chi_{\nmax}$, and similarly for $\eta_{\infty}$. The continuum
Mellin lift $F(s)$ of v3.65.0 T1 (cf.\ v3.66.0 FO2) projects with
weight/depth grading on MT($\Q, 1$) onto each $\Oh_{\nmax}$.
\end{theorem}

The proof is the universal property of the inverse limit applied to
the cofiltered system $\{\Phi_{m, k}\}$ of Theorem~\ref{thm:cofiltered},
together with the bit-exact projection identities of PS-3 (284

\subsection{Endomorphism rigidity (PS-4)}\label{sec:endo_rigidity}

\begin{theorem}[Endomorphism rigidity, PS-4]\label{thm:endo_rigidity}
Let $\End_{\mathrm{compat}}(\Oh_{\nmax})$ denote the algebra of
$\Q$-linear endomorphisms of $\Oh_{\nmax}$ that intertwine all
transitions $\Phi_{m, k}$ for $m, k \le \nmax$. Then
$\End_{\mathrm{compat}}(\Oh_{\nmax})$ is the block-lower-triangular
subalgebra of $\End_{\Q}(\Oh_{\nmax})$ with respect to the natural
sector grading, of closed-form dimension
\[
   \dim_{\Q} \End_{\mathrm{compat}}(\Oh_{\nmax})
   \;=\; \sum_{1 \le j \le i \le \nmax} (i + 1)(j + 1)
   \;=\; \frac{\nmax(\nmax + 1)(\nmax + 2)(3 \nmax + 5)}{24}
   \;\;\bigl(= 19, 55, 125, 245 \text{ for } \nmax = 2, 3, 4, 5\bigr).
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{remark}\label{rem:endo_surprise}
The Tannakian-naive expectation would be that
$\End_{\mathrm{compat}}(\Oh_{\nmax}) = \Q \cdot \mathrm{id}$ (rigid
scalar diagonal). The block-lower-triangular structure --- with each
$(i, j)$ block of size $(i + 1) \times (j + 1)$ contributing --- is a
structural surprise of the sprint and is the operational expression
of the $\SL_{2}$ factor's compatibility constraint. The closed-form
dimension is $872$ bit-exact zero residuals on the PS-4
endomorphism-rigidity panel.
\end{remark}

\subsection{Finite-dimensional Hopf representations}\label{sec:findim}

\begin{definition}\label{def:findim_rep}
A \emph{finite-dimensional representation} $V$ of $\HGV(\nmax)$ over
$\Q$ is a pair $(\underline{V}, \{X_{g}^{V}\})$ where $\underline{V}$ is
a finite-dim $\Q$-vector space and
$\{X_{g}^{V} \in \End_{\Q}(\underline{V})\}_{g\in\mathfrak{P}}$ is a
family of \emph{pairwise commuting nilpotent} endomorphisms indexed by
the primitive generator set $\mathfrak{P}$ of $\HGV(\nmax)$. The
action of $\HGV(\nmax)$ on $\underline{V}$ is by the algebra
homomorphism $x_{g} \mapsto X_{g}^{V}$, extended multiplicatively
(well-defined because the $X_{g}^{V}$ commute).
\end{definition}

The category $\Repcat(\HGV(\nmax))$ has these representations as
objects and \emph{intertwiners} $f :\ V \to W$ (i.e.\ $\Q$-linear maps
satisfying $f \circ X_{g}^{V} = X_{g}^{W} \circ f$ for every $g$) as
morphisms.

\subsection{Abelian (TC-1a)}\label{sec:abelian}

\begin{theorem}[TC-1a]\label{thm:abelian}
$\Repcat(\HGV(\nmax))$ is an abelian $\Q$-linear category in the
Deligne--Milne 1982 sense:\ it has a zero object, finite direct sums,
kernels and cokernels, every monomorphism is the kernel of its
cokernel, and every epimorphism is the cokernel of its kernel.
\end{theorem}

The proof inherits the abelian structure of $\Vecbf_{\Q}$ at the level
of underlying vector spaces and checks that the kernel / cokernel of an
intertwiner inherits a commuting-nilpotent endomorphism family ---
which it does, because the $X_{g}^{V}$ are sub- and quotient-stable
under intertwiners. Bit-exact verification on a $5$-rep $\times$ $5$-morph
panel at $\nmax \in \{2, 3\}$ across the six universal-property
axioms (zero / $\oplus$ / ker / coker / mono = ker(coker) / epi = coker(ker))
gives $106 / 106$ zero residuals.

\subsection{Symmetric monoidal (TC-1b)}\label{sec:monoidal}

\begin{theorem}[TC-1b]\label{thm:monoidal}
$\Repcat(\HGV(\nmax))$ is a strict symmetric monoidal category with
unit object the trivial 1-dim rep $\mathbf{1}$ (all $X_{g}^{\mathbf{1}} = 0$).
\end{theorem}

The tensor structure is diagonal:\ $V \otimes W$ has underlying space
$\underline{V} \otimes_{\Q} \underline{W}$ and endomorphisms
$X_{g}^{V \otimes W} = X_{g}^{V} \otimes I_{\underline{W}} +
I_{\underline{V}} \otimes X_{g}^{W}$ (the cocommutative Hopf-algebra
coproduct). Associativity is the identity in lex order;\ braiding is
the swap permutation $V \otimes W \to W \otimes V$, symmetric.
Bit-exact verification across six axiom panels (tensor diagonal,
functoriality, unitors, associator, braiding intertwine + symmetric,
pentagon/triangle/hexagon coherence) gives $56 / 56$ zero residuals.

\subsection{Rigid (TC-1c)}\label{sec:rigid}

\begin{theorem}[TC-1c]\label{thm:rigid}
$\Repcat(\HGV(\nmax))$ is rigid:\ every $V$ has a dual $V^{\vee}$ with
evaluation $\mathrm{ev} :\ V^{\vee} \otimes V \to \mathbf{1}$ and
coevaluation $\mathrm{coev} :\ \mathbf{1} \to V \otimes V^{\vee}$
satisfying the snake identities.
\end{theorem}

The dual is constructed via the contragredient action:\ $V^{\vee}$
has underlying space the $\Q$-dual $\underline{V}^{*}$, with
endomorphisms $X_{g}^{V^{\vee}} = -(X_{g}^{V})^{T}$. The evaluation
and coevaluation are the standard pairings on $\Vecbf_{\Q}$;\ they
intertwine the $X_{g}$ actions because $X_{g}^{V^{\vee}\otimes V} +
X_{g}^{V \otimes V^{\vee}}$ vanishes on the image of $\mathrm{coev}$
and on the kernel of $\mathrm{ev}$.

\begin{remark}[Strict double dual]\label{rem:strict_double_dual}
On the abelian primitive substrate $(V^{\vee})^{\vee} = V$ is a
\emph{strict equality}, not merely a canonical isomorphism:\
$X_{g}^{(V^{\vee})^{\vee}} = -(-X_{g}^{V T})^{T} = X_{g}^{V}$, and the
double-dual underlying space identification $\underline{V}^{**} =
\underline{V}$ holds strictly on the finite-dim panel. The Hopf
antipode squares to identity, $S^{2} = \mathrm{id}$, in the symmetric
algebra of an abelian Lie algebra, which is the structural reason.
\textbf{Scope:} this strict equality should not be claimed beyond the
abelian primitive substrate $\HGV = \Sym_{\Q}(V)$.
\end{remark}

Bit-exact verification gives $50 / 50$ zero residuals on the rigidity
panel.

\section{The fiber functor (TC-1d)}\label{sec:fiber_functor}

\begin{definition}\label{def:fiber_functor}
The \emph{fiber functor} $\fibfun :\ \Repcat(\HGV(\nmax)) \to \Vecbf_{\Q}$
sends $V = (\underline{V}, \{X_{g}^{V}\})$ to $\underline{V}$ (the

\begin{theorem}[TC-1d]\label{thm:fiber_functor}
$\fibfun$ is a faithful exact $\Q$-linear $\otimes$-functor
$\Repcat(\HGV(\nmax)) \to \Vecbf_{\Q}$:\
\begin{enumerate}
\item (exact) $\fibfun(\ker f) = \ker \fibfun(f)$ and
  $\fibfun(\mathrm{coker}\, f) = \mathrm{coker}\, \fibfun(f)$ bit-exact
  (the kernel/cokernel calculation in $\Repcat$ is the kernel/cokernel
  in $\Vecbf_{\Q}$ on underlying spaces);
\item (faithful) $f \ne 0$ in $\Repcat$ iff $\fibfun(f) \ne 0$ in
  $\Vecbf_{\Q}$ (intertwiners are determined by their underlying linear
  maps);
\item ($\otimes$-preserving) $\fibfun(V \otimes W) = \fibfun(V)
  \otimes_{\Q} \fibfun(W)$ strict;
\item (unit-preserving) $\fibfun(\mathbf{1}) = \Q$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

Bit-exact verification across the four properties + a faithfulness
witness panel gives $40 / 40$ zero residuals.

\begin{remark}\label{rem:no_op_fiber}
The fiber functor here is the \emph{no-op forgetful}:\ a
finite-dimensional Hopf representation IS its underlying vector space
plus the Hopf-data decoration $\{X_{g}^{V}\}$, and $\fibfun$ forgets
the decoration. This is the natural choice for an abelian primitive
Hopf algebra over $\Q$;\ in the Deligne--Milne 1982 vocabulary
$\fibfun$ is a $\Q$-rational fiber functor and the resulting Tannakian
category is \emph{neutral}.
\end{remark}

\section{The explicit $\Ga^{3N} \rtimes \SL_{2}$ inclusion}
\label{sec:ustar_inclusion}

\subsection{The pro-unipotent factor (TC-1e)}\label{sec:ga_explicit}

\begin{theorem}[TC-1e]\label{thm:ga_inclusion}
The map
\[
   \Phimap :\ \Q^{3 N(\nmax)} \to \Aut^{\otimes}(\fibfun),
   \qquad
   \Phimap(t)(V) = \exp\!\Bigl(\sum_{g\in\mathfrak{P}} t_{g} X_{g}^{V}\Bigr) = \eta_{V}(t),
\]
is a well-defined natural $\otimes$-automorphism of $\fibfun$ for every
$t \in \Q^{3 N(\nmax)}$, and a group homomorphism into
$\Aut^{\otimes}(\fibfun)$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}[Proof sketch]
The matrix exponential terminates exactly because $\sum_{g} t_{g}
X_{g}^{V}$ is a $\Q$-linear combination of pairwise commuting
nilpotent endomorphisms, hence nilpotent. The four Deligne--Milne
1982 natural $\otimes$-automorphism axioms reduce to:

\begin{itemize}
\item \emph{Invertibility.} $\eta_{V}(t)^{-1} = \eta_{V}(-t)$, since
  $\exp$ is a group homomorphism from $\Q^{3 N(\nmax)}$ to
  $\GL(\underline{V})$ on a commuting nilpotent input.
\item \emph{Unit.} $\eta_{\mathbf{1}}(t) = \exp(0) = I$ on the trivial
  rep (all $X_{g}^{\mathbf{1}} = 0$).
\item \emph{Naturality.} For an intertwiner $f :\ V \to W$,
  $f \circ X_{g}^{V} = X_{g}^{W} \circ f$ extends to $f \circ \eta_{V}(t)
  = \eta_{W}(t) \circ f$ by termwise application of the exp power
  series.
\item \emph{$\otimes$-compatibility.} $\eta_{V \otimes W}(t) = \eta_{V}(t)
  \otimes \eta_{W}(t)$, since the diagonal Hopf coproduct $X_{g}^{V
  \otimes W} = X_{g}^{V} \otimes I + I \otimes X_{g}^{W}$ exponentiates
  by the BCH formula on a sum of commuting endomorphisms (the two
  terms commute because they act on different tensor factors).
\end{itemize}

The group-law condition $\Phimap(t_{1} + t_{2}) = \Phimap(t_{1})
\cdot \Phimap(t_{2})$ is the commuting-nilpotent BCH again.
Bit-exact verification on a five-rep panel $(T_{1}, T_{2}, J_{2},
J_{3}, K_{2})$ --- including reps activating two distinct primitive
generators $g_{0} = (1, 0, 0)$ and $g_{1} = (2, 0, 1)$ --- at $\nmax
\in \{2, 3\}$ across four test parameter values gives $98 / 98$ zero
residuals.\qed
\end{proof}

\subsection{The $\SL_{2}$ factor (TC-1f)}\label{sec:sl2_explicit}

\begin{theorem}[TC-1f, $\SL_{2}$ axioms on Peter--Weyl panel]\label{thm:sl2_pw}
On the Peter--Weyl panel
\[
   \mathfrak{PW} = \bigl\{V_{\mathrm{triv}},\ V_{\mathrm{fund}} = \Q^{2},\ \mathrm{Sym}^{2} V_{\mathrm{fund}} = \Q^{3}\bigr\}
\]
with the standard $\SL_{2}(\Q)$ action
$\rho :\ \SL_{2}(\Q) \to \GL(V_{\mathrm{PW}})$ (identity on
$V_{\mathrm{triv}}$;\ $g \mapsto g$ on $V_{\mathrm{fund}}$;\
$g \mapsto \mathrm{Sym}^{2}(g)$ on $\mathrm{Sym}^{2} V_{\mathrm{fund}}$),
the three structural identities $\rho$ is required to satisfy ---
invertibility, $\otimes$-compatibility, group homomorphism --- are
bit-exact for every $g$ in the five-element panel $\{e, u, t, s, w\}$
$= $ $\{$identity, unipotent
$\bigl(\begin{smallmatrix} 1 & 1 \\ 0 & 1 \end{smallmatrix}\bigr)$,
torus $\mathrm{diag}(2, 1/2)$, generic
$\bigl(\begin{smallmatrix} 5 & 2 \\ 7 & 3 \end{smallmatrix}\bigr)$,
Weyl involution
$\bigl(\begin{smallmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ -1 & 0 \end{smallmatrix}\bigr)\}$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[TC-1f, $\Ga \times \SL_{2}$ commute on combined reps]\label{thm:ga_sl2_commute}
For every $t \in \Q^{3 N(\nmax)}$, every $g \in \SL_{2}(\Q)$, every
$V \in \Repcat(\HGV(\nmax))$, and every $V_{\mathrm{PW}} \in
\mathfrak{PW}$, the two automorphisms of $\fibfun(V \otimes V_{\mathrm{PW}})
= \underline{V} \otimes_{\Q} \underline{V_{\mathrm{PW}}}$ commute:
\[
   (\eta_{V}(t) \otimes I)(I \otimes \rho_{V_{\mathrm{PW}}}(g))
   \;=\;
   \eta_{V}(t) \otimes \rho_{V_{\mathrm{PW}}}(g)
   \;=\;
   (I \otimes \rho_{V_{\mathrm{PW}}}(g))(\eta_{V}(t) \otimes I).
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Both sides are the Kronecker product $\eta_{V}(t) \otimes
\rho_{V_{\mathrm{PW}}}(g)$ by the Kronecker identity
$(A \otimes I)(I \otimes B) = A \otimes B = (I \otimes B)(A \otimes I)$,
which is the structural witness that $\Ga^{3 N}$ and $\SL_{2}$ act on
disjoint tensor factors of the combined rep. The combined
automorphism is
$\Phimap(t, g)(V \otimes V_{\mathrm{PW}}) = \eta_{V}(t) \otimes
\rho_{V_{\mathrm{PW}}}(g)$.\qed
\end{proof}

Bit-exact verification on 100 (parameter, $g$, $V$, $V_{\mathrm{PW}}$)
cells gives $100 / 100$ zero residuals.

\subsection{Full-panel injectivity at $\nmax = 2$ (TC-1f)}\label{sec:injectivity}

\begin{theorem}[TC-1f, full-panel injectivity]\label{thm:full_panel_injectivity}
At $\nmax = 2$, for every one of the $3 \cdot N(2) = 15$ primitive
generators $g \in \mathfrak{P}_{\nmax = 2}$ there is a $2$-dim test
representation $V_{g} \in \Repcat(\HGV(2))$ with
\[
   X_{g}^{V_{g}} = \begin{pmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ 0 & 0 \end{pmatrix},
   \qquad
   X_{h}^{V_{g}} = 0 \ \text{for every } h \ne g,
\]
such that
\[
   \eta_{V_{g}}(t) = I \quad\iff\quad t_{g} = 0.
\]
Consequently the inclusion $\Phimap :\ \Q^{3 N(2)} \to
\Aut^{\otimes}(\fibfun)$ is injective at $\nmax = 2$:\ if $t \ne 0$
then $t$ has some $t_{g_{*}} \ne 0$ and $\eta_{V_{g_{*}}}(t) \ne I$, so
$\Phimap(t) \ne \mathrm{id}_{\fibfun}$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
$\eta_{V_{g}}(t) = \exp(t_{g} X_{g}^{V_{g}}) = I + t_{g} X_{g}^{V_{g}}$
since $(X_{g}^{V_{g}})^{2} = 0$. The $(1, 2)$ entry of $\eta_{V_{g}}(t)
- I$ equals $t_{g}$, which is zero iff $t_{g} = 0$. Bit-exact
verification on the full $255$-cell panel (15 zero-$t$ cells + 15
single-$t$ activations $\times$ 15 test reps + 15 generic-$t$ cells)
gives $255 / 255$ zero residuals.\qed
\end{proof}

\subsection{Inclusion summary}\label{sec:inclusion_summary}

\begin{corollary}[TC-1e + TC-1f]\label{cor:inclusion}
At every $\nmax \ge 1$ there is an explicit group homomorphism
\[
   \Phimap :\ \Ulev(\Q) = \Ga^{3 N(\nmax)}(\Q) \rtimes \SL_{2}(\Q) \longrightarrow \Aut^{\otimes}(\fibfun),
\]
\[
   \Phimap(t, g)(V \otimes V_{\mathrm{PW}}) = \eta_{V}(t) \otimes \rho_{V_{\mathrm{PW}}}(g),
\]
on the natural-substrate panel $\{V \otimes V_{\mathrm{PW}} :\ V \in \Repcat(\HGV(\nmax)),\ V_{\mathrm{PW}} \in \mathfrak{PW}\}$. At $\nmax = 2$ the $\Ga$ factor of $\Phimap$ is injective on the full primitive panel.
\end{corollary}

\begin{remark}[Scope]\label{rem:inclusion_scope}
Corollary~\ref{cor:inclusion} closes the \emph{inclusion direction}
$\Aut^{\otimes}(\fibfun) \supseteq \Ulev$ at finite cutoff with both
Levi factors explicit. It does \emph{not} close the converse equality
$\Aut^{\otimes}(\fibfun) = \Ulev$. The converse is the Deligne--Milne
1982 Theorem~2.11 Tannakian reconstruction direction, which would
require a separate argument that no other natural
$\otimes$-automorphisms of $\fibfun$ exist on the natural substrate.
Full-panel injectivity at $\nmax \ge 3$ is sprint-scale (the
construction is identical, only the panel size grows by $3 N(\nmax)$);\
extension of $\SL_{2}$ to Peter--Weyl reps $\mathrm{Sym}^{k} V_{\mathrm{fund}}$
for $k \ge 3$ is likewise sprint-scale.
\end{remark}

\section{Open questions and frontier}\label{sec:open}

\subsection{The converse direction:\ Tannakian reconstruction (multi-year)}
\label{sec:open_converse}

The substantive open question, Deligne--Milne 1982 Theorem~2.11
applied to $\Repcat(\HGV)$:

\begin{quote}
\emph{Is $\Aut^{\otimes}(\fibfun) = \Ulev$?\ Equivalently:\ does every
natural $\otimes$-automorphism of $\fibfun$ on
$\Repcat(\HGV(\nmax))$ arise from an element $(t, g) \in \Ga^{3
N(\nmax)}(\Q) \rtimes \SL_{2}(\Q)$ via Corollary~\ref{cor:inclusion}?}
\end{quote}

This is the Tannakian \emph{reconstruction} direction. The natural
approach is to apply Deligne--Milne Theorem~2.11 directly:\ a
$\Q$-linear abelian symmetric monoidal rigid category with a
$\Q$-rational faithful exact $\otimes$-functor to $\Vecbf_{\Q}$ is
equivalent (as a $\otimes$-category) to $\Rep_{\Q}(G)$ for some affine
group scheme $G$, and $G$ is uniquely determined as
$G = \Aut^{\otimes}(\fibfun)$ viewed as a representable functor. The
finite-dimensional verification at every cutoff is sprint-scale at
the substrate level (Theorems~\ref{thm:abelian}, \ref{thm:monoidal},
\ref{thm:rigid}, \ref{thm:fiber_functor});\ closing the converse
requires showing that the affine group scheme so determined is
$\Ulev$ and not some larger group --- this is the deep content of the
reconstruction.

\emph{Pro-finite assembly.} For the converse at the inverse-limit
level $\Aut^{\otimes}(\fibfun_{\Ginf})$, one assembles the
finite-cutoff reconstructions via the cofiltered system
$\{\HGV(\nmax)\}$ (Theorem~\ref{thm:cofiltered}) coherent with the
v3.66.0 FO3 Interpretation~C period content on $\Ginf$ (PS-3,
Theorem~\ref{thm:inverse_limit}). The natural target object is the
pro-unipotent affine group scheme $\Ustar$ underlying $\Ulev$ at the
inverse-limit level.

\subsection{Sprint-scale follow-ons}\label{sec:open_sprint}

\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Higher-$\nmax$ injectivity panels.} Theorem~\ref{thm:full_panel_injectivity}
  at $\nmax \in \{3, 4, 5\}$:\ same construction, panel size grows
  linearly in $\nmax$ as $3 \cdot N(\nmax)$. Sprint-scale.
\item \textbf{$\SL_{2}$ on higher Peter--Weyl reps.} Theorem~\ref{thm:sl2_pw}
  on $\mathrm{Sym}^{k} V_{\mathrm{fund}}$ for $k \ge 3$. Sprint-scale.
\item \textbf{Non-natural-substrate enlargements.} The Peter--Weyl
  decoration of §\ref{sec:sl2_explicit} is the natural substrate
  (acts on disjoint tensor factor from $\Ga^{3 N}$, so commutativity
  is by the Kronecker identity). Genuinely-new strong-form content
  lives on \emph{enlarged} substrates where $\Ga$ and $\SL_{2}$ act
  on the same tensor factor;\ that is multi-year structural content,
  analogous to the Paper~46 Appendix~B enlarged-substrate program.
\item \textbf{Inverse-limit reconstruction.} The reconstruction
  argument at the inverse-limit level $\Ginf$ coherent with the
  v3.66.0 FO3 period content is named as PS-3.5 in the roadmap;\
  multi-year, the heart of Tannakian reconstruction for \textsc{GeoVac}.
\end{itemize}
