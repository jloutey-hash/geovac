Time as Projection: The Klein-Gordon Spectrum on S^3 × ℝ and the Algebraic / Observation Split

Josh Loutey · GeoVac Paper 35 · Precision Observations · DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.21286852 · PDF

Abstract

The bare relativistic Klein-Gordon spectrum on $S^3 × ℝ$ is $π$-free in the algebraic-extension ring $\mathbb{Q}[\sqrt{d_1}, \sqrt{d_2}, \ldots]$ with $d_i$ positive square-free integers, for every rational mass-squared parameter. We verify this symbolically for $n \in [1, 50]$ across the panel $m^2 \in \{0, 1, 1/4, 2\}$ (200 cases, zero transcendentals). Mass is therefore a parameter projection that preserves the bare-graph algebraic ring; it does not introduce $π$. $π$ enters the spectrum at exactly one step: temporal compactification. On $S^3 × S^1_\beta$ the temporal direction acquires Matsubara modes $\omega^t_k = 2π k / \beta$, and the first $π$-bearing eigenvalue is the $(n{=}0, k{=}1)$ mode with $\omega^2 = 4π^2$. We exhibit the before/after explicitly: every $(n, 0)$ eigenvalue is $π$-free; every $(n, k {\neq} 0)$ eigenvalue contains $2π/\beta$. The conformally coupled massless scalar Casimir energy on unit $S^3$ (spatial only, no temporal compactification) evaluates to the exact rational $E_Cas = 1/240$, with no transcendental content at any step of the $ζ$-regularized derivation. The Stefan-Boltzmann constant $π^2/90$ appears in the high-temperature expansion of the free-energy density on $S^3 × S^1_\beta$ only via the Matsubara sum, exactly through the mechanism of the spectral $π$-injection. We propose a refinement of Paper 34's projection dictionary: the implicit ``scalar parameter projection'' should be split into (i) a rest-mass projection (introduces $m$, dimension mass, transcendental signature trivial, ring-preserving for $m^2 \in \mathbb{Q}$) and (ii) an observation / temporal-window projection (introduces $\beta$, dimension time, transcendental signature $2π \cdot \mathbb{Q}$ per Matsubara mode; Stefan-Boltzmann $π^2/90$ in the high-$T$ limit). These are categorically distinct; lumping them as a single ``parameter row'' under-resolves the transcendental axis of the dictionary. The structural reading is sharper than Paper 34's layer description: a GeoVac observable contains $π$ if and only if its evaluation includes a continuous integration over a temporal or spectral parameter that has been promoted from the discrete graph spectrum. The discrete graph itself is $π$-free; the projection that integrates is where $π$ appears. Existing GeoVac observables (Pauli counts, magic numbers, hydrogenic eigenvalues, Bargmann–Segal lattice, graph-native QED) are confirmatory; the canonical $π$-bearing observables (vacuum-polarization $\Pi = 1/(48π^2)$, $\beta$-function $2α^2/(3π)$, Stefan-Boltzmann) are all evaluated through continuous integrations of the temporal kind. This paper introduces no new computation beyond the verification sprint $\mathrm{KG}-1$, $\mathrm{KG}-2$, $\mathrm{KG}-3$. Its contribution is the structural observation that time, not mass, is the projection axis along which $π$ enters the framework, and that the observer's finite-time measurement window is the formal mechanism.
