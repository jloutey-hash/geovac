State-space Gromov–Hausdorff convergence of truncated tensor-product Camporesi–Higuchi spectral triples on S³ × S³

Josh Loutey · GeoVac Paper 39 · Operator Algebras / Noncommutative Geometry · DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.21286666 · PDF

Abstract

We prove that the Connes–van Suijlekom truncations of the tensor-product Camporesi–Higuchi spectral triple $\Tcal_{S³}^{\lambda_a} ⊗ \Tcal_{S³}^{\lambda_b}$ on $S³ × S³$ at two (possibly distinct) focal lengths $\lambda_a, \lambda_b > 0$ converge to the continuum tensor-product triple in van Suijlekom's state-space Gromov–Hausdorff distance, with explicit qualitative-rate bound \( \Lambda(\Tcal_{\nmaxa} ⊗ \Tcal_{\nmaxb}, \Tcal_{S³}^{\lambda_a} ⊗ \Tcal_{S³}^{\lambda_b}) \le C_3^{(2)} \cdot (1 + 2\sqrt{2}) \cdot \max(\gamma_{\nmaxa}/\lambda_a, \gamma_{\nmaxb}/\lambda_b), \) where $C_3^{(2)} \le \sqrt{2}$ is a closed-form Lipschitz comparison constant from the (tight) triangle bound on the Connes–Marcolli joint Leibniz rule, and $\gamma_n = (4/π)\log n / n + O(1/n)$ inherits from the single-factor result of [ref]. The proof proceeds factor-by-factor through tensor extensions of the five single-factor lemmas (operator system, central spectral Fejér kernel, Lipschitz comparison, Berezin reconstruction, propinquity assembly). The two technical risks specific to the tensor product — a closed-form Lipschitz comparison constant on the full operator system, and a rigorous bound on the cross-Stein–Weiss height term — both close via the triangle bound on the joint Leibniz rule. (An earlier Pythagorean refinement claiming $C_3^{(2)} < 1$ has been withdrawn: the graded anticommutation does not make the two Leibniz terms operator-norm orthogonal — on the actual Camporesi–Higuchi harmonics they add constructively, so the triangle bound is tight and $C_3^{(2)} \nearrow \sqrt{2}$, exceeding $1$ for cutoffs beyond $(3,3)$. The convergence theorem is unaffected, since $\sqrt{2}$ is a finite constant.) The strictly stronger Latrémolière propinquity is not claimed: the dual-reach ($\mathrm{reach}_P$) step of the joint assembly inherits the single-factor $\mathrm{reach}_P$ named gap of Papers 38/40 (the invalid cb-norm dual-reach), so the object established is van Suijlekom state-space Gromov–Hausdorff convergence. The result extends [ref] to the simplest non-abelian tensor case, and closes a tensor-product state-space-GH case that the published literature on products of metric spectral triples [ref] does not cover; existing tensor-product propinquity results require the second factor to be finite-dimensional, AF, abelian, or to reduce to a single-character / Hopf-$S^1$ structure.
