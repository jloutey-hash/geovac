State-space Gromov–Hausdorff convergence of truncated Camporesi–Higuchi spectral triples on S³

Josh Loutey · GeoVac Paper 38 · Operator Algebras / Noncommutative Geometry · DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.21286585 · PDF

Abstract

We prove, unconditionally, that the Connes–van Suijlekom truncations of the round $S³ = SU(2)$ Camporesi–Higuchi spectral triple converge to the continuum spectral triple in van Suijlekom's state-space Gromov–Hausdorff distance [ref], with explicit rate \( d_{\mathrm{GH}} \le \gamma_{n_max} = (4/π + o(1)) \log n_max / n_max . \) The truncated state spaces are metrized by the left-translation Lipschitz seminorm, which equals the Dirac/metric Lipschitz constant on the continuum and whose kernel at every finite cutoff is exactly the scalars (the truthful Dirac-commutator seminorm itself degenerates at finite cutoff; a remark quantifies this). The proof is a compression/lifted-state argument in the pattern of the compact-metric-group theorem of Gaudillot-Estrada–van Suijlekom [ref], with self-contained proofs: the dual map evaluates conjugated operators against the central spectral Fejér vector state, which embeds exactly into the spinor window via the $\mathrm{SU}(2)_L × \mathrm{SU}(2)_R$ block decomposition $V_j ⊗ (V_{j \pm 1/2})$ of the Camporesi–Higuchi eigenspaces, and both almost-inverse defects collapse to Fejér smoothing — one on functions, one as operator conjugation-averaging — at the common moment $\gamma_{n_max}$. The contribution relative to the scalar theory: the spinor-window transport, per-band multiplicity-one injectivity on the chirality-doubled multiplier system, the closed-form rate moment, and the $4/π$ asymptotic constant. Supporting structure is organized in five lemmas: (L1$'$) a truncated operator system on the full chirality-doubled spinor bundle; (L2) a positive central spectral Fejér kernel on $SU(2)$ with closed-form mass-concentration moment and Stein–Weiss-type quantitative rate $\gamma_n \sim (4/π)\log n / n$; (L3) a Lipschitz comparison bound $\opnorm{[\DCH, \Mat_f]} \leq C_3 \norm{\nabla f}_{L^\infty}$ with $C_3 = 1$; (L4) a Berezin map (convolution form) that is positive, contractive, an approximate identity, and L3-compatible; (L5) assembly of the distance bound via an approximation pair. The asymptotic constant $4/π = \Vol(S^2)/π^2$ identifies as the Hopf-base measure factor in the standard $SU(2)/U(1)$ Haar normalisation, exhibiting the expected one-log slowdown of the non-abelian SU(2) rate relative to the Leimbach–van Suijlekom [ref] torus rate $O(1/n_max)$. State-space Gromov–Hausdorff convergence of scalar truncations for all compact metric groups — including $SU(2)$ — is established by Gaudillot-Estrada–van Suijlekom [ref];\ the residual contribution here is the chirality-doubled Camporesi–Higuchi spinor substrate, the closed-form rate moment, and the explicit $4/π$ asymptotic constant. The construction supplies quantitative metric data for almost-commutative spectral-triple constructions hosted on $S³$ [ref].
