QED on S^3: Spectral Geometry of Perturbative Transcendentals

Josh Loutey · GeoVac Paper 28 · QED and Gauge Theory · DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.21286799 · PDF

Abstract

We compute perturbative QED spectral sums on the three-sphere $S^3$ using the Camporesi–Higuchi Dirac spectrum and the Hodge-1 (photon) spectrum with SO(4) vertex selection rules. Eight structural results emerge. (1) T9 theorem: the spectral zeta function of the squared Dirac operator $D^2$ is a two-term polynomial in $π^2$ with rational coefficients at every integer $s$; no odd-zeta content appears (Theorem (ref)). (2) Even/odd parity discriminant: operator order (2nd vs 1st) is the primary transcendental discriminant—even $s$ produces $π^{\mathrm{even}}$, odd $s$ produces $ζ(3)$, $ζ(5)$, etc. (Theorem (ref)). (3) \textbf{$\chi_{-4}$ identity:} $D_{\mathrm{even}}(s) - D_{\mathrm{odd}}(s) = 2^{s-1}(\beta(s) - \beta(s{-}2))$ at every integer $s \ge 2$, a concrete spectral-zeta to Dirichlet-$L$ bridge (Theorem (ref)). (4) Self-energy structural zero: the one-loop electron self-energy on $S^3$ vanishes identically at the ground state $n_{\mathrm{ext}}=0$ as a consequence of vertex parity (Theorem (ref)). (5) Anomalous magnetic moment: the one-loop vertex correction with a $q=1$ probe photon yields $F_2 / [α/(2π)] = 1.084$ at the lowest external mode ($n_{\mathrm{ext}}=1$), approximately 8.4% above the flat-space Schwinger value, matching the Parker–Toms leading curvature correction within 0.4%. The next-order coefficient $c_3 = -5.946(3) × 10^{-7}$ is nonzero at $200.9 \sigma$, so the curvature expansion does not terminate at second order (Sec. (ref)). (6) Depth-$k$ tower: loop order bounds the motivic depth of the level-2 (alternating-MZV) content; it does not automatically mint new irreducibles. The two-loop $S_{\min}$ decomposes exactly into Hoffman double $t$-values (classical periods of $\MT(\Z[1/2])$; the prior ``PSLQ-irreducible'' claim was a basis-coverage artifact); the three-loop $S^{(3)} = 31.5726\ldots$ (rigorous bracket $[31.57063, 31.57300]$) has realized depth $\le 2$ on every identified component: the catalogued depth-2 generators $t(5,1)$, $t(5,3)$, $t(7,1)$ enter at coefficients $\pm\tfrac12$, and nothing new is minted (Proposition (ref), Sec. (ref)). (7) $ζ(3)$ complementarity: the degeneracy-weighted fine-structure shell sum $\mathcal{S}(n) = n(5n-4)/2$ and the Dirac mode degeneracy $g_n^{\mathrm{Dirac}} = 2n(n+1)$ satisfy $\mathcal{S}(n) + g_n^{\mathrm{Dirac}} = 9n^2/2$ exactly, so the Dirichlet series identity $D_{\mathcal{S}}(s) + D_{g}(s) = \tfrac{9}{2} ζ(s{-}2)$ holds for all $s$: the $ζ(s{-}1)$ terms—all odd-zeta content—cancel identically (Theorem (ref)). (8) Fine-structure Euler sum cancellation and product survival rule: the degeneracy-weighted Dirichlet sums $D_p$ at order $(Zα)^{2p}$ in the exact Dirac hydrogen spectrum, computed through $D_6$ ($(Zα)^{12}$, weight 11), satisfy a product survival rule: $ζ(2) × ζ(\mathrm{odd})$ cancels algebraically at every order, while exactly $\max(0, \lfloor(2p{-}5)/2\rfloor)$ products survive (Observations (ref), (ref)). The cancellation mechanism is the $n^2$ Fock degeneracy — the same weighting that produces $F = ζ(2) = D_{n^2}(4)$ in Paper 2. However, PSLQ confirms that Paper 2's $K = π(B + F - \Delta)$ is NOT a $\mathbb{Q}$-linear combination of $D_2, \ldots, D_6$: the spectral-action ingredients of $K$ inhabit the $π$-polynomial ring of Theorem (ref), while the Sommerfeld sums carry irreducible odd-zeta from the Euler–Zagier MZV algebra. A complementary graph-native formulation (Sec. (ref)) computes all one-loop diagrams as finite matrix traces on the GeoVac Fock graph, requiring no infinite sums, regularization, or renormalization. All graph-native QED quantities are $π$-free (algebraic); the projection exchange constant relating graph and continuum VP is rational at every finite truncation, confirming Paper 18's calibration-tier classification. The continuum self-energy structural zero (Theorem (ref)) is broken on the finite graph: the pendant-edge theorem (Proposition (ref)) proves $\Sigma_{\mathrm{graph}}(\mathrm{GS}) = 2(n_{\max}-1)/n_{\max} \to 2$ exactly, never healing in the graph limit. The root cause is that the graph photon is a scalar 1-cochain, lacking the spin-1 vector structure whose SO(4) selection rules enforce the continuum zero. The graph-native anomalous moment $F_2(t)$ is an even rational function of the hopping coupling $t$ over $\mathbb{Q}(\sqrt{2},\sqrt{3},\sqrt{6})$, with $F_2(\kappa)$ decreasing as a power law $\sim 3.5 n_{\max}^{-0.57}$ ($F_2 = 2.353, 1.873, 1.589, 1.396, 1.253$ at $n_{\max} = 2, \ldots, 6$). A systematic selection rule census shows that only 1 of 8 continuum QED selection rules survives on the scalar Fock graph (Gaunt/CG sparsity). Building QED on the native Dirac graph with $(n, \kappa, m_j)$ node labels recovers 4 of 8 rules, establishing a three-tier partition: 1 always survives, 3 are spinor-recoverable (intrinsic to spinor quantum numbers), and 4 require vector photon structure (calibration tier). A five-track vector QED sprint (VQ-1–5) confirms this partition from a complementary direction: neither Pauli spin matrices at the vertex, nor direction-resolved edge Hodge decomposition, nor their combination recovers any of the 4 vector-required rules. However, the transverse photon propagator $G_T = (d_1 d_1^\top)^+$ built from Paper 30's plaquette boundary operator does recover the ground-state structural zero ($\Sigma_T(\mathrm{GS}) = 0$ exactly), refining the partition to 1+3+1+3: the plaquette-recoverable tier is topological (pendant-edge exclusion from closed walks), requiring no photon quantum numbers. Adding explicit vector photon quantum numbers $(q, m_q)$ with Clebsch–Gordan vertex coupling recovers 7 of 8 continuum selection rules for scalar electrons and 8 of 8 for Dirac electrons (Sec. (ref)). The calibration cost is exactly one transcendental per loop: $π$, entering as the $S^2$ Weyl exchange constant $1/(4π)$ from Paper 18's taxonomy. For scalar electrons, the sole failure is Furry's theorem; for Dirac electrons, the diagonal vertex coupling $V(a, a, q, m_q) = 0$ vanishes identically by the Dirac spinor phase constraint: the $α$-matrix is off-diagonal in large/small component space, and for $a = b$ the cross terms combine, via Hermiticity and the $i$-factor on the small component, into an imaginary part that $m_l$ conservation forces to vanish. This is a kinematic identity of the single-particle vertex, not the field-theoretic charge conjugation mechanism. For Dirac electrons, the 8 rules partition as $1$–$7$–$8$: graph topology $\to$ angular momentum conservation $\to$ Dirac spinor phase constraint (zero additional cost). The VP projection constant $C$ does not serve as a universal graph-to-continuum projector: $C × F_2$ diverges from $α/(2π)$, confirming that different diagram topologies require independent projection factors. Comprehensive transcendental tables for $D(s)$, $D_{\mathrm{even}}(s)$, $D_{\mathrm{odd}}(s)$, and the one-loop self-energy are presented, all backed by the test suite.
