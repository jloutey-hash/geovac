Lorentzian propinquity on truncated SU(2)×U(1)_T Krein spectral triples: a degeneracy theorem

Josh Loutey · GeoVac Paper 45 · Operator Algebras / Noncommutative Geometry · DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.21286689 · PDF

Abstract

We construct truncated Krein spectral triples over the compact-temporal Wick-rotated manifold $\Manifold = S³ × \SoneT$:\ $\Krein_{n_max,\Nt} := \HGV^{n_max} ⊗ \C^{\Nt}$ is the chirality-doubled Camporesi–Higuchi spinor space tensored with the $\Nt$-mode Fourier truncation of $L^{2}(\SoneT)$, with fundamental symmetry $\JL = \JL^{\spat} ⊗ I_{\Nt}$ at the Bizi–Brouder–Besnard [ref] classification $(m, n) = (4, 6)$, Lorentzian Dirac $\DL = i(\gamma^{0} ⊗ \partial_{t} + \DGV ⊗ I_{\Nt})$ per van den Dungen 2016 [ref] Proposition 4.1, and operator system built from chirality-doubled scalar $3$-$Y$ spatial multipliers tensored with momentum-diagonal temporal multipliers. The natural device for a ``Lorentzian propinquity'' on this family is to compress to the Krein-positive subspace $\Kplus$, where the Krein product is a positive-definite Hilbert inner product, and to apply Riemannian quantum-metric machinery to the compressed triple $(\Pplus\Op^{L}\Pplus, \Kplus, \Pplus\DL\Pplus)$. Our main theorem shows that this device fails structurally (Theorem (ref)): Krein-self-adjointness of the anti-Hermitian spatial block forces $\{\JL, \DGV ⊗ I_{\Nt}\} = 0$, so the compression annihilates the spatial Dirac, $\Pplus(\DGV ⊗ I)\Pplus = 0$, while the momentum-diagonal temporal multipliers commute with the surviving temporal block; the compressed-Dirac Lipschitz seminorm therefore vanishes identically on the operator system built from spatial and momentum-diagonal multipliers (verified bit-exactly at $(n_max, \Nt) \in \{(2,3), (3,5)\}$: kernel $= 100%$ of that multiplier basis), and no quantum Gromov–Hausdorff-type distance is defined on it. The annihilation is thus multiplier-algebra specific, not absolute: the Toeplitz temporal multipliers named below as the open target survive the same compression ($s_{\Kplus}(I_s ⊗ S_q) = \omega_q$ exactly; Status note 2026-06-18), but the surviving seminorm carries no indefinite-signature content even under a genuine temporal Krein involution — a sharpened ``weakens-to-convention'' verdict. What does hold: the operator-level $\JL$-equivariance of the Berezin/truncation pair; the structural identity $[\DL, a_{s} ⊗ a_{t}] = i[\DGV, a_{s}] ⊗ a_{t}$, which is precisely the diagnosis that the momentum-diagonal temporal algebra is metrically invisible; and a spatial convergence statement (Proposition (ref)): the spatial truncations converge in van Suijlekom's state-space Gromov–Hausdorff distance [ref] at the companion-paper rate $O(\logn_max/n_max)$ — unconditional, via the companion paper's translation-seminorm theorem — with the temporal factor a metrically invisible spectator carried $\JL$-equivariantly. The genuine Lorentzian/Krein quantum-metric convergence problem — a temporal multiplier algebra the Dirac can see (Toeplitz compressions of $e^{iqt}$) and a Krein-compatible Lipschitz seminorm that does not compress $\DL$ — is stated as an open target with a sharpened specification (\S (ref)). An earlier draft of this paper claimed a convergence theorem through the compression device; that claim is retracted (Remark (ref)).
