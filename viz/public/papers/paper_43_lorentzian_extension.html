Lorentzian extension of the four-witness Wick-rotation theorem on truncated S³ × ℝ spectral triples at finite cutoff

Josh Loutey · GeoVac Paper 43 · Operator Algebras / Noncommutative Geometry · DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.21286683 · PDF

Abstract

The four-witness Wick-rotation theorem (Hartle–Hawking [ref], Sewell [ref], Bisognano–Wichmann [ref], Unruh [ref]) was closed at the operator-system level on the Riemannian truncated Camporesi–Higuchi spectral triple over $SU(2)$ in the companion Paper 42 [ref]. The present paper extends that closure to signature $(s, t) = (3, 1)$ West-coast on the Krein space $\Krein_{n_max, \Nt} := \Hilb_{\mathrm{GV}}^{n_max} ⊗ \C^{\Nt}$ via the van den Dungen 2016 [ref] Proposition 4.1 lift and the Bizi–Brouder–Besnard 2018 [ref] $(m, n)$ classification at $(4, 6)$. On a hemispheric wedge $W_L = P_W^{\mathrm{spatial}} ⊗ P_{t \ge 0}$ of $S³ × ℝ$ with the Bisognano–Wichmann choice of local Hamiltonian $H_{\mathrm{local}} := \KL^{α, W} / \beta$, we establish: (i) the geometric BW-$α$ modular Hamiltonian $\KL^α = J_{\mathrm{polar}} ⊗ I_{\Nt}$ has integer spectrum and produces the bit-exact period closure $\sigma_{2π}^{L, α}(O) = O$; (ii) the Tomita–Takesaki BW-$\gamma$ modular Hamiltonian $\KL^\TT = -\log \Delta_L$ obtained from polar decomposition of the modular $S$ operator on the GNS Hilbert–Schmidt space of the wedge KMS state $\rho_W^L = e^{-\KL^{α, W}}/Z$ produces the bit-exact period closure $\sigma_{2π}^{L, \TT}(a) = a$; (iii) the flow conjugacy $\sigma_t^{L, \TT}(a) = \sigma_{-t}^{L, α}(a)$ holds bit-exact at general $t$; (iv) the six-witness collapse on the BW-aligned wedge KMS state is bit-exact, with $\rho_W^L$ itself $\beta$-independent. All four results hold at every tested $(n_max, \Nt) \in \{1, 2, 3\} × \{1, 11, 21\}$. The main substantive structural finding is that the load-bearing $H_{\mathrm{local}} \ne \DL^W$ scope finding of Paper 42 (the spectral-action Dirac is not the modular-Hamiltonian generator on the Bisognano–Wichmann vacuum) is signature-independent at the Riemannian reduction $\Nt = 1$: the Lorentzian-side residual $\Frob{H_{\mathrm{local}} - \DL^W}$ equals the Riemannian-side residual bit-exactly, sharpening Paper 42 open question O3 from ``open about signature-dependence'' to a deeper signature-independent feature of the framework's modular content. Two additional structural findings are recorded: the Bizi–Brouder–Besnard universal anticommutation $\{\chi, D\} = 0$ does not hold on the truthful Camporesi–Higuchi Dirac (chirality-as-$\gamma^5$ identification + chirality-diagonal $\DGV$ + BBB universal axiom is a mutually inconsistent triple), and the master Mellin engine M3 sub-mechanism is sectional in $n_fock$-parity rather than spacetime signature. The closure is at finite cutoff; a Lorentzian-propinquity quantitative-rate analog (no published construction as of May 2026) is the named open frontier, beyond sprint scale. The present paper does not supersede Paper 42: Paper 42 is the Riemannian closure; Paper 43 is the Lorentzian extension. Together they constitute the four-witness Wick-rotation arc at the operator-system level.
