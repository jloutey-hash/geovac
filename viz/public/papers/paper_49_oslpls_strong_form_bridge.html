Operator-system Lorentzian pre-length spaces and the strong-form Krein–Mondino–Sämann bridge: cross-KMS-state thermal-time stack and strict super-additivity

Josh Loutey · GeoVac Paper 49 · Operator Algebras / Noncommutative Geometry · DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.21286703 · PDF

Abstract

We introduce the category $\OSLPLS$ of operator-system Lorentzian pre-length spaces (OSLPLS), the non-commutative extension of Mondino–Sämann's covered Lorentzian pre-length space framework [ref] obtained by replacing the underlying set $X$ with the state space $\Scal(\Mcal)$ of an operator-system topography $\Mcal$, the time separation $\ell$ with the modular-flow time separation $\ellOS_{\omega}$ on a faithful basepoint state $\omega$, and the cover by sub-operator-systems $(\Mcal_{k})_{k\in\N}$. The commutative Mondino–Sämann category $\LorPLG$ embeds in $\OSLPLS$ as a faithful sub-category via $X\mapsto C(X)$, $\ell\mapsto L_{\ell}$, $o\mapsto\delta_{o}$. \noindentMain result. The bridge functor \[ \Wflip:\KreinMMflip\longrightarrow\OSLPLS \] extends the Paper 48 [ref] K$^{+}$-weak-form bridge $\Wfun$ to the full $M\ne 0$ Paper 46 [ref] Appendix B enlarged Krein substrate. The \emph{Bridge Theorem 6.4$^{\prime}$-Q1$^{\prime}$} (Theorem (ref)) is stated on four properties, the metric-dependent ones of which are descoped (see the Status note: the properties quantifying over the upstream Paper 45/46 metric quantity are open pending repair; the thermal-time-stack content is state-level and survives): (B1$^{\prime}$) structural correspondence inherited from the OSLPLS axiom transport (Theorem (ref));\ (B2$^{\prime}$) reverse triangle inequality with strict super-additivity on three-orbit triples, established via the Connes–Rovelli thermal-time stack across distinct KMS states, a construction not previously published; (B3$^{\prime}$) pre-compactness via the Paper 44 [ref] propagation-number bound $\mathrm{prop}\le 4$ on the enlarged substrate. The fourth property, (B4$^{\prime}$) convergence transport, is descoped:\ it would follow from Paper 46's strong-form propinquity bound, but that seminorm is degenerate (Paper 45/46), so the metric-level convergence leg is not established. \noindentThe thermal-time stack. The substantive new mathematics of this paper sits in Section (ref). Three structural ingredients combine: (i) the orbit-KMS lemma (Lemma (ref)): every modular orbit on the bridge image carries an intrinsic effective KMS state inherited from the Bisognano–Wichmann vacuum via GNS restriction; (ii) the Connes (1973) cocycle Radon–Nikodym derivative [ref] $\cocyc{i}{j}=[D\omegaOrb{j}:D\omegaOrb{i}]_{t}$ intertwines distinct modular flows on shared sub-operator-systems and provides a cross-orbit thermal-time bridge; (iii) the triple-intersection cocycle identity (TICI, Theorem (ref)):\ $\cocyc{1}{3}=\cocyc{1}{2}\cdot\sigma_{t}^{\omegaOrb{1}}(\cocyc{2}{3})$ on the triple overlap, implicit in Connes 1973 (C4) but not previously isolated as a stack-consistency property. Strict super-additivity then follows from the max-divergence chain inequality (elementary from the $D_{\max}$ definition; max-divergence due to Datta [ref]) applied to the cocycle entropy production: the detoured worldline through an intermediate KMS state pays two max-divergence cocycle costs $\Delta_{\max}S^{1\to 2}+\Delta_{\max}S^{2\to 3}$ while the direct worldline pays only $\Delta_{\max}S^{1\to 3}$, with the Datta chain inequality forcing $\Delta_{\max}S^{1\to 3}\le\Delta_{\max}S^{1\to 2}+\Delta_{\max}S^{2\to 3}$, strictly on a generic set of triples (Theorem (ref); pairwise distinctness is necessary but not sufficient). The Umegaki relative entropy itself does not satisfy this chain inequality in general (see \S(ref)); the max-divergence is the chain-stable surrogate. \noindentPhysical reading: twin paradox as quantum information. The strict super-additivity of $\ellOS$ on off-orbit triples is the operator-algebraic dual of the special-relativistic twin paradox. Where classical Lorentzian geometry reads the deficit as detour-accumulates-less-proper-time, the OSLPLS reading reads it as detour-pays-more-entropy. The max-divergence chain inequality (Datta 2009 [ref]) supplies the strict-inequality content. To the author's knowledge this is the first explicit identification of an information-theoretic underpinning for the reverse triangle inequality of synthetic Lorentzian geometry. \noindentNumerical panel. The bit-exact Paper 45 [ref] joint propinquity panel $\Lprop(n_max,\Nt)\in\{2.0746,1.6101,1.3223\}$ at $(n_max,\Nt)\in\{(2,3),(3,5),(4,7)\}$ transports verbatim through the strong-form bridge. An illustrative Umegaki relative-entropy panel shows substantially positive deficits ($\sim 67$, $69$, $81$ nats);\ the load-bearing $D_{\max}$ chain is verified at $96/96$ cells (tests/test_wh7_b3_phase3_sprint2.py). Riemannian-limit recovery at $\Nt=1$ is bit-exact (Frobenius residual $=0.0$ in float64). Propagation number $\mathrm{prop}=2$ is verified at $(n_max,\Nt)=(2,3)$ (Paper 46 Appendix B envelope-aware refinement predicts $\mathrm{prop}\le 4$). \noindentHonest scope. The bridge is established at the strict-strong-form OSLPLS level on the full $M\ne 0$ enlarged substrate, at finite cutoff. The closed-form expression for the cocycle entropy production $\DelS{i}{j}(t)$ as a function of OSLPLS state-space coordinates is named open. The genuine non-commutative Mondino–Sämann pre-length space concept (Q2$^{\prime}$, Section (ref)) remains the named NCG-research frontier, beyond sprint scale; OSLPLS replaces Mondino–Sämann rather than generalising it on the synthetic side. Cross-manifold ($\Tcal_{S³} ⊗\Tcal_{\mathrm{Hardy}(S^{5})}$) and inner-factor calibration data are orthogonal to the present strong-form bridge. \noindentPlace in the math.OA standalone series.
