Structural Sparsity for Quantum Simulation: A Synthesis of the GeoVac Quantum-Computing Arc

Josh Loutey · GeoVac synthesis document · Synthesis / Reading Guides · DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.21286864 · PDF

Abstract

The Geometric Vacuum (GeoVac) framework discretizes electronic structure on natural geometries, producing sparse graph Hamiltonians whose angular content is fixed by Gaunt selection rules. This synthesis assembles the framework's quantum-computing arc—the four Group 4 papers—into a single resource-oriented narrative. The spine is: the qubit encoding and its Pauli/$1$-norm scaling [ref]; the benchmarked resource library across three periodic-table rows [ref]; the $S_N$ representation-theoretic foundation that classifies the atomic building blocks [ref]; and the nuclear shell-model extension that delineates exactly which part of the framework transfers [ref]. The unifying value proposition is structural, not accuracy-based: after the Jordan–Wigner transformation the GeoVac lattice Hamiltonian yields $O(Q^{3.15})$ Pauli terms for atoms and $O(Q^{2.5})$ for composed multi-center molecules (versus $O(Q^{3.9–4.3})$ for Gaussian baselines), with a sub-quadratic Pauli $1$-norm $\lambda = O(Q^{1.69})$ ($R^2=0.997$) that controls Trotter depth and qubitization query cost. Two disciplines run through the arc. First, we separate the robust scaling exponents—unchanged under the choice of electron-repulsion selection rule—from the absolute multipliers, which depend on a stated pair-diagonal angular approximation and are disclosed as such. Second, every comparison is made at matched qubit count, not matched accuracy: the composed basis carries classical equilibrium-geometry errors of $5$–$26%$, so the framework is positioned for fixed-geometry NISQ/VQE simulation (Pauli count, QWC measurement groups), not for fault-tolerant geometry optimisation. The nuclear extension is honest in the same way: its binding energies are encoding-validation benchmarks far from experiment, and the Fock projection rigidity theorem states precisely that the conformal $S^3$ structure does not transfer to the harmonic-oscillator basis—only the angular machinery and the qubit encoding do.
