The Universal/Coulomb Partition of the GeoVac Spectral Triple What belongs to angular momentum, what belongs to -Z/r, and why the difference matters

Josh Loutey · GeoVac Paper 31 · Mathematical Foundations · DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.21286757 · PDF

Abstract

The GeoVac framework [ref] is a discrete spectral triple in the sense of Connes [ref] and the graph-spectral-triple lineage of Marcolli and van Suijlekom [ref]: an algebra $\mathcal{A}$ of functions on the Fock-projected discrete graph, a Hilbert space $\mathcal{H}$ of scalar and spinor states, and a Dirac operator $D$ inherited from the Camporesi–Higuchi spectrum on $S^3$. This paper identifies a clean structural decomposition of the framework's results into two disjoint sectors: • A universal sector, depending only on $\mathcal{A}$ and the angular structure of the basis. The angular sparsity theorem of Paper 22 [ref] — the angular ERI density is potential-independent (global-$M_L$ Coulomb $D=6.06%$ at $l_{\max}=3$; pair-diagonal realized $D_{\mathrm{pd}}=1.44%$), bit-identical across Coulomb, harmonic oscillator, Woods–Saxon, square well, and Yukawa potentials — is the canonical example. • A potential-specific sector, depending on the choice of $D$. The Fock projection rigidity theorem of Paper 23 [ref] ($S^3$ projection unique to $-Z/r$ by $\mathrm{SO}(4)$ symmetry) and the Bargmann–Segal rigidity theorem of Paper 24 [ref] (the 3D isotropic harmonic oscillator is the unique central potential whose spectrum arises from the Euler operator on $H^2(S^5)$ restricted to symmetric $\mathrm{SU}(3)$ irreps) jointly establish the partition's sharpness. The two rigidity theorems sit on opposite sides of an operator-order divide: Coulomb requires a second-order Riemannian Laplacian and a nonlinear stereographic projection that introduces calibration $π$; the HO uses a first-order complex Euler operator and a linear-affine Bargmann projection that is bit-exactly $π$-free. This asymmetry is not an accident of two examples; it is a structural feature of sphere-quotient discretizations. The Sprint 5 result on $S^5$ gauge structure [ref] sharpens the Coulomb/HO asymmetry from two layers to three (spectrum-computing role of $L_0$, calibration $π$, and Wilson physical content), all three stratifying along the same operator-order divide. We frame the partition in spectral-triple language and identify which features of GeoVac transfer to non-Coulomb systems for free, which require system-specific rederivation, and what this implies for nuclear, oscillator, and gauge-theoretic extensions of the framework.
