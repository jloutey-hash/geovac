Cross-n Overlap Bridges Diagnostic
====================================
Date: 2026-03-20
Version: v0.9.37 (codebase locked, exploratory)

Goal: Test whether SO(4) cross-shell coupling (Li 2s <-> H 1s)
fixes LiH R_eq (2.5 vs 3.015 bohr experimental).

Investigation Summary
---------------------
1. SO(4) D-matrix is block-diagonal in n by representation theory.
   Different n-shells are different irreps -> zero coupling by theorem.
   This is NOT an implementation limitation.

2. SO(4,2) conformal group could couple different n, but no closed-form
   "cross-n D-matrix" exists. The SW module explicitly notes this:
   "Cross-n coupling would require the full SO(4,2) conformal group."

3. Paper 8 Sec. "Geometric Inconsistency" explains: in the hydrogenic
   basis, cross-n matrix elements involve convolution over mismatched
   S3 spheres (bra on S3 with radius Z/n', ket on S3 with radius Z/n).
   No closed-form SO(4) expression exists.

Implementation: SW-Consistent Cross-n Bridges
----------------------------------------------
Formula: H_cross-n[i_A, j_B] = -K * (Z_eff/p0) * sin(gamma) * S(i_A, j_B)

Same scale as D-matrix (-(Z_eff/p0)*sin(gamma)), but replaces D-matrix
element (zero for cross-n) with STO overlap integral S.

Parameters added to MolecularLatticeIndex:
  use_cross_n_bridges: bool = False
  cross_n_K: float = 1.0  (scale relative to same-n D-matrix coupling)

Coupling Elements at R=3.015 bohr (K=1.0)
------------------------------------------
  Li 1s <-> H 1s: -0.131226 Ha  (same-n=1, D-matrix)
  Li 2s <-> H 1s:  0.052178 Ha  (cross-n, SIGMA BOND)
  Li 3s <-> H 1s: -0.035455 Ha  (cross-n)
  Li 2s <-> H 2s: -0.128371 Ha  (same-n=2, D-matrix)

-----------------

    R=2.0: -0.615  R=3.0: -0.401  R=5.0: -0.112  R=8.0: -0.010
  S(Li_3s, H_1s):  (Z/n=1 for both -> same exponent -> slow decay)
    R=3.0: +0.269  R=6.0: +0.214  R=10.0: +0.030

Conclusion
----------
Cross-n bridges create proper sigma hybridization (Li 2s <-> H 1s
coupling is nonzero at 0.052 Ha, ~40% of same-n D-matrix coupling).
However, they CANNOT fix R_eq because:

1. Base PES is monotonically decreasing (no minimum) in hybrid path.
   The Mulliken cross-nuclear diagonal overbinds at all R.

2. Cross-n bridges add MORE binding at short R (where overlap is
   largest), making the overbinding worse.

3. In 4-electron LiH, both bonding and antibonding sigma orbitals
   are occupied. Cross-n coupling gives net stabilization from
   correlation mixing, not directed sigma bond formation.

Root cause of wrong R_eq: Mulliken cross-nuclear diagonal is too
strong at short R, not missing cross-shell off-diagonal coupling.

The bottleneck is the DIAGONAL (potential energy landscape), not
the OFF-DIAGONAL (hybridization/hopping).
