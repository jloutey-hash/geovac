# GeoVac Chemistry Solver — Signed Two-Column Error Atlas

**Date:** 2026-07-05
**Purpose:** Diagnostic-sprint deliverable. Separate ENERGY error (electronic/total energy at a FIXED geometry — converges with basis) from GEOMETRY error (R_eq, the shift of the PES minimum — drifts with basis). These are different objects and are kept in different columns by design.

**Provenance rule:** Numbers pulled from the OWNING PAPERS (`papers/group2_quantum_chemistry/*.tex` + `papers/group4/paper_20`), cross-checked against `docs/validation_benchmarks.md` and `docs/accuracy_target_analysis.md`. Where a paper and a summary table (CLAUDE.md / accuracy_target) disagree, the paper wins and the conflict is flagged in the "Discrepancies" section below.

**Sign convention:**
- ENERGY error % = (E_computed − E_reference)/|E_reference| × 100. **Negative = over-binding** (energy too low / too much bound). **Positive = under-binding** (energy too high). For well-depth (D_e) rows the sign is stated as "D_e deficit" (well too shallow) since these papers report D_e recovery, not total-E.
- R_eq error % = (R_computed − R_experiment)/R_experiment × 100. **Positive = bond too long** (minimum pushed outward). "n/a" for atoms; "n/r" = not reported by the owning paper.

## 5-line PATTERN summary

1. **Energy and R_eq diverge most for the balanced-coupled LiH (row 13) and for HeH⁺ (row 8).** LiH balanced is the cleanest single-system demonstration: from n_max=2→3 the energy error *shrinks* 1.8%→0.20% (9× improvement) while R_eq error *grows* 7.0%→8.8% — the two columns move in opposite directions on the same system, same solver. HeH⁺ is the extreme case: 2.4% absolute energy but only ~5% of D_e recovered.
2. **Energy error tracks basis/correlation completeness and converges** (H₂⁺ 0.0002%, He 0.022%, LiH balanced 0.20% at n_max=3), whereas **R_eq error is a structural PES-shape defect that does NOT converge with basis** in the composed/balanced architectures (LiH balanced drift +0.053 bohr/n_max; composed drift +0.15 bohr/l_max).
3. **R_eq error tracks well-depth/floppiness as the shallow-well-amplification hypothesis predicts.** Ordering by increasing R_eq error — H₂⁺ (0.25%, deep 1-electron well) → LiH composed (5.3%) → LiH balanced (7–8.8%) → BeH₂ (11.7%) → H₂O (19.4%) → LiH-4N (63.5%, *unbound* — zero well depth) — matches decreasing well depth / increasing electron-count and charge-asymmetry complexity. HeH⁺'s tiny 0.075 Ha binding produces the worst relative D_e recovery (~5%). The floppier / shallower the well, the more a fixed absolute PES-shape error translates into a large *relative* geometry error.
4. **The mechanism is one-directional PES tilt, not noise:** composed R_eq overshoots *outward* (PK barrier over-repulsion, +sign) and balanced R_eq drifts *outward* with n_max (block-decomposition artifact, +sign) — both structural, both distinct from the electronic-energy accuracy that converges cleanly at fixed geometry.
5. **Atoms/one-electron systems (rows 1–6) have no R_eq axis**, so their energy errors (0.004%–21%) are pure electronic-structure accuracy — He/H₂⁺ excellent; H⁻ over-binds 21% and PsH under-binds 4.1% purely from the graph-validity boundary (Z_c≈1.84) and exotic charge function, with no geometry confound.

## Flags: missing same-convention references, and paper-vs-summary disagreements

- **[DISCREPANCY — H₂O composed] Paper wins: 19.4%, not 26%.** `docs/accuracy_target_analysis.md` and CLAUDE.md §5 best-results table list H₂O R_eq = **26%**. Paper 19 (owning paper) states **19.4%** vs 1.809 bohr and explicitly says this is "improved from the earlier 26% / 1.34 bohr recorded" (paper_19 l.1451–1453). The 26% is a **superseded** value still living in the summary docs. → Atlas uses 19.4%. *Recommend updating docs/accuracy_target_analysis.md and CLAUDE.md §5.*
- **[INTERNAL MULTIPLICITY — LiH composed] 5.3% vs 6.4% vs 1.5%, all in Paper 17.** The canonical headline (§2/§5, Paper 19 abstract) is **5.3%** (l-dependent PK, continuous Level-4 adiabatic solver, l_max=2). Paper 17's own detailed spectroscopic table (Table "lih") reports **6.4%** for ab initio PK (R_eq=3.21) and **−1.5%** for fitted PK (R_eq=2.97). `validation_benchmarks.md` lists the **6.4%** (ab initio PK) variant. These are different PK sub-conventions, not a contradiction, but a reader comparing the "5.3%" headline to the paper's own table will see 6.4% — worth a one-line reconciliation in Paper 17. Rows 9 / 9a / 9b make the three explicit.
- **[MISSING PRECISE REFERENCE — HeH⁺ D_e]** Paper 15 characterizes the HeH⁺ binding only as "the small 0.075 Ha binding" (abstract) and does not print a precisely cited D_e^exact for HeH⁺ in the same place it cites Kołos for H₂ (0.17447 Ha). The ~5% D_e-recovery figure is therefore relative to a loosely-stated 0.075 Ha. Absolute energy error (2.4%) is the better-grounded HeH⁺ number.
- **[SUPPLIED REFERENCE — H⁻]** Paper 13 states "over-binds by 21%" without printing E_CI or the exact energy in that passage. The atlas supplies the standard exact H⁻ energy (−0.5278 Ha) to give the sign/magnitude context; the 21% itself is the paper's number.
- **[CONVENTION SPLIT — composed vs balanced LiH, by design]** Composed LiH (rows 9–9b) reports **core-subtracted** energetics: E_total(R) = E_core + V_cross(R) with E_core(Li⁺ 1s²) = −7.280 Ha (Paper 17 l.447, Level 3), R-independent. Balanced LiH (row 13) reports the **full 4-electron BO total energy** −8.071 Ha with **no core subtraction** (full FCI). Energy-error percentages between these two rows are therefore NOT directly comparable — composed's R_eq comes from V_cross(R) shape only, balanced's from the full 4e PES. This is the single most important convention hazard in the table.
- **[METRIC TYPE — H₂ / HeH⁺]** Paper 15 reports **D_e recovery (well depth) at fixed R=1.4 bohr**, not a total-electronic-energy % and not an optimized R_eq. The "energy error" cells for rows 7–8 are D_e-deficit figures; R_eq is n/r because the geometry is held at the experimental value.
- **[Paper 20 / group4]** Paper 20 (`paper_20_resource_benchmarks.tex`) is the QC resource-census paper; it carries the balanced-coupled **resource** numbers (Pauli/1-norm) referenced in row 13–15 but not independent energy/geometry benchmarks beyond what Paper 19 reports. No conflict found.
