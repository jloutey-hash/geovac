## What the master theorem provides

The master theorem (Paper 39 ┬¦6 (v), today) gives:
$$\Lambda^{(k)}(\Tcal^{(k)}_{n_1, \ldots, n_k}, \Tcal^{(k)}_{G_1 \times \cdots \times G_k}) \le \sup_j C_3^{G_j}(n_j) \cdot \max_j \gamma^{G_j}_{n_j} \to 0$$
for **arbitrary compact connected Lie group** tensor-product spectral triples, with $k$-independent $C_3$ at universal $4/\pi$ rate.

**Crucially: this is a SUBSTRATE convergence theorem.** It says the multi-particle spectral-triple architecture is well-defined and the finite-cutoff truncations converge in propinquity to the continuum. It does NOT say which inner fluctuations / cross-register couplings are autonomously generated by the framework.
