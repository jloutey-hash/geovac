3. ROOT CAUSE ANALYSIS
======================
The monotonically attractive PES at l_max=1 has the SAME root cause as l_max=0:
the adiabatic approximation in hyperspherical coordinates produces a monotonically
decreasing U_min(R) in the effective potential. As R increases, the nuclei separate,
and the angular eigenvalue mu(R_e) becomes more negative at large R_e, pulling
U_min downward without bound.

The equilibrium in real LiH arises from the interplay of:
(1) Nuclear repulsion V_NN = Z_A*Z_B/R (repulsive, short-range dominance)
(2) Electronic energy E_elec(R) (attractive, decreasing as nuclei provide
    more binding at short R)

In the full 4-electron solver, E_elec(R) decreases monotonically as R decreases
(nuclei provide stronger attraction to all 4 electrons at shorter distances),
and this decrease is faster than V_NN increases. The result is net monotonic
attraction.

This differs from the composed geometry where:
- The PK pseudopotential creates a repulsive core exclusion effect
- The Level 4 valence solver naturally has an equilibrium (from e-e repulsion
  at short R_e in the restricted 2-electron space)

4. KEY PHYSICS FINDING: S_4 [2,2] vs PARITY
=============================================
At l_max=1, the S_4 [2,2] projector in channel space has rank 2. The two
surviving channels are superpositions of the 6 channels with exactly two l=0
and two l=1 electrons: (0011), (0101), (0110), (1001), (1010), (1100).

The (0,0,0,0) channel (all s-wave) is EXCLUDED because it has [4] (fully
symmetric) spatial character, which pairs with the S=2 quintet, not the
singlet. This is physically correct: the singlet ground state of LiH
must have [2,2] spatial symmetry.

However, the [2,2] projected space at l_max=1 misses the dominant contribution
from the core electrons. In reality, the core electrons ARE in s-waves, but
their antisymmetry with valence electrons requires BOTH s-wave and p-wave
channels simultaneously in the coupled angular expansion. The [2,2] projection
correctly captures this coupling, but only 2 effective channels are far too
few for accurate energies.

Parity antisymmetry (pair exchange via half-grids) includes all 16 channels
and gives lower energies, but enforces only partial antisymmetry (within each
electron pair, not between pairs). The full [2,2] symmetry requires cross-pair
antisymmetry that mixes alpha coordinates with channel labels — this is not
captured by either approach at l_max=1.

8. ASSESSMENT
==============
The l_max=1 result confirms the Track AJ/AG finding: the full 4-electron
mol-frame hyperspherical solver for LiH does not produce an equilibrium at
low l_max. The PES is monotonically attractive at both l_max=0 and l_max=1.

This is a STRUCTURAL result, not a numerical convergence issue:
- The adiabatic approximation produces a monotonically decreasing U_min(R)
- Nuclear repulsion V_NN = Z_A*Z_B/R is insufficient to overcome the
  monotonic electronic energy decrease
- The equilibrium in real LiH requires either:
  (a) Much higher l_max (l_max >= 3-4?) to describe core-valence correlation
  (b) The composed geometry approach with PK pseudopotential

This strengthens the case that the composed geometry (Level 5) is not just
a computational convenience but encodes essential core-valence separation
physics that the full solver needs many more channels to reproduce.

The 144x compression ratio of composed vs full (from Track AG) is confirmed
as necessary, not just convenient. At l_max=2, the full solver would have
81 channels, ~14000 angular dimension per R_e point — potentially tractable
but still unlikely to produce equilibrium without adequate l_max for both
core and valence description simultaneously.
