## 3. Ab Initio Spectroscopy -- STALE PIPELINE RESULTS

### Two different PES methods

| Method | Used by | D_e | omega_e | nu_01 |
|--------|---------|:---:|:-------:|:-----:|
| Hylleraas + Neumann V_ee | Paper 13 | 0.161 Ha (-7.5%) | 4435 cm-1 (+0.8%) | 4157 cm-1 (-0.1%) |
| Prolate CI (2x2) | run_pipeline.py | 0.165 Ha (-5.6%) | 4918 cm-1 (+11.8%) | 4584 cm-1 (+10.5%) |

Paper 13's figure caption explicitly says "Hylleraas basis with Neumann V_ee" (line 812). The pipeline (`benchmarks/ab_initio_nuclear/run_pipeline.py`) uses prolate spheroidal CI with a wider R range (0.8-6.0 bohr), including dissociation tail points where E(6.0) = -0.926 Ha (should be -1.0 Ha). These bad long-range points inflate omega_e by ~11%.

### Stale results.md

`benchmarks/ab_initio_nuclear/results.md` contains the pipeline numbers (omega_e = 4918), NOT the Hylleraas numbers used in Paper 13. This file is auto-generated by `run_pipeline.py` and does not reflect the paper's claims.

**Action needed:**
1. Either update `run_pipeline.py` to use Hylleraas PES data, or
2. Add a note to `results.md` that Paper 13 uses a different (Hylleraas) PES
3. The README's omega_e = 4435 is correct (it matches Paper 13's Hylleraas PES)
