## Background

Cyclotomic mixed-Tate period theory (Deligne–Goncharov 2005, Brown 2012,
Deligne 2010, Glanois 2015).  For a positive integer $N$, the category
$\mathcal{MT}(\mathcal{O}_N[1/N])$ of mixed Tate motives over the ring
of $S$-integers of the $N$-th cyclotomic field $k_N = \mathbb{Q}(\mu_N)$,
inverted at primes above $N$, is a Tannakian category.  Its motivic
Hopf algebra $\mathcal{H}^N$ embeds non-canonically into the universal
Hopf algebra of $\mathcal{MT}_N$ with $(2\pi i)^m \mapsto t$.

**Theorem (Deligne 2010, arXiv:math/0302267):** For $N \in \{2, 3, 4, 8\}$,
the period map $\mathrm{per}: \mathcal{H}^N \to \mathcal{Z}^N$ is an
isomorphism, where $\mathcal{Z}^N$ is the $\mathbb{Q}$-vector space
spanned by multiple zeta values at $N$-th roots of unity.  An explicit
basis is given.

**Theorem (Glanois 2015, arXiv:1411.4947, Cor.~1.1–1.2):** For
$N \in \{2, 3, 4, 8\}$, the basis
$$\mathcal{B}^N = \left\{ \zeta^{\mathfrak m}\!
   \begin{pmatrix} x_1,\ldots,x_p \\ \epsilon_1,\ldots,\epsilon_p \end{pmatrix}
   (2\pi i)^{s, \mathfrak m},\;
   x_i \in \mathbb{N}^*,\; s \ge 0 \right\}$$
spans $\mathcal{H}^N$, with $\epsilon_p = \sqrt{-1}$ for $N = 4$ and
$\epsilon_i \in \{\pm 1\}$ permitted at other entries.  Level filtration
at $N = 4$ is controlled by the number of even $x_i$ entries.  Each
multiple zeta value relative to $N$-th roots of unity is a
$\mathbb{Q}$-linear combination of $\mathcal{B}^N$-elements of the same
weight.

For $N = 4$ specifically, the relevant period ring is
$\mathcal{MT}(\mathbb{Z}[i, 1/2])$, generated by motivic iterated
integrals over paths on $\mathbb{P}^1 \setminus \{0, 1, i, -1, -i, \infty\}$.
At depth $1$, the generators are $\log 2$, $\pi i$, and polylogarithms
$\mathrm{Li}_n(\pm i)$ for $n \ge 2$; the Dirichlet $\beta$ values
$\beta(s) = L(s, \chi_{-4})$ are periods in this ring via
$$\beta(s) = \tfrac{1}{4^s}\bigl[\zeta(s, 1/4) - \zeta(s, 3/4)\bigr]$$
where Hurwitz $\zeta$ at quarter-integer shifts is a level-4 motivic
period.  Catalan $G = \beta(2)$ and $\beta(4)$ are therefore mixed-Tate
periods at level $4$, even though they are NOT classical MZVs over
$\mathbb{Z}$ (the level-$1$ ring) and are conjecturally not in
$\mathcal{MT}(\mathbb{Z}[1/2])$ either.

## GeoVac M3 enumeration

The M3 sub-mechanism of the master Mellin engine is the first-order
Dirac sector $\mathcal{M}[\mathrm{Tr}(D \cdot e^{-tD^2})]$ at $k=1$, on
the discrete Camporesi–Higuchi spectrum $|\lambda_n^{CH}| = n+3/2$ with
degeneracy $g_n = 2(n+1)(n+2)$.

### Un-restricted Dirac Dirichlet series $D(s)$ (Paper 28 Thm 2)

$$D(s) = \sum_n g_n / |\lambda_n|^s
     = 2\,\zeta(s-2, 3/2) - \tfrac{1}{2}\,\zeta(s, 3/2).$$

At integer $s \ge 2$ via the Hurwitz shift identity
$\zeta(s, 3/2) = \zeta(s, 1/2) - 2^s$:

| $s$ | $D(s)$ | Class |
|:---:|:--------|:-------|
| 4 | $\pi^2 - \pi^4/12$ | $\pi^{\mathrm{even}}\cdot\mathbb{Q}$ |
| 5 | $14\,\zeta(3) - \tfrac{31}{2}\,\zeta(5)$ | odd-zeta |
| 6 | $\pi^4/3 - \pi^6/30$ | $\pi^{\mathrm{even}}\cdot\mathbb{Q}$ |
| 7 | $62\,\zeta(5) - \tfrac{127}{2}\,\zeta(7)$ | odd-zeta |
| 8 | $2\pi^6/15 - 17\pi^8/1260$ | $\pi^{\mathrm{even}}\cdot\mathbb{Q}$ |
| 9 | $254\,\zeta(7) - \tfrac{511}{2}\,\zeta(9)$ | odd-zeta |
| 10 | $17\pi^8/315 - 31\pi^{10}/4725$ | $\pi^{\mathrm{even}}\cdot\mathbb{Q}$ |

All in $\mathcal{MT}(\mathbb{Z}[1/2])$ at level $2$, depth $1$.  At
integer $s$ the $1/2$-denominator structure is absorbed into rational
coefficients; the values further reduce to $\mathcal{MT}(\mathbb{Z})$
at depth $1$ (classical Brown MZV over $\mathbb{Z}$).

### Vertex-restricted $\chi_{-4}$ identity (Paper 28 Thm 3)

For integer $s \ge 2$:
$$D_{\mathrm{even}}(s) - D_{\mathrm{odd}}(s)
  = 2^{s-1}\bigl(\beta(s) - \beta(s-2)\bigr).$$

Underlying mechanism (Paper 28 Thm 3 proof): $D_{\mathrm{even}}$,
$D_{\mathrm{odd}}$ individually decompose into Hurwitz at quarter-integer
shifts $3/4$ and $5/4$; the $\zeta(s, 1/4)$ pieces cancel in the
difference via the Dirichlet $\beta$ definition
$\zeta(s, 3/4) - \zeta(s, 1/4) = -4^s \beta(s)$.

Explicit values at $s = 4$:
\begin{align*}
D_{\mathrm{even}}(4) &= \tfrac{\pi^2}{2} - \tfrac{\pi^4}{24} - 4G + 4\beta(4), \\
D_{\mathrm{odd}}(4)  &= \tfrac{\pi^2}{2} - \tfrac{\pi^4}{24} + 4G - 4\beta(4),
\end{align*}
where $G = \beta(2) \approx 0.9159656$.  $D_{\mathrm{even}}$ and
$D_{\mathrm{odd}}$ individually are level-$4$ periods; their sum
$D(s)$ (the un-restricted Dirac Dirichlet) lives in the smaller level-$2$
ring; their difference is purely Dirichlet-$L$ content.

Each of $D_{\mathrm{even}}(s)$, $D_{\mathrm{odd}}(s)$, and their
difference lies in $\mathcal{MT}(\mathbb{Z}[i, 1/2])$ at level $4$,
depth $1$.

### Sommerfeld K-sums $D_p$ (Paper 28 §K-Sommerfeld)

The Sommerfeld $D_p$ are vertex-restricted Dirichlet sums weighted by
harmonic numbers $H_{n-1}^{(r)}$.  Closed form at $p = 4$:
$$D_4 = -\tfrac{41\pi^6}{12096} + \tfrac{71}{8}\,\zeta(7)
        - \tfrac{\pi^4}{20}\,\zeta(3).$$
Three-tier decomposition: $\pi^6$ (M3-$\pi$-even), $\zeta(7)$ (M3-odd-$\zeta$),
mixed product $\pi^4 \cdot \zeta(3)$ (depth-$2$ MZV).  Generalization:
at Sommerfeld order $p \ge 2$, $D_p$ is a sum of weight-$(2p-1)$ MZV
elements with exactly $\max(0, \lfloor (2p-5)/2 \rfloor)$ surviving
products $\zeta(a)\zeta(b)$, $a + b = 2p-1$ (Paper 28 Obs.~product_rule).

Each $D_p$ lies in $\mathcal{MT}(\mathbb{Z})$ at depth $\le 2$, weight
$2p - 1$.  No level-$4$ Dirichlet-$L$ content (verified PSLQ at 80 dps
against $\{G, \beta(3), \beta(4)\}$ in Paper 28).

### Two-loop irreducible $S_{\min}$ (Paper 28 §smin)

$$S_{\min} = \sum_{k=1}^\infty T(k)^2,\quad
  T(k) = 2\,\zeta(2, k+\tfrac{3}{2}) - \tfrac{1}{2}\,\zeta(4, k+\tfrac{3}{2}).$$

200-digit value:\ $S_{\min} = 2.47993\,69380\,34222\,55441\ldots$

PSLQ at 200 dps against the 100-element basis (Paper 28 §smin):
$\{\pi^{2k}\}_{k\le 5}$, $\{\zeta(2k+1)\}_{k\le 4}$, $G$, $\beta(3)$,
$\beta(4)$, $G^2$, $G\pi^2$, $\beta(4)\pi^2$, $G\beta(4)$,
$\zeta(3)^2$, $\zeta(3)\zeta(5)$, $\zeta(a)\zeta(b)$ cross-products,
Euler sums $\{s_{2,3}, s_{3,2}\}$, Tornheim–Witten $\{T(2,2,2), T(2,2,4)\}$,
double Hurwitz at quarter-integer shifts, Hurwitz at $3/2$ for
$s = 2, \ldots, 6$, $D_{\mathrm{even}}(4)$, $D_{\mathrm{odd}}(4)$,
polygamma $\{\psi^{(1)}(3/2), \psi^{(3)}(3/2)\}$, $\mathrm{Li}_4(1/2)$,
$\mathrm{Cl}_2(\pi/3)$, Euler–Mascheroni $\gamma$.

NULL identification.  $S_{\min}$ is consistent with a new element of
$\mathcal{MT}(\mathbb{Z}[1/2])$ at depth $2$ not in the tested basis.

T(k) is built from Hurwitz at half-integer shifts (level $2$);
$T(k)^2$ and the sum over $k$ produce depth-$2$ structure.  Natural
prediction:\ $S_{\min} \in \mathcal{MT}(\mathbb{Z}[1/2])$ at level $2$,
depth $2$.

### Depth-$k$ loop tower (Paper 28 Prop.~depth_k)

At $k$-loop iterated sunset on $S^3$ with vertex restriction and CG
weights:
- $k = 1$: $\pi^{\mathrm{even}}$ only (T9 theorem, depth $1$ in level-$2$ MT).
- $k = 2$: $S_{\min}$ irreducible (depth $2$ in level-$\le 2$ MT, this sprint).
- $k = 3$: $S^{(3)}$ independent of $S_{\min}$ (depth $3$, predicted).
- $k$ general: new irreducible at depth $k$.

This matches the Glanois 2015 depth-filtration structure: the level-$N$
motivic Hopf algebra $\mathcal{H}^N$ has $\mathrm{gr}_p^D \mathcal{H}^N$
graded at each depth $p$, with new periods entering at each $p$.  The
GeoVac depth-$k$-tower IS the loop-order = motivic-depth-$k$
identification on the discrete CH spectral triple.

**Honest scope.**

   period ring (PROVEN: the Hurwitz / Dirichlet-$L$ / MZV building

   2012 for $N = 1$; Deligne 2010 + Glanois 2015 for $N \in \{2, 3, 4, 8\}$).
   The classification therefore inherits this proven motivic-Galois
