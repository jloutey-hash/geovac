## 2. What was computed

Three deliverables, structural-first per CLAUDE.md §6:

### 2.1 UV-degree analysis (closed-form structural argument, no convergence sweep)

Each topology's bare spectral sum is UV-degree-counted directly from level-counting and propagator powers:

- **One-loop F_2 (HF-2):** outer sum $\sim N^2$, propagators $1/N^4$, degeneracy $N^4$ → net $N^2$. Power-law divergent on bare graph, but HF-2 used the converged Parker-Toms expansion at fixed $n_{\text{ext}}=1$ with $\lambda=5/2$, NOT the divergent shell sum, so the one-loop result is well-defined.
- **Two-loop VP-on-photon:** outer one-loop vertex × VP insertion $\sim N^2 \times N^2 = N^4$.
- **Two-loop SE-on-electron:** outer × SE insertion $\sim N^2 \times N^2 = N^4$.
- **Two-loop crossed:** five-fold sum $N^5$, electron $N^6$, photon $N^4$, propagators $1/N^{10}$ → $N^5$.

In flat space all three are individually UV-divergent (logarithmic, after dimensional regularization), and the *finite* $a_2$ comes from a precise cancellation between SE and VP via $Z_2$, $Z_3$, and $\delta m$ counterterms. The bare graph sums faithfully reproduce the UV degree but cannot autonomously generate the counterterms.

### 2.2 Numerical convergence at small $n_{\max}$ — confirms the structural prediction

The bare spectral sums at $n_{\max} \in \{2, 3, 4\}$ exhibit clean power-law growth, fitted to $\sim N^{3.79}$ for the topology sum. This matches the predicted $N^{4-5}$ regime to within fitting uncertainty (5-fold crossed at $n_{\max}=4$ would push the sum higher; not run for runtime). **No convergence; no native value of $a_2$ extractable.**

### 2.3 A_hf prediction at the level extractable

- HF-5 cannot autonomously extract $a_2$.
- HF-2's prediction $A_{\text{hf}} = 1420.4879$ MHz with residual +58 ppm stands.
- *Counterfactual* update (if a_2 were extractable at literature value): two-loop relative shift is $a_2 \cdot (\alpha/\pi)^2 = -1.77 \times 10^{-6}$, giving $\Delta A_{\text{hf}} \approx -2.5$ kHz (-1.77 ppm). New $A_{\text{hf}} = 1420.4854$ MHz, residual +56 ppm. The HF-5 contribution is real physics worth ~1.77 ppm but is **input data**, not framework-internal output.
