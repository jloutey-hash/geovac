## §1. Problem statement

**G2 (verbatim, Paper 45 §1.4):**
> De-compactification limit $T \to \infty$. Our construction is at compact temporal radius $T$ canonical $T = 2\pi$ (the BW modular period). The non-compact $\mathbb{R}_t$ limit re-introduces the joint compact $\times$ non-compact issue and is a separate program (Sprint L3c in our internal taxonomy).

**The target:** extend the K⁺-weak-form propinquity convergence theorem of Paper 45 (and the strong-form of Paper 46 + Appendix B) from
$$\mathcal{T}^L_{n_{\max}, N_t, T} \to \mathcal{T}^L_{S^3 \times S^1_T} \quad \text{(compact temporal carrier, } T = 2\pi\text{)}$$
to
$$\mathcal{T}^L_{n_{\max}, N_t, T_n} \to \mathcal{T}^L_{S^3 \times \mathbb{R}_t} \quad \text{(non-compact temporal carrier, } T_n \to \infty\text{)}$$
with a controllable joint rate $\gamma^{\mathrm{joint}}(n_{\max}, N_t, T_n) \to 0$.

**Why it matters physically.** The canonical Wightman vacuum on Minkowski spacetime has $\mathbb{R}_t$ as the time direction with NO compactification. The BW modular period $T = 2\pi$ identifies the wedge KMS state's modular flow as a Lorentz boost orbit with period $2\pi$, but the wedge algebra itself is generated by observables on the FULL Minkowski wedge — not on a compactified $S^1_{2\pi} \times W_{\mathrm{spatial}}$. The Paper 45/46 construction at fixed $T = 2\pi$ realizes the *modular-period* sector cleanly but does not reach the full Minkowski-time setting.

**Why it is open.** Latrémolière propinquity in its published forms (2017/2018/2023/2025) requires a **compact** carrier. The Krein-positive substrate (Paper 44) and the operator-system / Berezin / Lichnerowicz machinery (Papers 45/46) all use the SU(2) × U(1)_T tensor structure where U(1)_T is the compact circle of circumference $T$. Replacing U(1)_T with the non-compact additive group $\mathbb{R}$ breaks the compact-group Plancherel theorem and breaks the Bożejko-Fendler central-multiplier equality (which is published only for amenable compact groups in the relevant form).

## §2. Current state after Paper 45/46

### What is closed

1. **Paper 45 K⁺-weak-form** at fixed $T = 2\pi$: $\Lprop(\Tcal^L_{n_{\max}, N_t, T}, \Tcal^L_{S^3 \times S^1_{2\pi}}) \le C_3^{\mathrm{joint}} \cdot \gamma^{\mathrm{joint}}_{n_{\max}, N_t, T}$ with $\gamma^{\mathrm{joint}} = O(\log n_{\max}/n_{\max} + T/N_t) \to 0$ at fixed $T = 2\pi$.

2. **Paper 46 strong-form** on natural chirality-doubled scalar-multiplier substrate at fixed $T = 2\pi$: same bound bit-exact, "free upgrade" from K⁺ to full Krein.

3. **Paper 46 Appendix B** strict-strong-form on enlarged substrate (chirality-flipping generators with $\{J, M\} = 0$) at fixed $T = 2\pi$: gradient-norm absorption mechanism, $\Lambda^{\mathrm{enlarged}} = \Lambda^{\mathrm{P46}}$ bit-exact.

### What β-L3 found (and why it matters for L3c)

The β-L3 memo (Sprint L3b-2f-β, 2026-05-22) established the closed-form bound
$$L_{\mathrm{op}}(a) \;\le\; \|[D_{\mathrm{GV}}, a]\|_{\mathrm{op}} \;+\; 2 \|D_t\|_{\mathrm{op}} \cdot \|a^{\mathrm{flip}}\|_{\mathrm{op}}$$
with $\|D_t\|_{\mathrm{op}} = (N_t - 1)/2$ on the canonical compactified temporal grid at $T = 2\pi$.

**Two regime statements** (β-L3 §1.3, §5.4, §7.2):

| Regime | $T$ behavior | Rate survival | Notes |
|:-------|:-------------|:--------------|:------|
| Fixed-$T$ asymptotic | $T = 2\pi$ canonical | **Yes** | $\gamma^{\mathrm{joint}} \to 0$ at fixed $T$; this is Paper 45/46. |
| Joint $(n_{\max}, N_t, T) \to \infty$ | $T \to \infty$ | **No** under current gradient form | $O(T)$ contribution from chirality-flip time-piece term diverges as $T \to \infty$ at any finite $N_t$. |

The β-L3 §1.3 explicit statement: "Rate survives in the fixed-$T$ regime; rate **does NOT survive** under joint $(n_{\max}, N_t) \to \infty$ when $T$ is also taken to infinity."

**This is the load-bearing structural finding for L3c.** The current gradient-norm absorption mechanism (Paper 46 Appendix B §B.2) is *specifically tuned* for fixed $T$. De-compactification reopens the $O(T)$ scaling in the gradient and requires a different mechanism.

### What L2-B/E already realized (partial G2 progress at a different level)

Sprint L2-B (2026-05-16) and Sprint L2-E (2026-05-16/17) built the Lorentzian Dirac and the Krein-level modular Hamiltonian on a **bounded but NOT compactified** temporal grid $t_k \in [-T_{\max}, T_{\max}]$ (uniform finite-difference + Dirichlet zero BC, NOT periodic). The choice was structural: compactifying $\mathbb{R}_t \to S^1_\beta$ creates closed timelike curves (Geroch's theorem), so the L2-level Lorentzian Dirac construction explicitly avoids periodic temporal boundary conditions.

The L3b foundation (compact-temporal substrate, 2026-05-17) made the **opposite** choice: $\mathbb{R}_t \to S^1_T$ with periodic boundary conditions, because Latrémolière's propinquity machinery requires a compact carrier.

**Net:** The framework already has TWO Lorentzian-extension architectures:

- **L2 / Paper 43**: bounded finite-interval temporal carrier $[-T_{\max}, T_{\max}]$, four-witness Wick-rotation theorem closes at the **operator-system level** (no propinquity).
- **L3b / Paper 45-46**: compact periodic temporal carrier $S^1_T$, propinquity convergence theorem closes at the **metric level**.

**G2 is the question of whether these two architectures can be merged.** The L2 architecture has the right physical carrier (non-compact, no CTCs); the L3b architecture has the right metric-convergence machinery. G2 asks for both.

## §3. Per-lemma obstruction audit

The five-lemma decomposition L1' / L2 / L3 / L4 / L5 from Paper 38 transports to the compact-temporal Lorentzian setting (Paper 45). For L3c we need to identify which lemmas fail under $S^1_T \to \mathbb{R}_t$.

| Lemma | Compact $S^1_T$ ingredient | Non-compact $\mathbb{R}_t$ status |
|:-----:|:---------------------------|:----------------------------------|
| **L1'** | Operator-system substrate with $D_t$ Fourier-diagonal at integer momenta $\omega_k = 2\pi k/T$ | **TRANSFERS FREELY.** Replace Fourier sum with Fourier integral; $D_t = i \partial_t$ has continuous spectrum $\mathbb{R}$ on $L^2(\mathbb{R})$. Operator system is multiplier algebra of polynomial-bounded functions of $t$. |
| **L2** | Joint Fejér kernel $K^{\SU(2)} \otimes K^{U(1)_T}$; Bożejko-Fendler central-multiplier equality on amenable compact group $\SU(2) \times U(1)_T$; cb-norm $\le 2/(n_{\max}+1)$ **$N_t$-independent** | **NEEDS NEW MATH.** Bożejko-Fendler is stated for compact groups; non-compact analogs (e.g., for $\mathbb{R}$) need a Fejér-type kernel on $\mathbb{R}$ with controllable cb-norm and mass-concentration rate. **Closest analog: Fejér-on-the-line $K_T(\omega) = T (\sin(T\omega/2)/(T\omega/2))^2 / (2\pi)$**, with $T \to \infty$ as the cutoff. The cb-norm on the abelian group $\mathbb{R}$ is well-defined (Bożejko–Fendler holds for amenable LCA groups, and $\mathbb{R}$ is amenable), but the **joint** SU(2) × $\mathbb{R}$ kernel with controllable rate is unpublished. **This is the dominant cost.** |
| **L3** | Joint Lichnerowicz $[D_L, a_s \otimes a_t] = i[D_{\GV}, a_s] \otimes a_t$ — time-chirality cross-term $\{\gamma^0, D_{\GV}\} = 0$ vanishes; $C_3^{\mathrm{joint}} = C_3^{\SU(2)}$ verbatim | **TRANSFERS FREELY on natural substrate.** The structural identity $\{\gamma^0, D_{\GV}\} = 0$ is signature- and carrier-blind. The β-L3 enlarged-substrate $O(T)$ term is the only new ingredient; **at $T \to \infty$ this term diverges with the current gradient form**, but on the natural-substrate L3 (Paper 45 §4), L3 transports without modification. |
| **L4** | Joint Berezin $\Bjoint = B^{\SU(2)} \otimes B^{U(1)_T}$ with PURE_TENSOR structure; rate $\gamma^{\mathrm{joint}} = O(\log n_{\max}/n_{\max} + T/N_t)$ | **PARTIAL TRANSFER.** $B^{\SU(2)}$ unchanged; $B^{U(1)_T}$ needs replacement by $B^{\mathbb{R}}$ acting on $C_0(\mathbb{R})$ (or polynomial-bounded smooth functions). Standard Fejér-on-the-line Berezin map gives positivity and contractivity; the rate becomes $\|B^{\mathbb{R}}_T(f) - P_{T_{\max}} M_f P_{T_{\max}}\|_{\mathrm{op}} \le ?$ — this is the non-compact analog of Hawkins / Connes-vS approximate identity. **Needs explicit construction.** |
| **L5** | Latrémolière propinquity assembly; tunneling pair $(\Bjoint, \Pjoint)$; published Latrémolière 2017/2018/2023 requires compact carrier | **STRUCTURALLY UNCLEAR.** No published Latrémolière propinquity construction on a non-compact carrier. Compact-carrier requirement enters via: (i) finite Berezin reach (needs $L^\infty$ control on a compact set), (ii) cb-norm finiteness (needs amenable LCA group, which $\mathbb{R}$ satisfies). The first is the bottleneck. |

**Net per-lemma:** L1' and L3 transfer free. L4 needs explicit non-compact Berezin construction with controllable rate. L2 needs joint SU(2) × $\mathbb{R}$ kernel with controllable rate. L5 needs Latrémolière propinquity definition extended to non-compact carriers — or a workaround.

## §4. Three candidate paths

### Path P1 — Coupled scaling, keep compact carrier

**Idea.** Stay with the compact $S^1_T$ carrier but treat $T$ as a third parameter going to infinity simultaneously with $(n_{\max}, N_t)$. Define a coupled scaling $T = T(n_{\max}, N_t)$ such that $T \to \infty$ slowly enough that the $O(T)$ gradient term stays bounded.

**Construction.** From β-L3 §1.3, the rate is $\gamma^{\mathrm{joint}} = O(\log n_{\max}/n_{\max} + T/N_t)$. Taking $T(N_t) = N_t / \log N_t$ would give $\gamma^{\mathrm{joint}} = O(\log n_{\max}/n_{\max} + 1/\log N_t) \to 0$. But the gradient norm picks up $\|D_t\|_{\mathrm{op}} \cdot \|a^{\mathrm{flip}}\|_{\mathrm{op}}$ on the enlarged substrate; at $\|D_t\|_{\mathrm{op}} = (N_t - 1)/2$ this scales linearly in $N_t$, which dominates.

**On the natural substrate (Paper 45 / Paper 46 main theorem, NOT Appendix B):** the gradient norm does NOT pick up the $O(T)$ term because the time direction is Lipschitz-invisible (Paper 45 §4.2 Lemma 4.3; sharpened in Appendix B §B.2 / Sprint L3b-2a). So **for the natural substrate, P1 might work**: coupled scaling $T(N_t)$ with $T \to \infty$, rate still $\gamma^{\mathrm{joint}} \to 0$.

**Status.** **Reachable, ~3–6 weeks**. The construction is: verify that Paper 45's natural-substrate rate survives joint $(n_{\max}, N_t, T) \to \infty$ with explicit scaling $T(N_t)$, prove a $T$-independent constant for the propinquity bound.

**Honest scope.** P1 gives a *parametric* de-compactification — the limit triple is $\Tcal^L_{S^3 \times S^1_{T_n}}$ with $T_n \to \infty$, NOT the genuine non-compact $\Tcal^L_{S^3 \times \mathbb{R}}$. The limit object is still a compact-temporal spectral triple at each finite stage. To recover the non-compact limit one needs Path P3.

### Path P2 — Switch to non-compact propinquity framework

**Idea.** Replace Latrémolière's compact-carrier propinquity with a non-compact analog. The published candidates are:

- **Hekkelman-McDonald 2024** (arXiv:2412.00628) "The semi-classical limit of Connes spectral triples on a torus" — Tauberian arguments on flat tori with extension to $\mathbb{R}^d$ via covering map. NOT a propinquity construction, but a related quantitative framework.
- **Toyota 2023** — Berezin-Toeplitz on $S^2$, compact carrier only.
- **Farsi-Latrémolière 2024/2025** — Riemannian path-of-metrics continuity, compact carrier only.

**As of May 2026, no published Lorentzian propinquity exists**, and no published non-compact propinquity in the Latrémolière line. The Mondino-Sämann synthetic Lorentzian GH program (2022–2025) is categorically different (pre-length spaces with causal diamonds, not operator-algebraic).

**Status.** **Multi-month original NCG-math**. P2 is structurally equivalent to attacking the G1' strong-form Lorentzian propinquity (Paper 45 §1.4 G1 in its original form, before L3b-2a–d closure on the natural substrate). Probably joins G1-genuine (the truly strong form without K⁺ restriction on a non-compact carrier) and G3 (cross-manifold) as a frontier program.

**Honest scope.** **Not a sprint-scale target.** P2 is the path to a publishable original-NCG-math theorem, but it is the wrong path to take FIRST. P1 should run first.

### Path P3 — Inductive limit

**Idea.** Apply the Paper 45/46 propinquity theorem on a sequence of compact carriers $S^3 \times S^1_{T_n}$ with $T_n \to \infty$, then take the inductive limit (in the C*-algebra / operator-system / spectral-triple sense) to define the limit object $\Tcal^L_{S^3 \times \mathbb{R}}$.

**Construction.** For each $T_n$, Paper 45 gives $\Lprop(\Tcal^L_{n_{\max}, N_t, T_n}, \Tcal^L_{S^3 \times S^1_{T_n}}) \to 0$ at the compact-carrier level. Define a refinement map $\Tcal^L_{S^3 \times S^1_{T_n}} \to \Tcal^L_{S^3 \times S^1_{T_{n+1}}}$ when $T_{n+1} > T_n$ (the longer-period spectral triple contains the shorter-period one as a sub-triple). Take the inductive limit.

**Status.** **Reachable but technically delicate, ~6–10 weeks**. The inductive-limit construction in the operator-algebraic / spectral-triple category is standard (Connes 1994 §VI for AF C*-algebras; Latrémolière 2018 §5 has inductive-limit propinquity for AF triples), but its application here requires:
1. Verifying that the spectral triples on $S^3 \times S^1_{T_n}$ form an inductive system (compatible refinement maps).
2. Verifying that the inductive limit is the right Krein spectral triple on $S^3 \times \mathbb{R}$ (Paper 43's L2-E construction on a bounded interval is the natural target).
3. Bounding $\Lprop(\Tcal_{S^3 \times S^1_{T_n}}, \Tcal_{S^3 \times \mathbb{R}})$ via tail estimates as $T_n \to \infty$.

**Honest scope.** Step 3 is the hard step — it requires showing that the *embedding* propinquity from a smaller-period triple into a longer-period one (or into the non-compact limit) is itself controlled. No published construction.

**Recommendation:** **L3c is a SPRINT-SCALE target via L3c-α**, with two follow-on sub-sprints (β and γ) conditional on outcomes. The original 2026-05-17 estimate of "4–8 weeks" for L3c is now refined to **3–5 weeks for the first sub-sprint** with an explicit decision gate before extending.

**This is NOT a multi-month frontier**, unlike G3 (cross-manifold, blocked by Paper 24 §V) or G1-genuine (full strong-form on enlarged substrate with non-trivial K⁺/K⁻ mixing). L3c is more tractable than these because:
1. The compact $\to$ non-compact transition is a parameter limit, not a framework extension.
2. The L2-B/E architecture (Paper 43) already realizes the right physical carrier at the operator-system level; L3c-γ would merge that architecture with L3b's metric-convergence machinery.
3. The natural-substrate path (Paper 45 / Paper 46 main theorem) avoids the $O(T)$ gradient-norm issue that broke the enlarged substrate.
