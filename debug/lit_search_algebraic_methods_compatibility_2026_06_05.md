### Category 1 — ERI tensor algebraic identifications

#### 1.1 Cholesky decomposition of ERIs

**Reference (verified):** Folkestad, Kjønstad, Koch 2019, "An efficient algorithm for Cholesky decomposition of electron repulsion integrals," *J. Chem. Phys.* 150, 194112. arXiv: none; DOI 10.1063/1.5083802 (confirmed via PubMed 31117774). Earlier foundational: Beebe & Linderberg 1977.

**Description.** The 4-index ERI super-matrix (μν|λσ) is positive semi-definite when reshaped into a (μν)×(λσ) Gram matrix, so it admits an exact Cholesky decomposition $(\mu\nu|\lambda\sigma) = \sum_P L_P^{\mu\nu} L_P^{\lambda\sigma}$. The Cholesky pivot procedure builds the auxiliary index P on-the-fly, retaining only pivots above a threshold. The output L is the same shape as a DF auxiliary-basis fit but is generated by the pivoting algorithm rather than by a fixed auxiliary basis.

**Algebraic core.** Cholesky and DF/RI produce **the same factorization form** $V_{ee} = \sum_P L_P \otimes L_P$ where $L_P \in A$ (one-body operator on the orbital space). The difference is the construction of P: Cholesky generates pivots numerically; DF/RI uses a pre-specified auxiliary basis. Pedersen 2024 (*WIREs Comput. Mol. Sci.* 14, e1692; abstract verified via search) explicitly catalogs the "CD and DF are algebraic cousins" reading.

**Connection to GeoVac.** This is the same `A⊗A` structure DF lives in. The DF=multipole bit-exact finding (memo 2026-06-05) implies: **Cholesky on a GeoVac ERI tensor should likewise produce L_P columns that lie entirely within the analytic multipole subspace**, because the multipole expansion gives the full set of `A` factors of `V_ee` analytically. The Cholesky rank at n_max=2 should equal the multipole rank (7), and at larger n_max should track the graded R^L spectrum the same way DF did.

**Verdict: EQUIVALENT** (HIGH confidence). This is the cleanest direct extension of the DF=multipole result.

**Suggested test (1-2 day sprint):** Apply Cholesky decomposition (`scipy.linalg.cho_factor` or pivoted Cholesky from PySCF) to the GeoVac ERI tensors at n_max ∈ {2, 3, 4} for LiH/BeH₂/H₂O. Verify (a) rank matches DF rank at each cell, (b) Cholesky vectors project entirely onto the multipole subspace, (c) per-L rank decomposition matches. If positive, write a one-paragraph footnote into the DF memo.

#### 1.2 Resolution-of-identity / density fitting (RI/DF)

**Reference (verified):** Aquilante, Pedersen, Lindh et al. 2008 lineage; standard modern: Weigend, Köhn, Hättig 2002 *J. Chem. Phys.* 116, 3175 (Karlsruhe def2 fitting basis); Hellweg, Hättig 2007 etc. Manninen-Vaara 2008 (verified via PySCF docs).

**Description.** RI/DF approximates the product of two Gaussian orbitals by expanding it in an auxiliary basis $\{\chi_P\}$: $\mu(r)\nu(r) \approx \sum_P C^P_{\mu\nu} \chi_P(r)$. The 4-index ERI becomes a sum of 3-index integrals: $(\mu\nu|\lambda\sigma) = \sum_{PQ} C^P_{\mu\nu} (P|Q) C^Q_{\lambda\sigma}$. After Cholesky-decomposing $(P|Q)^{-1}$, the form reduces to $\sum_P L_P^{\mu\nu} L_P^{\lambda\sigma}$.

**Algebraic core.** Same as Cholesky after the metric pre-multiplication: `V_ee = Σ_P L_P ⊗ L_P`.

**Connection to GeoVac.** Same as 1.1. The GeoVac multipole expansion already gives an analytic auxiliary basis: the angular-momentum eigenfunctions of fixed L on the Fock-projected S³. The "auxiliary basis" for GeoVac is structurally a Gaunt-coefficient projection, not a Gaussian fitting set. Identical algebra at the abstract level.

**Verdict: EQUIVALENT** (HIGH confidence). Same operator-system content as Cholesky/DF. Worth saying explicitly in any Paper 14/20 revision.

**Suggested test:** Trivial (subsumed by 1.1).

#### 1.3 McMurchie-Davidson / Obara-Saika / Pople-Hehre recurrence

**References (verified):**
- McMurchie & Davidson 1978, "One- and two-electron integrals over cartesian gaussian functions," *J. Comput. Phys.* 26, 218 (ADS-verified).
- Ahmadi & Almlöf 1995, "A new algorithm of two-electron repulsion integral calculations: a combination of Pople-Hehre and McMurchie-Davidson methods" (*Theor. Chem. Acc.* DOI 10.1007/s00214-007-0295-5, search-verified).
- Original Obara-Saika 1986, *J. Chem. Phys.* 84, 3963.

**Description.** Recurrence relations for two-electron integrals over Cartesian Gaussian functions. The McMurchie-Davidson scheme expresses overlap, kinetic, nuclear-attraction, and ERI integrals via Hermite Gaussian polynomial coefficients and auxiliary functions $F_n(T)$ (Boys functions). Obara-Saika is structurally similar but uses Cartesian-shell raising/lowering directly. Pople-Hehre rotates axes per shell-pair.

**Algebraic core.** Recurrence on Cartesian-Gaussian indices: $(a+1_i|s) = $ linear combination of $(a|s), (a-1_i|s)$ with rational coefficients in Gaussian exponents and centers. The auxiliary functions $F_n(T) = \int_0^1 t^{2n} e^{-Tt^2} dt$ are the Mellin transforms of `e^{-T t²}` at half-integer shifts — a Hermite-Gaussian heat-kernel signature.

**Connection to GeoVac.** This is a different algebra. The Hermite-Gaussian recursion lives in the Cartesian polynomial × Gaussian-exponent ring (cousin of the GeoVac Bargmann-Segal lattice for the HO, Paper 24). GeoVac's angular structure is spherical (Gaunt + Wigner 3j), not Cartesian. The recurrence couples the Cartesian shell-up/down indices, not angular momentum L directly.

The Boys function $F_n(T)$ DOES connect to GeoVac's master Mellin engine — $F_n$ at half-integer shifts is structurally the M2 sub-mechanism (Seeley-DeWitt-style, Mellin of heat kernel). So the **calibration constants in M-D / O-S recurrence sit in GeoVac's M2 ring**. But the recurrence itself does not reproduce the multipole rank structure of `V_ee`.

**Verdict: CATEGORICALLY DISTINCT** (HIGH confidence) at the operator-algebra level, but **the auxiliary functions $F_n(T)$ are M2-classified**. Useful translation note: Boys functions live in the same Paper 18 §III.7 cell (k=2 Mellin of heat kernel) as GeoVac's Seeley-DeWitt coefficients.

### Category 2 — Algebraic angular-momentum / spin-adapted methods

#### 2.1 GUGA (Graphical Unitary Group Approach) — Shavitt's distinct row table

**References (verified):**
- Shavitt 1977-78 (original DRT, *Int. J. Quantum Chem.* S11, S12); Paldus 1974 (unitary group basis); Brooks-Schaefer 1979 *JCP* 70, 5092 (search-verified DOI 10.1063/1.437452).
- Dobrautz, Smart, Alavi 2019, arXiv:1905.01346, *J. Chem. Phys.* 151, 094104 (verified). GUGA-FCIQMC.
- Recent: cuGUGA arXiv:2601.17729 (CUDA-accelerated; search-listed, not deeply verified).

**Description.** GUGA represents many-electron spin-adapted basis (CSFs) as paths through a layered DAG (the Shavitt graph), encoded by a distinct row table. CSFs correspond to root-to-leaf walks; Hamiltonian matrix elements are evaluated by traversing segment-by-segment with local rules. The underlying group structure is `u(d) × u(2)` (`u(d)` on spatial orbitals, `u(2)` on spin).

**Algebraic core.** Spin-free generators $E_{ij}$ of `u(d)` acting on CSF basis labelled by Gelfand-Tsetlin patterns. The Hamiltonian becomes a sum of products of $E_{ij}$'s, and matrix elements decompose into a Wigner-Eckart-style factorization (orbital × spin-coupling).

**Connection to GeoVac.** GeoVac's composed builder already uses Gaunt (Wigner 3j) selection rules to factor ERIs by `L`. GUGA's segment rules are the Wigner-Eckart-style separation between orbital pattern and spin-coupling pattern. The two are structurally compatible — GUGA gives the spin-coupling layer, GeoVac gives the orbital-angular-momentum layer.

A combined "GUGA-on-GeoVac" Hamiltonian would (a) replace the orbital-index `u(d)` labeling with GeoVac's quantum-number `(n, l, m)` labeling on the Fock S³, and (b) keep GUGA's `u(2)` spin segment evaluation. This would convert GeoVac's spin-orbital Slater-determinant basis to a spin-adapted CSF basis, **reducing the Hilbert space dimension** (~factor 2-3 for typical valence systems) while preserving the qubit count or reducing it further via tapering.

**Verdict: COMPATIBLE** (HIGH confidence). The Wigner-Eckart factorization is the bridge: GeoVac's orbital labeling on S³ × GUGA's spin segment evaluation = spin-adapted composed builder. This is currently NOT in the GeoVac codebase.

**Suggested test (4-week sprint):** Implement GUGA-style CSF basis on top of `composed_qubit.py`. Pick LiH at n_max=2 (Q=20). Build the spin-adapted Hamiltonian via segment rules. Verify (a) the FCI ground-state energy matches the determinant-basis FCI bit-exactly, (b) the Hilbert space dimension is reduced by the expected CSF factor, (c) the spin-adapted Pauli count is lower than the determinant Pauli count. If positive, document as a new section of Paper 14.

#### 2.2 Quantum Paldus Transform (THIS IS A NEW SIGNIFICANT FIND)

**Reference (verified):** Burkat & Fitzpatrick 2025, "The Quantum Paldus Transform: Efficient Circuits with Applications," arXiv:2506.09151 (verified June 10, 2025 submission, revised October 11, 2025).

**Description.** The QPT is a quantum circuit implementing an isometry between the occupation number basis of a fermionic Fock space on 2d modes and the Gelfand-Tsetlin basis indexing the irreducible representations of $U(d) \times SU(2)$. This implements Paldus's UGA in circuit form. Claim: $O(d^3)$ Toffoli complexity, "maximally sparse representations of spin-free Hamiltonians."

**Algebraic core.** The QPT realizes the UGA decomposition: every spin-free fermionic Hamiltonian admits a block-diagonal decomposition along irreducible representations of $U(d) \times SU(2)$, with the blocks labeled by GT patterns. The transform is an explicit unitary that maps the JW Fock basis into the GT basis.

**Connection to GeoVac.** GeoVac's composed builder produces a Hamiltonian whose orbital basis is already a representation of `SU(2) × SO(3) × SO(3)` (spin × angular × radial) via the Fock projection. The QPT operates on the `u(d) × u(2)` symmetry of the spin-free Hamiltonian. There is an obvious composition: GeoVac builds the Hamiltonian in spin-orbital basis with its natural angular labels; QPT block-diagonalizes by spin. **If the QPT can be composed with GeoVac's natural Fock-S³ angular labeling, the resulting Hamiltonian would be triply block-diagonal** (spin × angular × radial), maximally exploiting both angular-momentum sparsity (GeoVac) and spin sparsity (QPT).

This is the same shape as the DF=multipole finding: surface-different rank/factorization machinery sitting on top of the same underlying spectral-triple algebra.

**Verdict: COMPATIBLE** (HIGH confidence). Stackable.

**Suggested test (2-3 week sprint):** Use the QPT toolkit (Burkat-Fitzpatrick reference implementation if available, else implement minimal Paldus-block extractor) on the LiH/BeH₂/H₂O composed Hamiltonians at n_max=2. Compare (a) block dimension of QPT-blocks vs untransformed JW, (b) per-block Pauli count, (c) compare against the Z₂ Hopf-tapering result (debug/sprint_z2_per_subblock_memo.md, 12.6% Pauli reduction across panel) — these are different reduction axes and should STACK (the Z₂ tapering uses the orbital Hopf parity; the QPT uses the spin-irrep structure). If they stack additively, write a Paper 14 §sec:paldus_tapering subsection.

#### 2.3 Wigner-Eckart / Racah algebra at the operator level

**References (verified):** Wigner-Eckart standard refs (Wikipedia + ScienceDirect Wigner-Eckart 2021 "Interpreting" article verified); Racah 1942-43 lineage standard.

**Description.** Rank-k spherical tensor operator $T^k_q$ acting between angular momentum eigenstates: $\langle j'm' | T^k_q | jm \rangle = \langle j'm'|jm;kq \rangle \langle j'\|T^k\|j\rangle$ (Clebsch-Gordan × reduced matrix element). Reduces all matrix-element calculation to one "reduced" number per multiplet pair.

**Connection to GeoVac.** GeoVac's per-L multipole decomposition of `V_ee` IS a Wigner-Eckart factorization: the angular dependence is in Gaunt coefficients (sums of 3j symbols), the radial dependence (the `R^L(α,β)` block) is the reduced matrix element. The DF=multipole result is precisely the statement that DF rediscovers the W-E factorization of `V_ee`.

**Verdict: EQUIVALENT at the conceptual level**, but already implicitly built into GeoVac (HIGH confidence). The W-E theorem is the mathematical backbone of the multipole expansion. Doesn't generate a new sprint — it's a translation register entry.

### Category 3 — Coupled cluster algebra

#### 3.1 CCSD(T) / UCC / e^T ansatz with normal ordering

**References (verified):**
- Faulstich 2024, arXiv:2401.07383, "Recent mathematical advances in coupled cluster theory" (search-verified).
- Černá, Lasser, Wang 2024 arXiv:2403.05389 *Faraday Discuss.* (search-verified) — normal ordered exp ansatz multireference CC.
- Foundational: Coester 1958, Čížek 1966.

**Description.** Exponential ansatz $|\Psi\rangle = e^T |\Phi_0\rangle$ where $T = T_1 + T_2 + ...$ is a sum of excitation operators. The similarity-transformed Hamiltonian $\bar{H} = e^{-T} H e^T$ terminates at finite order by Wick's theorem (since $[T, T']=0$ for cluster operators). Energy and amplitude equations come from BCH expansion of $\bar{H}$.

**Algebraic core.** Operator algebra of fermion creation/annihilation in Fock space; the similarity transform is in the universal enveloping algebra of `o(2N)` (or `gl(2N)` for non-spin-adapted CC).

**Connection to GeoVac.** CC is a non-unitary transformation of the Hamiltonian operator in Fock space. GeoVac's spectral triple is `(A, H, D)` with `A` the function algebra on the truncated Fock-S³ graph and the algebra acting on a Hilbert space of states. The CC similarity transform changes the Hamiltonian; the spectral-triple-of-states perspective focuses on the geometry of states. These are **operating on different objects** — CC on the algebra, the spectral triple on the geometry of states.

That said: the **UCC ansatz** (unitary CC, $e^T$ with $T$ anti-Hermitian) IS a unitary transformation in the group of `A`-derivations, so it lives in the "inner-fluctuations" part of Connes' spectral-triple language. This is the same place gauge fields live in NCG. The connection is real but is at a level (gauge fluctuations of D, not direct algebra operations on V_ee) where the algebraic content is implicit.

**Verdict: CATEGORICALLY DISTINCT** at the operator-algebra-of-states level (HIGH confidence). CC's natural home is the operator algebra of fermionic Fock space; GeoVac's natural home is the function algebra on the orbital S³ graph. Translation register: UCC excitations are inner fluctuations of D in NCG language.

### Category 4 — Tensor network methods (DMRG / MPS / MPO)

#### 4.1 DMRG / MPS / MPO for quantum chemistry

**References (verified):**
- Keller, Dolfi, Troyer, Reiher 2015, arXiv:1510.02026, *J. Chem. Phys.* 143, 244118. **MPO representation of quantum chemical Hamiltonian** (verified).
- Standard intro: Schollwöck 2011 *Ann. Phys.* 326, 96 (DMRG → MPS); Chan-Sharma 2011 reviews.
- Cross-discipline survey: Friesecke et al. 2025 *arXiv:2501.18263* (tensor networks for QChem, search-listed).

**Description.** A many-body state $|\Psi\rangle = \sum_{\sigma_1...\sigma_L} \text{tr}(A^{[1]}_{\sigma_1} ... A^{[L]}_{\sigma_L}) |\sigma_1...\sigma_L\rangle$ is an MPS with bond dimension $\chi$. The Hamiltonian is an MPO. DMRG iteratively SVD-truncates bond indices. Pre-defined bond dimension $\chi$ = truncation parameter.

**Algebraic core.** Variational ansatz over a low-bond-dimension submanifold of Hilbert space, equivalent to truncating Schmidt-decomposition-rank at each bipartition. The MPO of the chemistry Hamiltonian is constructed by writing $H = \sum_{ij} h_{ij} a^\dagger_i a_j + ...$ in finite-state-automaton form along the orbital chain.

**Connection to GeoVac.** Both DMRG-truncation and Connes-vS spectral-truncation are **truncations of an underlying operator algebra**. The Connes-vS truncation (van Suijlekom 2020, arXiv:2005.08544 verified) replaces the spectral triple `(A, H, D)` by its compression `(PAP, PH, PD)` for a spectral projection P. The operator system `PAP` converges in propinquity / Gromov-Hausdorff to `A` as P → 1 (Paper 38 in GeoVac).

DMRG bond-dimension truncation is a Schmidt-rank truncation on states. Connes-vS truncation is a spectral-projection truncation on the algebra. **These are dual perspectives on the same kind of "low-rank approximation of a spectral triple"**, but the truncation parameter ($\chi$ vs $n_{\max}$) and the projection target (Schmidt rank vs Dirac spectrum cutoff) are different.

Hekkelman-McDonald 2024 (arXiv:2412.00628, verified) shows local Weyl laws on truncated spectral triples → quantum ergodicity. **There is a real, deep operator-algebraic identification waiting here**: GeoVac's n_max truncation IS a Connes-vS spectral truncation; MPS bond-truncation IS a different truncation of the same underlying triple. If we can show GeoVac's composed-builder truncation can be re-expressed as a particular MPO bond truncation in the GeoVac orbital chain, that's a Paper-grade theorem.

**Verdict: COMPATIBLE** (MEDIUM-HIGH confidence). Direct identification likely requires several weeks of math work, but the structural pieces are in place: van Suijlekom's GH convergence for spectral truncations + Keller-Dolfi-Troyer-Reiher's MPO machinery for the chemistry Hamiltonian + GeoVac's Paper 38 propinquity proof on S³.

**Suggested test (2-month sprint):** Take GeoVac's LiH composed Hamiltonian at n_max=2 and 3. Construct its MPO representation via Keller et al. 2015. Compute the bond dimensions $\chi(L)$ across bipartitions. Verify whether MPO bond-rank at fixed truncation threshold matches the rank of the spectral-truncation operator system. If matched: paper-grade identification. If not: clean categorical distinction recorded.

### Category 5 — Explicitly correlated methods (Hylleraas r12 / F12 / TC)

#### 5.1 R12 / F12 / Transcorrelated method

**References (verified):**
- Kong, Bischoff, Valeev 2012, "Explicitly Correlated R12/F12 Methods" (search-verified ResearchGate).
- TC review: Liao, Yang, Knizia 2024 (*JCTC*, search-verified).
- Recent: Vyboishchikov 2025 arXiv:2512.07530 "Modular Construction of Jastrow Factors for the Transcorrelated Method" (search-listed).
- Honest scope: this is the area where GeoVac §3 has documented dead-ends (cusp / TC).

**Description.** R12/F12 augment the basis with explicit $r_{12}$ (or correlation factor $f(r_{12})$) terms to capture the Kato cusp analytically without infinite partial-wave expansion. TC uses a Jastrow $J(r_1, ..., r_N) = \sum_{i<j} u(r_{ij})$ to similarity-transform the Hamiltonian: $\tilde H = e^{-J} H e^J$ has no cusp.

**Algebraic core.** $r_{12}$ enters the wavefunction as a polynomial × Gaussian factor, evaluated via four-electron integrals. The TC similarity transform produces a non-Hermitian effective Hamiltonian with explicit three-body terms.

**Connection to GeoVac.** GeoVac §3 has multiple cusp / TC dead-ends documented: "Cusp treatments (alpha-only, graph absorption, θ₁₂-adapted basis)" — 3 attempts; "1/r₁₂ graph absorption" — structural obstruction (Green's function singularity mismatch on S⁵); "TC Jastrow in adiabatic hyperspherical solver" — 46% error; "TC in second quantization" — 3.4% plateau across n_max=2..5; "TC 2D variational cusp correction" — 3 attempts.

The lesson from §3 is that **the cusp is an embedding exchange constant** (Paper 18): the singular content of `1/r_{12}` does not factor through the angular-momentum algebra `A` of the Fock-projected S³ graph. r12 sits in `A` (via the multipole expansion) as an L-sum that converges slowly at the cusp, but the cusp itself is a continuum projection feature.

A finite-rank Jastrow $J = \sum_{ij} u(r_{ij})$ DOES factor through `A⊗A` in principle (`J` is a one-body radial function tensored with itself, summed over pairs), but the F12 four-electron integrals couple to the full continuum measure of S³, not to the discrete graph.

**Verdict: CATEGORICALLY DISTINCT** (HIGH confidence). The cusp is genuinely an embedding-projection feature of `1/r`, not an algebra-of-states feature. Confirmed by §3 multiple-attempt failures.

### Category 6 — Fermion-to-qubit mappings

#### 6.1 Bravyi-Kitaev / ternary tree / parity mappings as a family

**References (verified):**
- Chiew, Harrison, Strelchuk 2024, arXiv:2412.07578, "Ternary tree transformations are equivalent to linear encodings of the Fock basis" (verified Dec 10, 2024). HEADLINE: **all product-preserving ternary tree transformations are equivalent to linear encodings**, which includes JW, BK, parity. They form one algebraic family.
- Tranter, Love, Mintert, Coveney 2018 *JCTC* "Comparison of BK and JW for Quantum Simulation of Quantum Chemistry" (search-verified).
- Standard: Seeley, Richard, Love 2012 arXiv:1208.5986 (BK in QChem).

**Description.** Every fermion-to-qubit mapping that respects the canonical anticommutation relations corresponds to a choice of binary representation of the Majorana operators. The encoding is `2N → 2N` qubits with locality / weight tradeoffs. JW gives O(N) weight per Majorana; BK gives O(log N); ternary tree achieves O(log N) optimal.

**Algebraic core.** All such mappings are unitarily equivalent: $U \alpha_i U^\dagger = \alpha'_i$ where $\alpha, \alpha'$ are different Majorana encodings. The differences are computational (Pauli weight) not algebraic.

**Connection to GeoVac.** GeoVac currently uses JW. The Chiew-Harrison-Strelchuk theorem tells us the choice of mapping does NOT change the underlying Hamiltonian's spectrum or algebra — all linear encodings are equivalent. **Switching to BK or ternary-tree should produce identical spectra at lower Pauli weight per fermion operator**.

The interesting question is whether the GeoVac composed structure (block-diagonal in angular momentum) interacts non-trivially with BK / ternary-tree locality patterns. BK groups qubits by binary tree levels; if GeoVac's blocks align with binary tree subtrees, BK could reduce the per-Pauli weight further than naive O(log N).

**Verdict: COMPATIBLE** (HIGH confidence). All linear encodings are unitarily equivalent (Chiew 2024 theorem). The choice is a downstream optimization, not an algebraic question.

**Suggested test (1-2 week sprint):** Apply BK and ternary-tree mappings to the LiH/BeH₂/H₂O composed Hamiltonians at n_max=2 (using `openfermion.bravyi_kitaev` and Chiew 2024 ternary tree). Verify (a) bit-exact spectrum match vs JW, (b) measure per-Pauli weight reduction, (c) compare per-Pauli weight reduction to baselines from non-composed (Gaussian) Hamiltonians at the same Q — does the GeoVac block structure compose favorably with BK?

### Category 7 — QSVT / qubitization / block encoding

#### 7.1 QSVT / qubitization / standard block encoding

**References (verified):**
- Low, Chuang 2017 arXiv:1610.06546 "Optimal Hamiltonian Simulation by QSP" (qubitization, foundational).
- Gilyén, Su, Low, Wiebe 2019, *STOC 2019*, "Quantum singular value transformation and beyond" (foundational QSVT).
- Block-encoding chemistry: Babbush et al. arXiv:1902.02134 (verified, *Quantum* 2019), and Lee et al. arXiv:2011.03494 (verified, *PRX Quantum* 2021).

**Description.** A block encoding of $H/\alpha$ is a unitary $U$ such that $(\langle 0|^a \otimes I) U (|0\rangle^a \otimes I) = H/\alpha$. Qubitization constructs a walk operator whose eigenvalues are $e^{\pm i \arccos(E_k/\alpha)}$ where $E_k$ are eigenvalues of H. QSVT generalizes to arbitrary polynomial transforms of H's singular values.

**Algebraic core.** QSVT operates on a matrix polynomial of `H`. The block encoding is a representation of `H` (rescaled) inside a larger unitary; the QSVT machinery is a representation theory of $SU(2)$ acting on this block-encoded structure.

**Connection to GeoVac.** GeoVac produces a Pauli-decomposed Hamiltonian `H = Σ_i h_i P_i`. Standard LCU / qubitization block-encodings consume this Pauli decomposition. The interesting question is: **does GeoVac's algebraic content provide a more efficient block-encoding than the generic LCU-of-Pauli construction?**

For DF / THC, the answer (in standard chemistry) is YES — the rank-r structure of DF gives a block encoding with $\alpha \sim \lambda_{DF}$ much smaller than $\sum_i |h_i|$. GeoVac's natural per-L multipole structure (which IS the DF structure, per the 2026-06-05 memo) should give a similar block encoding with $\alpha \sim \lambda_{multipole}$. **The GeoVac Hamiltonian admits a natural multipole-structured block encoding** — same machinery as standard DF block encoding, but with the auxiliary index structured by angular momentum L rather than by Cholesky pivot.

**Verdict: COMPATIBLE** (HIGH confidence). GeoVac can drop into standard QSVT machinery via its multipole / DF factorization. Worth a Paper 20 subsection on "GeoVac block-encoding via the multipole auxiliary basis."

#### 7.2 LCU decomposition

**Reference (verified):** Childs, Wiebe 2012 arXiv:1202.5822 (Hamiltonian Simulation Using LCU, *QIC* 12, 901). Foundational.

**Description.** Express $H = \sum_i \alpha_i U_i$ for unitaries $U_i$ (most naturally Paulis). LCU uses ancilla-controlled application with success probability and oblivious amplitude amplification.

**Connection to GeoVac.** GeoVac's Pauli-string Hamiltonian IS an LCU decomposition. The 1-norm $\lambda_{LCU} = \sum_i |\alpha_i|$ is what GeoVac quotes for Paper 20 baseline comparisons. The structured LCU using GeoVac's multipole-block structure (each L block is itself a smaller LCU) would give a nested LCU with lower effective 1-norm — same idea as DF block-encoding.

**Verdict: COMPATIBLE** (HIGH confidence). Subsumed by 7.1.

### Category 8 — Nuclear / interacting-boson algebraic methods

#### 8.1 Moshinsky-Talmi brackets

**References (verified):**
- Moshinsky 1959 *Nucl. Phys.* 13, 104 (ScienceDirect verified). Original Moshinsky bracket paper.
- Modern computation: Buck, Merchant 1996, *Nucl. Phys. A* (HOTB code lineage); Mickevičius-Germanas 2021 *Comput. Phys. Comm.* (Talmi-Moshinsky-Smirnov, search-verified).

**Description.** Brackets transforming two-particle HO wavefunctions between independent-particle $(n_1 l_1 n_2 l_2)$ and relative-CM $(nl NL)$ basis. Sum is finite (Talmi rule): $2n_1 + l_1 + 2n_2 + l_2 = 2n + l + 2N + L$.

**Connection to GeoVac.** Paper 23 already uses Moshinsky-Talmi brackets for the deuteron NN potential. So this is internal — already in GeoVac. The interesting follow-on is whether other nuclear-side algebraic methods (e.g., generalized seniority, IBM) plug in cleanly.

**Verdict: EQUIVALENT** (HIGH confidence), already in Paper 23. No new test needed.

#### 8.2 Interacting Boson Model (IBM) / SU(3) dynamical symmetry

**References (verified):**
- Iachello, Arima 1987 *Interacting Boson Model*, CUP (foundational).
- Recent: Casten et al. 2010 arXiv:1004.0735 (search-verified). Leviatan et al. 2020 arXiv:2010.10951 (PDS, verified).

**Description.** Collective nuclear excitations modeled with `s` (L=0) and `d` (L=2) bosons; overall U(6) symmetry; three dynamical symmetries U(5), SU(3), O(6). Hamiltonian written as quadratic Casimirs of subgroups → exactly solvable in symmetry limits.

**Connection to GeoVac.** IBM is a boson model on collective excitations — different fundamental degrees of freedom from GeoVac's discrete S³ graph Hamiltonian for nuclei. IBM uses a Lie-algebra Casimir labeling for spectra (operates on group-representation states); GeoVac uses the discrete graph Laplacian.

The Casimir machinery IS present in GeoVac (SO(4), SO(6) Casimirs appear at multiple places — Paper 7's S³ proof uses SO(4) Casimir eigenvalues n²-1; Paper 13's hyperspherical solver uses SO(6) Casimir ν(ν+4)/2). But these are angular-momentum Casimirs on the natural geometry, not the collective Casimirs of IBM.

**Verdict: CATEGORICALLY DISTINCT** (MEDIUM confidence). The Casimir-eigenvalue machinery is conceptually parallel, but applied to different physical degrees of freedom. The IBM is an effective theory of nucleon-pair collective modes; GeoVac is a microscopic theory of individual nucleon orbitals.

#### 8.3 Generalized seniority

**References (verified):** Talmi 1971 *Nucl. Phys.* A172, 1; modern: Caprio-Maris-Vary 2012 arXiv:1203.4856 *PRC* 85, 034324 (verified).

**Description.** Quasi-spin SU(2) algebra on pairs of fermions coupled to L=0 in a single j-shell. Seniority $v$ = number of unpaired particles. Generalized to many-shell via $S = \sum_j c_j S_j^+$ collective pair.

**Connection to GeoVac.** GeoVac's nuclear treatment in Paper 23 uses HO orbitals + Moshinsky for the NN potential. Generalized seniority adds an extra layer: pair states organized by quasi-spin SU(2). The quasi-spin algebra commutes with the orbital angular-momentum algebra used by GeoVac, so they could COMPOSE: GeoVac angular labels × generalized-seniority pair labels = nuclear basis with both angular and pair structure.

**Verdict: COMPATIBLE** (MEDIUM confidence, NUCLEAR-only). Not relevant for electronic-structure chemistry; useful for Paper 23 follow-on work on heavy nuclei.

### Category 9 — Effective-Hamiltonian / projection methods

#### 9.1 Schrieffer-Wolff / Löwdin partitioning / quasi-degenerate PT

**References (verified):**
- Schrieffer, Wolff 1966 *Phys. Rev.* 149, 491 (canonical).
- Bravyi, DiVincenzo, Loss 2011 *Ann. Phys.* 326, 2793, arXiv:1105.0675 ("S-W transformation for quantum many-body systems," search-verified).
- Quantum algorithm: Zheng, Krastanov, Khaneja 2022 arXiv:2201.13304 (search-verified).
- Pymablock: Vaikuntanathan et al. 2024 arXiv:2404.03728 (search-verified).

**Description.** Block-diagonalize $H = H_0 + V$ via $\tilde H = e^{-S} H e^S$ with anti-Hermitian $S$ chosen to eliminate the inter-block coupling order by order in $V$. The effective low-energy Hamiltonian acts on the low-energy subspace alone.

**Algebraic core.** Recursive solution for $S^{(n)}$ at each perturbation order. Operates on the operator algebra at a fixed Hilbert space.

**Connection to GeoVac.** S-W is a similarity transformation that takes one spectral-triple-with-D and produces another spectral-triple-with-tilde-D. The transformation is non-trivial at the algebra level. **This is not a re-expression of GeoVac's algebra**, it's a NEW spectral triple obtained from GeoVac's via projection + dressing.

The PK pseudopotential in GeoVac's composed builder is structurally a Löwdin-partitioning projector (core-valence partition + dressing). Paper 17 §IV documents this; recent W1e arc (CLAUDE.md §3) shows the limits.

**Verdict: CATEGORICALLY DISTINCT** as an algebra-of-states identification (HIGH confidence), but **PK in composed builder IS already a partial S-W transformation**. So the algebraic content of S-W is already partially internalized in GeoVac. A full S-W extension (active-space + Lowdin) WOULD produce a new GeoVac spectral triple downstream of the composed one.

### Category 10 — Sturmian and basis-completeness methods

#### 10.1 Coulomb Sturmians / Shibuya-Wulfman

**References (verified):**
- Avery, Avery 2004 *Int. J. Quantum Chem.* 100, 121, "Many-center Coulomb Sturmians and Shibuya-Wulfman integrals" (verified via search).
- Aquilanti et al. 1996 line (alternative Sturmian basis for H₂⁺, verified).
- Paper 19 (GeoVac) makes this connection explicit.

**Description.** Coulomb Sturmians $\{S_{nlm}(r, k)\}$ are eigenfunctions of $\nabla^2 + 2nk/r - k^2$ with shared exponent $k$. Form a discrete complete basis (no continuum). For molecular calculations, expansion of one center's Sturmian in the other center's gives the Shibuya-Wulfman integral.

**Connection to GeoVac.** Paper 19 documents the mathematical connection: GeoVac's S³ framework IS the Coulomb-Sturmian formalism in projected form. Papers 8-9 prove the negative theorem (Sturmian Structural Theorem) for shared-exponent single-center molecular encoding. So **the algebraic relation is fully internalized**, and the negative theorem is also fully cataloged.

**Verdict: EQUIVALENT** (HIGH confidence), already in Paper 19. Sturmian-based molecular methods reduce to GeoVac's natural-geometry hierarchy via the well-documented mapping.

### Category 11 — Algebraic-geometry / motivic methods

#### 11.1 Feynman-motives / mixed-Tate periods

**References (verified):**
- Marcolli 2010 *Feynman Motives*, World Scientific.
- Brown 2009 arXiv:0910.0114 *Comm. Math. Phys.* (search-verified).
- Marcolli, Aluffi 2013 arXiv:1308.5687 (search-verified). Configuration-space Feynman integrals as motivic periods.
- Closer to chemistry: NO direct connection. The mixed-Tate machinery operates on Feynman graph polynomials (Symanzik polynomials), not on chemistry overlap / ERI integrals.

**Description.** Feynman amplitudes admit a representation as integrals of rational functions over real positive orthants in projective space; the residues live in mixed-Tate motives over Z in many cases (the famous "all weights ≤ certain bound" structure of physical amplitudes).

**Connection to GeoVac.** GeoVac's Paper 55 ("Periods of GeoVac") classifies M1/M2/M3 in mixed-Tate / cyclotomic-mixed-Tate terms using the same Deligne–Glanois–Brown machinery. The S³ M2 sub-mechanism inherits Fathizadeh-Marcolli mixed-Tate. So GeoVac IS doing motivic-periods work, but on the **Mellin-transform side**, not on the Feynman-amplitude side.

For chemistry specifically: there is **no published quantum chemistry literature** classifying molecular integrals by mixed-Tate periods. The search returned only QFT-side results. **This remains GeoVac-novel within chemistry**.

**Verdict: CATEGORICALLY DISTINCT** from chemistry-literature standpoint (HIGH confidence). GeoVac's mixed-Tate framing is novel within chemistry. Closest cousin is Brown / Marcolli motivic-period work in QFT — same mathematical machinery, different physics domain.
