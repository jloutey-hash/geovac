Track AF Summary: 2D + l-dependent PK for LiH
======================================================================

HEADLINE: NEGATIVE RESULT. l-dependent PK does NOT improve the 2D
solver's l_max drift rate. The combination 2D + l-dep PK shows drift
of +0.15 bohr/l_max (average), matching or exceeding 2D + channel-blind.
R_eq does NOT converge with increasing l_max. The prediction that
"with the 2D solver, l_max drift should be eliminated" is FALSIFIED.

======================================================================
DRIFT ANALYSIS
======================================================================

Grid minimum drift:
  l=2 -> l=3: R_eq 3.200 -> 3.300, dR = +0.100 bohr
  l=3 -> l=4: R_eq 3.300 -> 3.500, dR = +0.200 bohr
  Average drift: +0.150 bohr/l_max (ACCELERATING)

Comparison:
  adiab + channel-blind PK: +0.400 bohr/l_max
  2D + channel-blind PK:    +0.100 bohr/l_max
  2D + l-dep PK:            +0.150 bohr/l_max (this work)

The drift accelerates from l=3 to l=4. At l_max=4, the PES is
extremely flat (R=3.5 and R=3.8 differ by only 0.07 mHa), making
R_eq poorly defined.

======================================================================
CUSP CORRECTION RESULT
======================================================================

At l_max=2, cusp correction is -0.17 mHa at R_eq (sub-dominant).
Grid R_eq unchanged (3.200 bohr with and without cusp). Cusp is a
nearly uniform energy shift that does not affect equilibrium geometry
at this level of error. Higher l_max cusp runs were not performed
(expected to be even smaller, scaling as ~1/(l+1/2)^4).

======================================================================
INTERPRETATION
======================================================================

1. l-dependent PK is IRRELEVANT to the 2D solver. When the 2D solver
   handles all channel coupling variationally, the PK channel mode
   makes no difference. The identical drift at l=2 and l=3 between
   2D+channel-blind and 2D+l-dep confirms this.

2. The 2D solver removes 75% of the adiabatic drift (reducing +0.40
   to +0.10 at l=2->3), but a residual +0.10-0.20 drift remains that
   is NOT from:
   - The adiabatic approximation (removed by 2D solver)
   - PK channel coupling (removed by l-dependent PK)
   - PK form/parameters (tested extensively in Tracks AD, Q, etc.)
   - Cusp correction (sub-mHa, no R_eq effect)

3. The remaining drift source is the composed-geometry approximation
   itself: the core/valence separation with Z_A_eff = Z_A - n_core.
   As l_max increases, valence electrons probe regions where the
   core screening approximation breaks down. This is intrinsic to
   the composed architecture.

4. The accelerating drift (l=3->4 is +0.200 vs l=2->3 is +0.100)
   suggests the problem worsens at higher l_max. The even-odd
   staircase from Track AC may contribute (l_max=4 is even, gaining
   more from directly-coupled channels than odd l_max=3).

5. The Morse D_e at l_max=3 (grid: 0.094 Ha) is coincidentally close
   to experiment (0.092 Ha), but this is NOT meaningful because R_eq
   is 9.5% wrong and the dissociation limit is unconverged.
