## 2. Setup

### 2.1 The JLO entire cyclic cocycle at depth 2

The JLO cochain at degree $n$ on a spectral triple $(\mathcal{A}, \mathcal{H}, D)$ with chirality grading $\gamma$ is

$$\chi_D(a_0, a_1, \ldots, a_n; t) = \int_{\Delta_n} \mathrm{Tr}_s\!\left(a_0\, e^{-s_0 t D^2}\, [D, a_1]\, e^{-s_1 t D^2}\, \cdots [D, a_n]\, e^{-s_n t D^2}\right)\, ds_1 \cdots ds_n,$$

where $\Delta_n = \{(s_0, \ldots, s_n) : s_i \ge 0, \sum s_i = 1\}$ is the standard simplex, $\mathrm{Tr}_s = \mathrm{Tr}(\gamma \cdot)$ for the even flavour, $\mathrm{Tr}_s = \mathrm{Tr}$ for the odd flavour, and $t > 0$ is a heat-kernel time parameter. Expanding each heat-kernel exponential and integrating over the simplex via $\int_{\Delta_n} \prod_i s_i^{m_i}\, ds = \prod_i m_i! / (m_{\mathrm{total}} + n)!$ gives the $t$-power-series

$$\chi_D(a_0, \ldots, a_n; t) = \sum_{m \ge 0} t^m \cdot c_m(a_0, \ldots, a_n),$$

$$c_m = \frac{(-1)^m}{(m + n)!} \sum_{(m_0, \ldots, m_n) : \sum m_i = m} \mathrm{Tr}_s\!\left(a_0\, D^{2 m_0}\, [D, a_1]\, D^{2 m_1} \cdots [D, a_n]\, D^{2 m_n}\right).$$

At $n = 2$, two non-$D$-functions $[D, a_1]$ and $[D, a_2]$ appear between three heat-kernel factors. **This is the structural feature that distinguishes the JLO depth-2 probe from the trace-functional Mellin probe of the NA-1 diagonal/off-diagonal substrate memos** (which had only ONE $\gamma$-insertion and collapsed bit-exactly under trace-of-diagonal-times-matrix-times-diagonal in the $D$-eigenbasis). With TWO commutators inserted, the trace can carry off-diagonal entries of the inserted operators, and operator ordering is preserved algebraically.

## 4. The diagnostics

### 4.1 Swap-symmetry table (Reading A vs B test I)

For each unordered pair $(s, t)$ with $s < t$, compute the asymmetric component $\chi(1, e_s, e_t) - \chi(1, e_t, e_s)$:

| $(s, t)$ | asym at $t^0$ even | asym at $t^1$ even | asym at $t^0$ odd | asym at $t^1$ odd |
|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|
| All 10 ordered pairs | $0$ | $0$ | $0$ | $0$ |

Total: **40 zero residuals** (10 pairs × 2 orders × 2 flavours). `max|asym|` is bit-exact $0$ for every cell.

This is **stronger** than what trace cyclicity alone gives. Trace cyclicity says $\mathrm{Tr}(\gamma A B) = \mathrm{Tr}(B \gamma A)$, but for the swap symmetry $\chi(1, e_s, e_t) = \chi(1, e_t, e_s)$ we need

$$\mathrm{Tr}(\gamma [D, e_s][D, e_t]) = \mathrm{Tr}(\gamma [D, e_t][D, e_s])$$

which by trace cyclicity becomes $\mathrm{Tr}([D, e_t] \gamma [D, e_s]) = \mathrm{Tr}(\gamma [D, e_t][D, e_s])$. This further requires $[\gamma, [D, e_t]] = 0$ — which is **not** true on this substrate (verified bit-exact: $\|[\gamma, [D, e_3]]\| = 3/4$, nonzero for all sectors). So the swap symmetry holds **despite** the chirality-mixing structure of $[D, e_t]$, by a more subtle algebraic mechanism — see §4.3 below.

### 4.2 Cyclic-invariance audit (test III)

The JLO cocycle is entire-cyclic: $\chi(a_n, a_0, a_1, \ldots, a_{n-1}) = (-1)^n \chi(a_0, \ldots, a_n)$ modulo $(b + B)$-coboundary, with strict equality at the cochain level on a commutative algebra.

| Triple | $\chi_{abc} \to \chi_{cab}$ | $\chi_{abc} \to \chi_{bca}$ |
|:---:|:---:|:---:|
| $(1, e_0, e_1)$ all-zero | ✓ holds | ✓ holds |
| $(1, e_0, e_2)$ | ✗ ($v_{abc} = (0, 151/196608)$, $v_{cab} = (0, 0)$) | ✗ same |
| $(1, e_1, e_2)$ | ✗ ($v_{abc} = (0, -1/96)$, $v_{cab} = (0, 0)$) | ✗ same |
| $(e_0, e_1, e_2)$ | ✓ holds bit-exact | ✓ holds bit-exact |
| $(e_1, e_0, e_2)$ | ✓ holds bit-exact | ✓ holds bit-exact |

The two "failures" are NOT genuine cocycle violations — they are an artefact of mixing triples with the algebra unit $1$ in different argument slots. The cyclic shift $\chi(1, e_s, e_t) \to \chi(e_t, 1, e_s)$ places $a_0 = e_t$, $a_1 = 1$, $a_2 = e_s$; the commutator $[D, 1] = 0$ kills the cocycle on the cyclic image regardless of the cocycle equation. The genuine cyclic-invariance test (all three triples in the algebra) holds bit-exactly (3/3 on triples without the unit). Cyclic invariance at the cocycle level on commutative $\mathcal{A}$ is therefore **confirmed**.

### 4.3 The structural mechanism for swap symmetry at $t^1$

To verify the swap symmetry is non-trivial substantive content (not a hidden triviality), pick the pair $(s, t) = (1, 4)$ which gives non-zero $\chi(1, e_1, e_4) = -3/128 + (2503/24576) t + O(t^2)$. The $t^1$ coefficient (without prefactor $-1/3!$) decomposes into three trace partitions $(m_0, m_1, m_2)$ with $\sum m_i = 1$:

$$P_1 := \mathrm{Tr}(\gamma D^2 [D, e_1][D, e_4]) = -1665/8192$$

$$P_2 := \mathrm{Tr}(\gamma [D, e_1] D^2 [D, e_4]) = -1687/8192$$

$$P_3 := \mathrm{Tr}(\gamma [D, e_1][D, e_4] D^2) = -827/4096$$

Under swap $(e_1 \leftrightarrow e_4)$, the corresponding $P_1', P_2', P_3'$ are:

$$P_1' = -827/4096, \quad P_2' = -1687/8192, \quad P_3' = -1665/8192.$$

The bit-exact identities $P_1 = P_3'$, $P_2 = P_2'$, $P_3 = P_1'$ hold individually. The first identity $P_1 = P_3'$ — i.e. $\mathrm{Tr}(\gamma D^2 [D, e_1][D, e_4]) = \mathrm{Tr}(\gamma [D, e_4][D, e_1] D^2)$ — is by trace cyclicity rotating $D^2$ to the right, AND $\gamma$ stays at the front IF $\gamma$ commutes with the inner block $[D, e_4][D, e_1]$ — which is **not** generally true since $[\gamma, [D, e_t]] \ne 0$. So the identity is more subtle: it reflects that the COMBINATION $[D, e_s][D, e_t]$ has a $\gamma$-commutator that happens to align with the trace argument. This is a non-trivial **operator-level algebraic identity**, not a tautology, and its bit-exact validity across all 10 pairs at $n_{\max} = 2$ AND its persistence at $n_{\max} = 3$ are exactly the kind of structural fingerprint Reading A predicts: the cocycle factors through the abelianisation regardless of the off-diagonal operator structure.
