## §1. Modular reframing of bound-state QED — the explicit tuple

Paper 36's bound-state QED architecture (LS-1..LS-7 closed at sub-percent on hydrogen Lamb shift, see `papers/group5_qed_gauge/paper_36_bound_state_qed.tex` and `debug/ls{1..7}_*_memo.md`) computes the Bethe logarithm $\ln k_0(n\ell)$ as a ratio $J/I$ of two spectral sums (velocity form) or via acceleration form $J_a/I_a$ with $[\mathbf{p}, H_0] = -i\nabla V$ substitution. The Sturmian basis at $\lambda = Z/n_*$ provides a discrete completeness on $L^2(\R^3, d^3r)$ that turns the continuum spectral sum into a finite sum over Sturmian quantum numbers.

The natural *modular* (algebra, module, Dirac, connection, module-Lipschitz) tuple for this construction:

| Slot | Concrete object | Role |
|:-----|:----------------|:-----|
| Algebra $\Acal$ | $C_0(\R^3)$ — multiplication operators on $L^2(\R^3)$ (no spinor structure on the algebra side; spinors live on the module) | Smooth bounded coordinate functions $f$ acting on amplitudes |
| Module $\Mcal$ | $L^2(\R^3) \otimes \C^4$ (Dirac spinor bundle over $\R^3$) with its Sturmian-basis decomposition $\Mcal = \overline{\bigoplus_{(n, \ell, m)} \C \cdot s_{n\ell m}^\lambda}$ at exponent $\lambda = Z/n_*$ | Bound-state amplitudes + continuum spinor amplitudes; the *primary* object Paper 36 computes on |
| Dirac operator $D$ | $D_{\mathrm{Coulomb}} = -i \alpha \cdot \nabla - (Z\alpha/r) \beta + m \beta$ acting on $\Mcal$ | The single-particle Coulomb-Dirac operator with bound-state spectrum |
| Connection $\nabla$ | Levi-Civita on $\R^3$ + spin connection on $\C^4$ + Coulomb gauge connection $A_\mu = (Z/r, 0)$ pulled into the module | The module's natural connection — used to define the module-Lipschitz seminorm |
| Module-Lipschitz $L_M$ | $L_M(\xi) := \|\nabla \xi\|_{\Mcal}$ for $\xi \in \Mcal$ with $\xi$ smooth + compactly supported, or equivalently $L_M(a) = \sup_{\xi : \|\xi\| \le 1} \|[\nabla, a] \xi\|$ for $a \in \Acal$ acting on $\Mcal$ | The module-side Lipschitz seminorm in Latrémolière 1608.04881 §3 / 1803.06601 Def 2.X language — distinct from the algebraic $L_A(f) = \|[D, M_f]\|$ |

**Why this is structurally cleaner than Z's spectral-algebra reading.** Z's reading bundled Sturmian projectors into the algebra ($\Acal \supseteq$ projectors) — which made the Latrémolière Def 1.29 axioms (ii) and (iii) trivially saturate at finite $N_{\max}$. Z's pinned-QLCMS algebra is artificial; the Sturmian projectors are not coordinate functions, they are *module-side cutoffs*.

In the modular reading, the Sturmian basis is a module basis (a fixed Hilbert-module structure on $\Mcal$), and the truncation $P_{N_{\max}}^{\mathrm{Sturm}}$ is a *module truncation*: $\Mcal_{N_{\max}} := P_{N_{\max}}^{\mathrm{Sturm}} \cdot \Mcal$. The algebra $\Acal = C_0(\R^3)$ is unchanged at every $N_{\max}$. This matches the structural intent of Paper 36: Sturmian convergence is convergence of *the amplitude representation*, not of *the coordinate algebra*.

**Honest scope.** This tuple is the *natural* modular tuple suggested by Paper 36's architecture, but I have not verified that it satisfies Latrémolière 1608.04881's full axiom set (D-norm conditions, connection compatibility, Leibniz on the bimodule structure). The structural identification is at the level of "this is the right slot to compare against," not at the level of "all axioms verified."

## §2. Sturmian basis as module basis (not algebra-generating sequence)

The Coulomb-Sturmian basis at $\lambda = Z/n_*$ has the saturating property:
- At $\lambda = Z/n_*$ with $n_* = n_{\mathrm{state}}$, the bound state $\psi_{n_*, \ell, m}$ is *exactly* the lowest-$n$ Sturmian at the matched $\ell, m$ (verified in `geovac/shibuya_wulfman.py` line 335 onward).
- The Sturmian basis $\{s_{n\ell m}^\lambda\}_{n \ge \ell+1}$ is complete on $L^2(\R^3, d^3r)$ via the Shibuya-Wulfman discrete completeness identity (it is a *generalized Fourier basis* with respect to the Sturmian weight, NOT the standard $L^2$ basis).
- Critically: the completeness identity is $\sum_{n\ell m} (1/r) |s_{n\ell m}\rangle \langle s_{n\ell m}| = I_{L^2}$, not $\sum |s\rangle\langle s| = I$. The factor $1/r$ is what makes Sturmians a non-orthogonal-on-$L^2$ basis that is orthogonal on $L^2(\R^3, r\, dr)$.

This pattern is *structurally* identical to the way Heisenberg modules over quantum 2-tori are built in Latrémolière 1803.06601 (arXiv): the module has a non-trivial inner product and connection, and the "basis" is a generating sequence of the module *as a Hilbert module*, not a basis of the underlying $L^2$.

**Verification.** The Sturmian basis IS a Hilbert module basis on $(\Mcal, \langle \cdot, \cdot \rangle_{\mathrm{Sturm}})$ where the Sturmian inner product is $\langle \xi, \eta \rangle_{\mathrm{Sturm}} := \int \overline{\xi}(r) \eta(r) (1/r) \, d^3r$. Under this inner product, the Sturmian basis is orthonormal:

$$\langle s_{n\ell m}^\lambda, s_{n'\ell'm'}^\lambda \rangle_{\mathrm{Sturm}} = \delta_{nn'} \delta_{\ell\ell'} \delta_{mm'}.$$

The truncation $P_{N_{\max}}^{\mathrm{Sturm}}$ is a Hilbert-module projector (idempotent, self-adjoint with respect to the Sturmian inner product) — this is the structurally correct truncation slot.

**Implication for Z's spectral-algebra reading.** Z's reading had $\mu(P_{N_{\max}}) = 1$ saturating trivially because the pin state $\psi_{n_*\ell m}$ is the first Sturmian basis function. In the modular reading, this saturation is *structurally expected* (the pin state is the first module basis element), and we no longer need to interpret the saturation as a triviality — it is the natural consequence of the module's basis structure. The modular framework absorbs Z's "saturating axioms" into a clean structural feature.

## §3. Modular convergence rate — what Latrémolière 1608.04881 / 1803.06601 give

**The published convergence theorems.** Latrémolière's modular propinquity (Latrémolière 1608.04881, "The Modular Gromov-Hausdorff Propinquity"; Latrémolière 1803.06601 with companion 1811.04534 on completeness) establishes:

1. **Heisenberg modules over quantum 2-tori form a continuous family** in the modular propinquity (Latrémolière 1803.06601 main theorem). The convergence is *existence of a limit*, not a rate.

2. **The dual modular propinquity is complete** (Latrémolière 1811.04534) — sequences of modules satisfying the propinquity Cauchy property converge to a limit module.

3. **D-norms generalize connection-induced norms** — for a smooth connection $\nabla$ on a Hermitian module $\Mcal$, the D-norm $D_\Mcal(\xi) := \max(\|\xi\|_\Mcal, \|\nabla \xi\|_\Mcal)$ is the canonical Lipschitz seminorm on the module side.

**What's missing for our case.** No published Latrémolière theorem provides:
- A convergence rate for module truncations of the form $\Mcal_{N_\max} = P_{N_\max}^{\mathrm{Sturm}} \Mcal$ on *non-compact* base spaces ($\R^3$ is non-compact).
- A rate analog of Paper 38's $4/\pi \log n/n$ for Sturmian or generalized-Fourier bases on non-compact spaces.

**The Heisenberg module case is structurally different.** Heisenberg modules in Latrémolière 1803.06601 are modules over quantum *2-tori* — compact noncommutative spaces with finite Haar measure. The Plancherel-weighted basis on the quantum 2-torus inherits Paper 38-class rates (compact-group machinery). Sturmians on $\R^3$ do not inherit this — they are modules over the *non-compact* algebra $C_0(\R^3)$.

**Honest verdict on rates.** Latrémolière's modular framework does NOT provide a closed-form convergence rate for the Sturmian truncation. The framework gives:
- Existence of a propinquity limit (the Sturmian module $\Mcal_{N_\max}$ converges to the full Coulomb-Dirac module $\Mcal$ as $N_\max \to \infty$).
- A Lipschitz-bound error model on Lipschitz observables (analogous to Z's local metametric, but at the module level).

The framework does NOT predict the empirical $1/N^4$ rate of Paper 36's LS-3 acceleration form (1S: 8.62%/3.13%/0.60% at N=12/16/20). The rate remains empirical.

**Mechanism of the empirical $1/N^4$ rate.** This is set by the *radial completeness* of the Sturmian basis at the matched exponent. For analytic observables on the bound-state subspace, Sturmian convergence is exponential in the basis size; for non-analytic observables (e.g., the Bethe-log spectral sum, which probes the continuum), convergence is polynomial — specifically $1/N^p$ with $p$ set by the smoothness of the continuum density of states near the bound state. For hydrogen 1S in the acceleration form, $p = 4$. This is a *radial-completeness rate*, set by the basis quality, not by a Plancherel-weighted compactness argument.

## §4. Why Paper 38's $4/\pi$ does NOT transport — modular reading

Paper 38's $4/\pi$ asymptote comes from a *Plancherel-weighted central Fejér kernel* on $SU(2)$ (Paper 38 §IV, see `papers/group1_operator_algebras/paper_38_su2_propinquity_convergence.tex` lines 215, 1304-1310). The structural mechanism is:

1. Peter-Weyl decomposition of $L^2(SU(2)) = \bigoplus_{j} V_j \otimes V_j^*$ with $\dim V_j = 2j+1$.
2. Plancherel weight $\sqrt{2j+1}$ on each summand makes the truncated Fejér kernel Cesàro-2-class (not Cesàro-1), producing $\log n/n$ rate (not $1/n$).
3. Hopf-base measure normalization $\Vol(S^2)/\pi^2 = 4/\pi$ on the $SU(2)/U(1)$ Haar quotient gives the constant $4/\pi$.

**The modular analog.** A module-side Plancherel-weighted truncation would look like:

$$P_{N_\max}^{\mathrm{Plancherel}} := \sum_{j \le j_\max} \sqrt{\dim V_j} \cdot \pi_j$$

where $\pi_j$ is the projector onto the $j$-th Peter-Weyl summand. This DOES exist for compact-Lie-group modules and DOES give $4/\pi$ rates (Paper 40 universal). For non-compact modules over $C_0(\R^3)$, the analog Plancherel decomposition is:

$$L^2(\R^3) = \int^\oplus_{k \in \R^3} \C \, dk \quad \text{(continuous Fourier)}$$

with Plancherel weight $1$ (Lebesgue measure on $\R^3$). There is NO discrete Peter-Weyl-style summand structure, hence NO discrete $\sqrt{\dim V_j}$ weight, hence NO Cesàro-2 mechanism, hence NO $\log n/n$ rate.

**The Sturmian basis is a *different* basis from Plancherel.** Sturmians at $\lambda = Z/n_*$ are NOT the Plancherel basis of $L^2(\R^3)$ — they are a *bound-state-adapted* basis that exists because the Coulomb potential has SO(4) hidden symmetry (Paper 7 / Fock projection). The Sturmian basis is the module-side analog of Peter-Weyl for the *hidden* $SO(4)$ symmetry, not for the explicit non-compact translation symmetry.

**Structural reading.** Paper 38's $4/\pi$ rate uses the *Plancherel weighting on the compact group $SU(2)$*. In the bound-state Coulomb problem, the relevant compact group is $SO(4) \cong SU(2) \times SU(2) / \Z_2$ acting on the *bound-state* subspace, but:

(a) The Sturmian basis decomposes the bound-state $SO(4)$ representation as $\bigoplus_{n} V_n$ with $\dim V_n = n^2$ (the principal series of bound states). This IS Peter-Weyl for $SO(4)$.

(b) However, the Bethe log spectral sum probes *not just bound states but the full continuum* — and the continuum is NOT in any $SO(4)$ representation, it is in the continuous spectrum. The Sturmian basis at fixed $\lambda$ provides a discrete completeness on $L^2(\R^3)$ that includes representations of the continuum, but these are NOT Peter-Weyl summands of a compact group.

**Net structural reason in modular language.** The $4/\pi$ does not transport because **the Sturmian module is a non-compact extension of the Peter-Weyl decomposition** — it includes both the bound-state $SO(4)$-Peter-Weyl content AND the continuum spectral content, and the Plancherel weight that gives $4/\pi$ on the compact piece is diluted to weight $1$ on the continuum piece. The resulting rate is set by whichever of the two pieces dominates: for the Bethe log evaluation, the *continuum piece dominates* (the spectral sum probes high-energy pseudostates), and that piece is unweighted, hence Cesàro-1, hence $1/N$ at best — but actually it's $1/N^4$ because of additional smoothness in the radial integral.

The cleanest way to say this: **the master Mellin engine's M1 mechanism (Sprint TS-E1 case-exhaustion theorem) requires a Hopf-base / compact-Lie-quotient structure. The Sturmian truncation in $L^2(\R^3)$ doesn't have an M1 Hopf-base — it sits at a *different* mechanism layer (radial-completeness on $\R^+$, which is non-Hopf).**

This is genuinely new structural content: it identifies where in the master Mellin engine taxonomy the Sturmian rate lives (NOT in M1), and explains why Paper 40's universality (all compact connected Lie groups) doesn't reach Sturmians.

## §5. LS-7/LS-8a residual decomposition in bimodule vocabulary

Paper 36 §VII attributes the −0.534% Lamb shift residual to two pieces (`debug/ls7_two_loop_se_memo.md`, `debug/ls8a_two_loop_renormalization_gap_memo.md`):
1. **Multi-loop QED contribution** ≈ +1.20 MHz from Eides Tab. 7.3 (LS-8a renormalization wall).
2. **Non-loop physics** ≈ +4.4 MHz, decomposed as recoil (−2.40 MHz, Bodwin-Yennie) + finite nuclear size (+1.18 MHz) + hyperfine averaging (~+5.0 MHz).

Z's pinned-QLCMS reading lumped both pieces as "operator-system content beyond the pin-state local metametric." The modular framework lets us split this cleanly.

**Reading the non-loop physics as bimodule cross-shift content.**

The recoil correction in Paper 36 / Sprint MH is, in production code, the cross-register $V_{eN}(\hat{r}_e, \hat{R}_n)$ operator (`geovac/cross_register_vne.py`, May 2026). This operator does NOT live in $\Mcal_{\mathrm{electron}}$ alone — it lives on the tensor product $\Mcal_{\mathrm{electron}} \otimes \Mcal_{\mathrm{nucleus}}$ as a *bimodule shift*:

$$V_{eN}: \Mcal_e \otimes \Mcal_n \to \Mcal_e \otimes \Mcal_n, \quad V_{eN}(\xi_e \otimes \xi_n) = \sum_{a,b} c_{ab} \cdot (a \cdot \xi_e) \otimes (b \cdot \xi_n)$$

where $a \in \Acal_e, b \in \Acal_n$ are coordinate-multiplication operators (the multipole expansion). This is *exactly* what Latrémolière's modular framework calls a **bimodule cross-shift** — a coupling between two modules over (possibly distinct) algebras, mediated by their coordinate algebras.

Similarly:
- **Finite nuclear size** (Foldy-Friar $\langle r^2 \rangle_E$, Paper 34 §III.17) is a bimodule shift between the electron module and a nuclear charge-density module — the nucleus is no longer a point in $\Acal_n$, it has an extended charge distribution, which is a bimodule deformation.
- **Hyperfine averaging** (Zemach radius $r_Z$, Paper 34 §III.18) is a bimodule shift between the electron module and a nuclear magnetization-density module.

**Net reading.** The +4.4 MHz "non-loop physics" is **bimodule cross-shift content between the electron module and three distinct nuclear-structure modules** (charge density, magnetization, recoil). This is a sharpening of the structural-skeleton-scope statement: GeoVac handles bimodule cross-shifts via the cross-register $V_{eN}$ operator and §III.17/18/19 Layer-2 inputs.

**Reading the LS-8a counterterms as module endomorphisms.**

The LS-8a renormalization counterterms $Z_2 - 1$ and $\delta m$ in Paper 36 / LS-8a memo are NOT bimodule shifts. They are *self-interactions* of the electron module:
- $Z_2 - 1$ rescales the electron wavefunction normalization — this is a module endomorphism (a unitary on $\Mcal_e$ at finite order in $\alpha$, but a non-trivial action on the *bare* module data).
- $\delta m$ shifts the electron mass — this is a deformation of the Dirac operator $D$, which in modular language is a deformation of the connection $\nabla$ that defines the module-Lipschitz seminorm $L_M$.

Neither is a shift between two modules; both are operations on the *single* electron module that are NOT generated by the module's coordinate algebra $\Acal_e = C_0(\R^3)$. In Latrémolière 1608.04881 vocabulary: these are operations on the *D-norm structure of the module itself*, not on the algebra-module action.

**The key distinction.** Bimodule cross-shifts compose modules; module endomorphisms deform a single module's D-norm. The modular propinquity bookkeeping handles the *cross-shift* case (this is what the propinquity *measures* — distance between modules with potentially different bimodule cross-shifts), but does NOT generate the *endomorphism* case (deforming D-norms is a renormalization operation, outside the propinquity's scope).

**Sharpened statement of the multi-focal-composition wall.** From CLAUDE.md §1.7 multi-focal-composition wall pattern ("framework couples discrete labels cleanly, doesn't compose multiple Fock-style projections"), this sprint refines:

- **Bimodule cross-shifts are handled** by the cross-register $V_{eN}$ operator + §III.17/18/19 multipole expansion (Paper 34 framework). Five Layer-2 walls (HF-3 recoil, HF-4 Zemach, charge-density, FNS, hyperfine averaging) ARE bimodule cross-shifts and the framework explicitly composes them in production code (Sprint MH, multi-focal sprint Phase C). Verdict: framework handles bimodule structure.
- **Module endomorphisms (renormalization counterterms) are NOT handled.** The LS-8a wall and Sprint H1 Yukawa-undetermined verdict and the inner-factor calibration data (Paper 18 §IV.6) are all *module endomorphism content* — they deform the D-norm structure / Dirac operator / module data in ways that are not bimodule cross-shifts. Latrémolière's modular propinquity does not generate these from the bimodule structure.

This is the genuinely new content from the modular reframing: it splits the multi-focal-composition wall into two pieces that GeoVac handles differently. Bimodule cross-shifts are the *operational* content of the multi-focal architecture; module endomorphisms are the renormalization wall.

## §6. Verdict

**Does modular framework give a SPECIFIC convergence rate for non-compact Sturmian-FCI?**

NO. Latrémolière 1608.04881 / 1803.06601 / 1811.04534 give existence-of-convergence theorems on Heisenberg modules over compact quantum 2-tori; they do not provide a Sturmian-specific rate on non-compact $L^2(\R^3)$. The empirical $1/N^4$ rate in Paper 36 LS-3 is set by radial completeness of the Sturmian basis at matched exponent, NOT by a Plancherel-weighted compactness mechanism. The framework gives Paper 36 the *vocabulary* (existence of a module-propinquity limit), but the explicit rate remains empirical.

This is a *negative result* in the sense that it does not close the rate question, but a *positive result* in that the modular framework correctly identifies *why* Paper 38's $4/\pi$ does not transport: the master Mellin engine M1 Hopf-base mechanism requires a compact-Lie-quotient measure that Sturmians on $\R^3$ do not have.

**Does the LS-7/LS-8a residual decomposition get sharper (bimodule cross-shift vs module endomorphism)?**

YES. This is the substantive new content of Track M-Z. The Paper 36 §VII non-loop physics (~+4.4 MHz) is identified as **bimodule cross-shift content** (cross-register $V_{eN}$, FNS, hyperfine averaging — all are bimodule deformations between electron module and nuclear-structure modules, handled by the production code). The LS-8a renormalization counterterms ($Z_2$, $\delta m$, ~+1.20 MHz multi-loop QED) are identified as **module endomorphisms** (self-deformations of the electron module's D-norm structure, NOT generated by bimodule cross-shifts).

This sharpens the multi-focal-composition wall from CLAUDE.md §1.7 ("framework couples discrete labels cleanly, doesn't compose multiple Fock-style projections") into a cleaner two-piece statement:

- **Bimodule cross-shifts are handled** — five Layer-2 walls (HF-3 recoil, HF-4 Zemach, FNS, hyperfine averaging, recoil-mixing) are all bimodule cross-shifts and the framework composes them in production via cross-register $V_{eN}$ + §III.17/18/19 multipole expansion.
- **Module endomorphisms are NOT handled** — LS-8a renormalization counterterms, Sprint H1 Yukawa selection, and inner-factor calibration (Paper 18 §IV.6) are all module-endomorphism content that the modular framework cannot generate from the bimodule structure.

**Is there a Paper 36 §IX or §X edit that should be applied to capture the modular reframing?**

YES, but a *conservative* one. The framing change is at the level of "the Lamb shift residual decomposition has a modular-bimodule reading" — a vocabulary refinement of Paper 36 §VII that does NOT close any wall and does NOT change any numerical claim. The recommended edit is a brief subsection (or remark within §VII or §VIII) stating:

The Latrémolière modular propinquity (arXiv:1608.04881, 1803.06601) provides a vocabulary for the bimodule-cross-shift content but not for the module-endomorphism content; the LS-8a renormalization wall is therefore consistent with the standing structural-skeleton-scope statement.
