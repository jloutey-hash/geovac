## Argument

### Step 1: Naming the ambient motive $M^{\mathrm{GV}}$

The Deligne–Glanois category $\mathrm{MT}(\mathbb{Z}[i, 1/2], 4)$ is
the Tannakian category of mixed Tate motives over the ring of
$S$-integers $\mathcal{O}_4[1/4] = \mathbb{Z}[i, 1/2]$ generated by
motivic iterated integrals on
$\mathbb{P}^1 \setminus \{0, 1, i, -1, -i, \infty\}$. By Deligne
2010 and Glanois 2015 (Cor. 1.1–1.2), the period map
$\mathrm{per}: \mathcal{H}^4 \to \mathcal{Z}^4$ is an isomorphism,
and the explicit basis $\mathcal{B}^4$ is given.

Let $M^{\mathrm{GV}} \subset \mathrm{MT}(\mathbb{Z}[i, 1/2], 4)$ be the
**Tannakian sub-category** generated by the following objects:

- $\mathbb{Q}(0), \mathbb{Q}(2), \mathbb{Q}(4), \ldots, \mathbb{Q}(2k_{\max})$
  — pure Tate twists at even weight. (M2 source.)
- $\log 2 \in \mathcal{B}^2 \subset \mathcal{B}^4$ — depth-1 weight-1
  generator. (M3 un-restricted source at even $s$ via Brown 2012
  embedding.)
- $\zeta^{\mathfrak{m}}(2n+1)$ for $n \ge 1$ — odd zetas at level 1.
  (M3 un-restricted source at odd $s$.)
- $\zeta^{\mathfrak{m}}\!\bigl(\!\begin{smallmatrix} s \\
  i \end{smallmatrix}\!\bigr)$, $s \ge 2$ — depth-1 level-4 motivic
  iterated integrals with $\epsilon = i$ corresponding to $\beta(s)$
  and Catalan $G$. (M3 vertex-restricted source.)
- Depth-$\ge 2$ generators (for the depth-$k$ tower, $k \ge 2$).
  This includes $S_{\min}$'s predicted motivic home in
  $\mathrm{MT}(\mathbb{Z}[1/2])$ at depth 2 (the natural prediction
  from Sprint M3 cyclotomic memo §depth-$k$ tower).

This sub-category is well-defined: it is the Tannakian closure (under
tensor products, duals, sub-objects, and quotients) of the named
generators inside the ambient $\mathrm{MT}(\mathbb{Z}[i, 1/2], 4)$.
The fiber functor $\omega_{M^{\mathrm{GV}}}: M^{\mathrm{GV}} \to
\mathrm{Vec}_\mathbb{Q}$ inherits from the standard Betti realization
on the ambient category.

### Step 3: Galois action correspondence

The motivic Galois group $G^{\mathrm{GV}} := \mathrm{Aut}^\otimes(\omega
_{M^{\mathrm{GV}}})$ sits inside $G^{\mathrm{MT}(\mathbb{Z}[i, 1/2], 4)}$
and has the structure (recall Q5'-Round-1 Tannakian obstruction memo):

- A pro-unipotent radical acting on the mixed-Tate piece (depth $\ge 1$
  generators), trivial on the pure-Tate piece.
- A reductive quotient containing $\mathbb{G}_m$ (Tate scaling) and
  $\mathrm{Gal}(\mathbb{Q}(i)/\mathbb{Q}) = \mathbb{Z}/2$ (complex
  conjugation acting on level-4 cyclotomic generators).

The $\mathbb{Z}/2$ acts:

- **trivially on M2 outputs** ($\bigoplus_k \pi^{2k}\mathbb{Q} \subset
  \mathbb{Q}$ via $\pi^{2k} \in \mathbb{Q}$-rational Galois-fixed
  sub-ring) — exactly what Sprint A7 observed for M2's $\mathbb{Q}(i)$
  in the Witt-splitting role,
- **non-trivially on M3 outputs** at the level-4 cyclotomic generators
  ($\beta(s) \mapsto -\beta(s)$ at odd $s$ via $\chi_{-4}(-1) = -1$) —
  exactly what Sprint A7 observed for M3's $\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_4)$ in
  the cyclotomic-conductor role.

These two actions ARE the same $\mathbb{Z}/2$ from the ambient category's
perspective. The trivial-vs-non-trivial pattern is the standard split
of a Galois action on a category containing both Galois-fixed and
Galois-non-fixed sub-categories. Q5'-Round-1's Tannakian obstruction
probe already confirmed this is not an obstruction; the present sprint
sharpens it to a positive correspondence statement.

### Step 5: What $M^{\mathrm{GV}}$ does NOT know about GeoVac

The pair $(M^{\mathrm{GV}}, \omega_{M^{\mathrm{GV}}})$ does NOT know:

(a) **Which GeoVac observable produced which period.** $M^{\mathrm{GV}}$
    sees $\pi^{2k}$ as an element of $\mathbb{Q}(2k)$;\ it does not see
    that $\pi^{2k}$ is the $a_k^\Delta$ Seeley–DeWitt coefficient on
    the discrete CH $S^3$ scalar Laplacian. The period value is shared
    by infinitely many algebraic-geometric integral representations;\
    GeoVac's representation is one of them.

(b) **Which Mellin slot $k \in \{0, 1, 2\}$ produced which period.**
    This is the master Mellin engine slot index. From the period
    value's perspective ($\pi^{2k}$ qua element of $\mathbb{Q}(2k)$),
    M1 (Hopf-base-measure, $k = 0$ slot of $\mathcal{M}[\mathrm{Tr}(e^{-tD^2})]$),
    M2 (Seeley–DeWitt, $k = 2$ slot of $\mathcal{M}[\mathrm{Tr}(D^2 e^{-tD^2})]$),
    and M3 (vertex parity, $k = 1$ slot of $\mathcal{M}[\mathrm{Tr}(D \cdot e^{-tD^2})]$)
    are *indistinguishable*. The period value $\pi^2$ arises as
    $\mathrm{Vol}(S^3)$ (M1, weight $+2$), as $a_0^\Delta / 2$ (M2,
    weight $+2$), and as the leading $D(s)$ value at $s = 4$ (M3, weight
    $+2$). $M^{\mathrm{GV}}$ sees only the period $\pi^2 \in \mathbb{Q}(2)$,
    not its three different GeoVac-internal origins.

(c) **The spectral triple data $(\mathcal{A}_{\mathrm{GV}}, \mathcal{H}_{
    \mathrm{GV}}, D_{\mathrm{GV}})$ of Paper 32.** The motivic content
    of GeoVac periods (their motivic home, their Galois orbit) is
    independent of which spectral triple produces them.

(d) **The Camporesi–Higuchi half-integer shift $3/2$.** This is a
    feature of the operator $D_{\mathrm{GV}}$, not of the resulting
    periods.

(e) **The Berezin reconstruction maps $B_{n_{\max}}: C(S^3) \to \mathcal{O}_{n_{\max}}$
    (Paper 38 §L4) and the GH-convergence rate constant $4/\pi$
    (Paper 38 Thm L2 + Paper 40 Thm 1).** $4/\pi \in \mathbb{Q}[\pi,
    1/\pi]$ is in $M^{\mathrm{GV}}$ at Tate weight $-1$, but the fact
    that it is the propinquity convergence rate (M1's
    operator-algebraic signature) is invisible to $M^{\mathrm{GV}}$.

Items (a) and (b) are the load-bearing ones for Q5'. Items (c)–(e)
are downstream consequences:\ once we have the spectral triple as a
substrate that produces periods, we get the Mellin slot index, the
specific origin per period, etc.

### Step 6: The k-slot index as the substantive residue

Sprint MR-A (CLAUDE.md §1.7 WH1, 2026-05-06 evening) returned a
structural negative on engine-domain mixing of the master Mellin
engine:\ the natural Dirac analog of L2 central Fejér gave $\gamma_n^{
\mathrm{Dirac}} = \pi$ exactly at every $n_{\max}$, not the $4/\pi$
asymptote of the scalar L2 rate. The structural reading documented
there: the index $k \in \{0, 1, 2\}$ in $\mathcal{M}[\mathrm{Tr}(D^k e^{-tD^2})]$
classifies BOTH the M-mechanism AND the natural class of observable
from which its signature can be extracted. M1 (k=0) ↔ state-space
propinquity rates;\ M2 (k=2) ↔ heat-kernel/spectral-action;\ M3 (k=1)
↔ vertex-restricted parity-character observables. Mixing the index of
the question with the index of the mechanism produces structural
degeneracy.

The MR-A reading is now the operational form of the deflation residue:\
the k-slot index is **predictive within each domain** (predicts which
class of observable will extract which mechanism's signature), but is
**not extractable from the period value alone** (the period $\pi^2$
admits three distinct readings, one per k-slot, and the ambient motive
sees only the period, not the slot).

The k-slot index is therefore the irreducible GeoVac-side enrichment.
It is a piece of data that lives on the spectral-triple side, has no
analog in the ambient motivic category, and is what the master-Mellin-
engine partition of Paper 32 §VIII Theorem (case-exhaustion) IS.

This is exactly the kind of structure that would distinguish a
"GeoVac is a natural-geometry realization of $M^{\mathrm{GV}}$"
deflation reading from a "GeoVac is just $M^{\mathrm{GV}}$ in
disguise" full collapse:\ the deflation reading says the period
*values* live in $M^{\mathrm{GV}}$ (correct);\ the full collapse
reading would say there is no distinguishing GeoVac structure beyond
$M^{\mathrm{GV}}$ (false — the k-slot index distinguishes).

### Step 7: Strength of the deflation

How serious is the residue? Two reads:

**Mild residue reading (favors DEFLATES).** The k-slot index is
substrate-side metadata about a particular Mellin representation. Any
period of $\mathrm{MT}(\mathbb{Z}[i, 1/2], 4)$ admits many integral
representations;\ each representation carries representation-side
metadata that is not in the period itself. The Glanois basis writes
each period as a motivic iterated integral with explicit
$(x_1, \ldots, x_p;\ \epsilon_1, \ldots, \epsilon_p)$ indexing data;
this indexing is representation-specific and not invariant under
period-level equivalence. The GeoVac k-slot index is the analog of
this indexing on the spectral-triple side. Just as Glanois's
$(x_i, \epsilon_i)$ data is "extra" relative to the period value but
intrinsic to the Glanois category-internal structure, the GeoVac
k-slot index is "extra" relative to the period value but intrinsic
to the GeoVac spectral-triple category-internal structure. The
deflation is a strong correspondence at the *period* level, with
non-correspondence at the *representation* level on both sides.

**Strong residue reading (favors PARTIAL).** The k-slot index is not
just substrate metadata;\ it is a structural prediction (Sprint TS,
Paper 32 §VIII Theorem) that classifies which sub-mechanism produces
each transcendental, with falsifiable consequences (the case-exhaustion
theorem's claim that every $\pi$ in GeoVac comes from one of M1, M2,
M3). This is more than substrate book-keeping: it is the framework's
empirically validated prediction about which observables host which
periods. The ambient motive cannot reproduce this prediction because
the prediction is about *which spectral object* produces *which
period* — a substrate-side question that the period-value-only
ambient motive is blind to.

The two reads are not in contradiction;\ they emphasize different
aspects. The DEFLATES bias of the verdict reflects the mild reading
at the *period-value* level (the question Q5' was originally posed
at). The PARTIAL flag reflects the strong reading at the *substrate-
labelling* level (which is where the master Mellin engine lives).

### Step 9: What WOULD make Q5' NOT deflate

For completeness:\ what kind of structure would invalidate the
deflation? Several candidates considered, none of which materialise
in this sprint or in the antecedent A7/Q5'-Round-1 work:

(i) **A GeoVac period outside $\mathrm{MT}(\mathbb{Z}[i, 1/2], 4)$.**
    No instance: every empirical witness sits inside it (Sprint
    Mixed-Tate Test + Sprint M3 Cyclotomic Mixed-Tate). The depth-$k$
    tower predicts new periods at depth $k$, but the prediction is "new
    element of $\mathcal{H}^4$ at depth $k$", not "element outside
    the ambient category."

(ii) **A Galois orbit larger than the ambient motivic Galois orbit.**
     If a GeoVac period had a Galois orbit strictly larger than what
     $G^{\mathrm{MT}(\mathbb{Z}[i, 1/2], 4)}$ predicts, the framework
     would carry hidden motivic structure beyond the ambient category.
     No instance:\ all observed Galois orbits are within
     $G^{\mathrm{MT}(\mathbb{Z}[i, 1/2], 4)}$, including the trivial
     M2-action and the non-trivial M3-action both within
     $\mathrm{Gal}(\mathbb{Q}(i)/\mathbb{Q})$ (the standard reductive
     quotient).

(iii) **A k-slot index that is a categorical invariant of the period.**
      If the k-slot were a function of the period value alone (e.g., if
      every $\pi^{2k}$ came uniquely from k = 2 = M2, every $\beta(s)$
      uniquely from k = 1 = M3, etc.), then the k-slot would be derivable
      from $M^{\mathrm{GV}}$ and would deflate too. Sprint MR-A's
      finding rules this out:\ the period value $\pi^2$ legitimately
      arises from all three k-slots (Vol($S^3$) at k=0, $a_0^\Delta/2$
      at k=2, leading $D(s)$ at k=1 plus rationals), so the k-slot is
      not period-determined.

(iv) **A natural-isomorphism between $M^{\mathrm{GV}}$ and a GeoVac
     spectral-triple-specific motive that is NOT the identity.** If
     GeoVac admitted a *different* fiber functor than the standard
     Betti, with corresponding Galois group strictly different from
     $G^{\mathrm{GV}}$, the framework would carry distinguishing
     structure. No instance:\ the standard Hodge/Betti realization on
     $M^{\mathrm{GV}}$ matches GeoVac periods bit-exact;\ no alternative
     fiber functor surfaces.

The absence of (i)–(iv) is exactly the DEFLATES bias. Item (iii) is
the closest to an obstruction;\ MR-A's structural finding flips it
into the residue argument of Step 7 instead of an obstruction.
