**Date:** 2026-05-15 (Sprint 3, Paper 34 dictionary-completion effort)
**Status:** Diagnostic-only scoping. No §III draft, no production-code edits, no other paper touched.
**Verdict (preview):** **ABSORBED**, with a one-paragraph cross-reference recommendation. See §6.

## 1. Question

Should "loop expansion / α-power" be a standalone §III subsection in Paper 34?

The order-α-power sorting is **operationally** a projection: GeoVac's QED machinery treats individual orders α^n distinctly. Paper 36's LS-1..LS-7..LS-8a sprint sequence is, structurally, a loop-by-loop walk: zeroth-order Dirac → one-loop self-energy / vacuum polarization → two-loop self-energy. Different machinery activates at each order:

- α⁰ (Dirac kinematics): Camporesi-Higuchi spinor lift on S³.
- α¹·α (one-loop): CC spectral action heat-kernel, vector-photon promotion, Drake-Swainson regularization, Eides §3.2 SE bracket. Closes Lamb shift to sub-percent (Paper 36).
- α²·α (two-loop, finite part): iterated CC spectral action reproduces $(α/π)² (Zα)⁴/n³$ prefactor with correct sign. UV-divergent integrand structurally faithful.
- α²·α (two-loop, finite extraction): **requires Z₂ and δm counterterms not generated by bare CC spectral action** (LS-8a renormalization gap).

The structural test for own-entry vs absorbed is the standard Paper 34 protocol (§VII T3 / Sprint TX-A): does the candidate projection (a) introduce a variable, dimension, or transcendental not already accounted for by an existing entry, or (b) produce an empirical anchor not already attributable to an existing entry?

If neither (a) nor (b), the candidate is absorbed.

## 2. Comparison to §III.6 (Connes–Chamseddine spectral action)

§III.6 (`sec:proj_spectral_action`) is the source of α^n content at every loop order. The heat-kernel expansion

$$\mathrm{Tr}\, f(D^2/\Lambda^2) \;=\; \sum_k a_k \Lambda^{d-2k} f_k$$

is **already an α-graded object** by construction. One loop is one iteration of the spectral action; two loops is iterated CC (LS-7 / LS-8a sprint scope); three loops is the O(N³)-factorized chain topology of Paper 28 §three_loop. The Seeley-DeWitt coefficient ring (√π·ℚ at one loop, π²·ℚ at one-loop observable level via T9, generic π^{2k} at higher orders) is **the** α-power structure of GeoVac's QED.

In Paper 35 §VII.3 the "Refined Prediction 1" makes this explicit:

> The GeoVac framework controls the π content of the *finite parts* of any QED observable; UV divergences of multi-loop contributions are inherited from the underlying field theory and renormalized by counterterms that the framework does not autonomously generate.

This statement is **about α^n content at every n**, and it is stated *as a property of §III.6 (spectral action)*, not as an independent projection. The α-power index n labels which iteration of CC is being invoked; the spectral-action projection itself already covers every n by repetition.

An own-entry would need to add structural content not already in §III.6's transcendental signature line ("$\pi^{2k} \times \mathbb{Q}$ at one loop (T9 theorem, Paper 28)"). The only candidate addition would be the renormalization-gap scope boundary itself (§5 below). That gap is a **scope statement about §III.6**, not a separate projection mechanism.

**Verdict for §III.6 comparison:** spectral action already encodes α^n content via iteration. Loop expansion is not structurally distinct from "applying §III.6 n times."

## 3. Comparison to §III.16 (Two-body Dirac / Breit retardation)

§III.16 (`sec:proj_breit_retardation`) carries an explicit transcendental signature "$\alpha^4 \cdot \mathbb{Q}$" and is structurally located at a specific α-order. The Ps 1S-2S empirical anchor closes at α⁴; the Layer-2 Penin-Pivovarov input is at the same order.

This is exactly the kind of α-graded entry a "loop expansion" projection would also produce — except §III.16 is **not** the α-power projection. §III.16 names a *specific kinematic mechanism* (two-body retardation in the equal-mass limit where the 1/M expansion breaks down) that happens to surface at α⁴. The variable introduced is m_l/m_n (the ratio that doesn't vanish at equal mass), not the α-power n itself.

The structural insight from §III.16's "Structural reading" subsection is informative here:

> This is the structurally cleanest known instance of the multi-focal-composition wall named in CLAUDE.md §2. The wall's other five instances ... all surface in the multi-loop / renormalization sector at α⁵ or higher, where two-body-projection failure is entangled with renormalization failure. The Breit retardation content surfaces at α⁴ --- one full order before the LS-8a renormalization wall --- so the two-body-projection failure is isolated and visible without renormalization confounds.

This is α-power language used to **distinguish** mechanisms (Breit at α⁴ ≠ multi-loop at α⁵+), not to **define** a projection. The α-power is the diagnostic axis that locates §III.16 in the catalogue; it is not the projection §III.16 names.

A separate loop-expansion entry would not change §III.16's empirical anchor (Ps 1S-2S at α⁴) and would not provide structural content beyond what §III.16's "α⁴·ℚ" transcendental signature line already carries.

**Verdict for §III.16 comparison:** the α-graded transcendental signature already lives in the per-projection transcendental tag. §III.16 demonstrates that α-power is a **bookkeeping property of each entry**, not a separate entry.

## 4. Comparison to §III.26 (Gauge choice)

§III.26 (`sec:proj_gauge_choice`, added Sprint 2 today) explicitly tags the per-loop calibration $1/(4π) = \mathrm{Vol}(S²)/(4 \cdot 4\pi²)$ as the Coulomb-gauge intermediate transcendental content. This is a **per-loop** statement — every loop in a QED diagram contributes one factor of $1/(4π)$ from the vector-photon promotion structure under Coulomb-gauge fixing.

The per-loop calibration is therefore already encoded in two existing entries:

1. §III.11 (`sec:proj_vector_photon`): "$1/(4π)$ per loop, identified as the $S^2$ Weyl exchange constant of the Hopf base (Paper 33)."
2. §III.26 (`sec:proj_gauge_choice`): "the vector-photon construction's per-loop factor is $1/(4π) = \mathrm{Vol}(S^2)/(4 \cdot 4π^2)$ ... the intermediate transcendental content sits cleanly in the calibration π-tier of Paper 18."

A loop-expansion entry would either duplicate this or merely cite it. The α-power exponent is a **counter for how many times $1/(4π)$ enters**, not a separate projection that introduces $1/(4π)$. Counting iterations of §III.11 + §III.26 + §III.6 gives the α-power decomposition for free.

**Verdict for §III.26 comparison:** the gauge-choice projection already captures the per-loop calibration. The α-power exponent counts these per-loop factors; it does not produce new ones.

## 5. Renormalization-gap distinction

The hardest case for "absorbed" is the LS-8a renormalization gap. Refined Prediction 1 of Paper 35 §VII.3 makes a clean structural statement:

> the GeoVac framework controls the π content of the *finite parts* of any QED observable; UV divergences of multi-loop contributions are inherited from the underlying field theory and renormalized by counterterms that the framework does not autonomously generate.

This is **about α-order content** — specifically about which α-order GeoVac handles natively (one loop, finite) versus which α-order requires external input (two loops and beyond, UV-divergent part). A reasonable case can be made that this scope boundary deserves explicit visibility in §III, since it is a categorical statement about α-power that runs orthogonally to any single named projection mechanism.

**Counter-argument 1 (the strong one):** The scope boundary is already stated in three places, all of them appropriate:

- Paper 35 §VII.3 (Refined Prediction 1): structural statement at the framework level.
- Paper 36 §VII Proposition 1 (LS-7 multi-loop test, finalized after LS-8a): empirical statement at the QED-observable level.
- §III.6 (spectral action) transcendental signature: "$\pi^{2k} \times \mathbb{Q}$ at one loop (T9 theorem, Paper 28)" — the qualifier *at one loop* is the scope-restricted version of the prediction.

A §III loop-expansion entry would either duplicate these three statements or merely point to them. The scope boundary is **not** a new projection mechanism; it is the *limit* of an existing mechanism (§III.6 applied iteratively).

**Counter-argument 2 (the structurally important one):** Adding a §III loop-expansion entry would invert the Paper 34 protocol. Paper 34 §III entries name **mechanisms** (Fock projection, Hopf bundle, Bargmann-Segal, spinor lift, …). Each mechanism carries its own variable / dimension / transcendental signature. "Loop expansion" is not a mechanism — it is a **counter on the application count of an existing mechanism** (CC spectral action iterated n times). Adding it as §III would set a precedent for "iteration counter" entries (Bessel-fold count, multipole-fold count, etc.) that would clutter the dictionary without sharpening the structural reading.

**Counter-argument 3 (the empirical-anchor one):** The α-power index has no independent empirical anchor. Every α^n match in Paper 34's §V/§V.B catalogue is already attributable to a specific projection chain that includes §III.6 (possibly iterated), §III.11, §III.16, §III.26, etc. No table row in §V/§V.B reads "loop expansion: framework reproduces α^n at level X for observable Y" — every row instead reads "Bohr × Schwinger × …" with the α-power emerging from the chain composition.

**The renormalization-gap distinction strengthens absorbed, not own-entry.** The gap is a property of §III.6 (and the chain that includes it), specifically the property that the bare spectral-action heat-kernel expansion cannot autonomously generate UV counterterms required for finite multi-loop extraction. Naming this property at the §III.6 level (or in §III.6's "Honest scope" subsection, if one were added) keeps it where it belongs — attached to the mechanism whose limits it describes.

## 6. Verdict: ABSORBED into §III.6 + §III.11 + §III.16 + §III.26

**Verdict.** Loop expansion / α-power is **absorbed** into the existing projection dictionary. It is not a mechanism in its own right; it is the iteration counter on §III.6 (CC spectral action), and the per-loop content it counts is already exhaustively named:

- The transcendental content per iteration is named at §III.6 (Seeley-DeWitt √π·ℚ; one-loop $\pi^{2k} \cdot \mathbb{Q}$ via T9) and §III.11 ($1/(4π)$ per vector-photon loop).
- The gauge-choice piece of the per-loop calibration is named at §III.26.
- The α-power location of two-body kinematic mechanisms is the per-projection transcendental tag (§III.16 carries "$α^4·\mathbb{Q}$" explicitly).
- The scope boundary at the multi-loop UV-divergent / counterterm wall is Paper 35 §VII.3 Refined Prediction 1, with its empirical witness in Paper 36 §VII Proposition 1 (LS-7 multi-loop test, finalized after LS-8a).

**Recommended cross-reference note (not a §III subsection).** Add one sentence to §III.6's "Used in" or "Structural reading" subsection (or a new "Honest scope" subsection mirroring §III.26's structure), reading approximately:

> The α-power index $n$ in the iterated CC spectral action $(\S\ref{sec:proj_spectral_action})^n$ counts how many loops the projection chain has traversed; per-iteration transcendental content is the Seeley-DeWitt $\sqrt{\pi}\cdot\mathbb{Q}$ ring at the heat-kernel level (T9 theorem, Paper 28) and the $1/(4\pi)$ vector-photon Hopf-base calibration per loop (\S\ref{sec:proj_vector_photon} in Coulomb gauge, \S\ref{sec:proj_gauge_choice}). The finite parts of α^n observables are framework-native at every n; UV-divergent multi-loop integrands are reproduced structurally (LS-8a verdict) but their counterterm-renormalized finite extraction requires external $Z_2/\delta m$ input not autonomously generated by the bare spectral action (Paper 35 §VII.3 Refined Prediction 1, Paper 36 §VII Proposition 1).

That single sentence makes the loop-order content visible without inventing a new §III mechanism. If Sprint 3 chooses to add it, it goes into §III.6, not into a separate §III.N.

**What would change this verdict.** A future "renormalization projection" sprint that actually constructs $Z_2$ and $\delta m$ counterterms from GeoVac-internal structure (the LS-8a-renorm extension named in CLAUDE.md §2) would deserve its own §III entry, because it would name a new mechanism (counterterm generation) with a new variable (regulator scale or scheme parameter), new transcendental content, and a new empirical anchor (the C_{2S} dimensionless bracket coefficient). That is the entry to consider in a future sprint, **not** "loop expansion."

**What this scoping does not foreclose.** If a future precision-catalogue observable surfaces at α^n for n ≥ 3 with structurally distinct mechanism content (e.g., a three-loop observable whose finite part depends on a transcendental class not in $\sqrt{\pi}\cdot\mathbb{Q} \oplus \pi^2 \cdot \mathbb{Q}$ at the corresponding Seeley-DeWitt order), the case for a separate loop-expansion entry should be re-opened. The three-loop O(N³) factorized chain of Paper 28 §three_loop is a natural test case; as of 2026-05-15 it has no empirical anchor in Paper 34's §V/§V.B catalogue.
