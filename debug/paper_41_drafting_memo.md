## 2. Key drafting choices

### 2.1 Framing: "structural compatibility" rather than "establishes 3D compact U(1)"

The task spec described the XCWG arc as having "landed positive results across four independent diagnostics" and asked for an honest framing. I chose **"structural compatibility with 3D compact $U(1)$ on a non-cubic substrate"** as the load-bearing claim throughout. This:

- Honors CLAUDE.md §1.5 (no ontological priority of discrete graph vs continuum; "structural" is the appropriate epistemic tier);
- Reflects the genuine scope of the four diagnostics (all leading-order or coarse-approximation; the 3D-vs-4D distinction is non-perturbative);
- Distinguishes this paper from a "we proved Rule B IS 3D compact $U(1)$" claim that the diagnostics do not support.

The verdict statement in the abstract and again in §6 is "structural compatibility holds across all four witnesses," not "Rule B is 3D compact $U(1)$." §6 explicitly enumerates three honest caveats: (i) no unique identification with the 3D universality class; (ii) continuum limit not computed; (iii) no continuum field-theoretic interpretation of Rule B is asserted.

### 2.2 Resolution of the XCWG-C "second witness fails" memo

The XCWG-C memo (`debug/xcwg_wilson_loop_scaling_memo.md`) ends with the verdict that the two-witness test FAILS: $\alpha \approx 1$ on the Gaussian probe was read as "perimeter law fails area law test." The XCWG-D memo (`debug/xcwg_strong_coupling_wilson_memo.md`) resolves this by distinguishing the Gaussian/free-photon sector from the strong-coupling/compact sector.

In the paper §5.1, I state this resolution explicitly: the Gaussian probe is the free-photon sector, which gives perimeter law in any dimension $D \le 4$ for any abelian theory (by linearization losing the $2\pi$ periodicity that admits monopoles). The compact-U(1) area law is generated by monopoles and shows up only in the strong-coupling character expansion (§5.2). The two probes look at structurally different sectors; the XCWG-C $\alpha = 1$ result CONFIRMS 3D free-photon geometry rather than CONTRADICTING compact confinement.

I also explicitly noted in §5.1 that "The earlier reading of the same numerical data ... that the two-witness verification is therefore not established ... conflated the free-photon sector ... with the full compact theory" — a transparent reference back to the older memo's framing without re-litigating it.

### 2.3 Four witnesses, named and tagged

Rather than three witnesses + one "second witness FAILS" (the original XCWG-C reading), the paper presents four witnesses each with a clean PASS verdict at the order it accesses:

- Witness 1: spectral dimension (XCWG-A spectral-dim memo) — Rule B trends toward 3, scalar plateaus at 2.
- Witness 2: MK RG flow (XCWG-B MK memo) — monotone to $\beta=0$, no UV fixed point.
- Witness 3: Gaussian Wilson loop (XCWG-C wls memo) — $\alpha \approx 1$ = 3D free-photon perimeter law.
- Witness 4: strong-coupling Wilson loop (XCWG-D memo) — area law with $\sigma(\beta) > 0$ everywhere.

The §6 "joint structural verdict" table presents these as independent diagnostics each consistent with 3D compact U(1) and inconsistent with the 4D phase-transition prediction.

### 2.4 Plaquette-boundary operator $K = d_1^\top d_1$ vs edge Laplacian $L_1$

The §3.4 "plaquette boundary operator and the correct Wilson kinetic" subsection explicitly distinguishes $L_1 = B^\top B$ (which has all closed cycles in its kernel) from the co-exact $K = d_1^\top d_1$ (which has only $\beta_1$-dim kernel of harmonic 1-cochains). This is a load-bearing technical point: using $L_1^+$ for Gaussian Wilson loops gives contact zeros that mask the actual scaling. The Hodge-Helmholtz decomposition $L_1 = d_0^\top d_0 + d_1^\top d_1$ is invoked to identify the gauge-fixed subspace.

This treatment matches the XCWG-C memo's §1.1 "Track B3 correction" and explicitly references the rank verification across $n_\text{max} \in \{2,3,4,5\}$.

## 3. Cross-memo inconsistencies found

### 3.1 XCWG-C vs XCWG-D verdict (the major one)

The XCWG-C memo (`xcwg_wilson_loop_scaling_memo.md`) §8 reads:

> "The two-witness test for "Rule B Wilson U(1) is 3D compact U(1) on a non-cubic graph" is **NOT established**. The first witness (MK β→0) holds; the second witness (Wilson-loop α≈2) **fails**."

The XCWG-D memo (`xcwg_strong_coupling_wilson_memo.md`) §8 reads:

> "Combined with the XCWG-B Track 1 Migdal–Kadanoff monotone flow to $\beta = 0$, the **two-witness verdict for "Rule B Wilson U(1) is 3D compact U(1) on a non-cubic graph" is established.**"

These two verdicts read literally as a direct contradiction. The resolution (which both the task brief and XCWG-D itself explain) is that they probe different sectors: XCWG-C operates on $K = d_1^\top d_1$ with linearized action (free-photon, monopole-blind), XCWG-D operates on the full Wilson action with compact integration domain (sees monopoles).

I handled this in §5.1 by:
- Stating both results explicitly and giving the correct $\alpha = 1$ "3D free-photon perimeter" reading;
- Naming the XCWG-C result a PASS (third witness for 3D-compatibility), not a fail;
- Adding an explicit paragraph acknowledging that "the earlier reading of the same numerical data... conflated the free-photon sector with the full compact theory";
- Pointing out that the discriminator at the Gaussian level is 3D ($\alpha = 1$) vs 4D ($\alpha = 2$), and the measured $\alpha \approx 1$ unambiguously selects 3D.

This treatment is the cleanest honest synthesis I can produce without re-litigating the memos. The body of the paper reflects the XCWG-D resolution, while the memo cross-reference makes the earlier reading visible.

### 3.2 MK RG verdict caveats

The XCWG-B MK memo states the MK result is "qualitatively the same as 2D scalar MK and 3D cubic MK." This is structurally correct (MK is universal at the level of "monotone flow to $\beta=0$, no UV fixed point" in dimensions $D \le 3$) and the memo correctly notes that MK is unable to resolve monopoles, so the 3D-vs-4D distinction is invisible to MK at the order tested.

In the paper §5.4 I explicitly stated three caveats:
- (i) MK is universal in a coarse sense across 2D, 3D, and Rule B;
- (ii) what is discriminating is the absence of a non-trivial fixed point at the tested cutoffs;
- (iii) Rule B's flow is quantitatively much faster ($k_\text{eff} = 37.5$ vs cubic $k = 4$) without indicating a distinct phase structure.

This is honest about MK being a coarser discriminator than the user's task brief suggested. I felt this was important to flag explicitly: the XCWG-B memo is generous in its reading ("consistent with 3D compact U(1) permanent confinement") but the MK alone cannot distinguish 3D from 4D.

### 3.3 Plaquette/edge ratio in XCWG-A infrastructure vs MK memos

The XCWG-A infrastructure memo §5 reports plaquette/edge ratio as 9.38 at $n_\text{max} = 3$ (from $994 / 106$). The MK memo §1.3 notes that the *mean plaquettes per edge* (a different statistic) is 37.51 at $n_\text{max}=3$. The discrepancy is a factor of 4: each plaquette has 4 edges, so the sum-over-edges $\sum_e q_e = 4 \cdot |P|$, and the mean is $4 \cdot |P| / |E| = 4 \cdot 9.38 \approx 37.5$.

The MK memo notes this in §1.3 explicitly. I reported the infrastructure-memo statistic (9.38) in §2.2 as "plaquette/edge ratio" for comparison to cubic-lattice values, and the MK-memo statistic ($\langle q_e \rangle = 37.51$) in §5.1 as "mean plaquettes per edge" for the MK exponent. Both are correct in their respective conventions; the paper makes the distinction explicit.

I went through all five XCWG memos and the task spec; no other substantive inconsistencies. Minor differences in word choice ("essentially 3D" vs "trending toward 3D", etc.) but no factual discrepancies.

## 4. Flagged uncertainties and scope caveats

### 4.1 The "structural compatibility" framing is the load-bearing claim

The paper's central claim is structural compatibility, not unique identification. Honest scope:
- All four witnesses are leading-order or coarse-approximation tests.
- The 3D-vs-4D distinction in compact lattice gauge theory is fundamentally non-perturbative; visible only at higher orders or via direct monopole-density computation.
- What is verified is absence of the 4D phase transition (witness 2) + presence of the 3D area-law mechanism (witness 4), not exclusion of other universality classes that share these features at the order tested.

This is stated three places in the paper: abstract (last sentence), §1 introduction (paragraph after "The verdict, stated up front"), and §7 "What this verdict does not establish" (three numbered caveats).

### 4.2 Continuum limit not computed

The $n_\text{max} \to \infty$ limit and whether it produces continuum 3D compact QED on a Riemannian manifold is open. §7 caveat (ii) and §8 outlook flag this explicitly. The relation to Papers 38 and 40 (propinquity convergence) is acknowledged in §8 but not pursued in this paper.

### 4.3 Spectral-dimension finite-size effects

The Weyl-vs-heat-kernel discrepancy ($\approx 1.2$ at $n_\text{max} = 5$) is a real finite-size phenomenon. I reported it transparently in §4 and noted that the *trend* is robust ($d_s$ climbing toward 3 monotonically) but the precise asymptotic value is uncertain ($n_\text{max} = 7$-$8$ verification would tighten the asymptote). This is consistent with the XCWG-A spectral-dim memo's honest framing.

### 4.4 Honest about MK universality

§5.4 caveat (ii) flags that the MK flow direction (monotone to $\beta=0$) is universal across 2D, 3D cubic, and Rule B — not 3D-specific. This is structurally important context that the XCWG-B memo addresses but doesn't lead with; I made it the lead caveat.

## 5. Recommendations for v2 (what XCWG-E/F would strengthen)

The honest-scope caveats above point to specific sprints that would tighten the structural-compatibility verdict:

### 5.1 XCWG-E: Higher-order strong-coupling character expansion

The leading-order area-law formula $\langle W \rangle = (I_1/I_0)^A$ is universal across dimensions. The next-to-leading correction introduces perimeter-dependent terms whose coefficients depend on plaquette-adjacency structure. On a 3D cubic lattice these are known; computing them on Rule B would test whether the next-order corrections match 3D-compact-U(1) at the structural level. Estimated effort: 2-3 weeks.

### 5.2 XCWG-F: Monopole density on Rule B

This is the canonical Polyakov mechanism check. Build the closed 2-surface enumeration on the plaquette adjacency graph (combinatorially nontrivial but well-defined), compute integer flux per cell via DeGrand-Toussaint, MC sample at various $\beta$, and verify $\rho(\beta) \propto \exp(-c \beta)$ with $c$ related to the inverse-Laplacian-at-origin on Rule B. Estimated effort: 3-6 weeks. Strongest single discriminator between 3D and 4D would be the existence and exponential decay of $\rho(\beta)$.

### 5.3 XCWG-G: Full MC Wilson loops over $U(1)^{|E|}$

Direct MC computation of $\langle W(C) \rangle$ at fixed $\beta$ would verify the area law beyond leading order and detect finite-size systematics invisible at leading order. Standard Metropolis sweep on $U(1)$ variables, $\sim 10^4$ sweeps per $\beta$, 4-6 weeks of careful implementation and convergence checks.

### 5.4 XCWG-H: Polyakov-loop analog via $G_B \otimes C_{N_t}$

Lift Rule B to a $(3+1)$D substrate by tensoring with a cyclic chain (the Matsubara circle), following Paper 35 §VIII's $T_{S^3} \otimes T_{S^1_\beta}$ construction. This permits a finite-temperature Polyakov-loop computation and tests for a deconfinement transition at finite $T$ analogous to the Svetitsky-Yaffe universality of 3D cubic compact U(1). Multi-week.

### 5.5 Higher $n_\text{max}$ in the spectral-dimension diagnostic

Pushing to $n_\text{max} = 7$-$8$ would tighten the $d_s$ asymptote and bring the Weyl/heat-kernel discrepancy closer to zero. The compute cost is moderate (graphs at $n_\text{max} = 8$ have $\sim 200$-$300$ vertices, exact eigensolve is sub-second). 1-2 weeks of careful runtime + analysis.

### 5.6 What would change the verdict

The current verdict is "structural compatibility holds across all four witnesses." It would change if:

- XCWG-E (higher-order character) reveals area-law corrections that are 4D-like rather than 3D-like.
- XCWG-F (monopole density) finds no exponential decay $\rho \sim \exp(-c \beta)$ or finds a finite-$\beta_c$ condensation.
- The continuum limit $n_\text{max} \to \infty$ does not exist or does not reproduce a continuum gauge theory.

None of these are precluded by the current data; they are open follow-ons.
