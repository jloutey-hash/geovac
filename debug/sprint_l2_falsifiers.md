## §3. Per-falsifier prose

### L2-B (Krein space construction)

**L2B-FALS-1 ($\mathcal{J}^2 = +I$, $\mathcal{J}^*\mathcal{J} = I$).** Most basic Krein structure check. The fundamental symmetry $\mathcal{J} = \gamma^0$ (temporal Dirac matrix in West-coast Cℓ(3, 1)) must square to the identity and be unitary on the underlying Hilbert space. Riemannian-side analog: the Connes $J$ on $\mathcal{H}_{\mathrm{GV}}$ at KO-dim 3 satisfies $J^2 = -I$ (Paper 32 §IV, bit-exact). The sign flip from $-I$ to $+I$ across signature is exactly the BBB Table 1 prediction at $(m, n) = (4, 6)$, and this falsifier is the first test of whether our Cℓ(3, 1) embedding is consistent with BBB. If it fails, the spatial gamma matrix embedding into Cℓ(3, 1) was non-unique up to similarity (R1 in L2-B brief) and the wrong choice was made.

**L2B-FALS-2 (indefinite inner product Hermitian).** The Krein inner product $(\xi, \eta) = \langle \xi, \mathcal{J} \eta \rangle$ must be Hermitian: $(\xi, \eta) = \overline{(\eta, \xi)}$. This is purely a property of the construction and should be automatic from L2B-FALS-1 plus $\mathcal{J}$ being self-adjoint on the Hilbert space; if it fails, a sign convention error has crept in (East-/West-coast confusion in $\eta = \gamma^0$ definition is the most likely cause).

**L2B-FALS-3 (Krein splitting $\mathcal{K}^+ \oplus \mathcal{K}^-$).** Standard Krein-space machinery requires the fundamental symmetry to diagonalize on a positive/negative subspace decomposition. Eigenvalues of $\mathcal{J}$ should be exactly $\pm 1$ within numerical noise, with dim$(\mathcal{K}^+) = $ dim$(\mathcal{K}^-)$ = dim$(\mathcal{K})/2$ in the standard convention. Failure would indicate a spinor-bundle dimensional bookkeeping error (e.g., Cℓ(3, 1) gives 4-component spinors while Cℓ(3, 0) gives 2-component Weyl spinors stacked into a 4-vector — these are isomorphic but the embedding matters for the inner product structure).

**L2B-FALS-4 (LOAD-BEARING — Riemannian-limit recovery).** This is the single most important falsifier in the L2-B set, named explicitly in Risk R1 of the L2-B brief: "Riemannian-limit check fails: STOP and escalate." The check is structural — if we set $N_t = 1$ (single temporal grid point, effectively static) and project the Krein space onto its zero-time slice, the result must be bit-identical to Paper 32 §III's Hilbert space $\mathcal{H}_{\mathrm{GV}}$ (the truncated Camporesi–Higuchi spinor space). This must hold not just up to isomorphism, but element-by-element on basis labels and inner-product values, because Paper 38's GH-convergence proof (WH1 PROVEN) used this exact Hilbert space. **Failure invalidates Paper 38 PROVEN status at the spinor-lift level** — the Camporesi–Higuchi bundle that Paper 38 uses would be structurally incompatible with the (3, 1) extension, which by symmetry would mean either Paper 38 or the (3, 1) attempt is using the wrong bundle. The Riemannian-side analog in Paper 42 §5–§6 is the construction of $P_W$ on the Riemannian $\mathcal{H}_{\mathrm{GV}}$ at finite $n_\max$, verified bit-exact across $n_\max \in \{2, 3, 4, 5\}$; the corresponding $P_W$ on the Lorentzian Krein space must reduce to it.

### L2-C (Lorentzian Dirac operator)

**L2C-FALS-1 (Krein self-adjointness $D_L^\times = D_L$).** This is the Lorentzian-side analog of GeoVac's Riemannian $D_{\mathrm{GV}}^\dagger = D_{\mathrm{GV}}$ on the Hilbert space (Paper 32 §III). The Krein-adjoint $T^\times = \eta T^\dagger \eta$ differs from the Hilbert-adjoint by conjugation with $\eta = \gamma^0$. Failure most likely indicates a wrong sign in the "$i^t$" factor of van den Dungen Prop 4.1 ($t = 1$ for $(3, 1)$ gives $i D \!\!\!/_{g_r}$; the alternative $-i D \!\!\!/_{g_r}$ would fail Krein self-adjointness by sign).

**L2C-FALS-2 (chirality anticommutation).** Standard Connes-axiom property; spacetime chirality $\gamma_5 = i\gamma^0\gamma^1\gamma^2\gamma^3$ on $S^3 \times \mathbb{R}$ must anticommute with the Lorentzian Dirac. Bookkeeping check; this is the unchanged Connes axiom from BBB Table 1 (same sign at both (3, 0) and (3, 1) — the chirality-D anticommutation is signature-blind).

**L2C-FALS-3 (LOAD-BEARING — Riemannian-limit recovery of $D_{\mathrm{GV}}$).** Same logic as L2B-FALS-4: the $N_t = 1$ static reduction of $D_L$ must reproduce the Camporesi–Higuchi spectrum $|\lambda_n| = n + 3/2$ with the canonical multiplicities $g_n = 2(n+1)(n+2)$. This is the Dirac analog of L2B-FALS-4's Hilbert-space check. If it fails, the Wick-rotation lift via van den Dungen Prop 4.1 has not been correctly instantiated for $S^3 \times \mathbb{R}$ (the scoping memo §3.7 noted this construction has not been published for any compact spatial × $\mathbb{R}$ — Sprint L2-C is writing it down). **Failure does NOT re-open WH1 PROVEN** (Paper 38 uses the static $D_{\mathrm{GV}}$ directly, not the Lorentzian lift), but it does mean the L2-C construction is wrong and must be redone.

**L2C-FALS-4 (real spectrum on Krein-positive subspace).** Krein-self-adjoint operators have real spectrum on the Krein-positive cone $\mathcal{K}^+$ (this is the natural generalization of the Hilbert-space self-adjoint $\Rightarrow$ real spectrum). The continuous spectrum from the temporal $\mathbb{R}_t$ direction (Risk R3 in L2-C brief) means finite-cutoff approximation may produce small spurious imaginary parts; the threshold accounts for this. Persistent imaginary parts above the numerical-noise floor would indicate a deeper structural issue with the temporal discretization scheme.

### L2-D (Connes axiom audit at (3, 1) + M3 trivialization)

**L2D-FALS-1 (LOAD-BEARING — $J^2 = +I$ at (m,n) = (4,6)).** This is the cleanest single bit-exact test of whether BBB 2018's classification applies correctly to GeoVac at the (3, 0) → (3, 1) extension. The BBB Table 1 entry at (4, 6) gives $\varepsilon = +1$, which is the sign $J^2 = +I$ — opposite to GeoVac's Riemannian $J^2 = -I$ at KO-dim 3. The Riemannian-side analog is Paper 32 §IV's bit-exact $J^2 = -I$ verification (and Paper 42's TomitaConjugation distinction $J_{\mathrm{TT}}^2 = +I$ vs $J_{\mathrm{GV}}^2 = -I$). If this fails bit-exact at finite $n_\max$, either (a) BBB Table 3 (s,t)$\leftrightarrow$(m,n) translation at $(s,t) = (3,1)$ does not give $(m,n) = (4,6)$ as the scoping memo asserts, or (b) the West-/East-coast convention was applied inconsistently between L2-B and L2-D, or (c) the BBB application is genuinely wrong for $S^3 \times \mathbb{R}$. Any of these requires the full L2-D Connes audit to be redone. The failure mode is conceptually distinct from L2B-FALS-4/L2C-FALS-3 (which are about Wick-rotation construction correctness); this falsifier is about signature-classification correctness.

**L2D-FALS-2 ($J_L D_L = +D_L J_L$).** The relative sign of $JD$ vs $DJ$ depends on the (m, n) entry via the BBB Table 1 $\varepsilon$ sign in combination with the $\eta D$ relation. The scoping memo §4.4 reads $JD = +DJ$ at (4, 6) but the L2-D brief lists this as a falsifier "BBB sign at (4,6) — verify" rather than asserting it. Failure would indicate the same family of bookkeeping issues as L2D-FALS-1; standard sub-sprint correction, not load-bearing.

**L2D-FALS-3 ($J_L \gamma_5 = -\gamma_5 J_L$, BBB $\varepsilon'' = -1$).** Standard BBB Table 1 sign. Same bookkeeping family.

**L2D-FALS-4 (order-zero / order-one).** Direct analog of the Paper 32 §IV truthful-CH verification of order-zero ($[a, JbJ^{-1}] = 0$, residual $\le 5$–$20\%$) and order-one ($[[D, a], JbJ^{-1}] = 0$, similar). The Paper 32 §IV scope reading is that the 5–20% residual is a finite-resolution artifact analogous to the multiplicative-closure failure that DEFINES the Connes–vS truncated operator system. The Lorentzian extension should preserve this scope: $\le 20\%$ uniform is acceptable, $\gg 20\%$ is anomalous.

**L2D-FALS-5 (M3 sub-mechanism trivialization — the most novel and predictive falsifier in the catalogue).** This deserves expanded discussion because it touches the master Mellin engine domain partition (Sprints MR-A/B/C; CLAUDE.md §2). The case-exhaustion theorem (Paper 32 §VIII `thm:pi_source_case_exhaustion`) states that every $\pi$-source in finite GeoVac chains comes from one of three sub-mechanisms — M1 (Hopf-base measure Vol($S^2$)/4), M2 (Seeley–DeWitt $\sqrt{\pi}\cdot\mathbb{Q}$), M3 (vertex-parity Hurwitz / Catalan G / Dirichlet-L from Camporesi–Higuchi half-integer Hurwitz at quarter-integer shifts; Paper 28 §QED-vertex). The Sprint L0 prediction (`debug/lorentzian_l0_audit_memo.md` §4) is that the BBB Table 2 entry at $(m, n) = (4, 6)$ flips the $\{J, \gamma_5\}$ anticommutation table relative to KO-dim 3, with the structural consequence that the vertex-parity sum that produces M3 content at (3, 0) **vanishes identically on the chirality-symmetric truncation of the Camporesi–Higuchi spectrum at (3, 1)**.

This is a falsifier in two directions. **Pass:** the master Mellin engine simplifies from a three-mechanism partition (M1, M2, M3) on the Riemannian side to a two-mechanism partition (M1, M2) on the Lorentzian side. This is a non-trivial structural observation worth a paragraph in Paper 32 §VIII and a row update in Paper 34 §V.E (the L0 audit table moves M3 row from "predicted" to "verified"). It would also feed into the standalone Lorentzian-extension paper outlined in Sprint L0 §5. **Fail:** the M3 trivialization prediction does not hold; M3 has residual content at (3, 1) that the BBB-sign-flip argument missed. This would refine the master Mellin engine M3 mechanism domain in unexpected ways and would suggest the chirality-symmetric truncation is structurally larger than the chirality-symmetric subspace of the Riemannian-side spectrum.

Either outcome is informative; this is NOT a sprint stopper. The test is concrete: compute the Camporesi–Higuchi vertex-parity sum at finite $n_\max$ on the chirality-symmetric truncation, compare against the Riemannian-side non-vanishing value (existing Paper 28 result), and document either way. The Riemannian-side analog in Paper 42 is the structural reading of $J_{\mathrm{TT}}^2 = +I$ vs $J_{\mathrm{GV}}^2 = -I$ as categorically distinct (Paper 42 §6.3 Prop 6.2); the Lorentzian-side analog of that distinction is what L2D-FALS-5 tests at the sub-mechanism level.

Threshold-ambiguity caveat: "trivializes" is not numerically defined in the scoping memo. We adopt $\le 10^{-12}$ as the bit-exact bar, but if the residual sits in the range $10^{-12}$ to $10^{-6}$ this is suggestive-but-not-conclusive and would need refinement. Document the actual value rather than reducing it to pass/fail.

**L2D-FALS-6 (uniform trivialization across all Fock shells).** Follow-up refinement of L2D-FALS-5: if the M3 sum vanishes at the leading shell but grows with $n$, this would indicate the trivialization is partial. Verify uniform vanishing across $n \le n_\max$ to establish the structural reading cleanly.

### L2-E (Krein-level Paper 42 redo)

**L2E-FALS-1 (LOAD-BEARING — bit-exact period closure $\sigma_{2\pi}^{L,\alpha}(O) = O$).** The direct Lorentzian-side analog of Paper 42 Theorem 5.4 (BW-α STRONG_IDENTIFICATION, bit-exact at $n_\max \in \{2, 3, 4, 5\}$). The Riemannian-side closure works because $K_\alpha = J_{\mathrm{polar}}$ has integer spectrum $2 m_j$ on the spinor basis, so $e^{i \cdot 2\pi \cdot n} = 1$ for integer $n$ (Paper 42 §5.3). The Lorentzian-side question is whether the Krein-space modular Hamiltonian $K_L^\alpha$ has the analogous integer-spectrum property under the BW choice $H_{\mathrm{local}} = K_\alpha^W / \beta$. Failure means the unified-strong four-witness theorem does not extend to the Lorentzian side via the natural construction; this would close Paper 42 §10 open question O1 ("Lorentzian extension to signature (3, 1)") in the negative, which would itself be a clean structural result but inverts the expected sign. The escalation step is to investigate the load-bearing scope finding of Paper 42 §7.2 (the alternative $H_{\mathrm{local}} = D_W$ also did not produce the Riemannian bit-exact closure; perhaps yet a third choice of local Hamiltonian is needed at (3, 1)) before declaring the Lorentzian extension structurally obstructed.

**L2E-FALS-2 (bit-exact period closure for Tomita–Takesaki construction $\sigma_{2\pi}^{L,\mathrm{TT}}(O) = O$).** Tomita-side analog of L2E-FALS-1; mirrors Paper 42 Theorem 6.3 (BW-γ UNIFIED_STRONG, bit-exact at finite cutoff). Failure with L2E-FALS-1 passing would indicate a Krein-GNS construction subtlety (Risk R6 in L2-E brief): the Krein-positive cone vs Krein-negative cone gives two GNS spaces, only one of which is suitable for the polar decomposition $S = J_{\mathrm{TT}} \Delta^{1/2}$. Resolution: follow van den Dungen 2016 §2 (Krein-positive completion) and document.

**L2E-FALS-3 (operator-action conjugacy $\sigma_t^{L,\mathrm{TT}}(O) = \sigma_{-t}^{L,\alpha}(O)$).** Lorentzian-side analog of Paper 42 Theorem 7.1 (flow conjugacy at general $t$). Failure at the period $t = 2\pi$ is subsumed by L2E-FALS-1 + L2E-FALS-2; failure at general $t$ with both period closures passing would indicate that BW-α and BW-γ are not operator-action conjugates at (3, 1), which would be a structural distinction from the Riemannian side. Document and refine.
