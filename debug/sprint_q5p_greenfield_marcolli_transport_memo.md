## 2. Greenfield–Marcolli structural data (from public abstracts + Connes–Moscovici parent paper)

### Twisted-spectral-triple framework (Connes–Moscovici 2006, arXiv:math/0609703)

A **twisted spectral triple** $(\mathcal{A}, \mathcal{H}, D; \sigma)$ replaces the ordinary spectral-triple bounded-commutator condition $\|[D, a]\| < \infty$ by

$$[D, a]_\sigma := D a - \sigma(a) D \in \mathcal{B}(\mathcal{H}),$$

with $\sigma \in \mathrm{Aut}(\mathcal{A})$. **Motivation (Connes–Moscovici):** type III examples (transverse geometry of codimension-1 foliations, conformal deformations of metrics) where no tracial state exists and the original spectral-triple framework fails. The twisted Dixmier trace becomes a **twisted hypertrace**, recovering KMS-state-flavored cyclic cohomology.

**Mechanical reduction at $\sigma = \mathrm{id}$:** The twisted commutator collapses to the ordinary commutator, and the construction reduces to a standard Connes spectral triple. (This is universally agreed across the survey literature; cf. Filaci–Martinetti 2023 / arXiv:2301.08346.)

### Greenfield–Marcolli–Teh construction (arXiv:1305.5492)

Input data: the **Bost–Connes (BC) C*-algebra** $\mathcal{A}_{\mathrm{BC}}$ with its canonical time evolution $\sigma_t$ generated by the BC Hamiltonian $H_{\mathrm{BC}}$ whose eigenvalues are $\log n$ for $n \in \mathbb{N}$. The twist $\sigma$ in the twisted spectral triple is derived from the **Liouville function** $\lambda(n)$ (the completely multiplicative function with $\lambda(p) = -1$ on primes), which fixes **simultaneously**:
- the sign $F = \mathrm{sgn}(D)$ of the Dirac operator (via $\lambda(n) \in \{+1, -1\}$ on the BC Hamiltonian eigenstates labeled by $n \in \mathbb{N}$);
- the twisting $\sigma$ as a regrading-style automorphism.

The construction reduces (Caltech preprint title) to a **type III σ-spectral triple**: the underlying BC factor is type III at low temperature (KMS phase transition at $\beta = 1$), with the twist $\sigma$ matching the modular automorphism of the canonical KMS state.

### Where the cyclotomic Galois action lives

The cyclotomic Galois group $\mathrm{Gal}(\mathbb{Q}^{\mathrm{ab}}/\mathbb{Q}) \cong \hat{\mathbb{Z}}^*$ acts as **symmetries on the extremal KMS states at zero temperature** of the BC system (Bost–Connes 1995 main theorem). This Galois action **does NOT live on the algebra $\mathcal{A}_{\mathrm{BC}}$ directly**; it lives on the **states** (KMS₀ extremals), and intertwines with the natural class-field-theory action on the values of states evaluated on the **arithmetic subalgebra** $\mathcal{A}_{\mathrm{BC},\mathbb{Q}} \subset \mathcal{A}_{\mathrm{BC}}$.

The arithmetic subalgebra is the locus of **cyclotomic input**: it consists of cyclotomic phases $e^{2\pi i r}$ for $r \in \mathbb{Q}/\mathbb{Z}$, embedded into the BC algebra via the canonical $\mathbb{Q}/\mathbb{Z}$-module structure. The Galois action's natural domain is **the arithmetic of the underlying input number field**, NOT the analytic spectral data.

### Connes–Marcolli–Ramachandran extension (arXiv:math/0501424)

Generalizes BC to imaginary quadratic fields $K$ of arbitrary class number, **fully incorporating class field theory of $K$**. Galois group of the modular field acts as symmetries on extremal KMS states. **Load-bearing arithmetic input:** the construction depends explicitly on the class field theory of an arithmetic input — without an input number field with abelian class field theory, the symmetry group is undefined.

### Endomotive framework (Connes–Consani–Marcolli arXiv:math/0512138, Connes–Marcolli book Ch. 4)

Generalizes the BC-type setup categorically. An **endomotive** is:
- a (pro-)commutative algebra $\mathcal{A}_0$ coming from a system of finite-rank étale algebras over $\mathbb{Q}$ (Artin motives);
- a semigroup $S$ of endomorphisms (often $\mathbb{N}^*$);
- the noncommutative crossed product $\mathcal{A}_0 \rtimes S$ with its canonical time evolution.

The endomotive produces a **C\*-dynamical system $(\mathcal{A}, \sigma_t)$** whose **low-temperature KMS states carry a canonical $\mathrm{Gal}(\bar{\mathbb{Q}}/\mathbb{Q})$-action**. The thermodynamic step (Connes–Consani–Marcolli) takes this dynamical system to its **dual** via the modular automorphism + scaling action, producing the type III structure where the cohomological interpretation of zeta zeros lives.

**Load-bearing categorical input:** the commutative starting algebra $\mathcal{A}_0$ **must come from arithmetic** (étale algebras over $\mathbb{Q}$, or more generally over a number field) for the absolute Galois group to act canonically. The categorical generation is "semigroup of endomorphisms of an algebraic variety." Pure-analytic / purely-geometric starting data do not produce an endomotive.

## 4. Four load-bearing obstructions

We now name the four independent structural mismatches. Each individually rules out mechanical or guided transport; together they rule out the entire QSM lineage as a precedent for Q5'.

### Obstruction A: GeoVac is type I, BC/CMR/endomotive is type III

**Connes–Moscovici's twisted-spectral-triple motivation is to handle type III settings where ordinary spectral triples fail.** GeoVac is finite-dim ⇒ type I at every $n_{\max}$, continuum limit is the standard type II_∞ / type I round-$S^3$ spectral triple. The ordinary (untwisted) Connes axioms hold cleanly (Paper 32 §III) — there is no type III obstruction to begin with.

**Reduction at $\sigma = \mathrm{id}$:** putting GeoVac inside the Greenfield–Marcolli framework as the $\sigma = \mathrm{id}$ degenerate case is precisely an **ordinary spectral triple**, recovering nothing beyond Paper 32. The twist mechanism is **vacuous on GeoVac data**. This is not a refinement; it's a no-op.

### Obstruction B: GeoVac has no KMS phase transition

The BC / CMR construction's Galois-action mechanism *requires* the KMS phase transition: cyclotomic Galois acts on **extremal KMS states at $\beta = \infty$** (zero temperature), which are well-defined only because the system has a nontrivial phase-transition structure ($\beta_{\mathrm{crit}} = 1$ for BC, $\beta_{\mathrm{crit}} = 2$ for CMR-type imaginary-quadratic, etc.). At high temperature the KMS state is unique and Galois symmetry is "unbroken."

GeoVac at finite $n_{\max}$ is finite-dim, so the KMS analysis is trivial: the canonical state is the (unique) normalized trace, with $\sigma_t = \mathrm{id}$, and there is **no $\beta$-parameterized family of KMS states** to start with. The continuum limit on $S^3$ similarly carries no nontrivial $\beta$-dependent KMS structure on $\mathcal{A}_{\mathrm{GV}} = C^\infty(S^3)$ (the canonical state is the normalized Riemannian integral, again with $\sigma_t = \mathrm{id}$).

**The Lorentzian wedge KMS state (Paper 42) is an external-projection structure**, not the canonical state of the Riemannian spectral triple, and its Galois content is the master-Mellin-engine M1 Hopf-base measure (Sprint L2-F.1, `pythagorean_orthogonality.md`), not a cyclotomic-arithmetic action.

### Obstruction C: GeoVac has no canonical semigroup of endomorphisms

Endomotives are generated by **semigroups of endomorphisms** of commutative algebras (Connes–Consani–Marcolli 2005). The semigroup is the load-bearing categorical input — without it, the crossed product $\mathcal{A}_0 \rtimes S$ is not defined, and the modular-thermodynamic dual (which produces the type III phase) does not exist.

GeoVac's $\mathcal{A}_{\mathrm{GV}}$ is the algebra of functions on the graph $(n, l, m_l) \mapsto \mathbb{C}$. There is no **canonical** semigroup of endomorphisms on this algebra — the Fock-projection structure of Paper 7 is a homeomorphism, not a semigroup of self-maps. The closest candidates are the natural-geometry level transitions ($S^3 \to$ prolate spheroidal $\to$ hyperspherical, Paper 5 §5), but these are between *different* algebras at different levels, not endomorphisms of a single algebra.

One could *artificially* equip $\mathcal{A}_{\mathrm{GV}}$ with a semigroup structure (e.g., multiplication by $n \in \mathbb{N}$ shifting the principal quantum number), but this would be **an exogenous arithmetic input** put in by hand — defeating the entire purpose of probing whether GeoVac has an *autonomous* motivic Galois group.

### Obstruction D: GeoVac has no arithmetic input number field

Both BC and CMR depend explicitly on the **class field theory of an input number field** — $\mathbb{Q}$ for BC ($\mathbb{Q}^{\mathrm{ab}} = \bigcup_n \mathbb{Q}(\zeta_n)$), imaginary quadratic $K$ for CMR (modular-field structure). The Galois group acting on KMS states is *the Galois group of the input number field's abelianization or modular field*, not a Galois group derived from spectral data.

GeoVac's input is **the packing axiom and Fock projection** (Paper 0, Paper 7). There is no number field, no arithmetic subalgebra, no cyclotomic embedding. The cyclotomic conductor at level 4 of M3 (Sprint A7) is a Mellin-engine **output**: it appears in the period ring of Tr($D \cdot e^{-tD^2}$) at $k = 1$, derived from the half-integer Hurwitz shifts of the Camporesi–Higuchi Dirac spectrum. There is no Galois group whose action on $\mathcal{A}_{\mathrm{GV}}$ or on the wedge KMS states is canonically determined by an input number field.

This is the **fundamental asymmetry between the QSM construction and Q5's target**:

- **QSM:** Galois group of input number field → acts on KMS states → restricts to action on arithmetic subalgebra → action on the values of states on arithmetic data.
- **Q5' target:** Galois group derived from spectral data → acts on the period ring (M1/M2/M3 Mellin moments) → potentially restricts to action on the analytic content of the triple.

These point in **opposite directions** in the data-flow. QSM has Galois → analytic; Q5' wants analytic → Galois. The QSM construction does not provide the second direction.

## 5. The "Galois on periods vs Galois on states" distinction

The task spec correctly identified the relevant categorical distinction: "Q5' wants a Galois action on PERIODS (the output ring); the QSM precedent gives a Galois action on STATES (KMS states parametrized by $1/T$)."

After this depth probe, we sharpen the distinction:

| Q5' target (periods, motivic) | QSM precedent (states, KMS / arithmetic) |
|:------------------------------|:------------------------------------------|
| Input: spectral triple + Mellin engine | Input: number field + semigroup of endomorphisms |
| Galois acts on: M1/M2/M3 output ring | Galois acts on: extremal KMS₀ states |
| Galois source: motivic Galois group of mixed-Tate at level 4 | Galois source: $\mathrm{Gal}(\bar K/K)$ for input number field $K$ |
| Data flow: spectral data → period ring → Galois | Data flow: arithmetic input → C*-dynamical → KMS → Galois |
| Category: Tannakian dual of period-realization functor | Category: arithmetic crossed product + modular flow |
| Requires type III? No (GeoVac is type I) | Yes (the canonical lineage requires KMS phase transition) |

**The two directions do not compose.** The QSM Galois action on states could in principle be evaluated *on periods of the system* (the values of KMS states on arithmetic-subalgebra elements ARE periods in a generalized sense), and this is the closest QSM analog of "Galois on periods." But this requires the **arithmetic subalgebra** structure — without arithmetic input, there is no canonical subalgebra whose state-values are periods.

The genuine precedent for "Galois on periods of a spectral triple" remains the **Marcolli–Tabuada noncommutative-motivic-Galois machinery (arXiv:1110.2438, 1112.5422)** + **Fathizadeh–Marcolli periods of the spectral action (arXiv:1611.01815)**. Round 1's Strands C and D are the right precedents; Strand B (Greenfield–Marcolli) is not. This depth probe refines Round 1 by demoting Strand B from "shape-precedent" to "categorically incompatible."

## 6. What this means for Q5' construction

### What Q5' does NOT inherit from QSM

The four obstructions rule out the following potential inheritances:

1. **Twisted-spectral-triple framework** (Connes–Moscovici / Greenfield–Marcolli): vacuous on GeoVac, no type III input.
2. **KMS-state Galois action**: no phase transition, no nontrivial state-temperature family.
3. **Endomotive thermodynamic step**: no semigroup of endomorphisms, no arithmetic input algebra.
4. **Arithmetic-subalgebra mechanism**: no number field, no cyclotomic embedding into $\mathcal{A}_{\mathrm{GV}}$.

## 9. Verdict

**One-line verdict: NOT TRANSPORTABLE.** The Greenfield–Marcolli / CMR / endomotive QSM lineage is mechanically incompatible with GeoVac at four independent load-bearing points: (A) GeoVac is type I, the QSM construction is built for type III; (B) GeoVac has no KMS phase transition; (C) GeoVac has no canonical semigroup of endomorphisms on $\mathcal{A}_{\mathrm{GV}}$; (D) GeoVac has no arithmetic input number field for Galois to act through. The QSM Galois action runs *from arithmetic → KMS states → period values*, the opposite direction from Q5's target *spectral data → period ring → Galois*. Q5' must be built from Strands A + C + D + E (Connes–Marcolli motivic Galois + Marcolli–Tabuada NC motives + Fathizadeh–Marcolli periods + Deligne–Glanois cyclotomic descent), without the QSM Strand B as an intermediate.
