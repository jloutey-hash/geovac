2. STRUCTURAL BREAKDOWN
========================

COMPOSED ENCODING (second-quantized):
  - Q = 2*M (M spatial orbitals -> 2M spin-orbitals -> 2M qubits)
  - h1 is block-diagonal: core, val-Li, val-H blocks
  - ERI tensor is block-diagonal: zero cross-block by PK approximation
  - Gaunt selection rules: most (pq|rs) integrals are zero
  - JW transform exploits sparsity -> O(Q^2.5) scaling
  - Pauli terms come from: h1 diagonals (Z strings), ERI (XXYY/ZZII strings)
  - At Q=30 (standard LiH): 334 Pauli terms

FULL N-ELECTRON ENCODING (direct spatial):
  - Q = ceil(log2(D)) where D = n_ch_S4 * n_grid^3
  - H_ang is the FULL 4-electron angular Hamiltonian in coordinate space
  - NOT second-quantized: fermion statistics are built into S4 projection
  - Matrix structure: FD kinetic (banded) + potential (diagonal) + channel coupling
  - Matrix sparsity (nnz/dim^2):
    - l=1,g=4: 832/16384 = 5.1%
    - l=1,g=6: 3024/186624 = 1.6%
    - l=2,g=4: ~1-2%
  - Pauli terms STRONGLY depend on dim vs 2^Q alignment:
    - dim = 128 = 2^7 exactly: only 45 Pauli terms (0.27% density)
    - dim = 250, padded to 256: 4,069 terms (6.2% density!)
    - dim = 686, padded to 1024: 51,907 terms (5.0% density)
  - Zero-padding to 2^Q introduces MANY Pauli terms to encode
    "this subspace is physical, this is padding"

3. SCALING ANALYSIS
====================

The Pauli term count for the full encoding is NOT well-described by a power law
in Q. The dominant factor is the dim/2^Q ratio (padding overhead), not the
physical Hamiltonian structure.

When dim = 2^Q exactly (n_grid=4, l_max=1): 45 terms at Q=7
When dim << 2^Q (n_grid=7): 51,907 terms at Q=10

The composed encoding scales as Q^2.5 (verified across LiH/BeH2/H2O, Paper 14).
At Q=30, composed gives 334 terms.

A comparable full encoding at Q=30 would require dim ~ 2^30 ~ 10^9, which means
~10^9 FD grid points. This is:
  - n_grid ~ 100, l_max ~ 50 channels
  - Wall time: years per rho-point
  - Matrix: 10^9 x 10^9 dense FD matrix -> intractable

4. FUNDAMENTAL ARCHITECTURAL DIFFERENCE
========================================

The two encodings answer different questions:

COMPOSED (JW of second-quantized H):
  - "What is the energy of 4 electrons distributed across M spatial orbitals?"
  - Qubits represent orbital OCCUPATION NUMBERS
  - Antisymmetry is handled by the fermion algebra (anticommutation)
  - Block-diagonal structure from PK approximation -> O(Q^2.5) scaling
  - Cost: M spatial orbitals -> O(M^5) ERI computation, then O(M^2.5) Pauli terms

FULL (direct Pauli decomposition of H_ang):
  - "What is the angular eigenvalue of the 4-electron Schrodinger equation?"
  - Qubits represent GRID POINT INDICES in the 11-dimensional angular space
  - Antisymmetry is handled by S4 projection (subspace reduction, not JW)
  - No block-diagonal structure -> exponential Pauli scaling with Q
  - Cost: D-dimensional eigenvalue problem, where D ~ n_ch * n_grid^3

The composed encoding has an EXPONENTIAL advantage because:
  1. It works in a compact orbital basis (M ~ 10-20 orbitals)
  2. Block-diagonal ERI eliminates most inter-orbital coupling
  3. Gaunt selection rules make the remaining ERIs sparse

The full encoding has dense coupling because:
  1. The FD kinetic energy couples all neighboring grid points
  2. Channel coupling (V_ee, V_nuc) is dense in the coupled basis
  3. Padding to 2^Q wastes qubits when dim is not a power of 2

5. VERDICT
==========

DENSER. The full N-electron Hamiltonian encoding is categorically denser than
the composed-geometry encoding for quantum simulation.

At comparable qubit counts:
  - Full Q=7: 45 Pauli terms (best case, exact 2^Q alignment)
  - Composed Q=6: 10 Pauli terms
  - Ratio: 4.5x denser (best case)

  - Full Q=10: 3,288-51,907 terms (depending on padding)
  - Composed Q=30: 334 terms at 3x the qubits
  - Even at 3x MORE qubits, composed is 10-155x sparser

The full encoding is structurally unsuitable for quantum simulation because:
  1. The coordinate-space Hamiltonian produces dense Pauli decompositions
  2. The 1-norm is orders of magnitude larger (738-3762 vs 16-37 Ha)
  3. The dimension scales exponentially with accuracy (n_grid^3 per channel)

The composed-geometry encoding is the correct approach for quantum computing
applications. The full N-electron solver's value is as a CLASSICAL benchmark
(Track AJ/AM) validating the composed approximation, not as a quantum circuit.

This result provides additional justification for the PK approximation:
  - PK enables block-diagonal ERIs -> sparse Pauli Hamiltonians
  - The "cost" of PK (introducing an approximate pseudopotential) is
    RECOVERED through exponentially better quantum resource scaling
  - Classified as a quantum-computing exchange constant: the irreducible
    computational cost of maintaining O(Q^2.5) Pauli scaling
