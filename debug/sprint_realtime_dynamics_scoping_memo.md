# Sprint Real-Time Dynamics Scoping Memo

**Date:** 2026-06-04
**Task:** Scope whether the Krein-space Lorentzian extension (Papers 42-49) gives an honest discretization for real-time quantum chemistry dynamics on quantum computers.
**Verdict:** **DEFER because the Krein-space extension is a structural framework about how the FRAMEWORK ITSELF (the Camporesi–Higuchi spinor spectral triple on S³ × ℝ_t) Wick-rotates, not a quantum-dynamics generator for second-quantized chemistry Hamiltonians on a separate fermionic Fock space — to use it for chemistry dynamics requires a multi-month inner-factor extension (G4) that has been explicitly named as orthogonal to the convergence theorem in every math.OA paper of the arc.**

## 1. What the Krein-space arc actually proves

Papers 42, 43, 44, 45, 46, 47, 48, 49 are about one structural object: the **truncated Camporesi–Higuchi spinor spectral triple on S³ × ℝ_t** lifted to KO-dimension (3,1) via the van den Dungen Wick-rotation prescription. The headline results are:

- **Paper 42:** Tomita–Takesaki modular structure on the hemispheric wedge of S³; four-witness (Bisognano–Wichmann / Hartle–Hawking / Sewell / Unruh) theorem closes at the operator-system level on the Riemannian side; bit-exact period closure at β = 2π.
- **Paper 43 / L2:** Lorentzian extension at finite (n_max, N_t) via Krein space at signature (3,1) with J_L = γ⁰ ⊗ I, Lorentzian Dirac D_L = i(γ⁰ ⊗ ∂_t + D_GV ⊗ I).
- **Paper 45 / 46:** K⁺-weak and strong-form Lorentzian propinquity convergence on the natural chirality-doubled scalar substrate.
- **Paper 47:** Norm-resolvent convergence to the non-compact carrier S³ × ℝ_t (G2 spectral-level), three-carrier identification.
- **Paper 48 / 49:** Krein–Mondino-Sämann bridge; OSLPLS; thermal-time twin paradox via Connes-Rovelli cocycle.

**Crucial Paper 42 §7.2 / Theorem 4 finding (load-bearing for this scoping question):**

> "The framework's intrinsic Dirac D_CH is the canonical spectral-action object on the Camporesi–Higuchi triple, and one might naively expect it to play the role of the local Hamiltonian for any natural KMS state on the truncated operator system. The above shows that this is not the case at β = 2π: the right local Hamiltonian for the BW vacuum on the wedge is the boost-class generator K_α^W, not the wedge-restricted Dirac D_W."

And the (3,1) refinement (Theorem `thm:h_local_sig_independent`):

> "The §sec:derived_hamiltonian / O3 distinction between the spectral-action Dirac D_W and the modular-Hamiltonian generator K_α^W/β on the BW vacuum is **not a peculiarity of the round-S³ Riemannian truncation**. It persists bit-exactly at signature (3,1) at the Riemannian limit."

In other words: even on the manifold side, the Krein framework does NOT use the spectral-triple Dirac as the time-evolution generator. The generator on the wedge KMS state is K_α^W (a boost), not D_W. This is signature-independent.

## 2. What the composed chemistry Hamiltonians actually are

`geovac/composed_qubit.py:build_composed_hamiltonian` produces a **second-quantized fermionic Hamiltonian** on the chemistry Fock space:

```
H_chem = Σ_pq h1[p,q] a_p† a_q  +  (1/2) Σ_pqrs eri[p,q,r,s] a_p† a_q† a_s a_r  +  E_nuc
       (JW-transformed to a Pauli operator on Q = 2M qubits)
```

The orbitals are hydrogenic with the natural Z_eff per block; h1 is diagonal in the block (atomic orbital energies −Z²/(2n²) plus PK/downfolded core), ERI is computed via hypergeometric Slater integrals (algebraic). The Hamiltonian acts on the **N-electron antisymmetric Fock space**, not on the Camporesi–Higuchi spinor bundle over S³.

This is in a categorically different mathematical category from the Krein Lorentzian Dirac D_L of Papers 43–47:

| Object | Paper 47 D_L | composed_qubit H_chem |
|:-------|:-------------|:-----------------------|
| Hilbert space | Camporesi–Higuchi spinor bundle ⊗ L²(ℝ_t) | N-electron fermionic Fock space (JW image: 2^Q qubits) |
| Generator | First-quantized Dirac (sum of one-body operators on spinors) | Second-quantized fermionic Hamiltonian (one-body + two-body) |
| Krein structure | J_L = γ⁰ ⊗ I (signature (3,1)) | None (positive-definite Hilbert) |
| Modular flow | Generated by K_α^W on hemispheric wedge | Not defined (the construction needs a wedge and a KMS state, neither of which the chemistry Fock space comes equipped with) |
| Time evolution | Norm-resolvent limit of i∂_t = D_L on S³ × ℝ_t | exp(−iH_chem t) on 2^Q-qubit space (Paper 14 Trotterization) |

Paper 14 already supplies a Trotter cost analysis (r ~ Q^{1.69} steps at ε = 10⁻³) for the composed Hamiltonians. That is the existing pathway for real-time chemistry dynamics on quantum hardware; the Krein arc is not in the same construction.

## 3. Where the Krein arc could connect to chemistry — three honest options

### Option A: First-quantized chemistry on S³ × ℝ_t via Camporesi–Higuchi (DEEP RE-CONSTRUCTION)

Take the Fock-projected hydrogen on S³ (Paper 7), lift to spinor bundle (Tier 2 / Paper 23 line), and add time as a fourth coordinate via Paper 47's S³ × ℝ_t carrier. The Lorentzian Dirac D_L = i(γ⁰ ⊗ ∂_t + D_GV ⊗ I) IS the time-evolution generator for single-electron Dirac dynamics on S³.

This is honest, structurally clean, and inherits norm-resolvent convergence from Paper 47. But it is **one-electron only** — it does not produce a multi-electron chemistry Hamiltonian. To extend to chemistry, one would need:
- A tensor-product version (Paper 39 / Paper 54 architecture) lifting to N spinor bundles.
- Antisymmetrization (which would go through Slater determinants on the spinor bundle, not via JW on a separate orbital basis).
- The two-body Coulomb piece. **Critical wall:** Sprint resolvent two-body (2026-06-01, in `memory/resolvent_two_body_negative.md`) showed that (D²)⁻¹ does NOT recover Coulomb on this substrate — the Fock-projection conformal factor stands in the way (Gegenbauer-on-S³ vs Slater-in-flat-space). Sprint nuclear + tensor-product spectral action (2026-06-01, Paper 54) gets the angular selection rules right but the radial weights only match Coulomb at Pearson 0.58, decreasing with n_max. **There is currently no proven route to a many-electron Coulomb chemistry Hamiltonian on the Krein S³ × ℝ_t carrier.**

### Option B: Wick-rotate the composed chemistry Hamiltonian directly (CATEGORY ERROR)

Treat H_chem as if it were a Krein-self-adjoint operator and apply BBB / van den Dungen. This **does not work** structurally:
- H_chem has no γ⁰ analog — it is a fermionic operator on Fock space, not a Dirac operator on a spinor bundle.
- There is no chirality grading γ_F = σ_x ⊗ I or similar on the chemistry Fock space (Sprint G3 confirmed γ_GV and γ_F are independent commuting Z₂'s, but the chemistry Hamiltonian does not sit in the γ_GV slot).
- The wedge construction K_α^W is a spatial rotation generator on the Hopf base of S³; the chemistry Fock space has no Hopf base.
- Without a Krein structure, there is no Lorentzian propinquity to convergence to.

Trying to force this would produce a paper with a fabricated framework — the kind of attribution error called out in `feedback_audit_numerical_claims` and the Fursaev-Solodukhin retraction (CLAUDE.md §3).

### Option C: Use the modular-flow / thermal-time technology to dress chemistry dynamics (SPECULATIVE)

Connes–Rovelli 1994 modular flow (Paper 49 substrate) gives a notion of "thermal time" from any KMS state on a von Neumann algebra. The chemistry Fock space supports KMS states (e.g., the canonical thermal state at temperature T for a chemistry Hamiltonian). The modular flow of that thermal state is generated by some operator related to H_chem; for a finite-dimensional bounded H, this is just σ_t(O) = e^{itH} O e^{−itH} (i.e., ordinary Heisenberg evolution).

This connection is **mathematically real but operationally vacuous** for chemistry simulation: the modular flow of the canonical Gibbs state of H_chem just IS the Heisenberg evolution under H_chem, which is what Paper 14's Trotter analysis already estimates. The Krein-space content adds no new structure here unless one is studying the **algebraic state** itself (entanglement entropy of a subsystem, KMS condition at finite β, twin-paradox-like inequalities) rather than time evolution of expectation values.

This would be a thin synthesis, not a discretization theorem.

## 4. Named obstructions

1. **Generator mismatch (load-bearing).** Paper 42 §7.2 / Theorem 4 + (3,1) Riemannian-limit refinement (Paper 42 Theorem 5): D_W ≠ K_α^W on the wedge KMS state, signature-independently. The framework's spectral-action Dirac is NOT the modular/time-evolution generator, even on the manifold side. Any chemistry-side application that treats H_chem as "the Lorentzian Dirac" would be re-creating the same conflation Paper 42 explicitly warns against.

2. **Inner-factor (G4) is orthogonal to the convergence theorem.** Every paper in the arc (45 §1.4 G4, 47 Q4, 48 §8) flags G4 as named-open. The chemistry Hamiltonian sits exactly on the inner-factor side (it parameterizes the matter content via Yukawa-like couplings — block exponents Z_eff, PK barriers, ERIs). The convergence theorems do not transport to the inner factor.

3. **Two-body Coulomb on Krein S³ × ℝ_t is not constructed.** Sprint resolvent two-body NEGATIVE (2026-06-01) and Sprint nuclear + tensor-product spectral action (2026-06-01) both got partial results; neither produces Coulomb with adequate fidelity at n_max → ∞. Without Coulomb, there is no chemistry.

4. **No Krein structure on the chemistry Fock space.** The composed JW Hamiltonian acts on a Hilbert space with positive-definite inner product. To apply BBB / van den Dungen Wick rotation, a Krein structure (J²=±I, γ⁰-like operator) must be constructed on the chemistry Fock space first. There is no published or in-house construction of this.

5. **Multi-focal-composition wall.** This sits inside the same class as Sprint H1 / LS-8a / HF-3/4/5 / W1e chemistry (6 instances in `memory/multi_focal_wall_pattern.md`): the framework has clean structural skeletons (here: Krein Lorentzian Dirac on S³ × ℝ_t) but no autonomous composition theorem for combining the skeleton with calibration-class inputs (here: the chemistry Hamiltonian's per-block data, PK barriers, ERIs).

## 5. What Paper 14 already does for chemistry dynamics

Paper 14 already supplies the only piece of real-time chemistry dynamics that the framework can honestly claim today: **Trotter cost estimation for the composed Hamiltonian**. This is the standard quantum-computing approach (Childs 2021 / Campbell 2019 commutator bounds, Lloyd's theorem, lambda-norm bounds). It works because:
- It treats H_chem as a Hermitian operator on a Hilbert space (which it is).
- It uses standard time evolution exp(−iH_chem t).
- The "natural geometry" advantage shows up in the Pauli count (O(Q^2.5) composed vs O(Q^3.9-4.3) Gaussian) and the lambda-norm scaling, not in any Lorentzian construction.

This is **exactly what the framework should offer for chemistry dynamics**, and it does not need or use the Krein arc. The Krein arc is doing different work: it is the operator-algebraic foundation for the GeoVac spectral triple's connection to Bisognano-Wichmann / Tomita-Takesaki / synthetic Lorentzian geometry, which is interesting math.OA and a substantive contribution to the field, but it is not a quantum-chemistry simulation primitive.

## 6. Verdict

**DEFER.** The framework is *in principle* compatible with real-time chemistry dynamics in two senses:
- (a) Paper 14's Trotter analysis already provides honest real-time dynamics on the composed Hamiltonians; this needs no Krein structure and is the production path.
- (b) The Krein-space framework as written applies to first-quantized Dirac dynamics on S³ × ℝ_t with one electron; extending to many-electron chemistry requires (i) a two-body Coulomb construction on the Krein carrier (currently failed at Sprint resolvent two-body and Sprint nuclear + tensor-product), (ii) an inner-factor (G4) extension that is named-open in every math.OA paper of the arc, and (iii) careful handling of the D_W ≠ K_α^W generator distinction.

The prerequisite for (b) is **multi-month**, not sprint-scale. Specifically:
- 1–3 month sprint: two-body Coulomb on Krein S³ × ℝ_t (deep extension of Paper 54's tensor-product spectral action to the Lorentzian carrier).
- Followed by: 2–6 month inner-factor sprint applying the G4 program to chemistry (which has no existing scaffolding in any math.OA paper).

**Recommended near-term direction:**
1. Continue using Paper 14's Trotter analysis as the production path for real-time chemistry dynamics on quantum hardware. Don't re-frame this as a Krein-arc product.
2. The Krein arc's substantive math.OA contribution is real and important — it should NOT be re-packaged as a chemistry tool. Doing so would be a category-error sales pitch.
3. If real-time chemistry dynamics with Krein structure is a strategic target, the right move is a **structural sprint** on the inner factor / two-body Coulomb on S³ × ℝ_t first, as a prerequisite. This is multi-month, but it builds on existing scaffolding (Paper 54, the resolvent / tensor-product negatives, Paper 47's three-carrier identification).
4. **Optional thin synthesis (sprint-scale, low risk):** The honest connection between the two arcs is via **algebraic-state observables of chemistry dynamics** — entanglement entropy of a fragment, KMS condition at finite T, modular-flow / thermal-time interpretation of canonical ensembles. This is observational, not constructive, and would be a Paper 26 / 27-style entanglement-arc extension. NOT a discretization theorem; not a chemistry dynamics product.
