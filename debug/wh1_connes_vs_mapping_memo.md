## §1. Connes–van Suijlekom framework summary (~430 words)

Connes & van Suijlekom (CMP 2021, arXiv:2004.14115) extend noncommutative geometry to
finite-resolution settings by allowing $C^*$-algebras to be replaced by **operator
systems** — $*$-closed unital linear subspaces of $\mathcal{B}(\mathcal{H})$ that are
*not* required to be multiplicatively closed. The motivation, in their words, is "imposing
an ultraviolet cutoff in momentum space" and treating "metric spaces up to finite
resolution".

**The spectral truncation construction.** Given a spectral triple $(\mathcal{A},
\mathcal{H}, D)$ with $D$ self-adjoint and compact resolvent, choose a self-adjoint
spectral projection $P_\Lambda = \chi_{[-\Lambda, \Lambda]}(D)$ onto the span of
eigenvectors of $D$ with eigenvalue magnitude $\le \Lambda$. The triple of
"truncated" objects is

$$
\mathcal{O}_\Lambda := P_\Lambda \mathcal{A} P_\Lambda, \qquad \mathcal{H}_\Lambda :=
P_\Lambda \mathcal{H}, \qquad D_\Lambda := P_\Lambda D P_\Lambda.
$$

Because $P_\Lambda$ does not commute with elements of $\mathcal{A}$, the compressed space
$\mathcal{O}_\Lambda$ is not closed under multiplication; it is an operator system but
not a $*$-algebra. Forcing closure (taking the $C^*$-envelope $C^*(\mathcal{O}_\Lambda)$)
typically collapses to a "non-informative full matrix algebra" (per ResearchGate
paragraph quoted above) — i.e. the algebraic structure surviving truncation is *strictly
weaker* than $C^*$, but strictly more than a vector space.

**Operator-system invariants.** Connes & van Suijlekom introduce the **propagation
number** $\mathrm{prop}(\mathcal{O})$ as an invariant of an operator system, stable under
Choi–Effros stable equivalence, that measures how far $\mathcal{O}$ is from its
$C^*$-envelope. For the circle truncation $\mathcal{O}_N$ (functions on $S^1$ truncated
to Fourier modes of magnitude $\le N$, realized as $(N+1)\times(N+1)$ Toeplitz matrices),
$\mathrm{prop}(\mathcal{O}_N)$ counts the bandwidth (off-diagonal reach) of allowed
operator products. They contrast spectral truncation against *circulant matrices* (the
finite Fourier transform): both are finite approximations of the circle, but circulants
form a $C^*$-algebra with a discrete isometry group, while spectral truncations are
operator systems with the **continuous isometry group preserved** (the rotations of
$S^1$ act on $\mathcal{O}_N$ verbatim).

**State spaces and the metric.** The fundamental object is now the *state space*
$\mathcal{S}(\mathcal{O}_\Lambda)$, equipped with the Connes distance
$d_D(\phi, \psi) = \sup\{|\phi(a) - \psi(a)| : \|[D, a]\| \le 1\}$, restricted to
$a \in \mathcal{O}_\Lambda$. States, not algebra morphisms, replace points.

**Recovery of the continuum.** van Suijlekom (arXiv:2005.08544) and the followups
Leimbach–vS (Adv. Math. 2024, tori) and the UCP-maps paper (arXiv:2410.15454) prove
**Gromov–Hausdorff convergence**: the state spaces $(\mathcal{S}(\mathcal{O}_\Lambda),
d_{D_\Lambda})$ converge in the GH metric as $\Lambda \to \infty$ to the Borel
probability measure space on the underlying classical manifold equipped with the
Monge–Kantorovich (Wasserstein-1) distance. The technical certificate is a
$C^1$-approximate (complete) order isomorphism (Leimbach–vS Definition 1.2; UCP-maps
Definition 3.4): unital completely positive maps $\rho_\Lambda, \sigma_\Lambda$ between
the truncation and the original triple, contractive in both norm and Lipschitz
seminorm $\|[D, \cdot]\|$, with composition error $\to 0$.

**Worked examples to date.** Circle $S^1$ (Connes–vS 2021; Hekkelman LMP 2022;
Hekkelman–McDonald 2024 noncommutative integral). Tori $T^d$ (Leimbach–vS 2024). Matrix
algebras converging to the **2-sphere** via Berezin–Toeplitz quantization (cited in
2005.08544 abstract). To my knowledge, no published Connes-vS-style spectral-truncation
analysis exists for $S^3$. *Curved manifolds beyond Berezin–Toeplitz $S^2$ are not
treated*; UCP-maps paper §4 is explicit that "applications address flat structures only".

## §3. Identified gaps and structural mismatches

Listed in priority order (most consequential first).

**Gap 1 (Row 3, VIOLATION-LEANING). The GeoVac algebra $\mathcal{A}_{\mathrm{GV}}$ is the
$C^*$-envelope, not the operator system.** This is the single most important finding of
this round. GeoVac's Paper 32 / Paper 7 conventions present $\mathcal{A}_{\mathrm{GV}}$
as multiplication operators on the discrete node set — i.e. as $\mathbb{C}^N$ acting
diagonally on $\mathcal{H}_{\mathrm{GV}}$. Connes & van Suijlekom warn that this is
exactly the *collapsed* $C^*$-envelope construction that destroys the operator-system
information. The genuine Connes-vS truncation of $C^\infty(S^3)$ is
$P_{n_{\max}} C^\infty(S^3) P_{n_{\max}}$, which has nontrivial matrix elements
$\langle (n, l, m) | f | (n', l', m') \rangle = \int_{S^3} \overline{Y_{nlm}}\, f \,
Y_{n' l' m'}\, dV$ between distinct shells — and these are *exactly* the Gaunt-coefficient
integrals that GeoVac already computes for ERIs (Paper 22, hypergeometric Slater integrals).
**The data exists; it has not been organized as the operator system $\mathcal{O}_{n_{\max}}$.**

The downstream consequences are large. The propagation number, the Connes distance on
states, and the Berezin-Toeplitz-style recovery limit all depend on $\mathcal{O}_{n_{\max}}$
having nontrivial off-diagonal structure. Without it, every Connes-vS test is degenerate.

**Gap 2 (Row 9, UNCLEAR). GH convergence is not published for $S^3$.** Connes-vS, the
Hekkelman thesis, the Hekkelman–McDonald 2024 paper, and Leimbach–vS 2024 cover the
circle, the $d$-torus, and (via Berezin–Toeplitz) the 2-sphere. The UCP-maps paper
(arXiv:2410.15454) is explicit that "applications address flat structures only". $S^3$
is curved, but it is also a *parallelizable* compact Lie group, and the Leimbach–vS
$T^d$ proof relies essentially on the spectral Fejér kernel constructed from group
characters — which has a clean Peter–Weyl analog on $\mathrm{SU}(2)$. A GH-convergence
theorem for $\mathrm{SU}(2) = S^3$ should exist; nobody has proved it. This is the
natural target of round 3.

**Gap 3 (Rows 7 and 8, UNCLEAR). The propagation number and Connes distance are not
computed on the GeoVac graph.** Both are computable — they are finite, exact-rational
operations once $\mathcal{O}_{n_{\max}}$ is given the correct off-diagonal structure
(Gap 1). These are the natural round-2 computations because they require no new
mathematics, only re-routing of existing GeoVac integral data into the Connes-vS object.

**Gap 4 (Row 10, UNCLEAR). The fate of the real structure $J$ under spectral truncation
is not explicitly covered in any Connes-vS paper I found.** Paper 32 §IV.4 makes the
expected match to KO-dim 3 of $S^3$, but flags as open whether the match holds at every
finite $n_{\max}$ or only in the continuum limit. This is a foundational axiom for
fermion physics on the truncation; without it the spinor sector lacks a charge
conjugation. Round 3 candidate.

**Gap 5 (Row 12, PROJECTION-MEDIATED). The combination of spectral truncation with
almost-commutative tensoring is itself novel.** Marcolli–vS 2014 develops the AC
tensoring on graphs but not in the context of the Connes-vS truncation framework.
Connes-vS 2021 develops the truncation framework but not in the AC setting. GeoVac
would be the first explicit construction *combining* both — provided Gap 1 is fixed.
This is significant because it means WH1's strongest reading ("GeoVac is an AC spectral
truncation of the continuum hydrogen ST") is, even if true, not directly verifiable
against any published worked example: it would itself be the first.

**Non-gap (worth flagging).** The cutoff $\Lambda$ being a Dirac eigenvalue (rather
than a continuous parameter) is *not* a mismatch. Hekkelman–McDonald Lemma 2.6 explicitly
allows passage between continuous-$\Lambda$ and discrete-eigenvalue indexing. The
Camporesi–Higuchi monotonicity $|\lambda_n| = n + 3/2$ in $n$ means picking
$\Lambda = n_{\max} + 3/2$ exactly recovers the Fock truncation.

## §4. Preliminary verdict on WH1

**ALIGN-WITH-CAVEATS.**

The Connes-vS framework was constructed for exactly the situation GeoVac is in: a
compact spectral triple, a Dirac operator with discrete spectrum, a finite-dimensional
truncation chosen by a spectral projection. The $\mathrm{SO}(4)$ isometry preservation
that distinguishes Connes-vS truncation from circulant-discretization (Row 13) is the
strongest single match in the table — and is a *non-trivial* property that any naive
discretization would lose. Rows 1, 2, 4, 11, 13 are CLEAN. The recovery limit (Row 9)
is plausible by analogy with $T^d$ but not proved for $S^3$.

The single named caveat is **Gap 1 (Row 3)**: GeoVac's currently-used algebra
$\mathcal{A}_{\mathrm{GV}} = \mathbb{C}^N$ (diagonal multiplication on nodes) is the
$C^*$-envelope, not the operator system. To literally instantiate "GeoVac at finite
$n_{\max}$ IS the spectral truncation of the continuum $\mathrm{H}$ spectral triple",
GeoVac must adopt $\mathcal{O}_{n_{\max}} = P_{n_{\max}} C^\infty(S^3) P_{n_{\max}}$ as
its algebra-image-data. The matrix elements are already computed (they are the
hypergeometric Slater / Gaunt overlap integrals); they are simply not organized into
the operator system.

This is not a falsification of WH1. It is a **routing observation**: GeoVac at finite
$n_{\max}$ contains exactly the information needed to *be* the Connes-vS truncation,
but the conventional GeoVac algebra throws it away. With the correction, alignment is
plausible across the entire table, modulo proving the GH-convergence theorem for $S^3$
that nobody has published.

The previously logged WH1 status of MODERATE-STRONG (CLAUDE.md §1.7) was based on
Marcolli-vS lineage placement (which is unaffected by anything in this memo). This
memo *adds* the Connes-vS spectral-truncation reading as a second, independent piece
of evidence for WH1 — at the structural level, *modulo* the row-3 routing fix and the
absence of a published $S^3$ GH-convergence theorem.
