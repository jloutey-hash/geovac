## 4. Chain composition counts (Step 2)

For each pair $(T_1, T_2)$ of single-step transitions, the matrix product $T_2 \cdot T_1 = e_{s_3} \cdot (\kappa A) \cdot e_{s_2} \cdot e_{s_1} \cdot (\kappa A) \cdot e_{s_0}$ is non-zero iff $s_2 = s_1$ (intermediate sector matches) AND the path $s_0 \to s_1 \to s_3$ has non-zero composite operator content. By sector orthogonality, $e_{s_2} e_{s_1} = \delta_{s_1, s_2} e_{s_1}$.

Classification of the resulting chain composition by the relation between $s_0$ and $s_3$:

| Category | $n_{\max} = 2$ | $n_{\max} = 3$ | Description |
|:---------|:--------------:|:--------------:|:------------|
| Total pairs $(T_1, T_2)$ | $12^2 = 144$ | $28^2 = 784$ | All ordered pairs |
| Non-chain ($s_1 \ne s_2$) | 114 | 688 | Vanish by sector orthogonality |
| Chain, zero product | 0 | 0 | All chain compositions are non-zero |
| Chain, idempotent-landing ($s_0 = s_3$) | 12 | 28 | Equals $n_{\mathrm{trans}}$ — each directed edge contributes one closed loop |
| Chain, single-step-block ($\|s_3.n - s_0.n\| = 1$, $s_0 \ne s_3$) | 14 | 44 | New element in an existing (s_0, s_3) single-step block |
| Chain, two-step-block ($\|s_3.n - s_0.n\| \in \{0, 2\}$, $s_0 \ne s_3$) | 4 | 24 | New element in a previously-empty (s_0, s_3) block |
| **Total chain compositions** | **30** | **96** | Total = number of directed two-step walks $u \to v \to w$ in the sector quiver |

### Quiver-combinatorial identification

The total chain compositions equal **the number of length-2 directed walks** in the (bidirected) sector quiver. Specifically:

- **Idempotent-landing count = $n_{\mathrm{trans}}$.** Each undirected edge $\{u, v\}$ contributes two directed transitions $T_{u \to v}$ and $T_{v \to u}$. The product $T_{v \to u} \cdot T_{u \to v}$ lands on $e_u$; the reverse product lands on $e_v$. So each undirected edge generates 2 idempotent-landing chains; total = number of directed edges = $n_{\mathrm{trans}}$.

- **Single-step-block + two-step-block landing counts = number of length-2 walks $u \to v \to w$ with $u \ne w$**, where $u = s_0$, $v = s_1 = s_2$, $w = s_3$. At $n_{\max} = 2$: 18 such walks (verified). At $n_{\max} = 3$: 68 such walks (verified bit-exact, see driver output).

This is a derived (not fit) identity — the chain composition count equals the quiver walk count by direct application of $e_t \cdot e_s = \delta_{ts} e_s$.

### Important nuance (not in T3b memo)

The T3b memo at $n_{\max} = 2$ stated "18 of the 30 chain compositions land on TWO-STEP transitions outside the basic single-step generator basis", lumping all non-idempotent landings as "two-step". The finer classification here separates these 18 into:
- 14 landings in **single-step blocks** ($|s_3.n - s_0.n| = 1$). For these, the (s_0, s_3) sector pair IS a basic generator's block, but the produced element $T_2 \cdot T_1$ is generally NOT a scalar multiple of $T_{s_0 \to s_3}$ — it's a linearly-independent matrix in the same block (the basic single-step transition occupies only 1 dim of a $\ge 4$-dim block).
- 4 landings in **genuinely-new two-step blocks** ($|s_3.n - s_0.n| \in \{0, 2\}$), where the (s_0, s_3) pair has no basic single-step generator at all.

Both classes contribute to algebra-closure failure; the algebra needs new generators in BOTH cases. The total dim added at the first iteration step is encoded in the closure dim 84 at $n_{\max} = 2$ (vs. 17 basic generators, so 67 new elements added under closure).

## 5. Per-block closure dimension (Step 3 + Step 4)

The closure $\mathcal{A}_{\mathrm{OD}}^{(n_{\max})}$ is the smallest matrix subalgebra of $M_{\dim H}(\mathbb{Q})$ containing the idempotents $\{e_s\}$ and single-step transitions $\{T_{s' \to s}\}$. Equivalently:

$$\mathcal{A}_{\mathrm{OD}}^{(n_{\max})} \;=\; \mathbb{Q}\langle e_s, T_{s' \to s} \rangle \;=\; \mathbb{Q}\langle e_s, A_{\mathrm{off}} \rangle$$

where $A_{\mathrm{off}} = D - \Lambda = \kappa A_{\mathrm{graph}}$ is the off-diagonal Dirac perturbation (since $T_{s' \to s} = e_s A_{\mathrm{off}} e_{s'}$ and any element of $\mathcal{A}_{\mathrm{OD}}$ is a $\mathbb{Q}$-linear combination of words $e_{s_k} A e_{s_{k-1}} A \ldots A e_{s_0}$).

By Cayley-Hamilton, words of length $\ge \dim H$ are linearly dependent on shorter words, so the closure has dimension at most $\dim H \cdot N^2$ (and at most $\dim H^2$ overall).

### Per-block computation

For each ordered sector pair $(s_{\mathrm{from}}, s_{\mathrm{to}})$, the closure restricted to that block is

$$\mathcal{A}_{\mathrm{OD}}^{(s_{\mathrm{from}}, s_{\mathrm{to}})} \;=\; \mathrm{span}_{\mathbb{Q}}\big\{ e_{s_{\mathrm{to}}} \cdot A_{\mathrm{off}}^k \cdot e_{s_{\mathrm{from}}} \;:\; k = 0, 1, \ldots, \dim H - 1 \big\}$$

as a subspace of $\mathrm{End}(e_{s_{\mathrm{from}}} \mathcal{H}, e_{s_{\mathrm{to}}} \mathcal{H}) \cong M_{\dim s_{\mathrm{to}}, \dim s_{\mathrm{from}}}(\mathbb{Q})$.

The driver computes $A^0, A^1, \ldots, A^{\dim H - 1}$ once, then extracts each block $(s_{\mathrm{from}}, s_{\mathrm{to}})$ for each power, flattens, and computes the rank via sympy.

### Structural pattern of per-block ranks

Examining the data per block at $n_{\max} = 3$:
- For (small × small) blocks where both $\dim_{s_{\mathrm{to}}} = \dim_{s_{\mathrm{from}}} = 2$ (the spin-only sectors $l = 0$): rank = **2** (not the naïve 4 = 2×2). These blocks fill 50% of the ambient $M_2(\mathbb{Q})$.
- For (small × medium-large) blocks (one dim = 2, other = 4, 6, 10): rank = **2 × small_dim_count_in_word**, generally close to $2 \cdot \min$ but with finer modifications.
- For (medium × medium) blocks where both $\dim_{s_{\mathrm{to}}} = \dim_{s_{\mathrm{from}}} = 6$: rank = **12 or 14** depending on diagonal vs off-diagonal.
- The biggest single block (3, 2) → (3, 2) of dim 100 has rank **15** — fills only 15%.

No simple closed form $\mathrm{rank} = f(\dim_{s_{\mathrm{to}}}, \dim_{s_{\mathrm{from}}})$ fits all blocks. The rank depends on the specific adjacency structure of $A$ around each sector.

### Growth analysis (closure dim)

$$\dim \mathcal{A}_{\mathrm{OD}}^{(n_{\max} = 2)} = 84, \qquad \dim \mathcal{A}_{\mathrm{OD}}^{(n_{\max} = 3)} = 592$$

Growth ratio: $592/84 = 148/21 \approx 7.05$ (bit-exact rational).

Candidate scaling laws:
- $\dim H^2$ ratio: $1600/256 = 25/4 = 6.25$
- $\dim H^{2.13}$ would give 7.05 (best fit, but **only two data points — extrapolation is unreliable** per `feedback_audit_numerical_claims`)
- $\dim H \cdot N$ ratio: $(40 \cdot 9)/(16 \cdot 5) = 360/80 = 4.5$
- $\dim H^2 \cdot N$ ratio: $25/4 \cdot 9/5 = 45/4 = 11.25$

**Honest scope**: the closure dim grows faster than $\dim H^2$ but slower than $\dim H^2 \cdot N$. The fill fraction $\dim \mathcal{A}_{\mathrm{OD}} / \dim H^2$ increases monotonically from 21/64 to 37/100 — extrapolating to identify a limit (1/2? log? bounded fraction?) requires at least one more data point. This is the named sprint-scale follow-on (a).

The honest structural claim is: **the closure is bounded by $\dim H^2$ but a non-trivial fraction of it (>1/3) as $n_{\max}$ grows.** This is the "path-algebra of the sector quiver realized in $M_N(\mathbb{Q})$" phenomenon — the closure encodes ALL multi-step E1 dipole transitions among sectors, not just single-step ones.

## 6. Iterative algebra closure (Step 3 alternate)

The closure can also be computed by iterative addition of products, level by level:

- **Level 0** (idempotents): 5 at $n_{\max} = 2$, 9 at $n_{\max} = 3$
- **Level 1** (single-step transitions): adds 12 (→ 17) at $n_{\max} = 2$, 28 (→ 37) at $n_{\max} = 3$

- **Level 3** (products of level-2 basis): adds 52 (→ 92) at $n_{\max} = 2$
- **Level 4** (products of level-3 basis): adds 7 (→ 99) at $n_{\max} = 2$

Then closure converges at level 5 to ~84 (the iterative process has redundancy — when a product is computed, all sub-products from prior levels are also computed; the per-block characterization (Section 5) gives the cleaner bit-exact answer 84 at $n_{\max} = 2$).

The iterative computation at $n_{\max} = 3$ would require many more iterations (the basis is much larger) and is computationally expensive. The per-block characterization via powers $A^k$ is the efficient route used in this sprint.

## 7. Lie sub-algebra closure test (Step 5)

The linear span of single-step transitions $\mathcal{T}^{(n_{\max})} := \mathrm{span}_{\mathbb{Q}}\{T_{s' \to s}\}$ has dimension $n_{\mathrm{trans}}(n_{\max})$ (transitions are linearly independent because their supports are disjoint sector blocks):
- $\dim \mathcal{T}^{(2)} = 12$
- $\dim \mathcal{T}^{(3)} = 28$

Commutator test: for each pair $T_a, T_b$ of distinct single-step transitions, compute $[T_a, T_b] = T_a T_b - T_b T_a$ in the matrix algebra and check whether $[T_a, T_b] \in \mathcal{T}^{(n_{\max})}$.

| $n_{\max}$ | Commutator pairs $\binom{n_{\mathrm{trans}}}{2}$ | $[T_a, T_b] = 0$ | $[T_a, T_b] \in \mathcal{T}$ | $[T_a, T_b]$ outside $\mathcal{T}$ |
|:---:|:--:|:--:|:--:|:--:|
| 2 | 66 | 42 | 0 | **24** |
| 3 | 378 | 296 | 0 | **82** |

**At every cutoff, NOT A SINGLE non-zero commutator lies inside the transition span.** Every non-vanishing commutator generates new linearly-independent matrix content (idempotent-landing or two-step-block content). This is the **structural source of non-primitivity** — the bracket structure of $\mathcal{T}$ alone does not close on $\mathcal{T}$.

Outside-span growth: $82/24 \approx 3.42$, close to (but not equal to) $(N(3)/N(2))^2 = 81/25 = 3.24$. The match is suggestive of a quadratic-in-$N$ scaling for the bracket-cost but not bit-exact (`feedback_audit_numerical_claims`).

## 9. Structural pattern + identifying the closure (synthesis)

The closure $\mathcal{A}_{\mathrm{OD}}^{(n_{\max})} = \mathbb{Q}\langle e_s, A_{\mathrm{off}} \rangle$ has the following structural identification:

1. As a $\mathbb{Q}$-subalgebra of $M_N(\mathbb{Q})$, it is the smallest unital algebra containing all sector projectors and the off-diagonal Dirac perturbation.

2. Equivalently, it is the algebra of **operators on $\mathcal{H}$ that respect the sector decomposition** in a graded way — sending $e_{s_0} \mathcal{H}$ to $\bigoplus_{k} e_{s_k} \mathcal{H}$ where the path $s_0 \to s_1 \to \ldots \to s_k$ runs through E1-connected sectors.

3. As a finite-dimensional algebra over $\mathbb{Q}$, it has a block-decomposition by (s_from, s_to) sector pairs:
   $$\mathcal{A}_{\mathrm{OD}}^{(n_{\max})} \;=\; \bigoplus_{s_{\mathrm{from}}, s_{\mathrm{to}}} \mathcal{A}_{\mathrm{OD}}^{(s_{\mathrm{from}}, s_{\mathrm{to}})}$$
   where each block has dimension $\le \dim_{s_{\mathrm{from}}} \cdot \dim_{s_{\mathrm{to}}}$ (the upper bound is achieved only if every entry in the block is realized as a Q-linear combination of multi-step E1 words).

4. The **path-algebra interpretation** of the closure is consistent with Connes-Kreimer's sub-graph nesting structure (Connes-Kreimer 1998, Connes-Marcolli 2007). The closure $\mathcal{A}_{\mathrm{OD}}$ is finite-dimensional at each cutoff, but its inductive limit as $n_{\max} \to \infty$ is the natural domain for the conjectural pro-unipotent factor of the Hopf algebra side of the cosmic-Galois $U^*$ bridge.

5. **Honest scope**: at this scoping step, we have not extracted the closed-form structure constants of the closure or the explicit Hopf-algebra cohomology classes that would emerge from non-primitive coproduct extension. The closure dim is bit-exact identified; the next sprint-scale step is to either (a) extend to $n_{\max} = 4$ for a third data point, or (b) compute the closed-form rank in each (s_from, s_to) block from quiver-combinatorial input alone (rank = some function of the sector dims and the cycle structure of the sector quiver — derivable in principle, computationally feasible for small $n_{\max}$).
