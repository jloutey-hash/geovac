INVESTIGATION 3: Self-Consistency from the Fibration
============================================================================

a) First Chern number of the Hopf bundle:
   c₁ = 1 (well-known: H²(S²; Z) = Z, generator is Hopf bundle)
   The Hopf bundle S¹ → S³ → S² has c₁ = 1.
   This is verified by: ∫_{S²} (i/2π)F = 1
   where F is the curvature 2-form of the connection.

b) Chern-Simons invariant CS(S³):
   For the Levi-Civita connection on S³ with the round metric:
   CS_grav = (1/16π²) ∫_{S³} Tr(ω∧dω + ⅔ ω∧ω∧ω)

   S³ = ∂D⁴ (bounds a 4-disk), so by the APS index theorem:
   CS(S³) ≡ 0 (mod 1)

   For SU(2) Chern-Simons at level k, the partition function is:
   Z_k(S³) = √(2/(k+2)) · sin(π/(k+2))
     k =  1: Z_k(S³) = 0.70710678
     k =  2: Z_k(S³) = 0.50000000
     k =  3: Z_k(S³) = 0.37174803
     k =  4: Z_k(S³) = 0.28867513
     k =  5: Z_k(S³) = 0.23192061
     k = 42: Z_k(S³) = 0.01520956

   Z_1(S³) = √(2/3)·sin(π/3) = 0.70710678
   1/Z_1(S³) = 1.41421356  (not related to α)

c) η-invariant of the Dirac operator on S³:
   Eigenvalues of Dirac on unit S³: ±(n + 3/2) for n = 0,1,2,...
   with multiplicity (n+1)(n+2) for each sign.
   The spectrum is perfectly symmetric ⟹ η(0) = 0.
   (This holds for ALL odd-dimensional round spheres by symmetry.)
   η(0) = 0

   The η-function η(s) = Σ sign(λ)|λ|^{-s} vanishes identically
   for S³ because the spectrum is symmetric: η(s) = 0 for all s.

d) Can the cubic arise from {c₁, CS, η, det} ?

   Available topological/spectral data:
     c₁ = 1
     CS(S³) = 0 (mod 1)
     η(0) = 0
     det'(Δ_{S¹}) = 4π² = 39.478418
     det'(Δ_{S²}) = 1.938054
     det'(Δ_{S³}) = 3.338851

   The cubic x³ − Kx + 1 = 0 is a depressed cubic (no x² term).
   Depressed cubics arise from:
     • Tschirnhaus transform of general cubics
     • Resolvent of a quartic
     • Algebraic relations on elliptic curves
     • Characteristic polynomial of a traceless 3×3 matrix

   CRITICAL OBSERVATION: S³ ≅ SU(2).
   SU(2) has a 3D adjoint representation (Lie algebra su(2)).
   The Killing form on su(2) provides a natural 3×3 metric.
   Could the cubic be the characteristic polynomial of a natural
   operator on the 3D Lie algebra?

   The SU(2) structure constants ε_{ijk} define an operator
   on ℝ³ (the Lie algebra). The Casimir C₂ = Σ T_i² = j(j+1)·I
   for spin-j representation. In adjoint (j=1): C₂ = 2·I₃.
   This is proportional to identity → char poly = (λ−2)³,
   which is NOT a depressed cubic. Dead end for this construction.

   Alternative: The 'structure matrix' of the fibration.
   Define M = diag(d₁, d₂, d₃) where d_i are dimensions of S^i.
   M = diag(1, 2, 3). Char poly = (λ−1)(λ−2)(λ−3)
   = λ³ − 6λ² + 11λ − 6. Not our cubic.

   A 3x3 matrix from c1=1, CS=0, eta=0 has too many zeros
   to produce a non-trivial cubic.

   FINDING: The topological invariants (c₁=1, CS=0, η=0) are too
   simple to generate the cubic directly. The cubic would need to
   come from SPECTRAL data (eigenvalues, determinants) rather than
   purely topological data.

INVESTIGATION 4: QED Comparison
============================================================================

a) One-loop QED correction vs cubic correction:

   Cubic correction:        δ(α⁻¹) = α² = 5.325135e-05
   QED 1-loop (α/π):        δ(α⁻¹) ~ α/π = 2.322819e-03
   Ratio: (α/π) / α² = 1/(πα) = 43.6199
   ≈ K/π = 43.6199

   The cubic correction α² is ~44× SMALLER than
   the QED one-loop correction α/π.
   They differ by a factor of 1/(πα) ≈ 43.6 ≈ K/π.

   The cubic correction is roughly at the THIRD-LOOP level in QED:
   QED 3-loop: ~ (α/π)³ ≈ 1.253275e-08
   α²          =         5.325135e-05
   Ratio: α²/(α/π)³ = π³/α = 4249.0
   Not a clean match. The cubic correction does NOT correspond to
   any specific order in the QED perturbative expansion.

b) Casimir energy on S³:

   For a conformally-coupled massless scalar on S³ of radius R:
   E_Casimir = 1/(240R)  [Dowker & Critchley]

   For the electromagnetic field (massless spin-1) on S³:
   Modes: transverse vector harmonics on S³
   Eigenvalues: (n+1)² for n = 1, 2, 3, ... with mult 2(2n+1)
   (two polarizations × angular degeneracy)
   E_Casimir (scalar, R=1) = 1/240 = 0.004167
   E_Casimir (EM, R=1)    = 11/120 = 0.091667
   α²                     =         5.325135e-05
   α²/E_Casimir(EM)       =         5.809239e-04
   E_Casimir(EM)/α        =         12.5616

   The Casimir energy is O(1), not O(α²). The correction α²
   does not naturally arise from the Casimir energy of a free
   field on S³. This would require a self-consistent coupling
   where the field strength depends on α.

c) Discrete mode sum (zeta-regularized):

   For the Hodge Laplacian on 1-forms on unit S³:
   Eigenvalues: n² − 1 (co-exact, n≥2) and n² − 1 (exact, n≥2)
   with multiplicities related to 2n+1 and 2n−1.

   Σ 2(2n+1)·n(n+2) [zeta-reg] = 4ζ(−3) + 10ζ(−2) + 4ζ(−1)
   = 4/120 + 0 + 4(−1/12) = 1/30 − 1/3 = -0.300000
   = −3/10 (not related to α)

   FINDING: QED corrections at no order match α² quantitatively.
   On compact S³, the Casimir energy is O(1/R), not O(α²).
   The cubic correction α² ≈ 5.3×10⁻⁵ sits between QED orders
   and does not correspond to any standard perturbative term.
   This is CONSISTENT with the paper's claim that the cubic is
   an 'exact resummation', not a truncation of perturbation theory.
