## 1. Headline (what is inherited wholesale vs ours to build)

On the **electron side the threshold-bridging analytic structure is fully published and
INHERITABLE**: Bander–Itzykson (1966) prove the bound spectrum carries the compact SO(4)
and the scattering continuum carries the non-compact SO(3,1), with the Euclidean group
E(3) sitting at the E=0 contraction point — the contraction parameter is literally our
own focal length p₀ = √(−2E) → 0; and Seaton's quantum-defect theory already supplies the
single analytic function μ(E) that continues discrete bound levels smoothly into the
scattering phase shift across E=0. We **ADAPT** (not invent) the threshold density-of-states
divergence, the Efimov/discrete-scale-invariance picture, and the position/momentum
information-entropy machinery (Bialynicki-Birula–Mycielski) to read this as our
closed→open boundary. The **non-unital spectral triple target is also inherited** (Rennie;
Gayral–Gracia-Bondía–Iochum–Schücker–Várilly Moyal planes) — R³/H³ as a non-compact
manifold is a standard non-unital triple. The **genuinely-ours-to-BUILD** piece is the
*spatial-geometric* propinquity convergence itself: the published propinquity results go
the *wrong direction* (finite matrices **collapsing onto** the curved sphere, Rieffel) or
handle *flat/temporal* non-compact limits (Latrémolière's 2025 pointed-proper hypertopology,
which we already used for S¹_β → R) — **none performs a curved, symmetry-contracting
spatial decompactification S³ → R³ / S³ → H³.** That theorem, marrying a propinquity limit
to an Inönü–Wigner group contraction across a signature boundary, does not exist in the
literature and is ours to construct.

## 3. Explicit answer to the headline NCG question (item 7)

**Question:** Does the non-compact propinquity machinery transport from a temporal
aperture (S¹ → R) to a spatial one (S³ → R³ or S³ → H³)?

**Answer: the container transports; the theorem does not exist; the hard part is genuinely
new. Transport = no. Analogy/template = yes. The spatial theorem is ours to build.**

Three structural reasons the temporal proof does NOT directly carry over, each verified
against the actual published state of the field:

1. **Published spatial propinquity goes the wrong way.** Rieffel's *Matrix algebras
   converge to the sphere* (arXiv:math/0108005, VERIFIED) and its descendants (Toeplitz
   algebras → odd spheres, arXiv:2211.04321; Podleś spheres → sphere, Commun. Math. Phys.
   2022 — both VERIFIED) all take **finite-dimensional algebras collapsing ONTO a fixed
   curved sphere**. That is *truncation convergence at fixed geometry* (our Paper 38
   n_max → ∞ on S³ is exactly this kind). The spatial aperture we need is the **opposite**:
   the curved compact manifold S³ itself opening up to a non-compact flat/hyperbolic
   target as p₀ → 0. No published GH/propinquity result does this curved→non-compact
   direction.

2. **Flat vs curved, and a double limit.** S¹_β → R (Latrémolière 2512.03573, VERIFIED,
   the machinery we used) is a *flat-circle* decompactification: translation-invariant
   metric, linear Matsubara ladder 2πk/β, "uncompactify" = ladder spacing → 0, abelian
   Fejér/Berezin reconstruction. S³ → R³ is **curved**: quadratic n²−1 Laplace–Beltrami
   ladder, non-abelian Peter–Weyl reconstruction (our Paper 38), and decompactification is
   a *conformal/stereographic* limit p₀ → 0 entangled with n_max → ∞ — a **double limit**
   absent in the temporal case. The closest published curvature-deformation work
   (*Collapse in Noncommutative Geometry and Spectral Continuity*, arXiv:2404.00240,
   VERIFIED) perturbs a metric direction to zero (vertical collapse) but does **not**
   produce a sphere→Euclidean geometric opening.

3. **Threshold is a signature boundary + a group contraction, not a one-sided endpoint.**
   Temporally, β → ∞ is a clean one-sided limit (T → 0). Spatially, E = 0 separates two
   *different* regimes — E<0 on compact discrete S³ (SO(4)), E>0 on non-compact continuous
   H³/R³ (SO(3,1)), with the flat Euclidean group E(3) as the fixed point at E=0
   (Bander–Itzykson, VERIFIED). The natural object is therefore a **family interpolating
   across signature that simultaneously realizes an Inönü–Wigner group contraction at the
   operator-algebra level.** The propinquity literature handles metric convergence but
   **not simultaneous dynamical-symmetry-group contraction** — that marriage is unpublished
   and is the sharp BUILD. (It is closer in spirit to our own Lorentzian/Krein-signature
   arc, Papers 42–49, than to the S¹→R thermal limit.)

**Recommended construction order (for the PM, not part of the survey):** build the E=0
fixed point first — the E(3)-symmetric flat R³ limit, where the contraction is "complete"
and the target is the standard non-unital R³ triple (item 6) — before attempting the full
E>0 hyperbolic H³ continuum. That stages the new theorem behind one inherited fixed point.
