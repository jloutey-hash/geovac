## 3. Why this test sharpens the inclusion direction

TC-1e/TC-1f built an explicit inclusion

$$
\Phi: U^*_{\mathrm{Levi}} = \mathbb{G}_a^{3 N(n_{\max})} \rtimes SL_2 \hookrightarrow \mathrm{Aut}^\otimes(\omega)
$$

and verified the four Deligne–Milne axioms bit-exactly on a witness panel. That fixes one direction:\ every element of $U^*_{\mathrm{Levi}}$ comes from a natural $\otimes$-automorphism.

The **converse** direction — that *every* natural $\otimes$-automorphism is in $U^*_{\mathrm{Levi}}$ — is the substantive content of the Deligne–Milne 1982 reconstruction theorem (Theorem 2.11) and the full content of the multi-year wall named by PS-4 (v3.69.0). TC-2a computes this converse direction directly on the **abelian factor at finite cutoff** by:

1. Parameterising every potential natural $\otimes$-automorphism on the witness panel by symbolic $\mathbb{Q}$-indeterminates,
2. Imposing naturality bit-exactly on every morphism in the panel (cross-rep + endomorphism + unit-object),
3. Solving the resulting $\mathbb{Q}$-linear system and reading off the dimension of the solution variety.

If the solution variety had been strictly larger than 15, the inclusion would have been strict at finite cutoff (and we would have found extra automorphisms requiring identification). If strictly smaller, the inclusion proof would have a hole.

It is **exactly 15**, matching $\dim \mathbb{G}_a^{15}$, with the parameter map $q_g \leftrightarrow t_g$ identifying the solved $\eta$ with $\Phi(t)$ bit-exactly.

This is the **first quantitative test of the converse direction**, at the abelian-factor sub-claim. It is the strongest pre-reconstruction evidence available at $n_{\max} = 2$.
