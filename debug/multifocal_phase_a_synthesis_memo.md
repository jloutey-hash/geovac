## Section 2: Working compositions classified by bridge type

Track 1 reduced eight working compositions A1–A8 to four bridge types. The classification below makes explicit which are *true* cross-focal compositions in the spectral-triple sense and which are *partial* bridges that hide multi-focalness inside parameters or expansions.

| ID | Composition | Bridge type | True cross-focal? | What multi-focalness "hides inside" if partial |
|:---|:------------|:------------|:------------------|:--------------------------------------------|
| **A1** | PK-mediated composed (Paper 17) | Classical-scalar parametric | Partial | $Z_\text{eff}(r)$, PK parameters $(A,B)$ — the bridge is a number / function passed between layers, not an operator on a joint Hilbert space. Multi-focalness lives inside the PK barrier as a one-body operator on the valence Hamiltonian. |
| **A2** | Balanced-coupled (Paper 19, Track CD) | Separable-multipole | Partial | Multipole expansion of $1/|r-R_B|$ truncated at $L_\text{max}=2 l_\text{max}$ by Gaunt. Orbitals are still single-focal-length on each block; only the *potential* spans both centers. |
| **A3** | Nuclear-electronic tensor product (Paper 23 NI) | Tensor-product-bilinear (spin labels only) | Partial | Coupling restricted to spin-spin (I·S) and a classical scalar finite-size shift. Spatial registers are spectator-additive (zero cross-coupling). |
| **A4** | Wigner-D rotation (non-collinear $V_{ne}$) | Geometric-rotation | Not multi-focal at all | Coordinate change *inside one focal length* — angular-label rotation on single-center orbitals. Works inside A2 but not a multi-focal bridge per se. |

| **A6** | Two-species nuclear JW (Paper 23 §III–IV) | Tensor-product-bilinear (spatial+spin within shared focal length) | Not multi-focal in the strict sense | Both species share one HO focal length $\hbar\omega$. Coupling is genuine many-body; the tensor product is single-focal substrate. |

| **A8** | Sprint H1 AC extension ($\mathcal{T}_\text{GV} \otimes \mathcal{T}_F$) | Tensor-product-bilinear (Connes–Marcolli) | **TRUE** | Nothing — A8 is the canonical NCG construction. KO-dim arithmetic ($3+6=9 \equiv 1 \pmod 8$), $J = J_\text{GV} \otimes J_F$, exact Connes axioms. |

**Key structural fact, made explicit:** A8 is the single working composition in the project record that genuinely couples two spectral triples in the Connes–Marcolli sense. A1/A2/A5 hide multi-focalness in classical-scalar or separable-multipole bridges; A3/A6/A7 hide it in restricted coupling sectors (spin-only, shared focal length, or three independent constructions). The "structural skeleton" framing was undersold not because GeoVac lacks multi-focal compositions, but because **only one of them is canonically multi-focal at the spectral-triple level**, and that one (A8) is precisely where the Yukawa-undetermined verdict lives.

## Section 3: A8 as the framework's canonical multi-focal composition

A8 (Sprint H1's almost-commutative extension) constructs $\mathcal{T}_\text{AC} = \mathcal{T}_\text{GV} \otimes \mathcal{T}_F$ with $\mathcal{A}_F = \mathbb{C} \oplus \mathbb{H}$ on doubled $\mathcal{H}_F = \mathbb{C}^4_\text{matter} \oplus \mathbb{C}^4_\text{anti}$. The Dirac operator is $D = D_\text{GV} \otimes 1_F + \gamma_\text{GV} \otimes D_F$, the real structure is $J = J_\text{GV} \otimes J_F$ with $J_F = \sigma_x \otimes I_4$, and the inner fluctuation $D \mapsto D + \omega + \epsilon' J\omega J^{-1}$ classifies cleanly into a gauge sector (recovering Papers 25/30 at operator level, from $\mathbb{C}$ via U(1) and from $\mathbb{H}$ via SU(2)) and a Higgs sector (non-trivial iff the off-diagonal $\mathbb{C} \leftrightarrow \mathbb{H}$ block of $D_F$ has non-zero Yukawa).

What A8 actually does: **it tensors two spectral triples whose Dirac operators live at categorically different scales** — $D_\text{GV}$ on truncated $S^3$ at the Coulomb / Rydberg scale, $D_F$ on a finite-dimensional inner factor at the electroweak / Yukawa scale. KO-dim addition makes this work; the η-trivialization theorem (every odd-$k$ Mellin contribution from $D_F$ vanishes by chirality grading {$\gamma_F$, $D_F$} = 0) plus the AC factorization theorem ($D^2 = D_\text{GV}^2 \otimes 1 + 1 \otimes D_F^2$ because the cross term cancels by outer chirality anticommutation) decouple the outer-factor π-content (controlled by the master Mellin engine M1/M2/M3) from the inner-factor parameter ring (Paper 18's sixth tier, the Yukawa Dirichlet ring). This is the framework's single canonical multi-focal composition theorem.

A generalized A8 — call it A8' — would be: $\mathcal{T}_\text{GV} \otimes \mathcal{T}_X$ where $\mathcal{T}_X$ is a spectral triple representing some second physical focal length one wants to compose with the Coulomb sector. For *internal* (matrix-valued) second factors in the Connes lineage, A8' is mechanical once $D_X$ is specified: KO-dim arithmetic, J construction, inner fluctuations all generalize directly. The Yukawa-undetermined limitation transfers as "$\mathcal{T}_X$'s parameters are input, not derived."

For *external* (real-space-multi-particle) second factors — which is exactly what W1a, W1b, and W1c need — A8' would require building $\mathcal{T}_X$ as a *spectral triple of a quantum-mechanical particle at a different focal length*. Track 3's S3 surprise is load-bearing here: **the closest published thing to an "atomic spectral triple" is GeoVac itself**, and Track NI's 26-qubit deuterium PoC may be the first explicit construction in the published-or-Zenodo literature of a Connes-style multi-particle real-space spectral triple. **Speculation (marked):** if Track NI's nuclear-electronic register were promoted from a tensor product of Hilbert spaces to a tensor product of *spectral triples* (giving the proton register its own algebra, Dirac, and real structure rather than just being a Hilbert-space sector with no Dirac data of its own), that would be A8 applied across spatial focal lengths rather than internal-symmetry focal lengths, and would close W1a structurally. This is not in the existing Track NI memos. It is a candidate research direction.

The relationship to W1a/b/c: **closing W1a requires building an A8-class bridge for spatial coordinates rather than internal symmetries**. The architectural extension is parallel — same KO-dim arithmetic, same J construction, same inner-fluctuation classification — but the inner factor changes from a finite matrix algebra to a (truncated) spectral triple of a quantum nucleus, and the cross-coupling becomes a spatial two-body operator (multipole-expanded $V_{eN}$ across registers) rather than a Yukawa. The Pachucki–Patkóš–Yerokhin 2023 Hamiltonian is the calibration target. The published "atom spectral triple" literature is essentially empty (Track 3 surprise S3); GeoVac would be establishing rather than transcribing it.

W3 (inner-factor parameter selection) is *not* closed by A8'-for-W1a: the value of the cross-coupling (e.g. the proton charge, the proton magnetic moment, the Zemach radius) is still an input, not derived. W3 is structurally orthogonal to A8'. **Speculation:** W3 may be permanently calibration-side regardless of how many A8'-class bridges are built; the inner-factor input data tier of Paper 18 (added 2026-05-07) is best read as the framework's honest scope statement about what it does not autonomously generate.

## Section 5: Frontier-of-field framing

The PI's pushback on the structural-skeleton framing was that real physics composes focal lengths daily — atoms, molecules, nuclei, hyperfine splittings, multi-loop QED all involve multiple scales. The honest response, given Tracks 1–3, is a graded one:

| Wall | Closure path | Frontier-of-field status |
|:-----|:-------------|:-------------------------|
| **W1a** (cross-register coordinate operator) | **Internal to GeoVac.** Tools 5/6/7/8 already implement most of the machinery; the missing piece is a multi-λ Shibuya-Wulfman (Candidate 3) plus an A8'-class bridge promoting the proton register to a spectral triple (Candidate 1). Calibrated against Pachucki et al. 2023. | **Tooling-addressable.** Real physics (atomic spectroscopy) routinely closes this wall via Foldy–Wouthuysen reduction; GeoVac's version would be a discrete-S³ spectral-triple analog. |
| **W1b** (magnetization-distribution) | Internal to GeoVac architecturally, but requires QCD-internal magnetization input ($r_Z$ as a Layer-2 focal length). Same A8'-class extension as W1a but with a different cross-coupling. | **Tooling-addressable**, but the input data ($r_Z$) is QCD-internal calibration, not GeoVac-internal. |
| **W1c** (cross-center frozen-core screening) | Internal to GeoVac. Sprint 7b made partial progress for SO splitting; the PES extension is mechanical once cross-center screening is wired through the multipole expansion. | **Tooling-addressable**, narrowest in scope of the W1 sub-walls. |
| **W2a** (multi-loop renormalization) | **Frontier of the spectral-action program.** Marcolli–vS 2014 partially addresses; muon-conversion EFT chain (JHEP 05 (2025) 171) is the QFT archetype; no published NCG framework solves counterterm generation autonomously. | **Frontier-of-field.** Track 3 surprise S6: this is the standard renormalization story translated into the spectral-action setting. GeoVac's failure is structurally identical to Connes–Chamseddine 1996's. |
| **W2b** (cross-manifold spectral-triple composition) | **Frontier of NCG.** Latrémolière 2026 closes the almost-commutative case (one infinite + one finite); two infinite metric spectral triples is open in the published literature. | **Frontier-of-field.** Track 3 surprise S2: the G4b cross-manifold blocker is not a GeoVac-specific limitation — it is a genuinely open NCG question. Closing it would itself be a substantial mathematical contribution. |
| **W3** (inner-factor parameter selection) | **Calibration-side.** The "second packing axiom" question. PROVEN structural absence (G3's commuting-$\mathbb{Z}_2$ theorem). | **Calibration-side.** Mirrors the standard NCG-SM situation: Connes' SM construction also prescribes $D_F$ rather than deriving it. May be a permanent open question of the spectral-action program. |

**The honest reply to the PI's pushback:** real physics closes W1a/b/c daily because real physics has the spatial coordinate operator built in by default — non-relativistic QM textbooks just write $H = T_e + T_N + V_{eN}(r_e, R_N)$ without thinking. GeoVac built the cross-register architecture (Track NI) but has not yet built the cross-register *coordinate operator*. Closing W1a internally is plausible (Candidate 1 above) and would constitute novel work at the intersection of NCG and atomic physics rather than a translation exercise.

W2a and W2b are different. They are at the broader NCG / spectral-action frontier, and "real physics composes them daily" only because real physics imports flat-space counterterm machinery from outside the spectral-action paradigm. The honest scope statement is that these walls are not GeoVac-specific failures but spectral-action-program-wide open problems — and that framing belongs in Paper 32 §VIII when next touched (Candidate 4).

W3 may be permanently calibration-side. The "second packing axiom" question — whether some structure beyond the Paper 0 packing axioms generates inner-factor data — is the speculative frontier. No concrete proposal exists in the project record.

## Section 6: Open questions for PI review

Five explicit decision points emerge from Phase A:

**Q1.** Whether to attack W1a aggressively now (Candidate 1: A8'-for-W1a Pachucki port at 6–10 weeks, novel-math sprint at NCG-atomic-physics intersection) or wait for Candidate 4 (W3-cataloguing + Paper 32 §VIII frontier framing) and Candidate 2 (Hekkelman–McDonald compatibility check) to land first. Argument for waiting: Candidates 2 and 4 are small, sharpen the framing, and potentially identify whether W1a's correct closure is internal or hybrid (importing Hekkelman–McDonald asymptotics could constrain the multi-λ Shibuya-Wulfman approach). Argument for attacking now: Candidate 1 is the highest-yield direction and Pachucki et al. 2023 is a calibrated, time-stable target.

**Q2.** Whether W3 (inner-factor parameter selection) should be its own wall or a sub-wall of W2a. Argument for W3-distinct: B12's failure mode (Yukawa not selected, $\| \cdot \|_\text{op}$ residual = 2 between commuting $\mathbb{Z}_2$'s) is structurally distinct from B3's (counterterms not generated for a divergent series). Argument for W3 ⊂ W2a: every multi-loop renormalization counterterm IS an inner-factor input in Paper 18's six-tier sense; under that reading W2a is W3 restricted to RG counterterms. Track 1's audit recommends W3 as distinct; this synthesis follows that recommendation but flags it as a framing choice not a forced one.

**Q3.** Whether Track NI deserves a Zenodo memo as a standalone real-space multi-particle Connes-style spectral-triple contribution before any further multi-focal work. Track 3 surprise S3 makes this surprisingly substantive: the closest published "atom spectral triple" appears to be GeoVac itself. **Speculative claim**: Track NI may be more original than the "we live in the Marcolli–vS lineage" framing suggests. Recommendation: small Phase B sprint to write a positioning memo verifying the literature gap, deciding whether to formalize as Paper 39 or as a Zenodo deposit, and updating Paper 32 §V cross-references.

**Q4.** Whether to write a brief "frontier-of-field" framing memo for Paper 32 §VIII naming W2a/W2b as broader spectral-action open problems — separating GeoVac-internal walls (W1a/b/c) from frontier-of-field walls (W2a/W2b). This is Candidate 4 above, ranked fourth. Argument for: tightens Paper 32's positioning, adds Marcolli–vS 2014 + Hekkelman–McDonald 2024 + Latrémolière 2026 citations that Track 3 flagged as missing. Argument against: Paper 32 §VIII is currently in equilibrium and edits may invite scope creep. Recommendation: yes, but defer to immediately after a Phase B sprint lands, rather than as a standalone effort.

**Q5.** Whether the multi-λ Shibuya-Wulfman with mismatched bra/ket (Candidate 3) should be done first as a standalone algebraic sprint, or as Phase 1 of Candidate 1 (with the second phase being the spectral-triple promotion + Pachucki port). Argument for standalone-first: the algebraic question (does the multipole expansion still terminate at $L_\text{max}=2\max(l_e,l_N)$ across mismatched exponents?) is well-posed and small (4–6 weeks); its outcome determines whether Candidate 1's full path is viable. Argument for-bundled: doing them together preserves momentum and produces a single coherent sprint result. Recommendation: standalone-first if the multi-λ termination is the binary risk; bundled if the algebraic question can be quickly resolved by inspection.
