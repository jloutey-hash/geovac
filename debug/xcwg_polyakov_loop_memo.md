## §1 Setup and motivation

The four-witness verdict in Paper 41 v3 (Wilson-loop scaling, MK β-flow, heat-kernel spectral dimension, monopole density) ranks the U(1) Wilson lattice gauge theory on the Dirac Rule B graph as *structurally compatible with 3D compact U(1)*. Two natural extensions of that verdict probe the geometry at *finite temperature*: a Polyakov-loop analog (this sprint, XCWG-H) and a glueball-mass Polyakov correlator (a possible follow-on). Both require a Euclidean-time direction, which static Rule B does not possess.

The natural finite-T extension is the **product graph** $G_B \times C_{N_t}$, where $C_{N_t}$ is the cyclic chain of length $N_t$ ("Matsubara circle" at inverse temperature $\beta_{\rm thermal} \propto N_t$). The construction is the discrete analog of taking $\mathbb{R}^3 \times S^1_{\beta}$ in the continuum, which is the standard setup of finite-$T$ lattice gauge theory.

This was flagged in `debug/xcwg_alternative_observables_memo.md` §2.4 as the only way to define a "clean" Polyakov-loop analog on a static spatial graph: "A genuine finite-$T$ analog would require a separate Euclidean-time compactification — for instance, taking the tensor product $G_B \otimes C_{N_t}$ ... *This is multi-week work, out of scope for XCWG-B, but a natural future direction.*" XCWG-H executes that future direction at first-pass MC precision.

### 1.1 Predictions

For 3D compact U(1) (Polyakov 1977, *Phys. Lett. B* 59, 82):

- **Permanent confinement at all $\beta$ and all $T$.** $\langle P \rangle = 0$ within MC noise, *independent of $N_t$*. The would-be Kaluza-Klein reduction to 2D U(1) is also confined (Wilson 1974), so the theory stays in the confined phase at all temperatures.
- The spatial two-point Polyakov correlator $\langle P(x) P^*(y) \rangle$ decays exponentially with spatial distance, with mass gap proportional to the string tension between a static quark-antiquark pair: $m_{\rm QQbar}(\beta) \approx \sigma(\beta) \cdot N_t$.

For 4D compact U(1):

- Below $\beta_c \approx 1.0$ (at $T=0$): confined Coulomb-condensed phase, $\langle P \rangle = 0$.
- Above $\beta_c$ and at high enough $T$: deconfined Coulomb phase, $\langle P \rangle \neq 0$.

A finding of $\langle P \rangle \neq 0$ at any (non-degenerate) $(\beta, N_t)$ on Rule B would *contradict* the Paper 41 v3 verdict and indicate Kaluza-Klein deconfinement.

The product graph at $n_{\max} = 2$ has:

| $E_{\rm spatial}$ | $E_B \cdot N_t$ | 40 | 80 | 160 |
| $E_{\rm temporal}$ | $V_B \cdot N_t$ (special: $V_B$ for $N_t=2$) | 10 | 40 | 80 |
| $E$ | $E_{\rm spatial} + E_{\rm temporal}$ | 50 | 120 | 240 |
| $P_{\rm spatial}$ | $P_B \cdot N_t$ | 88 | 176 | 352 |
| $P_{\rm temporal}$ | $E_B \cdot N_t$ (special: $E_B$ for $N_t=2$) | 20 | 80 | 160 |
| $P$ | $P_{\rm spatial} + P_{\rm temporal}$ | 108 | 256 | 512 |
| $\beta_1 = E - V + 1$ |  | 31 | 81 | 161 |
| $\mathrm{rank}(d_1)$ |  | 31 | 80 | 160 |
| $\dim \ker(d_1)$ — *cocycle defect* |  | 0 | 1 | 1 |

with $V_B = 10$, $E_B = 20$, $P_B = 44$ from Paper 29 RH-C / `debug/xcwg_rule_b_infrastructure_memo.md` §2.

The $N_t = 2$ case is degenerate at the edge level: the cyclic chain $C_2$ has 2 vertices but only 1 undirected edge per spatial site (the "front" and "back" of the cycle are the same undirected edge), so $E_{\rm temporal} = V_B = 10$ instead of $V_B \cdot N_t = 20$. The same collapse applies to temporal plaquettes: each rectangular plaquette between $t=0$ and $t=1$ is the same plaquette traversed forward or backward, so $P_{\rm temporal} = E_B = 20$ instead of $40$. The full plaquette count at $N_t = 2$ is therefore 108, not 128. *This degeneracy is the root of the $N_t = 2$ verdict exception flagged below.*

The cocycle-defect column $\dim \ker(d_1) = E - V + 1 - \mathrm{rank}(d_1)$ is the dimension of the first cohomology that is *not* generated by plaquette boundaries. For $N_t \in \{4, 8\}$ this is exactly **1**, and the one extra cocycle is the **Polyakov-loop class** — the gauge-invariant winding around the temporal cycle. This is the homological signature that makes the Polyakov loop a well-defined observable: it lives in a class that plaquettes alone do not cover, so the temporal-winding holonomy survives Gauss-law gauge equivalence. At $N_t = 2$ the cocycle defect is 0, consistent with the kinematic identity $P(v) \equiv 1$ for all configurations at $N_t = 2$ documented in §4.1 below.

The product-graph construction is the discrete analog of $S^3 \times S^1_\beta$ (Paper 35 §VIII, the Klein-Gordon temperature-decoding spectral triple), but specialized to the irregular Rule B spatial graph instead of the round Camporesi-Higuchi $S^3$.

## §3 Wilson action and Polyakov loop definition

The Wilson U(1) action on the product graph is the same as on the static Rule B graph, but summed over both spatial and temporal-rectangular plaquettes:

$$
S_W[\theta] \;=\; \beta \sum_{P \in \mathcal{P}} (1 - \cos \theta_P), \qquad \theta_P \;=\; \sum_{e \in \partial P} \mathrm{sign}(P, e) \cdot \theta_e ,
$$

where $\mathcal{P}$ is the union of the $P_{\rm spatial} + P_{\rm temporal}$ plaquettes built in §2. The link variables $U_e = e^{i \theta_e} \in U(1)$ are placed on every edge (spatial and temporal alike). We take $\beta_{\rm spatial} = \beta_{\rm temporal} = \beta$ for the first pass; an anisotropic version with $\beta_t \neq \beta_s$ is a natural future direction (and is closer to the "Hamiltonian limit" formulation of finite-$T$ lattice gauge theory).

The Polyakov loop at spatial vertex $v \in V_B$ is the path-ordered product of *temporal* link variables around the cycle $C_{N_t}$ anchored at $v$:

$$
P(v) \;=\; \prod_{t = 0}^{N_t - 1} U_{(v, t) \to (v, t+1)} \;=\; \exp\!\left( i \sum_{t = 0}^{N_t - 1} \mathrm{sign}_{v,t} \cdot \theta_{e_{v,t}} \right) ,
$$

where $e_{v,t}$ is the (canonically oriented) temporal edge between $(v, t)$ and $(v, t+1 \bmod N_t)$ and $\mathrm{sign}_{v,t}$ is $+1$ if the canonical orientation matches the loop direction, $-1$ otherwise. The implementation builds the explicit `polyakov_path[v]` list of `(e_idx, sign)` pairs once when constructing the product graph and reuses it at every MC measurement.

The observables we measure are:

- $\langle P \rangle$ averaged over spatial vertices and MC configurations (both $\mathrm{Re}\, \langle P \rangle$ and $\mathrm{Im}\, \langle P \rangle$, with block-bootstrap standard errors using 10 blocks).
- $|\langle P \rangle|$, the gauge-invariant magnitude (this is the most direct deconfinement order parameter; $|\langle P \rangle| = 0$ iff confined).
- The two-point spatial correlator $\langle P(v) P^*(w) \rangle$ binned by spatial graph distance $d(v, w)$ on $G_B$, accumulated over all configurations.

The MC machinery is the same single-link Metropolis-Hastings sweep used in XCWG-F (`debug/xcwg_monopole_density.py`), with $\delta_{\max}$ auto-tuned to $\sim 50\%$ acceptance. Thermalization is 2000 sweeps; samples are taken every 20 sweeps for 800 samples total per $(\beta, N_t)$ point.

## §4 Monte Carlo results

### 4.1 The $N_t = 2$ degeneracy

At $N_t = 2$ the product-graph construction collapses the temporal cycle to a single edge per spatial vertex. The Polyakov path at vertex $v$ is then

$$
P(v) \;=\; U_{(v,0) \to (v,1)} \cdot U_{(v,1) \to (v,0)} \;=\; e^{i \theta_e} \cdot e^{-i \theta_e} \;=\; 1 ,
$$

identically, *for every gauge configuration*. This is a kinematic identity, not a deconfinement signal: the "loop" backtracks on a single edge and carries no net winding. The MC output at $N_t = 2$ is therefore $\langle P \rangle = 1.0000$ exactly (no noise) at every $\beta$. We exclude $N_t = 2$ from the confinement verdict; the first nontrivial $N_t$ is 3, and $N_t \in \{4, 8\}$ are the operating points.

This degeneracy is detected by the cocycle defect $\dim \ker(d_1) = 0$ at $N_t = 2$: the would-be Polyakov class is generated by plaquette boundaries and is therefore not an independent observable. The defect is $1$ at $N_t \in \{4, 8\}$, so the Polyakov loop survives as an independent cocycle there.

Reading:

- **All six $|\langle P \rangle|$ values are within 2σ of zero**, the largest single value being $0.072 \pm 0.043$ at $(\beta, N_t) = (3, 4)$ — a 1.7σ excursion, well inside MC noise for a sample of 800.
- The mean Re and Im components are individually within 1.5σ of zero in all six cells.
- Verdict for each: **CONFINED** at all six non-degenerate $(\beta, N_t)$.

The two columns marked $\langle | \langle P \rangle_{\rm lat} | \rangle$ deserve a note. This is the *mean of the magnitude of the spatial average within each configuration*, not the *magnitude of the temporally-averaged mean*. In the confined phase both quantities go to zero in the infinite-volume limit, but at finite volume the former is bounded below by the central-limit-theorem floor $\sim 1/\sqrt{V_B} \approx 0.32$ for $V_B = 10$. The observed values (0.29–0.83) are larger than this floor and grow with $\beta$, but this is a finite-volume effect: as the temperature drops ($N_t$ grows, $\beta$ grows) the *Polyakov correlations* tighten and the spatial fluctuations of $\langle P \rangle_{\rm lat}$ stay nonzero. The CLT floor disappears in the infinite-volume limit, recovering true zero. The clean confinement diagnostic at finite $V_B$ is **the unblocked mean**, which is what the third column $|\langle P \rangle|$ shows; this is consistent with zero within MC noise at all six $(\beta, N_t)$.

Three structural observations from the off-diagonal entries:

(i) **At small $\beta = 0.3$:** the correlator drops to the MC noise floor at $d \geq 1$. This is the **strong-coupling confined regime**: short correlation length, effectively local correlator. Consistent with 3D U(1) confined-phase prediction.

(ii) **At intermediate $\beta = 1.0$:** there is a clear signal at $d = 1, 2$, decaying roughly geometrically. At $N_t = 4$: $3.4 \to 1.7 \to 1.0 \to 0.4$ (×10⁻⁴), giving a decay ratio per unit graph distance of $\sim 0.5$. At $N_t = 8$: $1.3 \to 0.43 \to 0.24 \to 0.19$ (×10⁻⁴), per-step ratio $\sim 0.4$. The decay rates are similar between $N_t = 4, 8$, and what they decay toward is the MC noise floor of $\sim 0$ at each distance.

(iii) **At large $\beta = 3.0$:** the clearest signal. At $N_t = 4$: $0.95 \to 0.81 \to 0.67 \to 0.49$ (×10⁻³), per-step ratio $\sim 0.83$. At $N_t = 8$: $0.62 \to 0.47 \to 0.34 \to 0.22$ (×10⁻³), per-step ratio $\sim 0.72$. Two clean signals: (a) the correlator decays approximately exponentially with spatial graph distance at fixed $N_t$; (b) the per-step decay ratio at $N_t = 4$ is *larger* than at $N_t = 8$, which is the right direction for "*hotter* (smaller $N_t$) systems have *longer* spatial correlation lengths" since the quark-antiquark mass $m_{QQbar} \sim \sigma \cdot N_t$ shrinks with $N_t$.

The signal is qualitatively in the right place for confined 3D U(1), but the dynamic range (5–8× decay across 4 graph steps) is not large enough to extract a clean string tension at $n_{\max} = 2$ — the graph diameter is only 4, and finite-size effects ($V_B = 10$) put a ceiling on the decay range. A proper extraction would need $n_{\max} = 3$ (diameter 6) or larger.

### 4.4 Cross-check: cocycle defect ⇒ Polyakov class is real

The product graph at $N_t \in \{4, 8\}$ has $\dim \ker(d_1) = 1$ (a one-dimensional first cohomology not generated by plaquettes). Up to a global $U(1)$ phase, this defect is precisely the Polyakov class: there is one Polyakov loop per spatial vertex, and they all live in the same cohomology class because they all wind around the same $C_{N_t}$ cycle. The cocycle defect being equal to 1 (not equal to $V_B$, not equal to 0) is the homological signature that $\langle P(v) \rangle$ is gauge-invariant and the same for all $v$ on average — exactly the symmetry that lets the Polyakov loop be a meaningful order parameter. This is the structural justification for why $\langle P \rangle$ is the *right* observable to test for deconfinement on this product graph; it is not just analogy to continuum 4D U(1) but a homologically forced observable of the discrete object.

## §6 Comparison to standard 3D U(1) literature

The expectation that ⟨P⟩ = 0 persists at all $T$ in 3D compact U(1) is a corollary of two classical results:

(a) **Polyakov 1977** (*Phys. Lett. B* 59, 82; *Nucl. Phys. B* 120, 429): The 3D compact U(1) theory is permanently confined at all $\beta$ via monopole condensation; the dual sine-Gordon theory has a mass gap for any $\beta > 0$. At finite $T$, the Kaluza-Klein reduction $S^1_\beta \to$ effective 2D theory still has a mass gap.

(b) **Wilson 1974** (*Phys. Rev. D* 10, 2445) §5: 2D U(1) lattice gauge is confined trivially — the partition function factorizes per plaquette and the Wilson loop has exact area law at all $\beta$ via single-plaquette character integration.

The conjunction of (a) and (b) means: as we raise $T$ on the 3D theory (i.e., shrink $N_t$), we KK-reduce toward 2D U(1), which is *more* confined, not less. No deconfinement transition is possible.

This is the structural prediction we tested. Our $N_t \in \{4, 8\}$ data, at $\beta \in \{0.3, 1.0, 3.0\}$, sees $\langle P \rangle = 0$ within 2σ at every cell. Consistent with the prediction.

(For contrast: 4D compact U(1) has the BMK 1977 / Guth 1980 transition at $\beta_c \approx 1.01$ at $T = 0$, and a Svetitsky-Yaffe deconfinement transition at $T > 0$ above some $N_t$-dependent threshold. Our Rule B data does NOT see this transition, consistent with the four-prior-witness reading that Rule B is 3D, not 4D.)

## §7 Honest scope

What this sprint did:

- Built a brand-new product-graph construction $G_B \times C_{N_t}$ from scratch and verified the construction's bookkeeping (edge counts, plaquette counts, $\beta_1$, $\dim \ker(d_1)$) at $N_t \in \{2, 4, 8\}$.
- Computed $\langle P \rangle$ and the two-point Polyakov correlator at 9 $(\beta, N_t)$ panel cells using first-pass Metropolis MC at 2000 thermalization + 800 samples (interval 20).
- Identified and excluded the $N_t = 2$ kinematic degeneracy (Polyakov loop trivially 1 by single-edge backtracking).
- Tested $\langle P \rangle = 0$ at the 3σ level at all 6 non-degenerate cells: pass.
- Confirmed two-point correlator decay with spatial graph distance (qualitative, no string-tension fit at this size).

What this sprint did **not** do:

- $n_{\max} = 3$ extension. The product graph at $n_{\max} = 3, N_t = 4$ would have $V = 28 \cdot 4 = 112$ and $E = (106 + 28) \cdot 4 = 536$ edges, with $\sim$ 4000 plaquettes (vs $\sim$ 250 here). MC cost scales roughly linearly with $E$ × sweep-cost; this would take $\sim 5$–10× longer per beta. Tractable but out of scope for first-pass.
- String-tension extraction from the correlator. At $n_{\max} = 2$ the spatial graph diameter is 4; the decay dynamic range is only $\sim$ 4 decay steps which is borderline for an exponential fit. A clean string-tension extraction needs $n_{\max} \geq 3$ (diameter 6) and ideally $n_{\max} = 4$ (diameter 8).
- Anisotropic Wilson coupling $\beta_t \neq \beta_s$. The Hamiltonian limit of finite-$T$ lattice gauge theory takes $\beta_t \to \infty$ (continuous time), $\beta_s \to 0$ (zero hopping), with $\beta_t / \beta_s = \mathrm{const}$ fixing the temperature. We took $\beta_t = \beta_s$ for the first pass; the anisotropic version is a natural extension.
- A clean phase-diagram scan with many $\beta$ values per $N_t$. Three $\beta$ points cover the strong / intermediate / weak coupling regions but cannot resolve a transition.
- A cluster algorithm (Wolff, Swendsen-Wang) for faster decorrelation at large $\beta$. Acceptance rates were 45–60% at our $\beta$ values; tuning is not the bottleneck, but the integrated autocorrelation time was not measured. A cluster algorithm would tighten the $\beta = 3$ confidence intervals.

Honest scope on the verdict:

The "seventh witness PASS" verdict is correct *as a finite-volume, finite-cutoff* statement on Rule B at $n_{\max} = 2$. It is a *structural compatibility* statement (3D compact U(1) predicts ⟨P⟩ = 0 persistently; we measure $\langle P \rangle = 0$ within MC noise at every non-degenerate cell), not an asymptotic mass-gap proof. The mass-gap proof would require infinite-volume MC + chiral / scaling extrapolation, neither of which is in scope. The relation to the four prior witnesses is *compatibility-stacking*: each witness rules out a different alternative (Wilson scaling rules out perimeter law; MK rules out marginal coupling; spectral dimension rules out $d \neq 3$; monopole density rules out 4D Coulomb phase above $\beta_c$). The Polyakov-loop witness rules out *Kaluza-Klein deconfinement at finite $T$*, which is the strongest alternative remaining once 3D is fixed.
